## Why the Month-to-Month Rate Is So High

VPN providers price monthly subscriptions at a premium for a simple reason: flexibility costs them more risk. When you commit to just one month, you might cancel after 30 days. They need to recoup customer acquisition costs and server infrastructure costs within a short window.

## How Streaming Arbitrage Changes the Math

Subscription arbitrage is the strategy of using a VPN to subscribe to streaming services at regional pricing — paying the local rate for Netflix Turkey, YouTube Premium India, or Spotify Argentina instead of the US or UK rate.

The price gaps are substantial. Netflix in Turkey can run under $4/month. YouTube Premium in India is roughly $2–3/month. Spotify in Argentina has historically been under $2/month. In the US or UK, those same services run $13–18/month each.

A VPN is the mechanism that makes arbitrage possible. It routes your internet connection through a server in your target country, allowing you to register and pay as a local subscriber. For this use case, the VPN is not a discretionary expense — it is the tool that generates the savings.

This is where the two-year plan math becomes compelling at a household level:

- **Breakeven point:** less than one month of Netflix savings covers the per-month VPN cost

Scale to two or three streaming services and the VPN pays for itself in the first billing cycle, then saves $20–35/month every month afterward. Over a 24-month plan, total savings on three services can clear $400–600 versus paying full US/UK pricing for the same subscriptions.

## What to Check Before Committing to Any Two-Year VPN Plan

If you are evaluating providers beyond Surfshark, these are the criteria that separate usable two-year plans from ones that look cheap until you try to use them.

**WireGuard support.** Any VPN still defaulting to OpenVPN or L2TP will deliver slower speeds on equivalent hardware. For streaming and general use, WireGuard is non-negotiable.

**Independent audit of the no-logs policy.** Self-reported no-logs claims are worth very little. Look for an audit by a named, reputable firm — Deloitte, PwC, Cure53 are the ones worth citing. If a provider has no audit, treat the privacy claims as unverified.

**Streaming unblock capability in your target regions.** Test this within the money-back window. Not all providers reliably unblock Netflix, Amazon Prime, or local streaming platforms in Turkey, India, or Argentina. Confirm it works for your specific use case before the refund period closes.

**Kill switch availability.** A kill switch automatically cuts your internet connection if the VPN drops unexpectedly, preventing your real IP from being exposed mid-session. Standard on major providers — confirm it is present and enabled.

**30-day money-back guarantee.** This converts the two-year commitment into a 30-day trial. Any reputable provider offers this. If a provider does not, walk away.
