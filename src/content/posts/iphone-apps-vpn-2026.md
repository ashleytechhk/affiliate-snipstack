## How to Choose the Right iPhone VPN App for Your Situation

The right choice depends on what you are optimizing for. If cost savings are the primary goal and you want the lowest long-term subscription price with a reliable iOS app, Surfshark is the answer. If you are targeting a specific streaming service known for aggressive VPN blocking and need the highest success rate, ExpressVPN's premium is justified. If privacy is your primary driver and streaming savings are a bonus, ProtonVPN's audited no-logs policy is worth the higher price.

One underrated factor on iPhone specifically: how the app handles the constant network switching iOS does automatically. Your iPhone moves between cellular and Wi-Fi dozens of times a day, and a poorly optimized VPN drops its tunnel every time. WireGuard-based apps (Surfshark, NordVPN, PIA) handle reconnection fastest. IKEv2 is the fallback that iOS handles natively. OpenVPN is slowest to reconnect and should be avoided on mobile unless you have a specific reason to use it.

### Will a VPN app slow down my iPhone's internet speed?

Yes, but the impact is typically small with a well-optimized provider. Lower-quality VPNs or congested servers can produce more significant slowdowns.

Rarely, and with significant trade-offs. Free VPN apps typically impose data caps, run slower servers, cover fewer locations, and face higher rates of IP blocks from streaming platforms. Many free apps also monetize through user data collection. A paid provider in the $2–$4/month range eliminates all of these problems and typically pays for itself within a month of subscription savings.

### Which iPhone VPN protocol should I use for streaming?

WireGuard is the best choice for most iPhone users. It is fast, battery-efficient, and reconnects almost instantly when your iPhone switches between Wi-Fi and cellular. IKEv2 is a solid fallback that iOS supports natively. Avoid OpenVPN on mobile — it reconnects slowly after network changes and uses more battery.
