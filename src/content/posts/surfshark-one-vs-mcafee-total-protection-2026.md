## The Bloatware Problem With McAfee

If you have used McAfee Total Protection for more than one renewal cycle, you already know the experience: the introductory year is affordable, the software installs additional components you did not ask for, and the renewal invoice arrives at a price that feels disconnected from the original deal.

The software itself is functional as antivirus. Detection rates from independent labs like AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives have been consistently good. But McAfee built its product around maximizing the footprint — on your computer, in your wallet, in your notification tray.

The CPU and memory overhead from McAfee's background processes is measurable on older or mid-range hardware. Users report sluggish boot times, delayed file opens during scan cycles, and notification fatigue from pop-ups urging password manager adoption, VPN upgrades, and parental control add-ons that may or may not be included in the plan they purchased.

That is the real cost of McAfee Total Protection: not just the subscription fee, but the friction embedded in every interaction with the software.
