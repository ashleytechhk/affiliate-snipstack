## What "Free Virus Scan Online" Actually Means

When you search for a free virus scan online, you will find two very different things:

**1. Online file scanners** — You upload a file. The website checks that file against many antivirus databases. You get a report. Your computer is not touched.

**2. Fake scanner pop-ups** — A website tells you "Your computer has 14 viruses! Click here to fix them." This is almost always a scam. Websites cannot actually scan your computer.

The tools we compare in this guide are all legitimate file scanners (category 1). They are safe to use and useful in specific situations. But please understand what they are: file-checking tools, not full computer protection.

## VirusTotal — The Most Trusted Name in Online Scanning

**What it is:** VirusTotal was founded in 2004 and is now owned by Google. It is the most widely used online file scanner in the world.

**How it works:** You go to [virustotal.com](https://www.virustotal.com), upload a file (or paste a web link), and it checks that file against more than 70 different antivirus programs at the same time. You get a results page showing which engines flagged the file and which passed it.

**What it is good for:**
- You downloaded a file and want a second opinion before opening it
- Your antivirus flagged something and you want to check if it is a real threat or a false alarm
- You received an attachment in email and feel unsure

**What it is not good for:**
- Scanning your whole computer
- Checking if malware is already running on your system
- Ongoing, automatic protection

**Privacy note:** When you upload a file to VirusTotal, that file may be shared with the antivirus companies that partner with them. Do not upload files that contain personal information — bank statements, passport scans, medical documents.

**Verdict:** VirusTotal is the go-to choice for a quick, trustworthy file check. It is free, it is fast, and checking against 70+ engines at once gives you a very reliable result.

## Jotti's Malware Scan — Simple and Straightforward

**What it is:** Jotti is an independent online scanner that has been running since 2006. It checks files against roughly 20 antivirus engines.

**How it works:** Go to [virusscan.jotti.org](https://virusscan.jotti.org), upload your file (up to 250 MB), and get results within a minute or two.

**What makes it different from VirusTotal:**
- Simpler, less cluttered interface — easier to read at a glance
- Fewer engines (20 vs 70+), so results are less comprehensive
- Smaller community — less traffic, slightly faster at off-peak times

**Who it is best for:** If VirusTotal feels overwhelming with too much information on screen, Jotti is a calmer alternative. Same basic idea, just a bit simpler.

**Limitation:** Fewer engines means a higher chance of missing something that a more thorough scan would catch.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can a website actually scan my entire computer for free?**

No. A website cannot access files on your computer unless you specifically upload them. Any pop-up that says "Scanning your computer now…" without you having uploaded anything is almost certainly a scam or fake alert. Do not click any links or call any [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/)s these pop-ups show you. Simply close the browser tab.

**Q: Is VirusTotal safe to use?**

Yes. VirusTotal is a legitimate service owned by Google and used by security researchers worldwide. The only caution is privacy: files you upload may be shared with VirusTotal's partner antivirus companies. Do not upload files containing personal information such as bank statements, medical records, or identity documents.

**Q: If VirusTotal says a file is clean, is it definitely safe to open?**

Mostly, but not with absolute certainty. VirusTotal checks against known threats. A brand-new piece of malware that no antivirus company has catalogued yet may not be detected. This is called a "zero-day" threat.
