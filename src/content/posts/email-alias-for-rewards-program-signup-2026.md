## Why Rewards Programs Are the Worst Offenders

Retail loyalty programs and hotel/airline points accounts are uniquely aggressive on email because their entire business model depends on repeat purchases. Your email address is not just a contact detail — it is the engine that drives traffic back to their stores and booking pages.

**Structural marketing overload.** When you join Starbucks Rewards, Sephora Beauty Insider, Marriott Bonvoy, Target Circle, or Best Buy Plus, you are automatically enrolled in marketing communications. Opt-out flows are buried, pre-ticked, and often country-specific in ways that don't apply if you're travelling or accessing from a different region. One loyalty signup routinely generates 10–15 marketing emails per month.

**Third-party partner sharing.** Rewards programs sell the most complete picture of you that any business holds: your purchase history, location data, preferences, and demographic profile — all attached to your email address. Airlines, hotels, and big-box retailers pass this data to advertising partners, co-branded credit card companies, and affiliate networks. Your email ends up on lists you never agreed to.

**Long-term exposure.** Unlike a one-time e-commerce purchase, rewards programs retain your data for years. A breach of a loyalty database — and they do get breached — puts your real address into circulation permanently. The 2020 Marriott breach exposed over 5 million customer records. The 2022 Starbucks UK app incident leaked customer data. Every program you join with your real address is another potential breach point.

**The alias advantage for rewards.** Because rewards programs must persist for years — you need point-balance updates, redemption confirmations, tier-status notifications — a throwaway inbox that expires is useless here. What you need is a *permanent* forwarding alias you control. One that you can delete the moment you've redeemed your balance and are done with the program.

### Apple Hide My Email — Best for iOS Users

Apple Hide My Email creates random `@privaterelay.appleid.com` forwarding addresses integrated directly into Safari, iOS, and macOS. On an iPhone or Mac, filling in an alias at a Starbucks or Sephora signup takes one tap.

**For rewards programs:** Excellent if you're managing your loyalty accounts primarily through a mobile browser or the programs' iOS apps. The domain is well-trusted and rarely rejected.

**Limitation:** Locked to the Apple ecosystem. Android users, Windows users, and anyone managing loyalty accounts through a non-Apple browser loses the seamless autofill experience. Requires iCloud+ from $0.99/month.

### Gmail Plus Addressing — Free, But Not Private

Gmail's `yourname+rewards@gmail.com` variant delivers to your real inbox and costs nothing.

## FAQ

**Will loyalty programs reject an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-car-dealership-contact-2026/)?**

Major programs — Starbucks Rewards, Marriott Bonvoy, Sephora Beauty Insider, Delta SkyMiles, Target Circle, Best Buy Plus, Kroger, CVS ExtraCare — accept forwarding aliases from established services like Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, and Apple Hide My Email. These tools use domains that pass standard email validation checks. A disposable-inbox service (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail) would likely be rejected; a persistent alias service is a different category and is generally accepted.

**Can I still earn and redeem points with an alias?**

Yes. Your points account is identified by your membership number, not your email address. The email address is only used for sending you notifications. You can log in, check balances, and redeem points entirely through the program's app or website without the email address being involved in the transaction. [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-rewards-program-signup-2026__faq) keeps your forwarding active for as long as you hold the account.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if I delete the alias while I still have points?**

The program can no longer email you — points balance updates, redemption confirmations, and account recovery emails will bounce. Before deleting an alias tied to an active account with a balance, update your email address in the program's account settings first (switch to a new alias), then delete the old one. Only delete without updating if you've redeemed everything and are abandoning the account.

**Is it against the terms of service to use a forwarding alias?**

Loyalty program terms require a valid, deliverable email address — which a forwarding alias satisfies. You're not using an expired throwaway; the alias receives email indefinitely. From the program's perspective, it's a functioning email address. Using a forwarding alias does not violate the terms of any major rewards program.

**How is this different from just unsubscribing from loyalty emails?**

Unsubscribing confirms to the program that your address is active and monitored — a signal that can actually increase the volume of third-party spam sent to it. Unsubscribing also doesn't protect you from data-broker sales or breach exposure. Deleting an alias is a harder cut: every future email to that address bounces immediately, with no confirmation that the address belongs to a real person. It's cleaner, more complete, and doesn't require trusting the program to honor your opt-out.

**Can I use one alias for multiple rewards programs?**

Technically yes, but it defeats the purpose. If you use the same alias for five programs and one sells it to spammers, you won't know which one. One alias per program gives you a precise kill switch and makes it easy to identify which program is the spam source.
