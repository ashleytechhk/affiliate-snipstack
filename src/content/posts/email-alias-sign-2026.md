## What "Signing Up with an Email Alias" Actually Means

When a site asks for your email during registration, you give it a throwaway alias address instead of your real one. The alias forwards all incoming mail to your real inbox, so you still receive confirmation emails, receipts, and account notifications. The site never sees your actual address.

When that alias starts attracting spam — or you just want to cut off a service entirely — you delete the alias. The site can no longer reach you, and your real inbox is unaffected.

It is a small change in how you register for things. The long-term payoff is a permanently quieter inbox and far less exposure in third-party marketing databases.

### Why the standard workarounds don't cut it

Gmail's `+tag` trick (`yourname+shop@gmail.com`) is the most common DIY alias method. It works for basic filtering, but any spammer or data broker can strip the `+tag` and recover your real address in under two seconds. It hides nothing.

A proper alias service generates a completely different address — something like `dusk44maple@altid.net` — with no visible link back to your real inbox. That is the meaningful difference, and it's what this guide covers.

## Troubleshooting

**Confirmation email not arriving after signup.** First, check your spam folder — some mail clients flag forwarded messages. Then check the Alt-ID dashboard for a delivery log. The most common causes are: the alias was mistyped during signup, the forwarding address was not confirmed, or the alias was accidentally paused.

**The site rejected my alias address.** Some platforms run checks against known alias or forwarding domains. If this happens, try generating a different alias (Alt-ID uses multiple domains) or, as a last resort, use your real address for that specific site. The goal is reduced exposure, not zero exceptions.

**Mail arriving from the wrong sender name.** Forwarded messages show the original sender's details in the "From" field but may display the Alt-ID relay address in the "To" field. This is expected behavior — your mail client received a forward, not a direct delivery.

**Forwarding stopped suddenly.** Check that your Surfshark subscription is current. A lapsed subscription pauses alias forwarding without deleting your aliases. Renewing the subscription restores forwarding automatically.

**I want to reply without exposing my real address.** Alt-ID is primarily a receive-and-forward service. For anonymous two-way email, SimpleLogin and Apple Hide My Email have more complete reply-from-alias support. For most users, one-way forwarding is sufficient — the vast majority of alias use cases involve receiving, not replying.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between an email alias and a [temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-generator-2026/)?**
A temporary email self-destructs after a short period and is unsuitable for ongoing accounts. An alias is permanent until you delete it, forwards all mail to your real inbox, and keeps working indefinitely. Use [temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-stop-spam-2026/) for one-time verifications. Use aliases for any account you intend to maintain.

**Can I use an alias with any email provider?**
Yes. Alt-ID and most alias services forward to any inbox — Gmail, Outlook, Proton, Apple Mail, Fastmail, or any custom domain address. Your underlying email provider is irrelevant to the alias setup.

**Will sites know I am using an alias?**
Most will not. A small number of sites run domain checks against known forwarding services and may reject the address. This is uncommon for reputable alias providers. If it happens, try regenerating a different alias (different domain) or contact the site's support.

**Do I need a separate alias service from my VPN?**
Not if you already have Surfshark One — Alt-ID is included. If you use a different VPN provider, you will need a standalone alias service. [Surfshark Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-sign-2026__faq) is worth considering precisely because it removes that redundancy.

**How many aliases do I actually need?**
More than you expect. If you commit to a new alias for every signup, you will accumulate 30 to 50 within a month. This is why an unlimited-alias plan matters. Free tiers on standalone services typically cap at 10 to 15 aliases — that ceiling arrives faster than most users anticipate.

**Is it legal to sign up for sites with an alias email?**
Yes, in virtually every jurisdiction. Using a forwarding alias to protect your primary email address is a standard privacy practice and entirely lawful. The narrow exception is platforms with explicit identity verification requirements, where their terms of service may prohibit alias addresses — read the terms for high-stakes services like financial accounts.

**What happens to emails already received if I delete an alias?**
Emails already forwarded to your real inbox remain there permanently. Deleting an alias only affects future messages — they will bounce or be silently dropped depending on the provider's settings. Your existing received mail is never affected.

**Can I use Alt-ID on mobile?**
The Alt-ID dashboard is fully mobile-responsive and works in any browser on iOS or Android. The browser extension is desktop-only, but you can generate and copy aliases from the web dashboard on any device and paste them into mobile app signup forms.
