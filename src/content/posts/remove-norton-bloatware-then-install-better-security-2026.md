## How to Remove Norton Bloatware From Windows

Do this cleanly before installing anything new. Running two antivirus programs at once causes conflicts, false alerts, and real slowdowns — so remove first, then install.

### Step 1 — Cancel Auto-Renewal First

Log into your Norton account online, go to **My Subscriptions**, and turn off auto-renew before you touch the install. This stops you from being charged again while you're mid-switch. Canceling auto-renewal does not remove your current protection — it just stops the next charge.

### Step 2 — Uninstall via Windows "Add or Remove Programs"

1. Open **Settings** → **Apps** → **Installed apps** (older Windows versions: **Apps & features**).
2. Scroll or search for **Norton** (or **McAfee**) in the list.
3. Click the three-dot menu or the entry itself, then select **Uninstall**.
4. Follow the on-screen prompts and restart when asked.

This standard uninstall is fine for most cases, but Norton and McAfee both leave behind extra files, registry entries, and background services more often than typical software — which is why a second pass is worth doing.

### Step 3 — Run the Norton Remove and Reinstall Tool for a Clean Sweep

1. Search for **"Norton Remove and Reinstall tool"** (or the equivalent McAfee Consumer Product Removal tool) and download it only from the official Norton or McAfee support page.
2. Close all browsers and other open programs first.
3. Right-click the downloaded file and choose **Run as administrator**.
4. Follow the prompts — the tool will do a deeper cleanup than the standard uninstaller and will typically restart your computer when done.

### Step 4 — Verify Nothing Is Still Running

After the restart, open **Task Manager** (Ctrl + Shift + Esc), click the **Processes** tab, and confirm there are no Norton or McAfee processes listed. If you still see browser toolbars, remove them manually from your browser's Extensions settings.
