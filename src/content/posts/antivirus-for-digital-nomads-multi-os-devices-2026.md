## Why Digital Nomads Over 60 Are a Prime Target

You might think hackers go after large companies. Many do. But a growing share of cybercrime targets individuals, particularly older adults who travel.

Here is why you stand out as a target:

**You carry more devices than you realize.** Most digital nomads over 60 travel with at least a laptop and a smartphone. Many also carry a tablet. Each device is a separate door into your personal data — your bank accounts, your email, your passwords.

**Public Wi-Fi is everywhere, and it is dangerous.** Airports, hotel lobbies, cafes, coworking spaces — these networks are not secure. Attackers can sit on the same network and quietly intercept your data while you sip your coffee. This is called a "man-in-the-middle" attack, and it works whether you are on Windows, Mac, or Android.

**Scam emails and fake websites look exactly like the real thing.** In 2024, the FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center reported that Americans over 60 lost more than $3.4 billion to cybercrime — the highest of any age group. Many attacks started with a single convincing email or a website that looked like a bank.

**Different devices run different operating systems — and each one has different vulnerabilities.** A virus that attacks Windows behaves differently from one designed for macOS. Malware built for Android will not look like malware built for a laptop. Having protection on only one device leaves the others exposed.

## What "Multi-OS Protection" Actually Means

When an antivirus product advertises multi-OS support, it means the same subscription works on Windows, macOS, and Android devices — each with software built specifically for that operating system.

This matters because:

- **Windows** is still the most targeted platform. Ransomware, phishing trojans, and spyware are most commonly written for Windows machines.
- **macOS** is not immune. The belief that Macs "don't get viruses" is outdated. Mac-specific malware has grown significantly since 2020.
- **Android** phones are increasingly targeted through fake apps, malicious links in text messages, and rogue Wi-Fi networks.

A product that only covers one of these operating systems leaves you with gaps. A product that covers all three — under one plan, with one password to remember — is far simpler to manage.

If installing the app on a second device requires finding a license key, navigating a complex dashboard, and downloading a different version for each OS, you will probably skip it. The best options for seniors make adding a device a two-minute task.

You should not notice antivirus running in the background. If it slows your device to a crawl or drains your battery on a flight, you will turn it off. Look for software with a small performance footprint.
