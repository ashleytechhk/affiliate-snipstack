## Why Kaspersky Is No Longer Safe for US Residents

### The US Government Ban — What Actually Happened

In June 2024, the US Department of Commerce issued a final ruling banning Kaspersky software from being sold or updated in the United States. The prohibition took full effect on September 29, 2024.

This was not a minor policy note. It means:

- **Kaspersky can no longer sell its software** to new customers in the US
- **Existing users stopped receiving security updates** after the effective date
- **[Antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-for-iphone-2026/) without updates is dangerous** — it cannot recognize threats that were created after the last update

Think of it this way: an [antivirus that](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-that-detects-zero-day-threats-2026/) does not get updates is like a lock on your door that the locksmith stopped supporting. New lock-picks still work on it. Old threats it knew about are blocked. New ones walk right through.

Multiple other Western governments, including those in the UK and Germany, also issued guidance warning against Kaspersky use on sensitive systems.

### What This Means If You Still Have Kaspersky Installed

If Kaspersky is currently on your computer, it is not protecting you the way it used to. The virus database has not been updated since late 2024. Every new piece of malware, scam tool, or ransomware created since then is invisible to it.

## What Seniors Are Most at Risk From in 2026

Before comparing the two products, it helps to understand what threats you actually face. Scammers specifically target people over 60 because they tend to be polite, trusting, and less familiar with online tricks.

The most common threats include:

- **Fake [tech support](https://snipstack.io/tech-support-scam-phone-call-identify-2026/) calls** — someone claims your computer is infected and asks you to pay them to fix it or give them remote access
- **Phishing emails** — messages that look like they come from your bank, Medicare, or the IRS asking for personal information
- **Ransomware** — software that secretly encrypts all your files and demands payment to unlock them
- **Fake shopping websites** — online stores designed to steal your credit card number
- **Spyware** — software that silently records everything you type, including passwords and account numbers

A good antivirus catches most of these threats before they can do any damage. But only if the antivirus is up to date.
