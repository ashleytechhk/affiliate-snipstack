## "But I Use Linux — Isn't That Already Safe?"

This is the most common thing seniors hear from their children or grandchildren when they first switch to Ubuntu. And for many years, it was mostly true. Linux had far fewer viruses written for it than Windows.

That has changed.

Cybercriminals follow the money and the people. In 2024 and into 2025, attacks targeting Linux systems rose sharply — not because Linux became weaker, but because more people are now using it, including older adults who may be less familiar with warning signs. According to cybersecurity researchers at Kaspersky and Malwarebytes, Linux malware detections increased by over 40% in a single year.

More importantly, the biggest risks to seniors on Ubuntu are not traditional "computer viruses." They come in forms your operating system cannot block on its own:

- **Phishing emails** that look exactly like messages from your bank, your doctor, or the government
- **Fake software update pop-ups** that install spyware when you click "OK"
- **Ransomware** that locks all your photos and documents and demands payment
- **Trojans** hidden inside files sent by someone pretending to be a family member

Ubuntu has no built-in antivirus. It has a firewall you can switch on, but that does not scan files, emails, or downloads. You are not fully covered.

## What Can Actually Happen Without Protection

Let us be specific about the risks, because vague warnings are easy to ignore.

### Your Files Can Be Locked Forever

Ransomware attacks on Linux systems are real. Once ransomware gets onto your computer — often through a file attachment or a fake download — it encrypts everything. Your photos of grandchildren. Your tax documents. Your email archive. All locked. The attackers then demand money, usually in a form that is very difficult to recover if you pay.

### Your Banking Details Can Be Stolen Silently

Keyloggers are a type of malware that record every key you press. If one is running on your computer while you type in your banking password, the attacker captures it — and you will not notice anything unusual until it is too late.

### Your Identity Can Be Used Against You

With enough personal data — your name, address, date of birth, NHS or Social Security number — criminals can open accounts, take out loans, or file fraudulent tax returns in your name. Recovering from [identity theft](https://snipstack.io/identity-theft-protection-software-2026/) takes months and causes enormous stress.

None of these outcomes are theoretical. They happen every day to real people who believed they were safe.
