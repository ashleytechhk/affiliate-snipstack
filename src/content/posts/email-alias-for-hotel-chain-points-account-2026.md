## Why Hotel Points Programs Are an Inbox Problem in Their Own Category

Most loyalty programs send promotional email. Hotel chain points programs send it at industrial scale, across multiple affiliated senders, with no practical way to stop it through conventional opt-out.

**The multi-sender problem.** When you join a major hotel points program, you are not signing up for one email sender. You are signing up for a network. Marriott Bonvoy, Marriott Vacations, Ritz-Carlton Rewards, and the co-branded Chase Bonvoy card are all technically separate senders with separate unsubscribe flows. Clicking unsubscribe on one has zero effect on the others.

**The re-opt-in loop.** Hotel program terms typically include a clause allowing them to re-enroll you in marketing emails when you complete a stay. So even if you successfully unsubscribe from every sender today, the next time you check in — even if you barely interacted with the desk — the opt-in clock resets. The spam restarts.

**The data broker leak.** Each hotel brand's partner network includes data-sharing arrangements that move your email across dozens of third parties. Once your address is in that pipeline, it circulates well beyond the hotel program itself. Frequent travelers who have accumulated multiple points memberships report their addresses appearing in breach databases linked to travel industry data brokers.

**The accumulation effect.** Five active hotel points accounts — which is not unusual for a frequent traveler working toward status in multiple programs — means five partner networks, each with dozens of affiliated senders. The math adds up fast. Fifty promotional emails a day from the travel sector alone is a conservative estimate for anyone with multiple memberships.

## Option-by-Option Breakdown

### Real Email + Unsubscribe

The default approach. You give your real address, manage spam reactively with the unsubscribe link.

This works fine for programs with responsible email practices. Hotel chain points programs are not that. Each partner sender requires a separate unsubscribe. Status match offers, resort vacation club pitches, and co-branded card solicitations each come from distinct senders. Working through them individually takes hours — and stays reset the cycle.

**Verdict:** Not viable for active hotel loyalty members.

### Gmail `+tag` Alias

You sign up with `yourname+bonvoy@gmail.com`. Gmail routes it to your regular inbox and you can filter by the tag.

The problem: hotel programs are sophisticated email marketers. They routinely strip everything after the `+` before storing the address. Your tag disappears from their database. When they share with a partner, they share the clean `yourname@gmail.com`. You have no way to trace which program leaked to which sender, and no ability to cut them off.

**Verdict:** Works for inbox organization. Provides zero privacy or spam-kill capability against hotel programs.

### Throwaway Address

Temporary inboxes like 10MinuteMail or Guerrilla Mail work for one-off sign-ups where you need a verification click and nothing else.

Hotel points accounts require ongoing email access: points balance updates, booking confirmations, redemption notifications, account recovery codes. A dead address after ten minutes means losing functional access to your own loyalty account.

**Verdict:** Wrong tool for this job entirely.

### iCloud Hide My Email / SimpleLogin / Firefox Relay

These generate persistent forwarding aliases — addresses that stay alive, forward reliably, and can be deleted on demand. They are genuinely useful for hotel loyalty accounts.

The practical limits: iCloud Hide My Email is tied to the Apple ecosystem; Android users cannot use it without workarounds. SimpleLogin's free tier caps at ten aliases — fine if you have two or three programs, tight if you have more. Firefox Relay's free tier limits you to five aliases and blocks reply-from-alias (useful if loyalty programs ever contact you with questions). None of these bundle additional privacy features.

**Verdict:** Solid options. Best fit if you are already paying for iCloud+ or want a free standalone tool with SimpleLogin.
