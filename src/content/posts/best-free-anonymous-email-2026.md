## Troubleshooting

### I am not receiving forwarded emails

- Check your spam folder — some email clients flag forwarded mail initially.
- Confirm the alias is toggled **On** in the Alt-ID dashboard.
- Add your alias domain to your email client's trusted senders list.

### A service rejected my alias address

Some services block known alias domains. In that case, use your real address only where truly necessary and apply inbox filters to manage those senders manually.

### I want to reply from my alias, not my real address

Alt-ID supports reply masking. When you reply to a forwarded email, the reply goes out from your alias address. Check that this feature is enabled in your Alt-ID settings — it is on by default for most account types.

### I accidentally deleted an alias I still needed

Deleted aliases cannot be recovered. Create a new one and update your account on that service. This is a good reminder to always label aliases clearly so you know which services depend on them before you delete.

**Can I use anonymous email for banking or government services?**
Not recommended. High-security services (banks, government portals, healthcare providers) often require a verified, persistent email address tied to your identity. Use your real address for those accounts and reserve aliases for lower-stakes signups.

**Does the sender know I am using an alias?**
No. From the sender's perspective, your alias looks like any other email address. There is no indication that it is an alias or that it forwards anywhere.

**How many aliases can I create?**
Alt-ID allows multiple active aliases. The exact limit depends on your Surfshark plan. Most users find that five to ten aliases cover all practical use cases.

**Will anonymous email stop all spam?**
It significantly reduces spam tied to email address harvesting and data-broker lists. It will not stop spam that arrives on your real address from before you started using aliases. For those, combine Alt-ID for future signups with inbox filters for existing senders.
