### 1. Apple XProtect and Gatekeeper (Built-In)

**What it is:** Apple builds two quiet security tools into every Mac. XProtect is a malware scanner that updates automatically. Gatekeeper blocks apps from unknown sources before they ever open.

**Ease of use:** 5 out of 5. You do not have to do anything. It runs silently in the background.

**Protection level:** Good for known threats. Apple updates its malware definitions regularly. It is not designed to catch brand-new or sophisticated attacks, but it handles the most common risks well.

**Real-time protection:** Partial. XProtect scans files when they are opened, not continuously. It will not warn you about dangerous websites in real time.

**Privacy:** Excellent. Apple does not sell your data to third parties. The tool collects only what is needed to function.

**Performance impact:** None noticeable.

**Verdict for seniors:** This is your baseline. It is already on your Mac. Make sure it is active before installing anything else (see Settings > Privacy and Security > Gatekeeper should be set to "App Store and identified developers").

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do Macs really get viruses?**

Yes, though less often than Windows computers. The more common threats on Mac in 2026 are adware (programs that flood your screen with ads), browser hijackers (programs that change your home page or search engine without permission), and phishing websites (fake pages designed to steal passwords). These are all real risks, and they affect Mac users regularly.

**Is Apple XProtect enough on its own?**

For many users, yes. If you mostly use your Mac at home for email and browsing familiar websites, XProtect plus Gatekeeper covers the most common threats. Adding Malwarebytes Free for occasional scans gives you a meaningful extra layer with no cost or complexity.

**Should seniors avoid Avast or AVG?**

Not necessarily avoid, but be informed. Both products offer genuine protection. The concern is that the parent company (Gen Digital) has a documented history of collecting and selling browsing data. If you are comfortable with that trade-off in exchange for free real-time protection, Avast or AVG are functional options. If privacy is a priority, Malwarebytes and Bitdefender are cleaner choices.

**Will installing a free antivirus slow down my Mac?**

Malwarebytes Free and Bitdefender Virus Scanner have very low performance impact. You will not notice them during everyday use. Avast and Avira can slow down older Macs slightly during a full scan, but day-to-day performance is usually unaffected.

**How often should I run a manual scan?**

Once a month is enough for most seniors using the XProtect plus Malwarebytes setup. If you download files frequently, click links in emails often, or have recently noticed odd behavior from your Mac (slower than usual, unexpected pop-ups, unfamiliar programs in your Dock), run a scan right away.

**Is Surfshark Antivirus really for non-technical people?**

Yes. It was designed with simplicity as a priority — one dashboard, large buttons, alerts in plain English. If you have never installed security software before, [Surfshark Antivirus](/blog/antivirus?sub=antivirus-mac-free-2026__faq) walks you through the setup step by step, and their support team is available around the clock if anything is unclear.

**Can I run two antivirus programs at the same time?**

On a Mac, running two full antivirus programs simultaneously can cause conflicts. The exception is Apple's built-in tools (XProtect, Gatekeeper) — these are designed to work alongside third-party security apps. The safe approach: pick one third-party scanner (Malwarebytes or Bitdefender), let XProtect run quietly alongside it, and do not install two separate third-party antivirus apps at the same time.
