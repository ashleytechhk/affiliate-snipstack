## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its core, a VPN does two things:

1. **Encrypts your internet traffic** — everything you send and receive is scrambled so that your ISP, network administrators, and anyone else monitoring the connection can only see meaningless data.
2. **Masks your IP address** — instead of websites seeing your real location (Poland, in this case), they see the VPN server's location. You appear to be browsing from wherever the server sits.

Think of it like mailing a letter through a trusted intermediary who uses their own return address. The recipient knows the letter came from the intermediary, not from you directly.

### What "Internet Privacy" Actually Means in Practice

Internet privacy is not a single switch — it's a stack of protections. A VPN handles the transport layer: the pipe your data travels through. It does not make you completely invisible online (nothing does), but it closes several major gaps:

- Your ISP cannot see which sites you visit or sell that browsing history to advertisers.
- Public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, airports, and hotels cannot intercept your data.
- Advertisers and trackers have a harder time linking your sessions across sites when your IP keeps changing.

## How a VPN Works Step by Step

Understanding the mechanics helps you use a VPN more confidently. Here's what happens when you connect:

1. **You launch the VPN app and choose a server** — say, one in the Netherlands or United States.
2. **Your device creates an encrypted tunnel** to that server using a protocol like WireGuard or OpenVPN. Surfshark supports both.
3. **All your internet traffic routes through that tunnel.** Your ISP can see you connected to a VPN server, but cannot read the contents.
4. **The VPN server makes requests on your behalf** — websites see the server's IP, not yours.
5. **Responses travel back through the encrypted tunnel** to your device.

The whole process adds only a small amount of latency (usually unnoticeable for browsing and streaming) while dramatically increasing the privacy of every request you make.

### What Protocols Matter

Not all VPNs are equal under the hood. Look for:

- **WireGuard** — modern, fast, and well-audited. Surfshark's default on most platforms.
- **OpenVPN** — battle-tested, open-source. A reliable fallback.
- **IKEv2** — good for mobile connections that switch between Wi-Fi and cellular.

Avoid any VPN that relies on outdated protocols like PPTP — these offer minimal real protection.
