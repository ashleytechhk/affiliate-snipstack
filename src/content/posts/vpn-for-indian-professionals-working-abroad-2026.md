## What is a VPN, and Why Does Region Matter?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. In simple terms, it is a piece of software that routes your internet traffic through a server in a country of your choice, masking your actual location from websites and apps.

When you open Netflix or Disney+ Hotstar from abroad, those platforms check your IP address — the unique identifier your internet provider assigns you. If your IP is registered in the UK, you get UK pricing and UK content. If it is registered in India, you get Indian pricing and Indian content. The difference can be dramatic: Hotstar's annual plan in India can cost a fraction of what the same service costs in Australia or Germany.

A VPN lets you "appear" to be browsing from India even when you are sitting in a Tokyo apartment or a Toronto office. The platform sees an Indian IP address and serves you accordingly.

This is not a loophole that only tech-savvy users know about. Millions of expats, students, and overseas workers use VPNs every day to keep access to the content and pricing they are used to back home.
