## Why Norton and McAfee Feel Worse Over Time

This is worth naming plainly.

Norton and McAfee rely heavily on first-year discounts to acquire customers, then recoup margin at renewal. The original price you paid is rarely the price you pay in year two. Many users report their annual bill increasing by 40–80% at first renewal — without any significant feature improvement.

Beyond pricing, there's the software experience itself. Norton's product has accumulated features over decades in ways that create UI clutter — settings buried across multiple dashboards, background processes that aren't easy to audit or disable. None of this means they're ineffective at core antivirus detection. Both perform adequately on AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives benchmarks. But detection rates alone aren't the full picture when the product also slows your machine, nags you to upgrade, and doesn't scale cleanly across a mixed household.
