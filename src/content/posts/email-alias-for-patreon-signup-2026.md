## Why Patreon Specifically Needs an Alias

There are at least three reasons to use an alias from the very first Patreon signup — not after spam arrives:

**1. Patreon has been breached before.** In 2015, Patreon suffered a major data breach that exposed over 2.3 million user records including email addresses. Breached addresses circulate on data-broker lists for years. Using your real address means that exposure is permanent.

**2. Patreon's marketing data-sharing.** Like most platforms, Patreon's privacy policy permits sharing data with third-party marketing partners. The email address you use to sign up becomes part of that data chain.

**3. Sensitive creator categories.** If you're pledging to NSFW, adult, or otherwise private creators, your email address ties your real identity to that subscription. An alias creates a clean separation — the alias is what Patreon knows, not you.

Once you understand these three factors, using your real address starts to look like unnecessary risk for a subscription that might cost a few dollars a month.

### SimpleLogin — Best Free Standalone Option

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service (now owned by Proton). Its free tier gives you up to 10 aliases with full forwarding. Paid plans are unlimited.

**What makes it work for Patreon:**
- Well-established domain reputation — Patreon's email validation rarely blocks it.
- You can reply from the alias, which matters if you message creators directly through Patreon.
- The dashboard is clean and works on any platform.

**Limitation:** Free tier is capped at 10 aliases. If you're using [email aliases to avoid spam](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/) across many services, you'll hit the cap quickly. Paid plan is €4/month.

### addy.io — Best for Technical Users Who Want Control

addy.io (formerly AnonAddy) is another open-source alias service. The free tier allows up to 10 shared-domain aliases or one custom-domain alias.

**What makes it work for Patreon:**
- Fine-grained forwarding rules — you can set aliases to forward, silently discard, or bounce.
- Self-hosting option if you want zero third-party dependency.
- Good for users who manage many aliases across many services.

**Limitation:** The UI is more complex than Alt-ID or SimpleLogin — not the right choice if you want something working in three minutes.

### Apple Hide My Email — Best for iOS/Mac Users

Apple Hide My Email generates random `@privaterelay.appleid.com` addresses that forward to your iCloud inbox. It's integrated directly into Safari, iOS, and macOS — filling in the alias when you tap a sign-up field takes one tap.

**What makes it work for Patreon:**
- Zero setup friction on Apple devices — it's built into the OS.
- Apple has a strong track record on privacy; your alias is not monetised.
- Works reliably; the domain is rarely blocked.

**Limitation:** Hard-locked to the Apple ecosystem. If you access Patreon on Android or Windows, you won't have the same auto-fill convenience. Also requires iCloud+ (from $0.99/month) or Apple One.

---

### Gmail Plus Addressing — Technically Free, Practically Useless

Gmail's `yourname+patreon@gmail.com` trick creates a variant address that delivers to your real inbox. It costs nothing and requires no third-party service.

**Why it doesn't work for Patreon privacy:**
- It doesn't hide your real address. Anyone who sees `yourname+patreon@gmail.com` can trivially derive `yourname@gmail.com`.
- Patreon (and most marketers) strip the `+patreon` portion before storing the address, so they have your real address anyway.
- There is no kill switch. If you want to [stop receiving](https://snipstack.io/stop-receiving-spam-from-unsubscribe-forms-2026/) emails to that variant, you have to create a filter — which is the same whack-a-mole as blocking senders.

Use this only if you have zero other options and need to distinguish Patreon mail in your inbox by label. For actual privacy, it provides none.

---

### Firefox Relay — Decent Free Option, Limited for Patreon

Firefox Relay's free tier gives you five aliases. The paid tier (bundled with Mozilla VPN at ~$6.99/month) is unlimited.

**What makes it work for Patreon:**
- Clean, simple interface. Good for users already in the Firefox ecosystem.
- Solid domain reputation — Patreon forms generally accept it.

**Limitation:** Free tier is five aliases total, and reply-from-alias requires paid. Workable for Patreon, but tight if you're using [email aliases across multiple services](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/).

## FAQ

**Will Patreon block an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-pinterest-signup-2026/)?**

Patreon does not specifically block alias domains used by mainstream tools like Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, or Apple Hide My Email. The domains these services use are well-established and not flagged as disposable-address services by most email validation APIs. If you encounter a rejection (uncommon), try a different alias service.

**Can I still message creators if I sign up with an alias?**

Yes. Patreon's creator messaging goes through Patreon's platform, not direct email. Your alias is just the address Patreon has on file for account notifications and billing. Direct messages to creators are handled inside Patreon's own interface and don't depend on your external email address.

**What if I forget which alias I used for Patreon?**

Check your Alt-ID (or whichever service you used) dashboard — aliases are listed with labels and creation dates. If you labelled it "Patreon" when you created it, it's easy to find. This is why labelling matters. Going forward, store alias-to-service mapping in your password manager alongside the login credentials.

**Is it against Patreon's terms of service to use an alias?**

No. Patreon's terms require a valid, functioning email address — an alias that forwards mail satisfies that requirement. You're not using a throwaway inbox that expires; you're using a permanent forwarding address that Patreon can reach. The alias is "real" from Patreon's perspective. [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-patreon-signup-2026__faq) and similar services provide exactly this kind of persistent alias.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my Patreon account if I delete the alias?**

Patreon can no longer send you email — billing receipts, password reset emails, and creator notifications will bounce. Before deleting an alias on an active account, update your Patreon email address to a new alias first, then delete the old one. This keeps your account functional while cutting off any compromised forwarding address.

**How is this different from using a [temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-stop-spam-2026/) service?**

[Temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-and-password-free-2026/) services (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail) generate a throwaway inbox that expires within minutes or hours. That's fine for a one-time download gate, but useless for Patreon — you need to receive billing confirmations, creator posts, and password resets on an ongoing basis. An alias service like Alt-ID creates a *permanent* forwarding address you control indefinitely. Same privacy benefit, but it works long-term.
