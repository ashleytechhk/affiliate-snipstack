## Why Your Android Phone Is a Target Right Now

Android powers roughly 72% of the world's smartphones. That enormous popularity makes it the number one target for cybercriminals. Unlike iPhones, Android allows apps to be installed from sources outside the official app store — which means a well-crafted fake can land on your phone from a single wrong tap on a link.

Seniors are specifically targeted for a few reasons:

- **More trust in technology**: Many older users assume that if something looks official, it is official.
- **Higher savings**: Fraudsters know that retirees often have pension accounts, investment portfolios, and fixed income deposits worth stealing.
- **Less familiarity with warning signs**: A pop-up that says "Your phone is infected — call this number immediately" looks alarming and real to someone who hasn't seen it before.

The threats are not theoretical. Real stories circulate every week: a grandmother in the UK had her banking app cloned by malware she unknowingly downloaded from a "free puzzle game." A retired teacher in Brazil lost access to his entire email history after clicking a fake courier notification.

Your Android phone is not automatically safe just because you paid for it or bought it from a reputable shop. The danger comes in through apps, links, Wi-Fi networks, and email attachments — none of which are blocked by the basic security layer built into Android.

## What Happens When Malware Gets In

Most people imagine a virus as something that immediately crashes your phone or steals your credit card number in seconds. The reality is quieter and more insidious.

Modern mobile malware often sits undetected for weeks or months. It might:

- **Record your keystrokes** every time you type a password or PIN.
- **Take screenshots** of your banking apps without any visible sign.
- **Forward your text messages** — including two-factor authentication codes — to someone overseas.
- **Access your contacts** to send scam messages to everyone who trusts you.

By the time you notice something is wrong, significant damage has already been done. This is why waiting for symptoms is not a strategy. Real-time protection that catches threats before they fully install is the only reliable defence.
