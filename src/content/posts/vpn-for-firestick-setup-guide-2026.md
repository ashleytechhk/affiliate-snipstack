## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its most basic, a VPN routes your internet traffic through a server in another location before it reaches its destination. From the perspective of any website or streaming app, your device appears to be wherever that server is.

When you connect to a VPN server in, say, Turkey or Argentina, Netflix, Disney+, or any other streaming service reads your location as being in that country. This matters because streaming services set their subscription prices based on local purchasing power — and the price gaps are significant. A plan that costs $15.49/month in the US might cost the equivalent of $4–6/month in a lower-income region.

A VPN for Firestick is simply a VPN application that runs natively on Amazon Fire TV devices. Because Firestick runs a version of Android, most quality VPN providers have built dedicated apps for it.

## How a Firestick VPN Actually Works (Technical Basics)

You do not need to understand networking to use a Firestick VPN, but knowing the basics helps you make better decisions.

![VPN tunnel diagram showing how traffic routes through a VPN server](/og/vpn-for-firestick-setup-guide-2026-inline-1.png)

When your Firestick connects to the internet normally, your Internet Service Provider (ISP) and every service you connect to can see your real IP address — a number that reveals your approximate location. Streaming services use this IP address to determine which regional catalog and pricing tier you should see.

When a VPN is active on your Firestick:

- Your Firestick connects to a VPN server in the country you chose
- All traffic between your Firestick and that server is encrypted
- The streaming service only sees the VPN server's IP address, not yours
- The service treats you as a user located where the server is

The practical result: pick a server in Turkey, and Turkish Netflix pricing and catalog applies to your session.

**Kill switch** — a feature worth understanding. If your VPN connection drops unexpectedly, a kill switch automatically blocks your internet traffic until the VPN reconnects. This prevents your real IP from briefly leaking to a streaming service, which could trigger a location flag on your account. Surfshark includes this feature.
