## The Risk Explained: How a Match Finds Your Professional Profile

### Step one: they have your face and your first name

A dating app match knows what you look like and has a first name — possibly your real first name if you use one. This is the starting point for a social media search. Most people on dating apps are discoverable on Instagram or LinkedIn from a face-and-first-name combination in under two minutes.

### Step two: your LinkedIn links to your professional contact

A freelancer's LinkedIn almost always lists contact information. If not directly, it links to a portfolio or a contracting profile where that information exists. The professional contact point — often a mobile number — is now in hand.

### Step three: the reverse-lookup confirms the match

A basic reverse-lookup on that mobile number returns a name, sometimes an address, and associated profiles. If the number on the LinkedIn matches the number used for dating app verification — and for most freelancers it does — the reverse-lookup confirms the connection. The match now has a professional identity, a contact number, and a location they were never supposed to have.
