## What Is Tor and Why Do Seniors Use It?

Tor stands for "The Onion Router." It is a free web browser designed to keep your internet activity private. When you use Tor, your connection bounces through several servers around the world, which makes it very difficult for websites, advertisers, or anyone else to track what you are doing online.

Many seniors have started using Tor after hearing about companies tracking their browsing habits, selling their data, or targeting them with advertisements. That concern is completely valid. Online privacy is a real issue, and wanting to protect yourself is smart.

But Tor was built for one specific job — hiding your location and identity. It was not built to protect your computer from malware. Viruses, ransomware, spyware, and other threats can still reach your computer through Tor just as easily as through any other browser.

## The Hidden Danger of Using Tor Without Antivirus

Here is what many Tor users do not realize: the very features that make Tor appealing — access to less-monitored corners of the internet, fewer tracking filters — also make it a riskier environment for picking up malware.

Think of it this way. Tor is like wearing an invisibility cloak outdoors. Nobody can see you, which is great. But you can still trip over a rock, step on broken glass, or walk into a wall. The cloak does not protect your body — it just hides you from view.
