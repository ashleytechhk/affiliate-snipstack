## The Real Cost of Using Your Personal Number for Business

Most small business owners start with what they have: one phone, one number, one inbox. It works fine at first. Then the problems compound.

**Your personal number leaks.** Every invoice you send, every Yelp listing, every business card you hand out at a trade show — your personal mobile is now permanently attached to your business identity. You can't unshare it. Years after a bad client relationship ends, they still have your number saved.

**You can't switch off.** When your business number is your personal number, there's no such thing as after-hours. A customer texts at 11pm expecting a reply. You either respond (and resent it) or ignore it (and lose the job). There's no middle ground because you have no way to mute business calls without muting everything.

**You look smaller than you are.** A local number showing up as "John Smith" on a customer's screen reads differently than a business line showing up as a professional number they dialed from your website. First impressions form fast. A shared personal number signals sole trader even when your operation is more substantial.

**Callbacks become a liability.** If you hand your personal number to a difficult client, a disgruntled customer, or a persistent salesperson, you can't easily take it back. Blocking one number doesn't help when they call from a friend's phone. The only clean exit is to have given them a number you control.
