## What is a VPN for Netflix?

VPN stands for **Virtual Private Network**. The simplest way to think about it: a VPN is software that places a server between your device and the internet. When you connect to a VPN server in the United States, all your outgoing internet traffic appears to originate from that US server — not from your home in Canada.

Netflix looks at your IP address to determine your location and serve you the corresponding regional library. When your IP address belongs to a US server, Netflix serves you the US library. That's the entire mechanism.

A few clarifications worth stating upfront:

- A VPN does **not** give you Netflix for free. You still need an active Netflix subscription.
- A VPN does **not** let you share an account across different country profiles in ways Netflix prohibits.
- A VPN does **not** work if Netflix detects and blocks the server you're using — which is why choosing a maintained provider matters.

Netflix actively tries to identify and block IP addresses belonging to VPN services. [Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) and low-quality paid options hit these blocks almost immediately. A premium VPN like Surfshark invests continuously in rotating server infrastructure to maintain Netflix access.

## Why Canada Gets a Different Netflix Library

The content gap between US and Canadian Netflix comes down to licensing economics. When Netflix acquires streaming rights for a show or film, it often buys those rights on a territory-by-territory basis. A studio may sell US rights to Netflix while retaining Canadian rights for a separate deal with a local broadcaster like Crave, Tubi, or a cable network.

Netflix Originals — content produced directly by Netflix — are generally available across all markets. But "generally" is not "always." Some originals carry licensing complications in specific regions, and certain titles appear on US Netflix months before they land in Canada, if they arrive at all.

The result: Canadian subscribers pay comparable monthly rates to their US counterparts but access a smaller catalog. For a subscriber who watches Netflix several times a week, this represents a real and recurring reduction in value received.

The cost math for a VPN is straightforward. A Surfshark annual subscription breaks down to a few dollars per month — significantly less than adding a second streaming service to fill content gaps. For the subscriber who already pays for Netflix and wants more from it, the VPN route is typically the more efficient spend.

Not every VPN works with Netflix. These are the criteria that separate a useful streaming tool from one that frustrates:

**Active Netflix compatibility:** The VPN must maintain working access, not just claim it. Netflix updates its block lists regularly, so a VPN that worked six months ago may not work today. Look for providers with recent, verified user reports and explicit support documentation for Netflix.
