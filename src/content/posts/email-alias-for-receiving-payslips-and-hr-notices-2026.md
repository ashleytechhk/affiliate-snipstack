## Why HR Emails Are a Spam Gateway

Most people assume that giving their employer an email address is a contained act. In practice, HR platforms function as hubs connecting to dozens of third-party vendors:

- **Payroll processors** (often separate from HR software)
- **Pension and retirement platforms**
- **Health and dental benefits administrators**
- **Employee assistance programmes**
- **Annual engagement surveys** run by external consultancies
- **Training and compliance platforms**

Each integration passes your address downstream. By the time you leave a company, your personal email address may be registered across 10 to 20 external systems — most of which have no obligation to delete your data when your employment ends.

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-real-estate-agent-property-alerts-2026/) solves this at the source. You give HR a forwarding address. Every legitimate notice — your payslip PDF, your P60, your pension contribution update — arrives in your real inbox without delay. But the alias itself can be replaced or switched off the moment it starts attracting spam.

## SimpleLogin: Best for Power Users Who Want Control

SimpleLogin is open-source and offers extensive customisation. You can create aliases on your own domain, set reply behaviour, and manage a large number of aliases across multiple email providers. It has a free tier with a limited number of aliases, and a paid tier that removes those limits.

For most employees who want a single alias for HR purposes, SimpleLogin is more tool than they need. But for freelancers juggling multiple clients, contractors working across several firms simultaneously, or anyone who wants granular control over alias management, it is worth considering.

## Apple Hide My Email: Convenient If You Are Already in the Apple Ecosystem

Apple's Hide My Email feature generates random forwarding addresses and ties them to your Apple ID. It works natively on iPhone and Mac, and setup is genuinely frictionless if you are already using iCloud Mail.

The limitation is ecosystem lock-in. If you use Gmail, Outlook, or any non-Apple mail client as your primary inbox, Hide My Email becomes awkward. The forwarded emails arrive in your iCloud inbox, not wherever you actually check your email. For workers who live in Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, this is a dealbreaker.

It is also worth noting that the generated addresses (e.g., `abc123@privaterelay.appleid.com`) look obviously like privacy aliases to any HR coordinator who notices them. This is rarely a practical problem — most payroll systems simply accept the address — but it is worth being aware of.

## Fastmail Masked Email: Solid, But Requires Fastmail

Fastmail's masked email feature is tightly integrated into its email client and works well. Like other alias services, it generates forwarding addresses and lets you disable them. The catch is that it only works if Fastmail is your primary email provider. If you use Gmail or Outlook, this option is not available to you without switching providers entirely.

For existing Fastmail users, it is a natural choice. For everyone else, the switching cost is high.

## What to Look for When Choosing an Alias for HR Use

![Choosing the right email alias for payslips](/og/email-alias-for-receiving-payslips-and-hr-notices-2026-inline-2.png)

Not every alias service is equally suitable for receiving official employment documents. Here are the factors that matter most:

**Reliable attachment forwarding.** Payslips, P60s, tax documents, and pension statements almost always arrive as PDF attachments. Verify that the service you choose forwards attachments without stripping or modifying them.

**Provider compatibility.** If your primary email is Gmail or Outlook, you need an alias service that forwards to any address — not one locked to a specific ecosystem.

**Address longevity.** Some alias services delete inactive aliases or require you to log in periodically to keep them active. For an alias tied to an employer, you want it to remain functional for the duration of your employment without manual maintenance.

**Easy disabling.** The ability to switch off an alias cleanly — without bounce messages confusing HR systems — is important when you leave a role.

**Reputation of the forwarding domain.** Some alias domains land in spam filters. If your payslip is being sent to `@sketchy-relay.io`, it may not reach your inbox at all. Established services with strong domain reputations are preferable.
