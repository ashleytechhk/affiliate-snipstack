## Why Returns Tracking Demands a Persistent Alias

Most [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-twitch-account-privacy-2026/) use cases can tolerate a temporary address. Returns tracking cannot.

A typical ecommerce return generates between 4 and 8 transactional emails: order cancellation or return request confirmation, prepaid label delivery, carrier pickup scan, warehouse received scan, refund initiated, and refund completed. These arrive over days to weeks — not minutes.

If your alias expires or gets deleted before the refund confirmation lands, you lose proof of return completion. For a disputed charge, that email is your evidence.

This means the alias tool you use for returns tracking must:

1. **Stay alive for weeks** without you touching it
2. **Forward reliably**, not just the first email
3. **Not get blocked** at the retailer's checkout
4. **Be searchable** in your real inbox so you can find confirmation emails fast

All four tools below are measured against these four criteria.

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple's built-in alias system generates `@privaterelay.appleid.com` addresses through Safari and iOS. It is seamlessly integrated into the Apple ecosystem — when you tap an email field in Safari on iPhone or Mac, Hide My Email surfaces as an autofill option.

**For returns tracking:** Forwarding is persistent and reliable. Apple's infrastructure is solid, and `@privaterelay.appleid.com` is a well-known domain that most retailers do not block. The limitation is ecosystem lock-in: Hide My Email only surfaces in Safari and Apple apps. If you are shopping on a Windows browser, an Android device, or a retailer's native Android app, you need to generate the alias manually via iCloud settings and paste it in — which adds friction.

**The spam kill:** Disabling forwarding for a specific Hide My Email address is done through iCloud settings (Settings → your name → iCloud → Hide My Email). It works, but it is a few taps deeper than Alt ID's single toggle.

**Cost:** Requires iCloud+ (starts at $0.99/month). Unlimited aliases at any iCloud+ tier.

### DuckDuckGo Email Protection

DuckDuckGo's email protection gives you a personal `@duck.com` address plus the ability to generate unique duck addresses per site. It is completely free and requires no subscription.

**For returns tracking:** Persistent forwarding works well. DuckDuckGo also strips email trackers (tracking pixels) from forwarded messages — a useful bonus if you care about retailers knowing you have opened their emails.

**The blocking problem:** `@duck.com` is a well-known privacy-focused domain. Some retail platforms recognize it and reject it at account creation. If you are using a well-known retailer (Amazon, Walmart, Target), this is rarely an issue. On more restrictive checkout flows — some fashion or electronics brands with tight email validation — duck.com addresses occasionally fail.

**Management:** The DuckDuckGo browser extension provides a popup showing your active aliases. There is no dedicated app dashboard; management is primarily through the extension.

**Cost:** Free.

---

### Firefox Relay

Firefox Relay generates `@mozmail.com` forwarding addresses. The free tier allows 5 aliases; paid unlocks unlimited.

**For returns tracking:** The forwarding is persistent, and the aliases work for long-running return processes. The hard constraint is the 5-alias limit on the free tier. If you regularly shop at more than five stores simultaneously, you hit the wall fast — particularly during returns season (post-holiday, sale events) when you might be managing multiple open return cases at once.

**The blocking problem:** `@mozmail.com` is the most commonly blocked of the four options here. Retailers that actively screen for alias services tend to catch Relay addresses at a higher rate than the alternatives.

**Platform dependency:** Relay is designed around the Firefox browser. You can use it outside Firefox, but the workflow is less seamless.

**Cost:** Free for 5 aliases; premium unlocks unlimited for a monthly fee.

## Feature Deep Dive: What Matters for Returns

### Alias Longevity

Every tool here provides persistent forwarding — none of them auto-expire. For returns tracking, this is table stakes. What differentiates them is how easy it is to manage a fleet of active aliases when you have returns open at multiple stores.

Alt ID and Hide My Email both provide clean in-app dashboards. DuckDuckGo's extension popup is functional. Firefox Relay's web dashboard (relay.firefox.com) is adequate but the fewest users will find it natural.

### Domain Blocking at Checkout

This is the practical friction point. You need to actually complete the checkout or account registration with your alias before the returns flow even begins. If the alias is blocked, none of the other features matter.

Based on real-world checkout testing:

- **Alt ID:** Passes most checkout validation checks
- **Apple Hide My Email:** Passes most, blocked occasionally on strict retail flows
- **DuckDuckGo @duck.com:** Passes most mainstream retailers, occasional blocks on fashion/lifestyle sites
- **Firefox Relay @mozmail.com:** Highest block rate of the four

### Kill Switch After Return is Complete

Once your refund lands, you have no reason to keep receiving emails from that retailer. All four tools support disabling forwarding per alias. Alt ID's single toggle is the fastest. Apple's process requires navigating through iCloud settings. DuckDuckGo and Firefox Relay are both accessible through their respective dashboards or extension popups.
