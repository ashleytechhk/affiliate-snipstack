## Why Your Real Number Is a Liability on a Crypto Exchange

Every major crypto exchange — Coinbase, Kraken, Binance, Gemini — requires phone number verification at registration. It is a KYC (Know Your Customer) requirement. You cannot opt out.

That verification does two things. First, it ties your phone number to your identity inside the exchange's database. Second, in most cases, it becomes the default or backup method for SMS-based two-factor authentication on your account.

This creates a specific, high-value attack surface.

### The SIM-Swap Playbook

A SIM-swap attack does not require hacking your phone. An attacker calls your mobile carrier's support line, impersonates you using personal information scraped from data breaches or people-search sites, and convinces the carrier to transfer your number to a SIM card they control. Once they have your number, they trigger a password reset on your exchange account, receive the SMS verification code on their device, and drain your funds.

The personal information they need to impersonate you — your name, billing address, last four digits of your account — is frequently available through data brokers and breach databases for a few dollars.

The phone number they need to target is the one sitting in the exchange's KYC record. If that number is your real SIM, the attack has a direct route to your funds.

## What Happens When an Exchange Gets Breached

Crypto exchanges are among the most consistently targeted organizations in financial services. A phone number inside an exchange database is not just vulnerable to SIM-swapping — it is vulnerable to the same data-broker pipeline that funnels breach data into marketing lists and people-search sites.

When an exchange's user database leaks (and they do leak), the records typically include email addresses, phone numbers, and partial identity information. Those records get sold on dark web markets, combined with data from other breaches, and used to build high-fidelity profiles of users who are likely to hold significant crypto balances.

Your real phone number in that dataset means:

- Your carrier is now a social engineering target
- Your real name and number become linkable across other breach databases
- Data brokers can connect your crypto exchange identity to your dating profile, your social media, and your home address
