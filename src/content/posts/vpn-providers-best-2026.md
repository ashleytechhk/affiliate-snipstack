## How Subscription Arbitrage Works (and What Can Go Wrong)

Streaming and subscription services set prices by country. They use your IP address at account creation — and sometimes at billing renewal — to determine which country's pricing applies. A VPN routes your connection through a server in the target country, so the service sees a local IP.

**The savings are real:**

| Service | US Price/mo | Turkey Price/mo | India Price/mo | Argentina Price/mo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spotify Premium | ~$11 | ~$2–3 | ~$2–3 | ~$2–3 |
| YouTube Premium | ~$14 | ~$2–3 | ~$1–3 | ~$2–3 |
| Apple TV+ | ~$10 | ~$3–4 | ~$2–3 | ~$2–4 |
| Adobe Creative Cloud | ~$60 | ~$15–20 | ~$10–15 | ~$8–12 |

*Prices are approximate, vary by plan tier, and change over time. Always verify current pricing in the target market before subscribing.*

**What goes wrong with cheap or unreliable VPNs:**

1. **IP leaks during billing.** If the VPN leaks your real IP address during a billing renewal, the service detects the mismatch and may reset your pricing to your actual country — or flag the account.

2. **Rotating IPs that break consistency.** Some VPNs rotate IPs constantly for privacy. This is good for privacy; it is bad for subscription accounts that flag suspicious location changes.

3. **Server detection and blocking.** Services like Spotify have become more aggressive at detecting known VPN IP ranges. A VPN provider that actively cycles and refreshes its IP pool (rather than running static datacenter ranges) performs significantly better.

4. **Slow speeds during peak hours.** Some budget VPNs throttle bandwidth — not ideal when you are also trying to stream the content you subscribed to.

## Is Subscription Arbitrage Against the Rules?

**Legal status:** Using a VPN is legal in most countries. Signing up for a subscription service from a different region may violate that service's terms of service — this is a contractual matter between you and the service, not a criminal or civil legal issue in most jurisdictions.

**Enforcement:** Services enforce regional pricing restrictions by detecting VPN IP addresses and flagging location inconsistencies, not through legal action against individual users. The practical risk is account pricing reset or, in rare cases, account suspension — not legal consequences.

**What actually gets accounts flagged:**
- IP address country not matching payment method country (most common trigger)
- Sudden location changes mid-billing cycle (IP rotation problem)
- Using the same VPN IP as many other users simultaneously (known VPN IP ranges)
- Payment method that doesn't match the sign-up country

Surfshark's stable server IPs and clean infrastructure in arbitrage markets minimize the first and third risks. Managing billing consistency (renewing while connected to the same country server) handles the second.
