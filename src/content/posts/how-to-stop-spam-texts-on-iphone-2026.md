## Why Your iPhone Gets So Many Spam Texts

Spam texts reach iPhones through a few well-worn paths:

**Data broker lists.** When you sign up for apps, contests, or loyalty programs with your real number, that number often gets bundled into lists that data brokers sell to marketers. Dating apps are a notable source — their ad-tech stacks are dense, and your number can travel far.

**Number harvesting from data breaches.** A breach at any company holding your number is enough to land it on spam lists permanently.

**Random number generation ("smishing").** Some spam campaigns just try every possible number sequence and see what sticks.

The first two sources are the dominant ones for dating-app users. That is why blocking spam after it arrives always feels like Whack-a-Mole — the lists are constantly refreshed with new sending numbers, and your real number remains exposed.

## The 5 Options Compared

### Option 1: iOS Built-In Spam Filter (Filter Unknown Senders)

Apple added SMS filtering in iOS 14. Go to **Settings > Messages > Filter Unknown Senders** and toggle it on. Messages from numbers not in your contacts land in a separate "Unknown & Junk" tab instead of your main inbox.

**How it works:** iOS sends unknown-sender messages to a junk folder automatically. You can also install message filter apps (like Truecaller or Robokiller) and iOS will route filtering decisions through them.

**Ease of setup:** One toggle. Thirty seconds.

**Free or paid:** Completely free. Built into iOS.

**Recall rate (how much spam it catches):** Moderate. Apple's native filter is keyword and sender-pattern based. It keeps your inbox cleaner but misses sophisticated spam that mimics legitimate senders. It also has no learning component — it doesn't get smarter over time.

**Dating-app limitation:** Already got spam? This hides it from your main inbox but doesn't stop it arriving. Your number is still exposed. Spam volume does not decrease — it just moves folders.

### Option 2: Carrier Filters — AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile

All three major US carriers now offer spam text filtering, usually at no extra cost for basic tiers.

- **AT&T ActiveArmor** — free with a qualifying AT&T plan. Blocks suspicious texts before they reach your device.
- **Verizon Call Filter** — free basic tier, paid Plus tier adds more features. SMS protection included.
- **T-Mobile Scam Shield** — free for all T-Mobile customers. Scam Likely labels and SMS filtering built into your plan.

**How it works:** Carriers analyze traffic patterns at the network level. Known spam numbers are blocked or labeled before SMS hits your device.

**Ease of setup:** Requires activating via each carrier's app or account portal. Straightforward but not automatic — you have to opt in.

**Free or paid:** Free basic filtering on all three carriers. Premium tiers ($3–4/month per line) add more aggressive blocking and reporting.

**Recall rate:** Good for known spam campaigns. Less effective against new numbers and short-lived [burner number](https://snipstack.io/burner-number-for-verification-2026/)s that spammers rotate constantly. Carrier filters work on sender reputation, which takes time to build.

**Dating-app limitation:** Carrier filters are reactive. Your real number is already on the lists. Carrier blocking reduces spam volume meaningfully but doesn't prevent future exposure when you keep signing up with the same number.

### Option 3: RoboKiller

RoboKiller is a dedicated spam-blocking app for iOS (and Android). It integrates with iOS's message filtering API and adds its own intelligence layer.

**How it works:** RoboKiller maintains a database of known spam numbers and uses audio fingerprinting for calls. For texts, it filters unknown senders through its database and blocks matches before they reach your Messages app.

**Ease of setup:** Install the app, grant permissions, activate as your iOS message filter. Takes about two minutes.

**Free or paid:** Paid. Around $4.99/month or $39.99/year. No meaningful free tier for full functionality.

**Recall rate:** High compared to native iOS filtering. RoboKiller claims significant reduction in spam calls and texts for subscribers. The database is regularly updated and the app has been in this space since 2016. Real-world user experience suggests it catches considerably more than carrier or native filters alone.

**Dating-app limitation:** RoboKiller is reactive. It works after your number has been exposed. It is excellent at reducing the symptom, but if you gave your real number to a leaky dating app three years ago, that number is on lists RoboKiller will keep needing to fight against for the foreseeable future.

### Option 4: Truecaller

Truecaller is a caller-ID and spam-blocking app with over 350 million users. Its spam database is crowd-sourced — every user who marks something as spam contributes to the shared blocklist.

**How it works:** When a message or call comes in from an unknown number, Truecaller cross-references it against its database. Known spam numbers are labeled or blocked. In iOS's filtering framework, Truecaller can act as your message filter app.

**Ease of setup:** Install the app, enable as SMS filter in iOS settings. Fast setup.

**Free or paid:** Free tier available with basic filtering. Premium tier ($2.99/month) adds more features including ad removal and enhanced blocking.

**Recall rate:** Strong for numbers that appear on the crowd-sourced list. Very effective against known spam campaigns with high report volumes. Slightly weaker against new numbers that haven't been reported yet — the lag before a new spammer number gets flagged is a real limitation.

**Privacy note:** Truecaller's model involves uploading your contact list to build its database. Users who care about contacts' privacy may find this a meaningful trade-off.

**Dating-app limitation:** Same fundamental issue as RoboKiller. Truecaller cleans up the symptom but does not prevent your real number from continuing to attract spam from apps you've already signed up with.

**Does enabling Filter Unknown Senders mean I'll miss real messages?**

Messages from numbers not in your contacts land in the "Unknown & Junk" folder — they aren't deleted. You can check the folder anytime. The risk is missing a legitimate text from someone new (a job contact, a delivery service) if you don't check regularly. Most people find it a worthwhile trade.

**Will my carrier's spam filter work automatically, or do I have to turn it on?**

You have to opt in. AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile all offer free spam protection but require activation through their apps or account portals. Check yours — there's a good chance it's sitting there unused.
