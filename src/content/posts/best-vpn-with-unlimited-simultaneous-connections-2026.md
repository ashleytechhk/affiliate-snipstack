## What Does "Unlimited Simultaneous Connections" Actually Mean?

Let's define the term before going further, because it matters more than most VPN marketing copy suggests.

A **simultaneous connection** is an active VPN tunnel running on a single device at a given moment. When a VPN provider says "5 simultaneous connections," they mean you can have 5 devices running that VPN at the same time — but not a sixth.

**Unlimited simultaneous connections** means there is no enforced cap. You can connect your laptop, your phone, your partner's phone, a tablet, a smart TV, a games console, and a spare travel router — all at once, under one subscription, without the provider cutting any of them off.

This is distinct from installing the VPN on multiple devices (most providers allow many installations) — the limit is about how many of those devices can be connected at the same moment.

For single users, a 5-device cap is often sufficient. For households, anyone running a side-by-side comparison of streaming services across multiple screens will hit it quickly. And for subscription arbitrage specifically, the unlimited policy matters in a more nuanced way.

Where unlimited connections help: if you are managing arbitrage across multiple services, each potentially requiring the VPN to be active on different devices during payment windows, a device cap forces you to juggle. With a capped plan, you are constantly disconnecting one device to free a slot for another. With an unlimited plan, this is not a consideration.
