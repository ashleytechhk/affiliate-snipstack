## Quick Verdict

If you are entry-level, **CompTIA Security+** is the fastest credible on-ramp — DoD-approved, vendor-neutral, and recognized by virtually every hiring manager. If you already have five years in the field and want the salary jump, **CISSP** is the gold standard. **CEH** is solid for offensive/pentesting specialization, but its real-world reputation has softened compared to OSCP. **CISM** and **CISA** belong in the management track, not the technical one. Skip any cert that is not accredited by ANSI/ISO 17024 unless it is a vendor-specific credential you genuinely need for a job posting.

## Entry Level: CompTIA Security+

Security+ is where most people should start. It covers six domains — attacks/threats/vulnerabilities, architecture, implementation, operations/incident response, governance/risk/compliance, and cryptography — without demanding years of prior experience. The SY0-701 version (current as of 2026) leans harder into cloud security and zero-trust concepts than its predecessor, which makes it actually relevant to modern job descriptions rather than a checkbox from 2015.

**Who it is for:** Career changers, IT generalists moving toward security, recent graduates, anyone who needs a DoD 8570/8140 baseline qualification.

**Who should skip it:** Experienced security professionals who already hold CySA+ or CISSP. You will not learn much you do not already know, and hiring managers may see it as a step backward on a senior resume.

Prep time is typically 60–90 hours of focused study for someone with a general IT background. Professor Messer's free video course combined with Darril Gibson's study guide remains a proven stack.

## Intermediate Track: CEH vs. CySA+ vs. PenTest+

This is where the choice gets interesting.

### CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker)

EC-Council's CEH is the most recognized name in the offensive-security space outside of OSCP. It covers 20 modules from reconnaissance through session hijacking to cloud hacking. The brand recognition is real — you will see CEH listed on government and enterprise job postings constantly.

The knock against it: the exam is multiple-choice-heavy and some practitioners argue it teaches you how to answer questions about hacking more than how to actually do it. That gap has grown as OSCP became the industry's preferred proof-of-hands-on-skill. Still, for regulated industries and government contracting, CEH on a resume opens doors that OSCP might not, purely because procurement officers recognize it.

![Certification path flowchart](/og/cyber-security-certifications-2026-inline-1.png)

### CompTIA CySA+ and PenTest+

CySA+ sits in the defensive/analyst lane — threat intelligence, SIEM use, vulnerability management. If you are heading toward a SOC analyst or threat hunter role rather than a pentesting role, CySA+ is often a better fit than CEH and costs significantly less. PenTest+ covers the offensive side with a performance-based format that requires you to demonstrate practical skills, not just recall definitions.

**Bottom line on intermediates:** CEH for career signaling in regulated environments. CySA+ for defensive analyst roles. PenTest+ as a stepping stone toward OSCP if budget is tight.

## Advanced: CISSP

CISSP is the cert that makes hiring managers at large enterprises and government agencies stop scrolling. The eight CBK domains are broad — security and risk management, asset security, security architecture, communications/network security, identity management, security assessment, security operations, and software development security — which means it tests breadth over depth.

The real barrier is the experience requirement: five years of paid work experience in at least two of the eight domains (one year can be substituted with a relevant degree). You cannot fake your way through the endorsement process. That experience gate is also why CISSP commands the salary premium it does.

The exam itself is adaptive (CAT format), capped at 125–175 questions, and notoriously tricky because it asks you to think like a manager, not a technician. The correct answer is often the one that says "assess and report to management" rather than "fix it yourself."

## Management Track: CISM and CISA

CISM (Certified Information Security Manager) and CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor) are ISACA credentials that belong to a different career path entirely. If you are moving into a CISO, security manager, or IT audit role, these are your targets. They are not technical certifications — they are governance and management credentials. Do not pursue them if you want to stay hands-on. Do pursue them if you are being groomed for leadership or audit functions.

---

## The Wildcard: OSCP

Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP) is the one cert that almost every experienced pentester respects without qualification. It requires you to compromise machines in a 24-hour practical exam, write a professional report, and submit it for review. There are no multiple-choice questions. You either pop the boxes or you do not.

The trade-off: it is not recognized by many automated applicant-tracking systems that filter on acronyms, it does not fulfill DoD 8140 requirements, and the prep course (PWK/PEN-200) is a significant time investment. If you want the most credible proof of technical pentesting skill, OSCP wins. If you need a cert that survives a government procurement checklist, CEH or CISSP wins.

## FAQ

### Which [cyber security](https://snipstack.io/cyber-security-solutions-2026/) certification is best for beginners in 2026?

CompTIA Security+ remains the strongest entry point. It is vendor-neutral, widely recognized by US government and enterprise employers, and the SY0-701 exam covers genuinely current topics including cloud security, zero-trust architecture, and supply chain risk. Expect 60–90 hours of prep time from a general IT background.

### Is CISSP worth it in 2026?

Yes, if you meet the experience requirement. CISSP continues to command one of the highest salary premiums among IT certifications. The five-year experience gate means it genuinely signals seniority rather than just exam-passing ability. If you have the background, it is one of the highest-ROI certifications you can hold.

### CEH vs. OSCP — which should I get?

It depends on what you need. CEH is better for government contracts, DoD environments, and job listings that name it explicitly. OSCP is better for proving practical pentesting ability to technically literate hiring managers. Many serious pentesters hold both — CEH for compliance, OSCP for credibility.

### How long does it take to get CISSP?

Most candidates with the required experience spend 3–6 months studying. The CBK is broad, so the preparation time reflects domain coverage rather than difficulty. (ISC)² official study materials plus a practice exam tool is the standard approach. The adaptive exam format means you could finish in 100 questions or go up to 175 — you will not know which until you are done.

### Do I need a VPN while studying for security certifications?
