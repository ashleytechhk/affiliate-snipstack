## Why Your Real Phone Number Is a Threat on Dating Apps

Most people think of a phone number as just a communication tool. But data brokers, reverse-lookup services, and even basic Google searches can turn a ten-digit number into a full profile: your full name, home address, employer, social media accounts, and in some cases, family members.

When you share your number with a dating match and that person turns out to be unsafe, they have everything they need to locate and contact you far beyond what you intended.

Here is what can happen after a real number lands in the wrong hands:

- **Reverse phone lookup** — sites like Spokeo, WhitePages, and BeenVerified index phone numbers against public records. A search often returns a full name and address within seconds.
- **Caller ID spoofing and spam loops** — once a number is sold or shared, it circulates across spam and harassment networks.
- **Social media cross-referencing** — your number may be linked to WhatsApp, Instagram, Snapchat, or LinkedIn. A match who receives it can find your personal accounts even if you never gave them out.
- **Unwanted physical contact** — multiple documented cases from dating app users involve matches who used phone data to show up uninvited at homes and workplaces.

The risk is highest in the early stages of dating, when you know almost nothing about the other person but feel social pressure to exchange contact details to move the conversation off the app.
