## What Is AdGuard?

AdGuard is an ad-blocking tool made by a company of the same name. Its job is simple: stop ads from loading before they appear on your screen.

When you visit a website, your browser sends dozens of requests — not just for the article you want to read, but also to ad networks, tracking services, and data brokers embedded on the page. AdGuard sits between your browser and those requests and blocks the ones connected to ads and trackers. The result is a faster, quieter page with fewer things competing for your attention.

AdGuard has been around since 2009 and is one of the most widely used [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-free-chrome-2026/)s in the world. The company is based in Cyprus. The browser extension is open source, meaning anyone can inspect how it works.

## The AdGuard Product Lineup: What You Can Actually Download

This is where things get confusing. "AdGuard download" can refer to any of four different products. Here is what each one is.

### 1. AdGuard Browser Extension (Free)

This is the most common download and the one most people actually need. You install it like any other browser extension — directly from the Chrome Web Store, Firefox Add-ons, or Edge Add-ons.

**What it does:**
- Blocks display ads on websites — banners, sidebars, pop-unders
- Blocks YouTube ads (AdGuard maintains a dedicated YouTube filter list)
- Blocks tracking scripts that follow you from site to site
- Hides the empty white space left behind when an ad is removed

**What it does not do:**
- [Block ads in](https://snipstack.io/block-ads-in-free-games-ios-2026/) apps on your phone
- Block ads outside your browser (in desktop apps, for example)
- Protect other devices — it only works in the browser where you installed it

Installation takes about 30 seconds. There is no account required. This is the right starting point for most people.

### 2. AdGuard for Windows / Mac (Paid After Trial)

This is a full desktop application that installs at the operating system level. Instead of only filtering what your browser sees, it monitors and filters traffic from every application on your computer.

**What it adds over the browser extension:**
- Blocks ads in all desktop applications, not just browsers
- Can filter encrypted HTTPS traffic
- Includes parental controls and content category filtering
- Provides app-level rules — you can tell AdGuard to [block ads in](https://snipstack.io/block-ads-in-games-android-2026/) one app but not another

The free trial lasts 14 days. After that, you need to pay for a license. Pricing varies by how many devices you want to cover.

If you only browse the web and do not notice ads in desktop apps, the free browser extension probably covers your needs. The desktop app is for people who want deeper, system-wide coverage.

### 3. AdGuard for iPhone / Android (Separate Apps)

These are mobile-specific versions of AdGuard. Each is a separate product.

On iPhone, AdGuard integrates with Safari's content blocking system and can also apply DNS-level filtering. It works well within Safari but cannot filter ads in other apps the way the Android version can.

On Android, AdGuard is more capable because Android allows more permission for filtering apps. However, AdGuard for Android is not available on the Google Play Store — you download it directly from AdGuard's website as an APK file. If that sounds unfamiliar or complicated, there are simpler alternatives.

### 4. [AdGuard DNS](https://snipstack.io/adguard-dns-hostname-2026/) (No Download Required)

[AdGuard DNS](https://snipstack.io/top-adguard-dns-2026/) is a different approach entirely. Instead of installing software, you change a setting on your device called the DNS server. AdGuard's DNS servers then block connections to known ad and tracking domains before they even reach your device.

No software to install. No browser extension. You just change one network setting.

The free version works without an account. It uses AdGuard's default block lists and covers all apps on the device — not just your browser. AdGuard DNS Premium ($3-5/month) adds a personal dashboard, custom block lists, and per-device rules.

## How AdGuard Actually Works (Simple Version)

You do not need to understand the technical details to use AdGuard, but knowing the basics helps you understand what it can and cannot do.

When you type a web address, your browser sends a request to load that page. The page then loads additional content — articles, images, and dozens of ads and tracking scripts from outside sources. AdGuard keeps a list of addresses belonging to ad networks and trackers. When your browser tries to load content from those addresses, AdGuard says no and the request is blocked.

The browser extension does this inside the browser. The desktop app does this at the operating system level, which is why it can see traffic from all apps, not just the browser. DNS blocking does this even earlier — at the moment your device looks up the address, before any data is even sent.

None of these approaches are perfect. Ad networks adapt constantly. Some ads are served from the same address as the content you want, so blocking the address would also block the content. But in practice, a good [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-extensions-2026/) eliminates the overwhelming majority of what interrupts you while browsing.

## Is AdGuard Safe to Download?

Yes. AdGuard is a well-established, legitimate security company. The browser extension is open source — meaning its code is publicly available for anyone to inspect.

A few things worth knowing:

- AdGuard for Windows and Mac uses a technique called HTTPS filtering to inspect encrypted traffic. This is more powerful but requires AdGuard to install a certificate on your computer. Some security professionals flag this as a concern because it puts AdGuard in a position to see encrypted traffic. - The free browser extension has no such concerns. Install it from the official Chrome Web Store, Firefox Add-ons, or Edge Add-ons page and you are good.

## FAQ

### Is [AdGuard free](https://snipstack.io/adguard-free-vs-paid-2026-best-alternatives/) to download?

The AdGuard browser extension is completely free with no paid tier. AdGuard for Windows and Mac is free for a 14-day trial, then requires a paid license. AdGuard for iOS and Android are paid apps with a limited free tier. AdGuard DNS has a free basic version that requires no account.

### Does AdGuard block YouTube ads?

The browser extension does block YouTube ads using a dedicated YouTube filter list that AdGuard maintains and updates regularly. It works better than most alternatives for Chrome users in 2026. It is not perfect — YouTube actively works to detect and route around [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-chrome-2026/)s — but it catches most ads most of the time.

### Which AdGuard version should I download first?

Start with the free browser extension from the Chrome Web Store, Firefox Add-ons, or Edge Add-ons. It is free, installs in under a minute, and handles web-browsing ads effectively. You can decide whether you need the full desktop application later — but most people do not.

### Does AdGuard work on iPhone?

AdGuard has a dedicated iOS app that works with Safari's content blocking system and DNS filtering. It is a separate purchase from the desktop apps and does not cover other apps the way a VPN-level solution would. For whole-device ad blocking on iPhone, a DNS-level option or Surfshark CleanWeb is more effective.

### Is it safe to download AdGuard for Android from outside the Play Store?

AdGuard for Android is distributed directly from AdGuard's official website because Google's Play Store policies prohibit apps that block ads in other apps. Downloading from AdGuard's official site is safe. However, if you are not comfortable installing apps outside the Play Store, consider the browser extension on desktop or a DNS-based solution instead.

### Can AdGuard block ads in apps like Spotify or games?

The browser extension only blocks ads inside your browser. AdGuard for Android (installed directly from AdGuard's site) can block ads across all apps using a local VPN mode. On iOS, full in-app ad blocking is not possible due to Apple's platform restrictions. For cross-app blocking on any device, a DNS or VPN-level solution like Surfshark CleanWeb is more consistent.

### What is the difference between AdGuard and other [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-reddit-2026/)s like AdBlock?

AdGuard and AdBlock are completely different products from different companies — the name similarity is coincidental. The biggest practical difference: AdBlock enables a feature called "Acceptable Ads" by default, which whitelists ads from advertisers who pay AdBlock. AdGuard does not do this. For users who want complete ad blocking, AdGuard is the stronger default choice. AdGuard's free extension also outperforms AdBlock on YouTube ad blocking in most tests.
