## What Protection Does a Chromebook Already Have?

Before spending money, it helps to know what you already have.

Google builds four layers of protection into every Chromebook:

- **Verified Boot.** Every time your Chromebook starts, it checks itself for tampering. If anything looks wrong, it repairs itself automatically.
- **Sandboxing.** Each browser tab and app runs in its own sealed container. If one tab gets infected, the problem cannot spread to the rest of your computer.
- **Automatic updates.** ChromeOS updates silently in the background. You do not need to remember to install patches.
- **Google Safe Browsing.** This checks websites you visit against a list of known dangerous pages and warns you before you land on them.

These are genuine protections — not marketing fluff. They are the reason Chromebooks have far fewer virus problems than Windows laptops.

So what is left to worry about?

The honest answer: **you**. Not your Chromebook. Scammers target the person sitting in front of the screen, not the computer itself. A phishing email that tricks you into typing your password on a fake website bypasses every built-in security layer — because you opened the door yourself. An unsecured Wi-Fi network at your doctor's waiting room lets strangers potentially watch your internet traffic, regardless of how secure your laptop is.

Those are the real risks in 2026. And they are the gaps that a good security tool can help close.

It works reasonably well for catching well-known scam sites. The limitation is that it only knows about threats Google has already identified. Brand-new scam pages — which criminals create and discard quickly — can slip through before they make it onto Google's list.

For seniors who want an extra layer of real-time phishing detection, something that checks links before you click them rather than after you arrive, a dedicated security tool adds meaningful protection.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do Chromebooks really need antivirus software?

Chromebooks do not need a traditional virus scanner the way older Windows computers do. The built-in protections handle most classic viruses automatically. What Chromebooks do not protect against are phishing scams, insecure public Wi-Fi, and stolen passwords — which are the threats that actually target everyday users, including seniors. A security tool that covers those three areas adds genuine value.

### Can I get a virus just by visiting a website on my Chromebook?

It is very unlikely. ChromeOS's sandboxing means that even a malicious website is confined to its own tab and cannot affect the rest of your computer. The risk is not the website infecting your machine — it is the website tricking you into handing over your password or bank details. That is a human problem, not a technical one, which is why phishing protection matters.

### What is a VPN, and why do I need one on a Chromebook?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) hides your internet activity from other people on the same Wi-Fi network. Think of it like sending a sealed envelope instead of a postcard. It matters most when you are connected to public Wi-Fi — in a cafe, a library, or a waiting room — where strangers on the same network could potentially see your unprotected internet traffic. On your home broadband, it is less critical but still a good habit.

### Is it safe to install Android apps on a Chromebook?

Most Chromebooks can run Android apps from the Google Play Store, and most Play Store apps are safe. The risk comes from apps that slip through Google's review process carrying hidden malware. An antivirus scanner for Android apps — included in all four paid options above — adds a useful check before those apps run on your device.

### What happens if my password gets stolen?

Breach monitoring tools — included in all four paid options — watch databases of stolen credentials that criminals share online. If your email address or password appears in a known breach, you receive an alert telling you which account was affected so you can change that password immediately. Acting quickly dramatically reduces the chance that a stolen password is used against you.

### How do I know if a link in an email is safe before I click it?

The safest rule is: do not click links in emails about your bank account, a payment, a parcel delivery, or a prize. Go directly to the website by typing the address into the browser yourself. Security tools with phishing filters add an automatic check, but human judgement is your first line of defence.

### Will security software slow down my Chromebook?

No noticeably. Chromebooks are lightweight machines and the security apps listed here are designed to run quietly in the background without affecting your browsing speed. Surfshark's VPN adds a small amount of overhead to your connection speed, but for everyday browsing and video calls the difference is not significant.
