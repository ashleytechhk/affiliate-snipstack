## Feature-by-Feature: Is the Security Actually Good?

The natural question when a bundle is this cheap is whether the individual components are any good or just checkbox filler. Fair question. Here's an honest breakdown.

### VPN: Strong

Surfshark's VPN is one of the better standalone VPNs in the market independent of the bundle. WireGuard as the default protocol means fast connection speeds, and the no-logs policy has been independently audited by Cure53. For couples, the unlimited device policy is the headline — no counting how many devices are connected, no one getting bumped because the other partner is already using the "slot."

Features worth knowing: split tunneling (route only some apps through the VPN), multi-hop (route through two servers for extra privacy), and [GPS spoofing](https://snipstack.io/vpn-for-content-creators-geo-testing-2026/) on Android. These aren't features you'll use every day, but they're there when you need them.

### Antivirus: Solid for Real-World Use

[Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-review-2026/)'s antivirus component uses real-time scanning and catches malware, ransomware, and [phishing](https://snipstack.io/how-to-avoid-phishing-attacks-2026/) attempts. It won't match the detection-rate stats of a standalone AV from Bitdefender or Kaspersky in independent lab benchmarks, but it covers the threat categories that affect most people: drive-by downloads, malicious email attachments, and infected files. For couples who aren't running high-risk downloads, it's more than adequate.

What it doesn't do is hammer your CPU with aggressive scan schedules or throw up a notification every time you do something it's not sure about. That restraint is genuinely appreciated once you've dealt with a scan-heavy AV eating your battery on a call.

### Breach Monitoring: Genuinely Useful

[Surfshark Alert](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-with-breach-alert-notifications-2026/) monitors multiple email addresses per account — useful for couples where each person has two or three email addresses across work, personal, and older accounts. When a breach hits, you get a notification and enough context to know what to do about it. This is one of those features that feels unnecessary until it isn't: credential stuffing attacks are common, and catching a compromised password before someone uses it matters.

### CleanWeb: Effective

Blocking ads and trackers at the network level is different from a browser extension that only covers one browser on one device. CleanWeb applies across apps and browsers on every connected device. This matters for couples because it covers both partners' phones — including in-app ads on Android, which browser extensions don't touch.

The antivirus component also runs light on CPU — it won't drain your laptop battery or slow down your machine noticeably during normal use.
