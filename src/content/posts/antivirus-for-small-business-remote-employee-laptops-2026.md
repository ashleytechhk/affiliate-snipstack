## The Risk, Explained Simply

Let's break down what actually happens when a remote employee laptop gets infected, in plain language — no jargon.

### Ransomware Locks Your Files and Demands Payment

Ransomware locks every file on a computer — invoices, customer lists, contracts, photos — and demands payment to unlock them. It often arrives through a link in an email that looked completely normal. Once it is on one laptop, it can spread to any shared folder within minutes. Small businesses get targeted because owners, desperate for their records, tend to pay quickly.

### Phishing Emails Steal Logins

A phishing email is a fake message built to look exactly like it came from a bank, a supplier, or a coworker. It asks the recipient to click a link and "confirm" their password on a fake page that looks identical to the real one. Once a criminal has that password, they can access bank accounts or customer data — often unnoticed for days.

### Spyware Watches Silently

Spyware sits quietly on a device, recording keystrokes, capturing passwords, and sometimes switching on a webcam without any visible sign. It does not slow the computer down or show a warning. Many victims never know it was there.
