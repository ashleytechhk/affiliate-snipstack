## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect through a VPN, your internet traffic is routed through a server in the location you select before it reaches any destination site or service. Everything on the other end sees the VPN server's IP address — not yours, and not your actual location.

For streaming, this matters because platforms like Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, and most other subscription services set prices based on which country your account is registered in. Those price gaps reflect local purchasing-power differences, and they are significant. A plan priced at $15.49 per month in the United States may cost the equivalent of $4–6 per month in Turkey or Argentina.

A VPN for MacBook is simply a VPN application that runs natively on macOS. Because macOS is a first-class platform for major software publishers, the leading [VPN providers](https://snipstack.io/vpn-providers-best-2026/) — including Surfshark — maintain dedicated Mac apps with full-featured interfaces optimized for the operating system.

## How a MacBook VPN Actually Works

You do not need to understand networking to use a MacBook VPN, but the basics help you make smarter choices about where to connect and why.

![Diagram showing how VPN traffic routes from MacBook through a VPN server to a streaming platform](/og/vpn-for-macbook-setup-2026-inline-1.png)

When your MacBook connects to the internet without a VPN, every service you reach sees your real IP address. That address reveals your approximate location with enough accuracy for streaming platforms to determine which pricing tier applies.

When a VPN is active:

- Your MacBook connects to a VPN server in the country you chose
- All traffic between your MacBook and that server is encrypted
- The streaming service only sees the VPN server's IP address, not yours
- The platform treats your session as originating from that server's country

Pick a server in Turkey, and Turkish Netflix pricing applies. Pick Argentina, and Argentine rates apply for the platforms that price aggressively there.

**Kill switch** — worth understanding before setup. If the VPN connection drops unexpectedly, a kill switch blocks your internet traffic immediately until the VPN reconnects. Without it, your real IP could briefly appear to a streaming service during the gap — which is how accounts get flagged for location inconsistency. Surfshark includes a kill switch in its macOS app.
