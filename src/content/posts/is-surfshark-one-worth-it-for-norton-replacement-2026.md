## The Norton Problem: Why People Switch

Norton has one of the strongest brand reputations in antivirus history. But reputation and day-to-day experience diverge sharply for many users.

### Bloatware and Performance Drag

Norton installs multiple background services that run at all times. On older Windows machines or lower-RAM laptops, users frequently notice slower boot times, sluggish browser performance, and scan windows that hijack focus mid-workflow. The product has improved over the years, but it still carries more system overhead than lean competitors.

### Aggressive Upsells

Open Norton's dashboard and you will see prompts to add LifeLock, VPN upgrades, cloud backup, and parental controls — most requiring an additional fee. The interface is optimized to expand your subscription, not to stay out of your way.

### The Renewal Price Trap

Norton's introductory offer is competitive. The renewal price is not. Users who forget to cancel auto-renew routinely find charges of $80 to $130 for what they thought was a $30/year product. Surfshark does run promotional pricing too, but the gap between first-year and ongoing costs is considerably smaller.
