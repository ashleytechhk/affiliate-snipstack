## What Is ProtonVPN Android?

ProtonVPN is a VPN service developed by Proton AG — the same Swiss company behind ProtonMail. It launched in 2017 and has since grown into one of the most respected privacy-focused VPNs on the market. On Android, it ships as a standalone app available on the Google Play Store, free to download and free to use on the basic tier.

A **VPN (Virtual Private Network)** reroutes your internet traffic through an encrypted tunnel to a server in another location. From the outside — your ISP, a public Wi-Fi network operator, or a website — your real IP address is hidden behind the VPN server's address. Your traffic appears to come from wherever that server is located.

For Android users, this matters in several practical scenarios:

- **Public Wi-Fi protection:** Coffee shops, airports, hotels — your traffic is encrypted and shielded from anyone snooping on the network.
- **IP privacy:** Websites you visit see the VPN server's IP, not yours.
- **Regional access:** Connecting to a server in a different country lets you access content or services available in that region.
- **ISP tracking prevention:** Your internet provider sees encrypted traffic to a VPN server, not your browsing activity.

ProtonVPN's Android app covers all of these with a clean interface and the backing of a company whose entire brand rests on privacy credibility.

## How ProtonVPN Android Actually Works

When you open the app and connect, here is [what happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) under the hood:

1. **Protocol negotiation** — ProtonVPN defaults to WireGuard on Android, currently the fastest and most efficient VPN protocol available. IKEv2 and OpenVPN are available as fallbacks.
2. **Encrypted tunnel** — Your device establishes an AES-256 encrypted connection to a ProtonVPN server. Traffic inside the tunnel is unreadable to anyone intercepting it in transit.
3. **IP masking** — Once connected, your traffic exits through the VPN server's IP address. Websites and services you visit see that IP, not yours.
4. **DNS handling** — ProtonVPN routes DNS queries through its own encrypted DNS servers, preventing DNS leaks that would expose your activity to third-party resolvers.
5. **Kill switch activation** — If the VPN connection drops unexpectedly, the kill switch blocks all traffic until the VPN reconnects or you manually disable it — preventing accidental IP exposure.

On Android specifically, ProtonVPN integrates with Android's native Always-on VPN setting in network preferences, meaning it can automatically reconnect after the app restarts or your phone reboots.

## Why It Matters: Swiss Jurisdiction and Audited No-Logs

ProtonVPN's privacy case is not marketing copy — it rests on two structural foundations.

### Swiss Jurisdiction

Switzerland is outside both EU and US legal frameworks. It has strong statutory privacy protections and is not a member of the Five Eyes, Nine Eyes, or Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing agreements. For a VPN company, Swiss incorporation means it cannot be compelled to hand over user data by EU or US court orders. It would take a Swiss legal proceeding — under Swiss law — to force disclosure.

This matters most for journalists, activists, researchers, or anyone whose threat model involves state-level or cross-border surveillance. For everyday users primarily concerned with Wi-Fi security or regional access, the practical day-to-day difference is smaller — but the structural protection is real.

### Independently Audited No-Logs Policy

ProtonVPN's no-logs policy has been independently verified by Securitum, a specialist security auditing firm. The audit examined ProtonVPN's infrastructure and confirmed that connection logs, IP addresses, session metadata, and browsing activity are not stored. Proton also publishes annual transparency reports documenting every government request it receives and what data (if any) was provided.

The combination — Swiss law plus independent audit plus public transparency — is among the strongest privacy foundations in the consumer VPN market.

## FAQ

**Is ProtonVPN really free on Android?**

Yes. The free tier is available on Android with no data cap and no advertising. It is limited to servers in three countries (US, Netherlands, Japan) and one simultaneous device connection. Speed may be slower than the paid tier during peak hours. No payment information is required to use it.

**Is ProtonVPN safe on Android?**

ProtonVPN uses AES-256 encryption, WireGuard/IKEv2/OpenVPN protocols, a verified no-logs policy, and DNS leak protection. Its apps are open-source and available for review on GitHub. Its no-logs claims have been independently audited. For an Android user, it is one of the most security-credible VPN options available.

**Can I use ProtonVPN Android for streaming?**

The free tier is not designed for streaming — server selection is limited and speeds can vary. ProtonVPN Plus includes streaming-optimized servers that work with several major platforms. For a streaming-first use case where you want the most consistent regional unblocking across the most devices, [Surfshark's Android app](/blog/vpn?sub=protonvpn-android-2026__faq) offers unlimited device connections and dedicated streaming servers at a lower paid price point.

**Does ProtonVPN Android have a kill switch?**

Yes. ProtonVPN's Android app includes a kill switch that blocks internet traffic if the VPN connection drops unexpectedly, preventing your real IP from being exposed. The feature is available in the app settings and can be set to always-on mode.

**How does the ProtonVPN Android app perform on battery?**

On WireGuard protocol, ProtonVPN has minimal battery impact. WireGuard's lightweight design means it runs efficiently compared to older protocols like OpenVPN. In typical use, the battery overhead is not meaningful.

**What is the difference between ProtonVPN free and Plus on Android?**

Free gives you unlimited data, no ads, and servers in three countries on one device. Plus adds 90+ country server access, streaming-optimized servers, Secure Core multi-hop routing, NetShield ad blocking, Tor over VPN, and up to 10 simultaneous devices. The upgrade path is straightforward from within the app.

**Can I use ProtonVPN on multiple Android devices?**

The free tier allows one simultaneous connection. ProtonVPN Plus allows up to 10 devices at once. If you need coverage across more than 10 devices — or want unlimited connections on any number of Android phones, tablets, and TV sticks — Surfshark's unlimited-device policy is the more flexible option.
