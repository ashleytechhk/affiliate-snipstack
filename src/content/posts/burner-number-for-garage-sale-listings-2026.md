## Why Your Garage Sale Listing Is a Dating Safety Problem

Most people think of their dating life and their side-hustle reselling furniture as completely separate worlds. They are not.

When you post a garage sale listing on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, OfferUp, or any similar platform, you typically include a phone number so buyers can reach you. That number is public. Anyone — not just serious buyers — can see it, copy it, and run it through any number of free reverse-lookup services available online.

Now consider what else is tied to that same phone number: your Apple ID, your Google account, your WhatsApp, and very likely your dating app profiles on Tinder, Hinge, or Bumble. Dating apps ask for phone verification precisely because it creates a unique identity anchor. The same anchor that keeps the apps safe from bots makes you traceable if the number ever gets into the wrong hands.

Data broker sites like Whitepages, Spokeo, and BeenVerified aggregate phone numbers against names, addresses, and social profiles automatically. You do not have to do anything wrong for this to happen. Your number gets scraped, matched, and listed — often within days of appearing publicly anywhere online.

For an online dater, this creates a specific threat model: someone who finds you unattractive or feels rejected on a dating app can search your number, find your Marketplace listing, learn your neighborhood from the listing photos, and show up. This is not a hypothetical. It is the plot of hundreds of police reports filed every year across the UK, Canada, and Brazil.

This is the simplest vector. Someone types your number into Google or a dedicated reverse-lookup site and gets your name, approximate address, and links to associated social media accounts within 30 seconds. Free tier results alone are often enough for a determined person.

### WhatsApp Profile Photos

When a buyer texts your number via WhatsApp, your profile photo is immediately visible. If you use the same photo on a dating app — which most people do — you have just confirmed the link between your marketplace identity and your dating identity.

### iMessage and Apple ID Correlation

Apple's "name and photo sharing" feature can display your first name and a photo to anyone who has your number in their contacts. If a buyer saves your number, they may see your name and face without you knowing.

### Facebook Cross-Matching

Facebook Marketplace listings are often paired with your Facebook profile. If your Facebook account is linked to the same phone number you list publicly, the connection between your marketplace activity and your social graph is trivially established.
