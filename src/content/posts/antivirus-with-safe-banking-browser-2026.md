## Why Seniors Are Targeted First

Cybercriminals follow the money. Older adults, on average, have more in savings, receive regular pension or retirement payments, and are more likely to respond to authority — messages that appear to come from a bank, a government office, or a well-known company.

The three most common attacks look like this:

**Phishing pages** — You receive an email or a text message saying your bank account has been locked or that an unusual payment was detected. You click the link. The page looks exactly like your real bank's website. You type your username and password. The criminal now has full access to your account.

**Keyloggers** — A piece of software installed without your knowledge records every key you press. When you log into your bank, the keylogger silently captures your username, password, and any security code you type. You never notice anything unusual.

**Man-in-the-browser attacks** — Malware embeds itself inside your web browser and intercepts your banking session in real time. You are on your real bank's website, logged in correctly, but everything you do — transfers, payments, balance checks — can be watched, copied, or altered.

Standard antivirus software catches many of these threats. But by the time a new variant of banking malware reaches your computer, it may take days before your antivirus receives the update needed to recognize it. A safe banking browser solves this differently: instead of trying to recognize and block each new threat, it removes the environment those threats need to operate.

## What a Safe Banking Browser Actually Does

Think of your normal browser — Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari — as a busy open office. Every extension you have installed, every website you visited recently, every other app running on your computer has some access to that environment. A safe banking browser is more like a locked private room.

When you open it, it starts fresh with no extensions, no cookies from other sites, and no connection to the rest of your computer's activity. The room has no windows anyone can look through. When you close it, it disappears, leaving nothing behind.

More specifically, a safe banking browser typically does four things your regular browser does not:

![How a safe banking browser isolates your session from malware](/og/antivirus-with-safe-banking-browser-2026-inline-1.png)

**1. Blocks keyloggers.** The secure session intercepts keyboard inputs at a different level than ordinary applications can read. A keylogger watching your regular desktop cannot see what you type inside the protected window.

**2. Prevents screenshot and screen-capture malware.** Some banking trojans take screenshots every few seconds and send them to the attacker. A safe banking browser blocks screen-capture tools from working while it is open.

**3. Verifies the real website.** Before the page loads, the browser checks that the address you typed matches the real, verified certificate of your bank — not a fake copy. If something does not match, it warns you before you enter anything.

**4. Isolates the session.** Nothing you do inside the safe browser is visible to other apps or browser extensions running on your computer at the same time.

**Step 1 — Check your bank's real web address.** Every legitimate bank website uses HTTPS (the padlock icon in your browser address bar). **Step 2 — Never click banking links in emails or texts.** Banks do not ask you to log in by clicking an email link. If you receive a message saying there is a problem with your account, open your browser and type your bank's address directly. Do not click.

**Step 3 — Use a strong, unique password for your bank account.** A unique password means that if one of your other accounts is breached, criminals cannot use that password to access your bank. If remembering multiple passwords is difficult, a password manager handles this automatically.

**Step 4 — Enable two-step verification with your bank.** Most banks now offer this. When you log in, the bank sends a code to your phone by text. Even if a criminal has your password, they cannot get in without that code.
