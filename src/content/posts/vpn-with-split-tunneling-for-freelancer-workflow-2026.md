## What Is Split Tunneling, and Why Does It Matter for Freelancers?

A standard VPN connection routes 100% of your device's internet traffic through an encrypted server. That's fine for a single-purpose laptop, but freelancers rarely have that luxury. You might need:

1. **Full encryption** for a client's shared drive or an invoicing platform holding payment details.
2. **Direct, unencrypted speed** for large local file transfers, video calls, or apps that don't play well with VPNs.
3. **A specific region** for one browser tab (checking software pricing abroad) while everything else stays on your home connection.

Split tunneling gives you all three at once, on the same device, at the same time. Instead of an all-or-nothing tunnel, you build a rule set: this app goes through the VPN, that one doesn't.

### The Specific Friction Freelancers Run Into

Freelancers often work across several client environments in a single day — a design agency's asset server in the morning, a startup's project management tool in the afternoon, your own invoicing software in between. Each may have different security expectations, and none of them care about the others.

Running a full VPN tunnel for all of it can throttle video calls, break VOIP quality, or trigger fraud checks on payment platforms that flag connections from unexpected VPN exit servers. Turning the VPN off entirely for convenience defeats the point of having one. Split tunneling removes that trade-off by letting the protection follow the task, not the whole device.

Some VPNs only let you exclude whole networks (like your home Wi-Fi) from the tunnel. Freelancers need per-app granularity — the ability to say "route Chrome through the VPN, but not Zoom" — since client work and personal use often happen in the same browser window.

Surfshark's Bypasser feature supports both app-level and website-level exclusion, so you can build rules that match how you actually work rather than a blanket on/off switch.

### Speed for the Traffic That Stays Tunneled

Whatever you do route through the VPN — client portals, invoicing, cloud sync — needs to stay fast. Surfshark uses WireGuard, IKEv2, and OpenVPN, automatically selecting the fastest available protocol for whichever app you've routed into the tunnel.

### Unlimited Devices

Freelancers often work across a laptop, a phone for client calls, and a tablet for reviewing deliverables. A VPN that caps simultaneous connections becomes a real limitation the moment you add a second device to your setup.

Surfshark places no limit on simultaneous connections, so split tunneling rules can be configured independently across every device you use for client work.

### Reliability and a Kill Switch

A VPN kill switch cuts your internet if the tunnel drops unexpectedly, so a client portal session never falls back to an unencrypted connection without you noticing. For freelancers handling sensitive client data, this is a non-negotiable safety net alongside split tunneling.
