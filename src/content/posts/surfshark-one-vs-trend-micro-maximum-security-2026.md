## The Problem With Switching to Another Heavy Suite

Here is the mistake that Norton and McAfee refugees make most often: they pick the next big-name antivirus on the list without asking whether it has the same problems under a different logo.

Trend Micro Maximum Security is a competent product. Its malware detection scores from independent labs like AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives are consistently strong. Its Folder Shield feature, which protects designated folders from ransomware encryption, is genuinely useful. Its Pay Guard browser adds a sandboxed environment for online banking.

But it shares structural DNA with the suites you just left:

- **The VPN gap.** Trend Micro does not bundle a full VPN into Maximum Security. Its Wi-Fi protection features exist, but a capped or partial network privacy tool is not a substitute for a proper VPN if you ever use a laptop at a cafe, airport, or hotel.
- **The device ceiling.** Maximum Security covers up to 5 devices. For a household with two laptops, two phones, and a tablet, that is your entire allowance — add anything else and you are either paying for another tier or leaving a device unprotected.
- **The renewal pattern.** Like Norton and McAfee, Trend Micro uses introductory pricing that does not reflect what you will actually pay after the first year. The gap between year-one and year-two cost is worth checking before you commit.

None of this makes Trend Micro a bad product. It makes it a familiar product. If you are looking for a genuinely different experience, the question becomes whether that difference exists in [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-norton-360-comparison-2026/).

## Where Trend Micro Maximum Security Is Genuinely Better

A fair comparison means acknowledging where Trend Micro wins outright.

**Folder Shield** is a dedicated ransomware protection layer that lets you designate specific folders as protected — only trusted applications can modify files inside them. Surfshark's antivirus covers ransomware through its real-time engine, but Trend Micro's folder-level approach is more granular for users who want explicit control over their most important files.

**Pay Guard** is a hardened browser mode for online banking and financial transactions. It launches a sandboxed environment that isolates your session from other running processes. This is a meaningful feature for users who do significant banking online and want the session itself protected, not just the network.

**Password Manager.** Trend Micro Maximum Security includes a password manager. [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-plus-vs-one-comparison-2026/) does not offer a native equivalent. If you do not already use a standalone password manager and want one bundled into your [security suite](https://snipstack.io/security-suite-for-android-and-windows-together-2026/), this is a genuine advantage for Trend Micro.

**Longer Track Record.** Trend Micro has been in the security space since the early 1990s. Its malware detection engine has decades of development and consistently ranks well in independent lab tests. Surfshark's antivirus is newer. For users who weight heritage and test-lab pedigree heavily, Trend Micro earns that deference.
