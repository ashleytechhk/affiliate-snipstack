The result: you back a smartwatch campaign in 2024 and by 2026 you are receiving newsletters from three accessory brands, a tech blog, and a coupon service — none of which you deliberately subscribed to.

Using an [email alias for](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-privacy-without-creating-new-account-2026/) each Kickstarter campaign you back solves this at the root. You give each creator a disposable address. Legitimate shipping updates and campaign news still arrive in your inbox. The moment one alias starts flooding you with off-topic mail, you disable it. Your real email stays clean.

## SimpleLogin: Best Free Option

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service with a generous free tier — 15 aliases at no cost, unlimited on the paid plan. It integrates with Bitwarden and ProtonMail, making it a natural fit for privacy-conscious users already in those ecosystems.

For a Kickstarter backer who supports a handful of campaigns per year, the free plan is likely enough. The interface is clean, alias creation is fast, and you can add custom domain names on paid plans for a more professional look.

The catch is the ceiling. If you back 20+ campaigns annually, you burn through the free 15 aliases quickly. The paid plan at roughly $4 per month solves that but without the bundled extras Alt-ID includes.

## Apple Hide My Email: Convenient for iOS Users

If you are already paying for iCloud+, Hide My Email is built in. You generate aliases directly from Safari autofill or the Settings app, and Apple handles forwarding to your real address. It works elegantly within the Apple ecosystem.

The limitation for Kickstarter backers is platform lock-in. If you pledge on a browser other than Safari, or on an Android device, Hide My Email is unavailable without workarounds. It also lacks a dashboard with alias-level analytics, so identifying which campaign alias is generating the most spam requires manual checking.

---

## DuckDuckGo Email Protection: Best Free No-Frills Alias

DuckDuckGo's Email Protection service gives you a @duck.com address that forwards to your real inbox with tracking pixels stripped. It is free, unlimited, and requires no account beyond a DuckDuckGo account.

For Kickstarter backers, the main limitation is that it gives you one primary duck.com address rather than per-campaign unique aliases (though you can generate unique addresses from the extension). It is a solid option if you want zero cost and minimal setup, but managing which campaign generated which spam is harder without per-campaign isolation.

---

## Fastmail + Masked Email: Best for Power Users

Fastmail's Masked Email feature, when paired with Bitwarden's integration, lets you generate unique aliases directly inside Bitwarden's autofill. It is a tight workflow — open a pledge form, Bitwarden suggests a masked email, you click accept, Fastmail handles the forwarding.

The downside is cost and complexity. You need both a Fastmail subscription and Bitwarden (free or premium). That is two subscriptions to manage, and the integration requires some setup. For technically comfortable backers who already use both tools, it is excellent. For everyone else, it is more friction than necessary.
