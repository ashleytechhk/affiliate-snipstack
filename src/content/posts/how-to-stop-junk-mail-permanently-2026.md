## Part 1 — Stopping Physical Junk Mail

Paper junk mail is a bigger privacy problem than most people realise. Your address is sold by retailers, loyalty programs, and public records. Three registries cover the vast majority of physical mail spam.

### Step 1 — Register on DMAchoice (DMA OptOut)

The Data & Marketing Association's opt-out registry is the single largest suppression list for direct mail in the United States. Marketers who are DMA members are required to check it before sending bulk mail.

1. Go to **dmachoice.org** in your browser.
2. Create a free account using your name and current mailing address.
3. Select the mail categories you want to opt out of: catalogs, magazine offers, credit offers, and other mail.
4. Complete registration. The DMA charges a nominal $2 processing fee to verify your identity — this is the only cost in the entire guide.
5. Allow 30–60 days for mail to slow down. Mailers print and send in batches, so the effect is not immediate.

**Pro tip:** Register every adult in your household separately. The suppression list is name-and-address specific.

### Step 2 — Opt Out of Pre-Screened Credit Card Offers

Those envelopes promising "You've been pre-selected for a Platinum card" come from a different source: the major credit bureaus. Credit card issuers pay Experian, Equifax, TransUnion, and Innovis for lists of consumers who meet certain credit criteria. You can opt out directly.

1. Go to **optoutprescreen.com** — the official, FTC-referenced opt-out site operated by the credit bureaus.
2. Choose **5-Year Opt-Out** (online, takes effect in days) or **Permanent Opt-Out** (requires a mailed form but lasts indefinitely).
3. For the permanent option: download and print the form, sign it, and mail it to the address shown. Keep a photo of the form before you mail it.
4. For the 5-year option: fill in your name, address, date of birth, and the last four digits of your [Social Security](https://snipstack.io/social-security-scam-call-verify-2026/) Number. The site is encrypted and operated by the credit bureaus themselves.
5. Confirm your submission. You will [stop receiving](https://snipstack.io/stop-receiving-spam-from-unsubscribe-forms-2026/) pre-screened offers within a few weeks.

**Note:** Opting out of pre-screened offers does not affect your credit score and does not prevent you from applying for credit cards normally.

### Step 3 — Request Removal from Data Brokers

DMAchoice and OptOutPrescreen cover most bulk mailers, but data brokers (Spokeo, Whitepages, BeenVerified, Intelius, etc.) sell your information to smaller businesses and individuals. Each broker has its own removal process.

1. Search for your name on the top five brokers: Spokeo, Whitepages, Intelius, BeenVerified, and MyLife.
2. Find your listing and look for a "Remove my information" or "Opt out" link — usually in the site footer or on the listing page itself.
3. Complete each form individually. Most require you to verify via email or a [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/).
4. Screenshot or log each removal request with a date. Some brokers re-list profiles after several months; having records makes follow-up easier.
5. Repeat quarterly. Set a calendar reminder.

If you want to automate this step, paid data broker removal services (not covered in this guide) can handle the recurring sweep on your behalf.

## Part 2 — Stopping Digital Spam

Paper mail is annoying. Digital spam is a security risk. Phishing, credential stuffing, and spam all rely on your real email address being in as many databases as possible. The most effective long-term fix is to never give out your real address again.

### Step 4 — Audit Where Your Email Address Is Exposed

Before you fix anything, understand the scale of the problem.

1. Go to **haveibeenpwned.com** and enter your email address. This shows you how many data breaches have included your address.
2. Note the number. If it is above five, your address is already widely distributed among spam lists.
3. Accept that unsubscribing from existing spam is a losing battle once your address is breached — spammers trade lists and ignore unsubscribes. The only winning move is a new alias layer going forward.

### Step 6 — Unsubscribe from Legitimate Mailing Lists

For newsletters and marketing emails from companies you actually deal with, unsubscribing is worth doing — these companies are legally required to honor opt-outs under CAN-SPAM.

1. Open your inbox and sort by sender. Identify senders you recognise but no longer want email from.
2. Click "Unsubscribe" in the footer of each email. Do not unsubscribe from emails you do not recognise — that confirms your address is active.
3. Use Gmail's "Unsubscribe" button at the top of promotional emails if available — it is faster than the footer link.
4. Allow 10 business days per sender. CAN-SPAM requires opt-outs to be honored within this window.

## Troubleshooting

**Mail is still coming after 60 days.**
Check whether the sender is a DMA member. Local businesses, political campaigns, nonprofits, and religious organisations are exempt from DMAchoice. You will need to contact these senders individually.

**OptOutPrescreen says my information cannot be verified.**
The form uses soft credit bureau matching. If your address has changed recently or your credit file has limited information, try the paper mail option instead — it uses a signed form rather than digital verification.

**Spam emails keep arriving even though I unsubscribed.**
If the sender is not a recognisable company, do not interact with the unsubscribe link — it confirms your address. Mark as spam and delete. An alias system (Step 5) prevents this problem for all future sign-ups.

**A data broker relisted my profile.**
Some brokers refresh their databases from public records periodically. Check the broker's opt-out policy — some require annual re-submission. Log your original opt-out date so you can demonstrate prior removal if needed.

**My alias is forwarding spam but I do not want to lose it.**
Most alias services let you pause or mute an alias rather than delete it. Pause it, wait a week, and if no legitimate mail is missed, delete it permanently.

**How long does it take to stop junk mail permanently?**
Physical mail: 30–90 days for DMAchoice; a few weeks for OptOutPrescreen credit offers. Digital spam from existing lists may take longer, but new spam stops immediately once you switch to an alias system for all new sign-ups.

**Is OptOutPrescreen safe to use?**
Yes. OptOutPrescreen.com is operated by the major credit bureaus and is referenced directly by the FTC's consumer advice pages. The 5-year online opt-out requires partial SSN for identity matching, which is standard for credit bureau processes.

**Does opting out of pre-screened offers hurt my credit score?**
No. Opting out removes you from marketing lists, not from credit eligibility. You can still apply for any card or loan; lenders will still pull your credit file when you apply directly.
