## What Is a Geo Restriction and Why Does Sling TV Use One?

Sling TV is a US-only live TV streaming service. Because Sling TV holds its content licenses specifically for US distribution, it is required to block access from other countries. The mechanism is IP-based: when Sling TV sees an IP address registered outside the United States, it blocks playback. Your account credentials are valid; your detected location is not.

This affects more people than most expect:

- **US subscribers traveling abroad** — the subscription keeps billing; access stops at the border.
- **American expats** living outside the US long-term.
- **Students studying abroad** who want US sports or news coverage.
- **Sports fans outside the US** — live sports on Sling TV is a major draw; geo-blocks cut off access to events viewers are actively paying for.

A VPN does not change your account — it changes what location Sling TV sees.
