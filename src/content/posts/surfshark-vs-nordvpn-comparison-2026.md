## How Streaming Unblocking Actually Works

### The Technical Basics

When you connect to a VPN server in Turkey, your IP address changes to one registered in Turkey. Streaming platforms use IP geolocation to determine which regional library to show you and which price tier to apply. If the platform accepts your VPN-assigned IP as a legitimate Turkish IP, you get Turkish prices and content.

The catch is that platforms actively identify and block VPN IP ranges. A VPN provider's ability to stay ahead of that blocking — by rotating IP addresses, maintaining residential IPs, and pushing updates — determines whether it remains useful for arbitrage.

### Where Surfshark and NordVPN Stand

Both services maintain strong track records on the streaming unblocking front. Independent tests consistently show both successfully accessing Netflix libraries across the US, UK, Japan, Australia, and arbitrage-popular regions including Turkey and India.

**Surfshark's Smart DNS** is worth highlighting for arbitrage users. It works on devices that cannot run a VPN app natively — smart TVs, streaming sticks, certain gaming consoles. You configure the DNS settings on the device itself rather than installing software. This means you can apply regional pricing to a streaming box without needing router-level VPN configuration.

**NordVPN's SmartPlay** is equivalent in function and equally well-supported.

Neither service is meaningfully ahead of the other for streaming access. The differentiation lies elsewhere.
