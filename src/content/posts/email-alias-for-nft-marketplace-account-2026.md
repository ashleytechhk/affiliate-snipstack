## Why NFT Marketplace Accounts Attract Spam

NFT platforms monetise their user lists aggressively. When you register on OpenSea, Magic Eden, Blur, or any emerging marketplace, your email address enters a marketing funnel that may persist for years — even after you stop using the platform.

The risks are not limited to annoying newsletters:

- **Phishing attacks**: Scammers clone legitimate platform emails and send fake "your wallet is at risk" warnings to harvest seed phrases.
- **Data breaches**: Several NFT platforms have experienced user data leaks. A compromised email address is the first step in a credential-stuffing attack.
- **Cross-platform profiling**: If you use the same email across wallets, forums, and marketplaces, advertisers and bad actors can build a detailed profile of your activity.

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-pharmacy-prescription-alerts-2026/) breaks all three attack vectors. The marketplace gets a disposable address. Your real inbox stays clean. If the alias starts attracting spam or is compromised in a breach, you delete it and generate a new one — your real address is never exposed.

## SimpleLogin — Best for Power Users

SimpleLogin (now owned by Proton, the makers of ProtonMail) is the most feature-complete standalone alias service available. You can create unlimited aliases on the paid plan, attach custom domains, and reply to forwarded emails — the recipient sees the alias, not your real address.

For NFT users who manage multiple wallets, run Discord communities, or collaborate on projects under a pseudonym, SimpleLogin's custom domain support is genuinely useful. You can have all marketplace correspondence forwarding to one inbox while appearing to come from different project addresses.

**Trade-off**: SimpleLogin is a dedicated service, not a bundle. You pay for the alias service separately from any VPN or antivirus you use. The free tier is functional but limits you to one custom domain and imposes alias limits that fill up quickly if you sign up for many platforms.

## AnonAddy — Best Free Option

AnonAddy (recently rebranded as addy.io) offers a generous free tier: up to 20 shared domain aliases per month, with forwarding and basic reply support. For a casual NFT collector who registers on one or two platforms occasionally, this is a workable free option.

The paid plan unlocks unlimited aliases and custom domains at a price point below SimpleLogin. The interface is functional rather than polished, which suits technically confident users but may frustrate beginners.

**Watch out for**: AnonAddy's shared domains (e.g., @anonaddy.com) are publicly known alias domains. Some platforms flag or block signups from known alias providers. If you encounter signup rejections, a custom domain alias (available on paid tiers) resolves this.

## Apple Hide My Email — Best for iOS-Only Users

If you live in the Apple ecosystem — iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Safari — Hide My Email is already sitting in your iCloud settings waiting to be used. It generates random @privaterelay.appleid.com addresses that forward to your Apple ID email.

The catch is the platform lock-in. You cannot use Hide My Email on Android, Windows, or non-Safari browsers. If you use MetaMask on Chrome desktop to interact with NFT marketplaces, Hide My Email is not in play. It also does not support reply-from-alias on most web platforms.

For NFT users who primarily use mobile apps (some marketplaces have solid iOS apps), it is a genuinely zero-friction option — but it covers only part of the workflow.

## What to Look For When Choosing an Alias Tool

Not all alias services behave the same way under pressure. Before committing, check these four things:

**Reply support**: Can you reply to forwarded emails without revealing your real address? Essential if you need to contact marketplace support or respond to collaborators.

**Alias deactivation speed**: If a breach exposes your alias, how fast can you kill it? Alt ID and SimpleLogin both support instant deactivation from the dashboard.

**Platform acceptance**: Some marketplaces check email domains against known alias provider lists and reject signups. Custom domains (paid feature on most services) avoid this. Alt ID's forwarding domains are less likely to be blocked than well-known shared alias domains.

**Data handling**: The whole point of an alias is privacy. Check whether the service logs forwarded email content or metadata. SimpleLogin (Proton-owned) and AnonAddy both publish open-source code that can be audited. Surfshark publishes a no-logs policy for its core VPN product.

## FAQ

**[Can I receive](https://snipstack.io/can-i-receive-calls-on-virtual-number-2026/) calls or texts using an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-figma-account-registration-2026/)?**
No. An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-hotel-chain-points-account-2026/) only forwards email. It does not cover phone verification steps. If an NFT marketplace requires SMS verification, use a separate [virtual number](https://snipstack.io/virtual-number-for-international-business-calls-2026/) service for that step.

**Will NFT marketplaces reject alias email addresses?**
Some platforms do scan for known alias domain patterns and block registration. Using a custom domain alias (available on SimpleLogin and AnonAddy paid tiers) typically bypasses this. Alt ID's alias domains are less widely blacklisted than older shared alias services. **[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if the alias service shuts down?**
Your alias addresses would stop forwarding, and you would lose access to accounts that used them unless you update your email on each platform first. This is a real risk with smaller or free-tier-only services. Paid tiers on established services (Surfshark, Proton/SimpleLogin) carry lower shutdown risk.

**Can I use one alias across multiple NFT marketplaces?**
Technically yes, but it is not recommended. Using a unique alias per platform means you can pinpoint exactly which service leaked or sold your address if spam starts arriving. One alias per platform is the best practice.

**[Is using an](https://snipstack.io/is-using-an-ad-blocker-legal-2026/) [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-insurance-comparison-sites-2026/) legal?**
Yes. Using a forwarding [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-recruitment-agency-privacy-2026/) is no different from having multiple email addresses. There are no laws in most jurisdictions that require you to register for commercial web services using a primary personal address.

**Does an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-price-comparison-websites-2026/) protect my wallet address too?**
No. An alias only shields your email address. Your wallet address is public on the blockchain by design. For wallet-level privacy, you need different tools — such as a VPN to mask the IP address you broadcast transactions from.
