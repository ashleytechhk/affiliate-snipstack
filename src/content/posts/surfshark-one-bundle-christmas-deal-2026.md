## VPN Performance: Real-World Speed Across Key Regions

The VPN component is where Surfshark genuinely leads. Norton's VPN has a data cap on lower plans and is often flagged as a secondary product. McAfee's VPN is similarly bolted on.

Surfshark VPN is the primary product — it is where the engineering focus lives. What that means practically:

- **WireGuard protocol** — fastest modern protocol, typically 300–500 Mbps on a gigabit connection
- **100+ countries** — servers in Japan, the UK, Canada, Brazil, Turkey, Argentina, and dozens more
- **Camouflage mode** — obfuscation that makes VPN traffic look like normal HTTPS, useful on restrictive networks
- **MultiHop** — route through two servers for extra layering (slower, but there when you need it)
- **Split tunneling** — send specific apps through the VPN while others use your local connection directly

For a family sharing one subscription, the unlimited simultaneous connections policy is the biggest practical difference. Norton 360 Deluxe covers 5 devices. [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-norton-360-deluxe-features-2026/) covers every device in the house under one login — phones, tablets, laptops, smart TVs, and routers.
