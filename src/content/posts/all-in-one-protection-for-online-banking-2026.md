## Why Online Banking Needs More Than Basic [Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/vs-antivirus-2026/)

Standard antivirus stops malware that's already on your device. That's useful. But the threats that target banking sessions in 2026 operate before anything touches your hard drive.

**Network interception on public Wi-Fi** — coffee shops, airport lounges, hotel lobbies. An unencrypted connection lets anyone on the same network see your traffic. Banking apps typically use HTTPS, but DNS requests and metadata remain visible.

**Credential phishing** — a convincing login page one character off from your real bank's domain. Antivirus alone rarely catches these in real time. You need active web threat blocking that checks URLs as you navigate, not after the fact.

**Data breach exposure** — your banking email address and password may already be sitting in a breach database from an unrelated site where you reused credentials. If nothing is monitoring for that, you won't know until something goes wrong.

**Session hijacking malware** — banking trojans that inject fields into legitimate banking pages to capture form data. This one is where antivirus earns its keep — but only if the engine is current and real-time protection is running.

One tool rarely addresses all four. That's the actual problem with the "I already have Norton" answer.
