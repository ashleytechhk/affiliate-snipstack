## What "Disposable Subdomain Support" Actually Means

Most alias tools give you a flat alias: `random-string@service.com`. Subdomain support goes a layer deeper. It gives you a dedicated subdomain tied to your account — something like `yourname.service.com` — so every address you create under it (`shop@yourname.service.com`, `newsletter@yourname.service.com`) is instantly identifiable by sender category.

The practical benefit: when a site leaks or sells your alias, you do not just kill one address. You can disable the entire subdomain scope, or inspect which category of alias is drawing the spam. It is structured noise cancellation rather than reactive whack-a-mole.

Key capabilities to evaluate:

- **Forwarding fidelity** — does the alias forward cleanly, including attachments and HTML formatting?
- **Reply masking** — can you reply from the alias without revealing your real address?
- **Bulk management** — can you create, pause, or delete aliases in batches?
- **Custom domain support** — can you bring your own domain in addition to the shared subdomain?
- **Catch-all mode** — does any address on your subdomain route to your inbox automatically?

## SimpleLogin: Best for Power Users and Self-Hosters

SimpleLogin is the most technically complete standalone alias service available. The free tier is genuinely useful — 10 aliases, 1 subdomain, unlimited forwarding. The paid tier ($4/month) unlocks unlimited aliases, custom domains, catch-all mode, and the ability to self-host the entire stack on your own server.

**What works well:**

The subdomain implementation is thorough. You get a dedicated subdomain on SimpleLogin's shared domain or your own domain. Catch-all mode means any address at `*@yoursubdomain.slmail.me` routes to your inbox automatically — useful when signing up to services that generate confirmation links to addresses you did not create in advance.

PGP encryption is supported end-to-end. The browser extension integrates alias creation directly into signup forms. The API is documented and usable.

**Limitations:**

The interface takes longer to learn than Alt-ID. Self-hosting requires a server and some technical confidence. The mobile apps are functional but less polished than the web dashboard.

**Who it is for:** Developers, privacy researchers, and users who want maximum control and are comfortable with configuration.

## Apple Hide My Email: Best for iPhone-First Users

Apple's Hide My Email generates random `@privaterelay.appleid.com` addresses that forward to your iCloud inbox. It is available to iCloud+ subscribers and integrates directly into Safari, Settings, and the App Store signup flow.

**What works well:**

The friction is nearly zero for Apple users. If you are creating an account in Safari on iPhone, Hide My Email surfaces automatically. Managing aliases from Settings > iCloud > Hide My Email is straightforward.

**Limitations:**

There is no subdomain structure. Every alias is a flat randomized string — you cannot organize by category or enable catch-all routing. Reply masking exists but is limited to Apple Mail. If you use Gmail or Outlook as your client, the experience degrades. Aliases are tied to your Apple ID: if you ever cancel iCloud+, forwarding stops.

**Who it is for:** iPhone and Mac users who want quick, low-friction alias creation inside the Apple ecosystem.

## DuckDuckGo Email Protection: Best Free Entry Point

DuckDuckGo Email Protection gives you a `@duck.com` address that strips trackers and forwards clean email to your real inbox. It is free, requires no account beyond a DuckDuckGo username, and adds tracker-blocking to every forwarded message.

**What works well:**

The tracker stripping is the differentiator. DuckDuckGo reports which trackers were removed from each forwarded email, which is a useful educational layer on top of simple alias forwarding.

**Limitations:**

No subdomain structure. No reply masking. No custom domains. Alias management is limited compared to any of the paid alternatives. It is a single-alias-per-account tool for most users rather than a structured inbox privacy system.

**Who it is for:** Privacy newcomers who want a free, no-configuration introduction to email aliasing.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between an email alias and a disposable email address?**

A disposable email is temporary — it exists to receive one confirmation email and then expires. An email alias is persistent and managed. You control when it stops forwarding, and you can reply from it. Aliases with subdomain support add a structural layer on top, letting you group aliases under one routing address for easier management.

**Do email aliases work with two-factor authentication codes?**

Yes, in most cases. SMS 2FA bypasses email entirely. Email-based 2FA codes forward through the alias to your real inbox without delay in services like Alt-ID and SimpleLogin. There is no meaningful difference in delivery speed for most use cases.

**Can websites detect that I am using an alias?**

Some registration forms attempt to block known alias domains. Services with large shared domains (like `@duck.com`) are more likely to be flagged than services with less-known forwarding domains. If a site blocks an alias, using a custom domain via SimpleLogin is the most reliable workaround.

**What happens to my emails if I cancel the service?**

This varies by tool. With Alt-ID, forwarding stops when your Surfshark subscription ends. With SimpleLogin, free-tier aliases remain active; paid-tier features revert to free limits. With Apple Hide My Email, aliases stop forwarding if you cancel iCloud+. Always export your alias list before cancelling any service. **[Learn more about Alt-ID's alias management >](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-with-disposable-subdomain-support-2026__faq)**

**Is it possible to use multiple alias services simultaneously?**

Yes, and it is a common pattern. Some users maintain DuckDuckGo Email Protection for throwaway signups (free, no management overhead) and Alt-ID or SimpleLogin for aliases they actively manage and reply from. There is no technical conflict between running aliases across multiple services.

**Does reply masking hide my real email from the recipient?**

In Alt-ID and SimpleLogin, yes — the reply sender appears as the alias address, not your real address. The recipient sees no trace of your real inbox. Apple Hide My Email supports reply masking only within Apple Mail; third-party clients may reveal your real address depending on configuration.
