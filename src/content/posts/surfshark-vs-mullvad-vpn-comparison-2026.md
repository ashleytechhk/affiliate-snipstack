## What Is a Subscription Arbitrageur — and Why It Changes Everything

Before getting into specs, it is worth naming the use case plainly.

A subscription arbitrageur uses a VPN to connect to a country where streaming services price their subscriptions in local currency — often significantly lower than US or UK retail pricing. You are not pirating content. You are paying a real subscription, in a real market, at that market's real price. The VPN is simply the tool that lets your device appear to be in that country when you sign up or renew.

For this to work reliably, you need:

1. A large server network across many countries (more options for cheaper pricing regions)
2. Servers that streaming platforms have not blocked
3. Smart DNS for devices that do not support VPN apps natively (smart TVs, some consoles)
4. Unlimited simultaneous connections (one subscription covering your phone, laptop, tablet, and TV)
5. A low per-month cost, so the VPN itself does not eat your savings

Mullvad was not built around any of these requirements. Surfshark was.
