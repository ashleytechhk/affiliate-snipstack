## Why Real Estate Inquiries Are a Spam Magnet

Property searches generate more commercial email than almost any other online activity. Here is why:

**Listing portals sell leads.** When you request more information on Zillow, Realtor.com, Rightmove, or any regional equivalent, your email is packaged as a "lead" and sold to multiple agents simultaneously. That is the business model. You asked about one property; six agents now have your address.

**Agent CRMs auto-import contacts.** The moment an agent enters your email into their CRM, it often syncs to marketing automation tools. You get added to drip campaigns you never opted into — monthly market reports, listing alerts, seminar invitations.

**Open-house sign-in sheets are harvested.** Physical sign-in sheets at open houses are digitised and uploaded. That handwritten email goes into the same lead database.

**Data brokers pick up the rest.** Any time your email appears on a real estate portal, a data broker may index it alongside your probable buying intent. That profile gets sold to adjacent industries — mortgage lenders, home insurance providers, renovation services.

The result: 50+ spam emails a day from a single property search, many from senders you have no way to trace back to their source.

### SimpleLogin

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service with a free tier and a paid plan that unlocks unlimited aliases and reply masking. It is more technical than Alt-ID — the interface is functional rather than polished, and setup assumes you know what you are doing.

**What works:** Strong privacy credentials (open source, self-hostable), browser extension for one-click alias generation at checkout or form-fill, and solid reply masking on the paid tier.

**What does not:** The free tier caps you at 15 aliases, which is genuinely limiting if you are running a serious property search across multiple markets. The interface is dated. Support is community-driven. No bundled VPN or data-removal features.

**Real estate fit:** Good for developers or privacy-first users who want full control and do not mind a steeper setup. Not ideal for someone who just wants the spam to stop.

---

### Apple Hide My Email

Built into iCloud+, Hide My Email generates random Apple-managed aliases that forward to your Apple ID address. It is seamless on Apple devices — Safari surfaces the option in form fields automatically.

**What works:** Zero friction on iPhone, iPad, and Mac. No additional app or subscription beyond iCloud+ (which most Apple users already pay for). Unlimited alias creation.

**What does not:** Useless on Android and Windows. No reply masking — replies go out from your real Apple ID address. No browser extension for Chrome or Firefox. If your property search crosses platforms (desktop research on a Windows PC, viewings booked on iPhone), the experience fragments badly.

**Real estate fit:** Only practical for buyers who do everything on Apple hardware and never need to reply to agents from a non-Apple device.

---

### DuckDuckGo Email Protection

DuckDuckGo's free email protection service gives you a `@duck.com` forwarding address and strips email trackers before delivery. It is the simplest tool on this list and the only fully free option.

**What works:** No account required beyond the DuckDuckGo browser or app. Tracker stripping is a genuinely useful bonus — real estate marketing emails are loaded with tracking pixels. Free forever.

**What does not:** No reply masking — replies reveal your real address. No per-alias creation (you get one `@duck.com` address, not separate aliases per portal). No dashboard or alias management. Cannot identify which portal leaked your data because everything arrives at the same address.

**Real estate fit:** A reasonable starting point for casual use, but the lack of per-alias creation makes it useless for the forensic tracking that makes alias systems powerful for property searches.

---

### Temp-Mail and Guerrilla Mail

These are throwaway inbox services — you visit the site, grab a random address, use it, and discard it. No account, no setup.

**What does not work for real estate:** Absolutely nothing about the real estate inquiry flow is one-shot. You submit an inquiry today; the agent follows up tomorrow; the portal sends listing alerts next week; the mortgage broker calls in a month. A throwaway address that expires in 10 minutes or resets when you close the browser is useless for any of this.

Additionally, most listing portals and agent CRMs detect and block known throwaway domains. Submissions from Temp-Mail addresses are often silently rejected or flagged as low-quality leads.

**Real estate fit:** None. Use these only for one-time form fills where you will never need the address again.

## FAQ

**Will agents be put off if I use an alias address?**

No. Your alias looks like a regular email address — agents cannot tell the difference. Correspondence arrives and leaves normally. The only thing hidden is your real address.

**Can I reply to agents from my alias without revealing my real email?**

Yes, with Alt-ID's reply masking enabled. Replies go out from the alias address. Your real email never appears in any outgoing header or thread.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if a portal rejects my alias address?**

Some portals aggressively block known alias domains. If this happens, note the portal name, use your real address for that specific submission, and monitor that inbox manually. This is the exception rather than the rule for established alias services like Alt-ID.

**Do I need a separate alias for every agent I contact?**

Not mandatory, but it makes leaker identification precise. If you use one `agents-` alias for all agents and spam starts arriving on it, you know an agent sold your data but not which one. Per-agent aliases tell you exactly who. Given that Alt-ID is unlimited, creating one alias per agent costs nothing.

**Is an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-insurance-comparison-sites-2026/) the same as a [disposable email](https://snipstack.io/disposable-email-that-receives-messages-2026/)?**

No. A disposable email expires quickly and does not forward to your real inbox. An alias is persistent, forwards everything, supports replies, and can be deleted on demand. For real estate — where you need ongoing communication over weeks or months — you need a persistent alias, not a disposable.

**What if I am already drowning in real estate spam on my current address?**

Start fresh. Create a new primary email address and migrate only your trusted contacts and essential accounts to it. Use [Alt-ID](https://snipstack.io/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-real-estate-inquiries-2026__faq) for every new real estate touchpoint from this point forward. Your old address will keep receiving spam, but your new primary stays clean indefinitely.
