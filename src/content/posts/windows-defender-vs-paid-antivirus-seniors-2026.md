## What Is Microsoft Defender?

Microsoft Defender (sometimes called [Windows Defender](https://snipstack.io/windows-defender-download-2026/)) is a built-in security program that Microsoft includes with Windows. You do not have to install it. You do not have to pay for it. It turns on automatically.

It does a few important things:
- Scans files you download for known viruses
- Checks programs before they run
- Warns you if something suspicious is happening

For basic protection, it works. Independent security labs rate it as "good" — not the best, but not bad either.

**The problem is that scams in 2026 have gotten much smarter.** Criminals are now using tricks that go beyond simple viruses — and that is where Defender starts to show its limits.

## [How to Check](https://snipstack.io/how-to-check-if-your-pc-is-hacked-2026/) If Defender Is Running on Your Computer

Before adding anything new, let's make sure your current protection is switched on.

**Step 1.** Click the **Start** button (the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner of your screen).

**Step 2.** Type **[Windows Security](https://snipstack.io/windows-security-2026/)** and press Enter.

**Step 3.** You will see a screen with green checkmarks or red warnings.
- Green checkmarks = good, protection is on
- Red X or yellow warning = something needs attention

**Step 4.** Click **Virus and threat protection**.

**Step 5.** Make sure it says **"Real-time protection: On"**.

If everything is green, Defender is working. But remember — working does not mean complete.
