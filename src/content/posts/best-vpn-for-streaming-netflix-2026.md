## Why Most VPNs Fail at Streaming in 2026

Before the rankings, the context matters: streaming VPN blocking has become sophisticated enough that generic VPN recommendations are often wrong.

**How streaming services detect VPNs:**

1. **IP range blacklisting.** Streaming platforms maintain lists of known VPN provider IP ranges and block them. Major providers update these lists frequently — some daily.

2. **DNS leak detection.** If your DNS queries go to your ISP while your traffic goes through the VPN, streaming services can detect the geographic mismatch.

3. **IPv6 leak detection.** Some VPNs route IPv4 through the tunnel but allow IPv6 to leak — revealing your real location.

4. **Datacenter IP identification.** Most VPN servers run in commercial datacenters. Streaming services have gotten very good at identifying datacenter IP ranges versus residential ISP ranges.

5. **Traffic pattern analysis.** Unusual traffic patterns consistent with VPN routing can trigger secondary verification.

VPNs that actively cycle their IP pools, maintain dedicated streaming servers, and monitor for blocks fare significantly better than those running static server pools. This is why a VPN with 3,000 servers can perform worse at streaming than one with 500 servers that are specifically maintained for streaming access.

## Does a VPN Slow Down Streaming?

Yes — all VPNs add some latency because your traffic routes through an additional server. The practical impact on streaming:

- **4K streaming:** Requires ~25 Mbps. A fast VPN server (Surfshark, NordVPN) adds 10-25% overhead. On a 100 Mbps connection, 4K streams without issue.
- **1080p streaming:** Requires ~5-8 Mbps. No practical impact from a good VPN on typical broadband connections.
- **SD streaming / mobile data:** VPN overhead becomes more noticeable on slower connections.

**Rule of thumb:** If your connection is above 50 Mbps, you will not notice VPN overhead for streaming. Below 20 Mbps, choose VPN servers geographically close to you to minimize latency.

Surfshark's WireGuard protocol (enabled by default) is the fastest protocol available and minimizes overhead compared to older OpenVPN or IKEv2 options.

## FAQ

### Does Netflix ban you for using a VPN?

Netflix does not ban accounts for VPN use — they block the VPN IP addresses rather than the account. If a VPN server is detected and blocked, you see an error message but your account is not affected. Switching to a different VPN server in the same country resolves it. Netflix's enforcement is against VPN IP addresses, not against users.
