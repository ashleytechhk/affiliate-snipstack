## What Is an [Ad Blocker for](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-online-learning-platforms-2026/) Arc Browser?

An **[ad blocker for](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-slow-internet-connections-2026/) Arc browser** is software that prevents advertising content — banners, video pre-rolls, tracking pixels, cookie-consent walls — from loading when you browse the web. On desktop, this usually means a browser extension like [uBlock Origin](https://snipstack.io/ublock-origin-vs-adguard-which-is-better-2026/) or AdGuard. On mobile, it is more complicated.

Arc Search is The Browser Company's mobile browser for iPhone. Unlike desktop Arc (which is Chromium-based and supports Chrome extensions), **Arc Search does not support third-party browser extensions**. There is no plugin store, no manifest file to install, and no way to run uBlock Origin inside it. Instead, The Browser Company built the blocking capability directly into the app itself.

### What Arc Search's Built-In Blocker Covers

According to the App Store listing, Arc Search includes:

- **Ad blocking** — removes banner and display ads from web pages you visit inside Arc
- **Tracker blocking** — stops tracking scripts from firing while you browse
- **Cookie-banner blocking** — suppresses the GDPR consent popups that cover pages on most news sites
- **Reader mode** — strips page clutter entirely for distraction-free reading

This covers the common annoyances well. For pure in-browser web browsing, Arc Search's defaults are better out of the box than Safari without a content blocker installed.

## Why the Built-In Blocker Is Not the Whole Story

Here is the part most guides skip: **Arc Search's ad blocker only works inside Arc Search.**

When you tap into the Instagram app, open a YouTube video, check your news aggregator, or play a mobile game, none of that traffic passes through Arc. Each of those apps runs on its own network stack, separate from whatever browser you use. Arc's blocker has no visibility into them.

This is not unique to Arc — it applies to every mobile browser's built-in blocker. Safari content blockers, Brave Shields, Firefox Focus — they all face the same structural limit. The browser controls the browser. The apps are their own world.

![In-browser blocking versus system-wide network-level blocking on mobile](/og/best-ad-blocker-for-arc-browser-2026-inline-1.png)

Think of it this way: Arc Search cleans up the street outside your front door. The advertising inside every other room of your house is untouched.

### The Surface Area You're Missing

On a typical smartphone in 2026, here is what a browser-only blocker cannot reach:

- **Social media apps** — in-feed sponsored posts and video ads inside Instagram, TikTok, X
- **Video streaming apps** — pre-roll and mid-roll ads in the YouTube app, Twitch, Peacock
- **News and content apps** — interstitial ads between articles
- **Mobile games** — reward ads and banner overlays
- **Email apps** — tracking pixels that tell senders when you open their messages

That is the majority of the ads you encounter in a day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Arc Search have a built-in ad blocker?**

Yes. Arc Search includes a built-in ad blocker, tracker blocker, and cookie-banner blocker as part of the default browser experience. You do not need to install a separate extension. The blocker works automatically when you browse inside Arc Search.

Arc Search's blocker only covers traffic that flows through Arc Search itself.
