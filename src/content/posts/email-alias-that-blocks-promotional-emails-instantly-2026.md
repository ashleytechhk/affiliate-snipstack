## What "Blocks Instantly" Actually Means

Not all [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-grubhub-and-food-delivery-signups-2026/) services work the same way. There are three fundamentally different mechanisms, and only one of them is truly instant:

**1. Filter-based blocking** — The alias stays live; incoming emails get sorted into folders or marked as spam. This is what Gmail's built-in plus-addressing does. Spam still arrives, it just lands somewhere else. Not instant.

**2. Forwarding with a kill switch** — The alias forwards everything to your real inbox. When you deactivate the alias, new emails bounce or disappear. This is instant — once the alias is off, nothing gets through.

**3. Identity-layer aliases** — The alias is tied to a fake identity (alternate name, alternate profile). No connection to your real identity exists at the source, so even if your alias leaks into data broker lists, your real inbox stays untouched. This is the most robust approach.

The best tools in 2026 sit in category 2 or 3. The comparison table below makes the difference clear.

## SimpleLogin — The Open-Source Alternative

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias tool acquired by Proton in 2022. It offers up to 10 aliases on the free plan and unlimited aliases on the paid plan ($4/month or included with Proton Unlimited).

The key advantage over Alt ID is custom domain support. You can point your own domain at SimpleLogin and create aliases like hello@yourprivacydomain.com. For privacy-conscious developers or small businesses, this is a meaningful feature.

The limitation is that SimpleLogin is an alias-only tool — no identity layer, no bundled VPN, no fake name generator. If you just need to mask email addresses and prefer open-source software you can self-host, SimpleLogin is excellent. If you want a broader privacy stack, you will need to assemble the pieces separately.

## Apple Hide My Email — If You Live in the Apple Ecosystem

Apple's Hide My Email feature, part of iCloud+, generates random [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-receiving-payslips-and-hr-notices-2026/)es directly in Safari and Mail. For iPhone and Mac users who already pay for iCloud+ storage, this is the path of least resistance.

The aliases integrate natively into iOS and macOS — when you fill in an email field in Safari, Apple can offer to generate a masked address on the spot. Deactivating an alias stops forwarding immediately.

The downside is the walled garden: this only works smoothly on Apple devices, and requires an active iCloud+ subscription. No Android support, no Windows app, no custom domains.

## 33mail — The Long-Running Budget Option

33mail has been around since 2010 and offers unlimited alias addresses on its free plan. You create aliases in the format anything@username.33mail.com, and forwarding starts immediately with no account setup required.

The free plan is limited to 10MB of forwarded email per month, and you cannot reply from an alias on the free tier. The interface is dated. For users who just need a quick alias and do not mind the monthly cap, it works. For heavy inbox management, the paid plans ($1–2/month) are needed.

## FAQ

### What is an email alias that blocks promotional emails instantly?

An email alias is a disposable address that forwards to your real inbox. "Blocks instantly" means that when you deactivate the alias — whether because it is receiving spam or you simply no longer want it — new emails stop arriving immediately. You do not need to unsubscribe from each sender individually. Tools like Alt ID let you kill an alias in one tap, and the blocking takes effect right away.

### Does deactivating an alias affect emails I already received?

No. Emails already in your inbox stay there. Deactivating an alias only stops future emails from being forwarded. Anything already delivered is unaffected.

### Can I use these aliases for two-factor authentication codes?

It depends on the tool. Surfshark Alt ID and SimpleLogin both forward all incoming email, including authentication codes, to your real inbox. This means yes — you can use an alias for 2FA. Just be aware that if you deactivate the alias later, you will need to update the 2FA email on that account first.

### Will senders know I am using an alias?

Typically no. The alias address looks like a normal email address to the sender. Some services may flag certain alias domains if they recognise them, but this is uncommon for everyday newsletter signups, loyalty programmes, and trial registrations. [Alt ID aliases are designed to look like standard addresses.](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-that-blocks-promotional-emails-instantly-2026__faq)

### Is there a free email alias tool that blocks instantly?

SimpleLogin offers 10 free aliases with instant deactivation. Apple Hide My Email is free with iCloud+. 33mail has a free tier with monthly limits. Surfshark Alt ID does not have a standalone free plan but is included with paid Surfshark subscriptions. If cost is the primary concern, SimpleLogin's free tier is the strongest no-cost option.

### What happens if I reply from an alias address?

SimpleLogin and some other tools support replying from an alias, so the recipient sees the alias address rather than your real one. Surfshark Alt ID focuses on inbound alias forwarding — for outbound replies, you would typically use your real inbox. Check the specific tool's documentation for reply support.

### Can I create multiple aliases for different sites?

Yes. All tools covered here support multiple aliases. Surfshark Alt ID and SimpleLogin (paid) offer unlimited aliases. This is the recommended approach: one alias per site or service, so you can pinpoint exactly which one leaked your address and kill it without affecting others.
