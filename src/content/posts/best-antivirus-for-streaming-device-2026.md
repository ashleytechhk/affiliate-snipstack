## How Streaming Devices Get Compromised — And What Actually Protects You

A lot of guides make this more complicated than it needs to be. Here is the simple version.

Your Fire Stick, Roku, Apple TV, or smart TV connects to your home Wi-Fi. You control it using a phone app or a remote. You log into streaming accounts — Netflix, Disney+, Prime Video — and those logins are stored on both the device and your phone.

Criminals do not usually try to hack the device directly. They go after the easier targets:

- **Your phone.** A fake link arrives by text or email — "Your streaming account is suspended, click here." You tap it and land on a site that steals your password.
- **Your home network.** If your Wi-Fi has a weak password, someone nearby can get onto your network and see what devices are connected.
- **Fake apps.** On Android-based streaming devices, it is possible to install apps from unofficial sources. Some of these look like real streaming services but are actually designed to steal your payment details.

**Antivirus software on your phone and computer** is what stops most of these attacks. It checks every link before your browser loads it. It scans every app you download. It alerts you if your email address shows up in a known data breach. And it does all of this automatically, without you having to remember anything.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I install antivirus directly on my Fire Stick or Roku?

No — and you do not need to. Fire Stick and Roku are simple, locked-down devices that do not allow traditional security software to be installed. The real targets for criminals are the phone or tablet you use to control your streaming accounts, and the home Wi-Fi network those devices share. Protecting your phone with a reputable antivirus tool like [Surfshark Antivirus](/blog/antivirus?sub=best-antivirus-for-streaming-device-2026__faq) is the right approach.

### What is the biggest threat to streaming device users?

For most people over 60, the biggest threat is a phishing message — a text or email that looks like it comes from Netflix, Amazon, or your internet provider, asking you to click a link and log in. The link goes to a fake site designed to steal your password. Once a criminal has your streaming account login, they can change billing details, access your payment method, and use the account to sign up for other services. Web protection in a good antivirus catches these fake sites before you can land on them.

### My Roku has a warning on screen saying it has a virus. Should I call the number shown?

No. Do not call any number shown in a pop-up warning on your TV or streaming device. These are scam messages, not real warnings from your device or from any antivirus company. They are designed to frighten you into calling a number where scammers will ask for remote access to your devices or your payment details. Turn off your TV and streaming device, wait a minute, then turn them back on. The message will likely be gone. If you are worried, run a scan on your phone using a legitimate antivirus app.

### Does antivirus slow down my phone or tablet?

A well-designed modern antivirus has no noticeable effect on your phone's speed during everyday use. [Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-bitdefender-speed-test-2026/) runs quietly in the background without draining your battery or making apps open slower. During a full manual scan you may see slightly slower performance for a few minutes — that is temporary and normal.

### Do I need separate protection for my Wi-Fi router?

Your home Wi-Fi router is a separate piece of equipment that antivirus software does not directly protect.
