## What Is a VPN and Why Does KBS Block You?

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server in a country of your choice. Once connected, websites and streaming platforms see the IP address of that server — not your real location. This is how South Korean expats in the US, UK, Australia, Canada, or anywhere else can appear to be browsing from Seoul.

KBS (Korean Broadcasting System) and its international variants restrict certain content based on geography for two reasons: licensing agreements and regulatory requirements. Some dramas, sports broadcasts, and live news segments are only licensed for distribution within South Korea. When you connect from abroad, the platform's system detects a non-Korean IP address and blocks playback.

This affects more than just KBS. MBC, SBS, tvN, and Wavve all use the same geo-restriction technology. A VPN with a reliable South Korean server solves the problem across all of them with one subscription.
