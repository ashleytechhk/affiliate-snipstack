## What Is a VPN, Really?

A VPN is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server somewhere else in the world. When your traffic exits through that server, websites and streaming platforms see the server's IP address and location — not yours.

Think of it this way: without a VPN, you are sending a letter with your home address on the envelope. With a VPN, the letter is rerouted through a mailroom in Stockholm. The recipient sees Stockholm's address, not yours.

This has two effects that matter for streaming:

1. **Geo-restriction bypass.** Platforms that restrict content by location see a local IP address and serve up content they would otherwise block.
2. **Traffic encryption.** Your internet service provider cannot see what you are watching. This is a privacy benefit, though not the main reason most people use a [VPN for streaming](https://snipstack.io/vpn-for-streaming-on-firestick-2026/).

What a VPN does *not* do: it does not make you invisible, it does not guarantee any particular streaming quality, and it does not work if a streaming service actively detects and blocks VPN IP addresses.

## Why Scandinavian TV Is Geo-Locked in the First Place

Public broadcasters like SVT (Sweden), NRK (Norway), DR (Denmark), and Yle (Finland) are funded partly or entirely by public licensing fees or government budgets. Their mandate is to serve residents of their respective countries. Streaming rights for the shows they broadcast are also licensed on a territory-by-territory basis, meaning a production company may only permit SVT to stream a show within Sweden.

The result: these platforms use IP address detection to enforce borders. Access from a French, Australian, or American IP address gets blocked.

Private Nordic streaming services — Viaplay, C More, and TV2 Play among them — have a different problem. They sell subscriptions and hold regional rights. Streaming outside the licensed territory can technically violate their terms of service, even if you are a paying subscriber who has simply traveled.
