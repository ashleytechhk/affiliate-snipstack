## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### My forwarded emails are going to spam in my real inbox

Add your Alt ID forwarding domain to your email client's safe senders list. In Gmail, open a forwarded message, click the three-dot menu, and select "Filter messages like this" — then choose "Never send to Spam."

### I am not receiving forwarded emails at all

Check that forwarding is enabled in the Alt ID dashboard. Also verify that your real inbox is not full — a full mailbox silently rejects incoming messages.

### A site is rejecting my Alt ID address

Some aggressive spam filters block known forwarding domains. If this happens, try creating a second Alt ID — the domain pool is large and most sites accept them without issue. For services that explicitly block forwarding (rare), you may need to use a real address; consider creating a dedicated secondary Gmail/ProtonMail account for that category.

### I accidentally gave my real email somewhere — now what?

Create a new Alt ID, update that service's account email to the new alias, and treat your real address as compromised for that use case. You do not need to change your actual email address.
