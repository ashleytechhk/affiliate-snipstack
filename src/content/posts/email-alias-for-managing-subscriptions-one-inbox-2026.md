### SimpleLogin

SimpleLogin (now owned by Proton) is the power-user's choice. It supports custom domains, PGP encryption of forwarded emails, and a clean browser extension for generating aliases on the fly. The free tier gives you ten aliases — enough to test the concept. The paid plan unlocks unlimited aliases and custom domains, useful if you want aliases like `newsletters@yourowndomain.com`.

**Limitation:** no identity generator, and the UI has more friction than Alt ID. It is a better fit for technically comfortable users who want granular control.

### DuckDuckGo Email Protection

Free and dead simple. Every DDG alias forwards through DuckDuckGo's servers, which also strip tracking pixels before delivery. That is a genuine extra layer of protection most alias tools skip. The downside: your alias is always `@duck.com` — no custom domains, no identity layer.

**Best for:** casual users who want tracker removal for free without a subscription.

---

### Apple Hide My Email

If you are already paying for iCloud+ (starting at under a dollar a month), Hide My Email is built right in. Works seamlessly across Safari, Mail, and the App Store. Generate an alias, use it to sign up, done. Like DDG, there is no identity generator and no custom domain option.

**Limitation:** locked to the Apple ecosystem. Android or Windows users need not apply.

**Best for:** iPhone users who want zero-effort alias creation without a new tool.

---

### Firefox Relay / Addy.io

Firefox Relay has a compelling free tier (five aliases) and a cheap paid plan. Addy.io (formerly AnonAddy) goes even further with unlimited aliases on the free plan, custom domains on paid tiers, and an API for power users who want to automate alias creation.

Neither tool includes an identity generator or a VPN bundle, so they work best as standalone alias tools within a broader privacy setup you already have.

## Key Features to Look For in an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-grubhub-and-food-delivery-signups-2026/) Tool

Not every alias tool is equal. When comparing options for managing subscriptions in one inbox, weigh these factors:

**Platform coverage:** browser extension for desktop, mobile app for on-the-go signups. Both matter.

**Bundled features:** a standalone alias tool is fine. A tool that also covers VPN, breach alerts, and antivirus in one subscription is better value for most people.

## FAQ

### What is an [email alias for](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-privacy-without-creating-new-account-2026/) managing subscriptions?

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-real-estate-agent-property-alerts-2026/) is a forwarding address that sits in front of your real inbox. You give the alias to subscription services instead of your actual address. Mail from those services still arrives in your inbox, but the sender only ever knows the alias — not your real email. If a service starts spamming you, you delete the alias and the mail stops.

### [Does using](https://snipstack.io/does-using-email-alias-protect-from-spam-2026/) an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-receiving-payslips-and-hr-notices-2026/) actually stop spam?

Yes, reliably. Unlike unsubscribe links (which sometimes confirm your address is active and increase spam), deleting an alias makes you genuinely unreachable. The sender has no valid address to contact. [Surfshark Alt ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-managing-subscriptions-one-inbox-2026__faq) makes this process one click.

### Can I still reply from my alias?

It depends on the tool. SimpleLogin and Addy.io support two-way reply. Surfshark Alt ID is primarily a receive-and-forward setup — ideal for newsletters and subscription signups where you never need to reply. For services you actively correspond with, keep a real address.

### Will my real email address ever be exposed?

As long as you always use the alias when signing up, no. The alias is what lives in the service's database. Your real address stays on your device and with your email provider. This is why pairing an alias tool with a strong, unique real address (not a generic Gmail) matters.

### Is Surfshark Alt ID worth it if I already have a free alias tool?

If your only need is a forwarding alias, free tools like DuckDuckGo Email Protection or Addy.io work fine. The case for Alt ID is the bundled value: you also get a full VPN, breach monitoring, and the identity generator for forms that want more than an email. If you were going to pay for a VPN anyway, Alt ID comes along for the ride at marginal extra cost.

### How many aliases should I use?

One per service is the cleanest approach — it tells you exactly which company leaked or sold your address. You can be more casual (one alias per category: shopping, newsletters, trials) if you want less overhead. Unlimited-alias tools like Alt ID and Addy.io make the one-per-service approach practical.
