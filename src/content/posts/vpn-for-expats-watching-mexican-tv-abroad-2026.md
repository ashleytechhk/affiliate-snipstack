## What Is a VPN and Why Do Expats Need One?

A VPN is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server in another country. When you connect through a Mexican VPN server, websites and streaming platforms see that server's IP address — not your real one in Germany, Australia, or wherever you are living.

For Mexican expats, this solves two separate problems:

1. **Geo-restrictions** — Streaming platforms license content by territory. If you are in the Netherlands and try to load Blim TV or ViX, the platform detects a Dutch IP address and blocks you. A Mexican VPN server makes you appear local.

2. **Subscription price differences** — Many streaming services charge dramatically different prices depending on which country your account is registered in. A Mexican Netflix, Spotify, or ViX subscription can cost a fraction of the equivalent plan elsewhere.

Both problems are solved by a single VPN subscription that costs a few dollars per month.

## How Geo-Restrictions Work (And Why They Are So Frustrating)

When a broadcaster like Televisa or TV Azteca negotiates rights to show a football match, a telenovela, or a news program, those rights are tied to specific territories. International rights are sold separately — often to different companies at different prices. This means the streaming platform is contractually required to block viewers outside Mexico.

Your device broadcasts your IP address with every web request. IP addresses are registered to geographic locations, so a streaming server can look up your IP and immediately know you are outside the licensed territory. The block happens automatically, before you even see the login screen.

A VPN swaps your visible IP for one belonging to a Mexican server. As far as the streaming platform can tell, you are connecting from Mexico City or Guadalajara.
