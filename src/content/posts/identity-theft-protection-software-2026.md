## What Identity Theft Protection Software Actually Does

Before comparing products, it helps to know what these tools do — and what they do not.

**What they do:**
- Scan the internet's hidden corners (the "dark web") for your personal information — email, passwords, Social Security number
- Warn you when that information appears in a data breach
- Block dangerous links before you click them
- Scan files and attachments for malware (malicious software) before it can cause harm
- Hide your internet activity from scammers and tracking companies

**What they do not do:**
- Prevent every possible scam or theft — no software can do that
- Replace good habits (being cautious on the phone, not sharing personal details with strangers)
- Retrieve stolen money on their own — only services with insurance coverage (like LifeLock) can assist with financial recovery

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most important thing identity theft protection software does for seniors?

The single most valuable feature is breach alerts — notifications when your email address or passwords appear in a data breach. This gives you time to change passwords before a thief can use the stolen information. Antivirus protection (blocking malicious links and files) is equally important, since many scams targeting seniors arrive by email or text. It depends on your situation. Monitoring tells you when something is wrong so you can act quickly. Insurance covers the financial and time costs of fixing it if theft does occur — restoration support, legal fees, and in some cases reimbursement for stolen funds. If you have significant savings or investments, insurance adds meaningful peace of mind. If your priority is prevention at a reasonable price, strong monitoring and antivirus protection handle most everyday risks.
