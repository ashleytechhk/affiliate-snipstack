## 10 Warning Signs Your Phone Has a Virus

### 1. Battery Draining Much Faster Than Usual

Your phone used to last all day. Now it dies by lunch. Malicious apps run in the background without your knowledge, consuming power constantly.

**What to do:** Check which apps are using the most battery (Settings → Battery on both iPhone and Android). If you see an unfamiliar app near the top, delete it. Then run a security scan.

---

### 2. Phone Feels Hot Even When Idle

Phones get warm during calls or video streaming. They should not feel hot when sitting on the table doing nothing. Background malware processing causes excess heat.

**What to do:** Close all apps and leave the phone alone for ten minutes. If it stays hot, something is running without your permission. Restart the phone, then scan for threats.

### 3. Apps You Never Downloaded

Open your app list and scroll slowly. Do you see apps you do not remember installing? Malware sometimes installs additional software without asking.

**What to do:** Delete any app you do not recognize. On Android, go to Settings → Apps to see everything installed. On iPhone, swipe across your home screen pages and look carefully. When in doubt, delete it.

---

### 4. Pop-Up Ads Appearing Constantly

Ads that appear even when you are not using a browser — covering your screen, playing sounds, or asking you to tap something — are a strong sign of adware, a type of malicious software that earns money by forcing ads on you.

**What to do:** Do not tap anything in the pop-up, including the "X" to close it. Press your phone's home button instead. Then check recently installed apps and remove anything suspicious.

---

### 5. Sudden Spike in Mobile Data Use

Go to your phone's data usage screen (Settings → Mobile Data on iPhone; Settings → Network on Android). If an app is using far more data than makes sense for what it does, it may be sending your personal information to someone else.

**What to do:** Note which app is the heavy data user. If you do not recognize it or use it regularly, delete it. Contact your phone carrier if you notice unusual charges on your bill.

---

### 6. Phone Running Slowly

A phone that suddenly becomes sluggish — apps take longer to open, typing lags behind your fingers, the screen freezes — may have malware consuming its processing power in the background.

**What to do:** Restart your phone. Clear the browser cache (Settings → Safari → Clear History on iPhone; Settings → Apps → Browser → Clear Cache on Android). If slowness continues, run an antivirus scan.

### 7. Strange Text Messages Sent From Your Number

A friend calls and says you sent them a weird link. You check your messages and find texts you never wrote. Some malware sends spam or phishing messages from your phone to your entire contact list — without you knowing.

**What to do:** Tell your contacts to ignore and delete the message without clicking any links. Change your phone account password immediately. Contact your phone carrier and let them know. Run a full security scan.

---

### 8. Browser Redirects to Unknown Websites

You type a website address and end up somewhere completely different. Or your search results look strange and unfamiliar. This is browser hijacking — malicious software that controls where your browser takes you, often leading to scam pages.

**What to do:** Do not enter any personal information or passwords on pages you were redirected to. Clear your browser history and cache. On Android, check your browser settings for any extensions you did not install and remove them.

---

### 9. Accounts Getting Locked or Sending Strange Messages

Your email password suddenly stops working. Friends say your social media account sent them odd messages or friend requests. Malware can steal your login credentials and use your accounts to spread further.

**What to do:** Use a different device (such as a computer) to change your passwords immediately. Enable two-step verification on all important accounts — email, banking, social media. Check with your bank if you notice anything suspicious.

---

### 10. Contacts Complaining About Spam From You

Multiple people in your address book tell you they received strange emails or texts from you — links to prize claims, urgent requests for money, or suspicious attachments. You did not send any of them.

**What to do:** This is a serious sign. Change every password. Warn your contacts not to click anything. Consider a factory reset if the problem does not stop after scanning and password changes.
