## Method 1: Add an Alias Directly in Outlook (Microsoft's Built-In Method)

This is the quickest route if you want a second address that still delivers to your main Outlook mailbox.

### Step 1 — Open Your Microsoft Account Settings

Go to [account.microsoft.com](https://account.microsoft.com) and sign in. Click **Your info** in the top navigation, then select **Manage how you sign in to Microsoft**.

*[Screenshot placeholder: account.microsoft.com → Your info → Manage sign-in]*

### Step 2 — Add an Alias

On the alias management page, click **Add email**. You will see two options:

- **Create a new email address and add it as an alias** — Microsoft creates a new Outlook.com address for you
- **Add an existing email address as a Microsoft account alias** — use a Gmail or other address you already own

Select the first option to create a fresh alias. Type your preferred username and choose a domain (outlook.com, hotmail.com, or live.com from the dropdown).

### Step 3 — Verify and Set as Primary (Optional)

Microsoft will send a verification code to your current primary address. Enter it to confirm. If you want this alias to be your new primary sign-in address, click **Make primary** — but for spam management purposes, leave your original address as primary and keep the alias as a secondary.

### Step 4 — Configure Mail Delivery

By default, all email sent to your new alias lands in the same inbox as your primary address. To separate alias mail into its own folder, go to Outlook settings (the gear icon in the top right of outlook.com), select **Mail → Rules**, and create a rule:

- **Condition:** Recipient address contains [your new alias]
- **Action:** Move to folder → [create a new folder, e.g., "Alias Mail"]

### Step 5 — Use the Alias When Signing Up for Services

Copy your new alias address and use it whenever a website asks for an email. Any spam that comes through this alias stays isolated. When it gets unbearable, you can delete the alias and create a new one — your primary address is never exposed.

**Limitation:** Microsoft caps personal accounts at 10 aliases total. If you sign up for a lot of services, you will hit this ceiling fast.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Alias mail is not arriving in Outlook**
Check your Junk/Spam folder first. Some ISPs flag forwarded mail. If it is consistently landing in spam, add the forwarding domain (e.g., surfsharkmail.net) to your Outlook safe senders list: **Settings → Mail → Junk email → Safe senders**.

**"This alias is already taken" error on Outlook**
Common usernames are exhausted. Try adding a number or a short word. Alternatively, switch to a less common domain (live.com instead of outlook.com).

**Alt-ID forwarding is delayed**
Forwarding usually arrives within seconds. Delays over a few minutes are rare but can happen during high-traffic periods. If delays persist, check the Surfshark status page or try logging out and back into the Alt-ID dashboard.

**I cannot find the alias management page on my Microsoft account**
If you are on a work or school Microsoft 365 account, alias management is controlled by your organisation's admin. The personal alias feature at account.microsoft.com only works for personal Microsoft accounts.

**My Outlook rules are not sorting alias mail correctly**
Rules execute in order. Make sure your alias-sorting rule is not being overridden by a higher-priority rule. Drag it to the top of the rules list in Outlook settings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I send email from my Outlook alias?**
Yes. In Outlook on the web, go to **Settings → Mail → Sync email → Manage aliases**, then select the alias as a sender. When composing a new message, click **From** and choose your alias address. The recipient sees the alias, not your primary address. [Set up Alt-ID for unlimited alias options](https://get.surfshark.net/aff_c?offer_id=6&aff_id=45462&sub_id=email-alias-outlook-2026__faq).

**Does deleting an alias delete my emails?**
For Outlook built-in aliases: deleting the alias does not delete any emails already received. For Alt-ID: disabling or deleting an alias stops forwarding but does not affect emails already delivered to your Outlook inbox.

**Is an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/how-email-alias-2026/) the same as a forwarding address?**
Not exactly. A built-in Outlook alias is a second name on the same mailbox — no forwarding involved. Alt-ID is a separate address that forwards to your real inbox. Both achieve the goal of hiding your primary address, but they work differently under the hood.

**How many aliases can I have with Surfshark Alt-ID?**
Alt-ID does not publish a hard cap. In practice, you can generate as many as you need. This makes it far more practical than Outlook's 10-alias limit for people who sign up to many services.

**Will spam senders know my real address if I use Alt-ID?**
No. Alt-ID is designed so that your real address is never shared with the service you are signing up for. Only Surfshark's forwarding infrastructure knows the link between your alias and your real inbox. Spammers who buy or scrape email lists only ever see the alias address.
