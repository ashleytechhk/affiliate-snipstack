## How Your Number Gets Out

### The Masked Messaging Failure

Instacart routes shopper-customer communication through an in-app messaging layer designed to keep both parties' real numbers hidden. During an active order, customers see an anonymized chat thread; shoppers see the same. In theory, neither party's real number is exposed.

In practice, masking fails under specific conditions:

- **Fallback calling:** If the in-app call drops or fails to connect, some versions of the Instacart app fall back to a standard cellular call. When this happens, the shopper's real carrier number can appear in the customer's call log.
- **Voicemail headers:** When an in-app call goes to voicemail and the voicemail system logs the call, the callback number recorded is sometimes the shopper's actual number rather than the masked proxy.
- **Third-party apps:** Customers using SMS archiving apps or screen-record tools can sometimes capture number metadata that the standard UI conceals.
- **Tech outages:** During platform-wide connectivity issues, masked routing has historically degraded, reverting to direct connection.

None of these require any technical sophistication on the customer's end. A fallback call is something that just happens when the app has a bad day. The customer's phone logs it automatically.

### One Number Is Enough

Once a customer has a real mobile number, the gap to a full identity takes minutes to close. Reverse-lookup services — freely available, no account required — return name, general address, linked social profiles, and email within seconds of a query. A number associated with your real name connects directly to your Facebook, Instagram, or LinkedIn if any of those accounts use it for recovery or login.

The shopper in Atlanta didn't give that customer permission to know her schedule or her car. She didn't need to. One callback number from a connectivity glitch was enough.

## Why Instacart Shoppers Are Higher Risk Than Other Gig Workers

Not all gig work creates equal exposure. A remote copywriter on Fiverr never meets clients. A TaskRabbit Tasker meets clients at their homes but usually in daylight, in residential settings, with gig duration measured in hours.

Instacart shoppers operate differently:

**You enter grocery stores, parking lots, and apartment buildings alone.** The job takes you to locations — including client residences — during hours that can extend to late evening. A customer who knows your general operating zone can predict where you'll be.

**Your photo is visible to customers before and during delivery.** Instacart shows customers a shopper photo to help them identify who is handling their order. That photo, combined with a first name and an approximate delivery zone, is enough for a determined person to find a social media profile.

**You cannot screen customers.** Drivers can decline rides. Shoppers cannot decline orders from specific customers — not without contacting support after an order is already assigned. By the time you know who you're delivering to, your name and photo are already visible to them.

**The volume is high.** An active Instacart shopper can complete dozens of deliveries per week. Each delivery is a new customer with a new opportunity to capture your contact information if the masking slips.
