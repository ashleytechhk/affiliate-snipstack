## The Password Problem You Probably Have Right Now

### One Password Everywhere

Research consistently finds that the majority of internet users reuse the same password — or a small variation of it — across multiple accounts. It is the only practical approach when you have accounts on thirty or forty different websites and you are expected to remember distinct login details for all of them.

The problem is that when any one of those websites is breached — and breaches happen constantly, to companies of every size — criminals take your email and password and try them on other sites automatically. This is called "credential stuffing." If your banking password is the same as your email password, and your email was leaked in a data breach from a shopping site three years ago, criminals may already have access.

A password manager fixes this at the root. It:

1. Generates a completely different, random password for every website you use
2. Stores those passwords securely, encrypted, so only you can access them
3. Fills in your login automatically when you visit a site — you never have to type or remember individual passwords

You only need to remember one password: the master password that unlocks the manager itself. Everything else is handled for you.
