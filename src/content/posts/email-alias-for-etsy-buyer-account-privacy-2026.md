## Why Etsy Buyer Accounts Attract Spam

Most buyers focus on the purchase experience and don't think about email exposure. But Etsy's platform creates several specific paths from your inbox to unwanted messages.

**Etsy's marketing list.** When you create an account or complete a purchase, Etsy adds your address to its marketing communications list. Etsy sends promotional emails featuring items from sellers you've browsed or purchased from, curated recommendations, and platform-wide sales events. This is opt-out rather than opt-in by default, and the volume is substantial if you're an active buyer.

**Seller messaging.** Etsy allows sellers to send messages to buyers through the platform. While the system is designed for order-related communication, sellers can also send follow-up messages after delivery asking for reviews, offering discounts on future orders, or promoting new products. Some sellers treat this channel as a recurring marketing touchpoint. Your email address itself may not be visible to sellers, but your inbox certainly feels the effects.

**Review request emails.** After each purchase, Etsy sends automated review request emails on behalf of sellers. These are transactional, but they add to the volume — and they continue arriving even for orders you've reviewed or have no intention of reviewing.

**Third-party advertising partners.** Etsy operates a large advertising ecosystem. Their privacy policy acknowledges sharing data with advertising partners for targeting purposes. The practical result is that your email address — or a hash of it — may appear in audience segments used by third-party ad platforms, which can lead to email-based targeting from services you've never directly engaged with.

**Breach risk.** Etsy has had security incidents in its history. Any platform that holds your real email address is a potential breach source. If your actual address appears in a breach, it circulates through spam lists for years. A dedicated alias limits the damage: if the alias appears in a breach, you delete it and create a replacement.

## Why Gmail `+tag` Addressing Falls Short for Etsy Buyers

Gmail plus-addressing — signing up as `yourname+etsy@gmail.com` — is a widely used but ineffective privacy approach for Etsy accounts. The problem is structural.

The base address `yourname@gmail.com` remains fully visible in the email header. When Etsy's marketing systems or third-party ad partners process your address, they strip the `+etsy` tag before adding the address to their lists. This is a standard operation in bulk email processing. The protection you think you have doesn't exist at the point where it matters most.

Additionally, you cannot disable the variant. If `yourname+etsy@gmail.com` starts receiving off-topic spam, your options are an inbox filter rule (which you'll need to maintain indefinitely) or changing your entire Gmail address. There's no revocation for a single `+tag` variant without affecting your primary account.

A dedicated alias that generates an unrelated-looking address — and that can be deleted independently — provides both protection and control that plus-addressing can never offer.
