## Why Google Voice Is Getting Blocked in 2026

The core problem is number reputation. Google Voice numbers are VoIP — they're not issued by a mobile carrier. Dating apps, verification platforms, and fraud-prevention systems have spent years building databases of known VoIP number ranges.

When you enter a Google Voice number at signup, the app checks it against these databases. If the number range is flagged as VoIP (which Google Voice numbers almost always are), the verification SMS either doesn't send, or the app returns an error telling you the number isn't eligible.

This wasn't a major issue three or four years ago. It's a significant one now. Tinder, Hinge, Bumble, and OkCupid have all tightened their verification requirements. Google Voice, TextNow, and similar VoIP services are on the blocked list for a growing share of platforms.

The solution isn't to find a "trick" to get Google Voice working — it's to use a number that isn't in the VoIP database to begin with.
