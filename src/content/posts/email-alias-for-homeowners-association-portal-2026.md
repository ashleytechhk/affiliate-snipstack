### Gmail's `+tag` Trick

Gmail lets you register as `yourname+hoa@gmail.com`, with mail landing in your normal inbox where you could theoretically filter and mass-delete it later.

The catch is that HOA management platforms — like many form-based portals — frequently strip everything after the `+` before saving your address. The tag vanishes, your base Gmail address gets stored, and every vendor that platform shares data with now has your permanent email with no way to revoke it. There's no kill switch, and no way to tell which vendor sold your address once the flood starts.

It also does nothing about secondary use. Even when the tag survives, your real address is already inside the management company's system and every partner they share data with. Deleting a filter afterward tidies your inbox view; it doesn't undo the exposure.

---

### Apple Hide My Email

Included with iCloud+ starting at $0.99/month, Apple's alias service forwards to your iCloud address and can be deactivated from Settings or iCloud.com in a few taps, with tight Safari autofill integration.

The catch is platform lock-in. If your main inbox lives in Gmail or Outlook, you're forwarding iCloud mail into a second address or checking two inboxes — friction that adds up over a multi-year HOA relationship. It also doesn't fabricate alternative personal data, so anything beyond your legally required name still enters the portal as real.

For homeowners already paying for iCloud+, it's a solid option. For anyone outside the Apple ecosystem, the setup cost outweighs the benefit.

### Free Throwaway Services (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail)

Throwaway inboxes are free, require no sign-up, and hand you an address instantly. For a one-time confirmation link, they're fine.

For an HOA portal, they fail on the two things that matter:

1. **They frequently get blocked at registration.** HOA management platforms check submitted addresses against disposable-domain lists. Registration either fails outright or silently drops confirmation and dues-reminder emails.
2. **They expire long before your next dues cycle.** Your first monthly statement arrives to an inbox that's already gone, with no forwarding and no way to keep receiving notices without re-registering a new address every few minutes — unworkable for a portal you'll use for years.

Throwaway addresses are the right tool for a single click-through. They are the wrong tool for anything that resembles an ongoing account.
