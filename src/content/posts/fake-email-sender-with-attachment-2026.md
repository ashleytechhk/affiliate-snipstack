## Troubleshooting

**Forwarded emails are arriving in my spam folder.**
Add your Alt ID forwarding domain to your email client's safe senders list. In Gmail: open a forwarded message, click the three-dot menu, select "Filter messages like this," and choose "Never send to Spam." In Outlook: Settings > Mail > Junk email > Safe senders, and add the Alt ID domain.

**The alias address is being rejected by the form or signup page.**
Some services blocklist known email forwarding domains. If one alias is rejected, generate a second one — Alt ID's domain pool is broad and most sites accept them without issue. For services that explicitly require a non-forwarding address and that you genuinely trust, consider using a dedicated secondary inbox (a separate Gmail or ProtonMail account) rather than your primary address.

**I submitted the form but no email arrived.**
First, check that the alias is toggled Active in Alt ID. Second, check that your real inbox is not full — a full mailbox silently rejects incoming messages. Third, check your spam folder for the forwarded message. If none of these resolve it, the issue is likely in the sending system, not the alias.

**The attachment is not forwarding correctly.**
Alt ID forwards the full email including MIME attachments. If an attachment is missing from the forwarded copy, check whether the sending system is actually attaching the file or just linking to it inline. Some "attachment" delivery systems embed a link rather than a true file attachment — in that case the forwarded email will contain the link but not a downloaded file.

**I want to also test what the email looks like from the sender's perspective.**
Use your alias as the recipient and a secondary email account (or a colleague's address) as the sender. This gives you visibility into both sides of the email transaction without your primary inbox being involved.
