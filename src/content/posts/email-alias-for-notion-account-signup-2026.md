## Why Notion Accounts Attract Spam

Notion requires an email address to create an account, and that address circulates further than most users realize.

**Template marketplace creators.** Notion's public template gallery lets thousands of creators publish templates. Some of those pages embed tracking pixels, newsletter sign-up prompts, or lead-capture forms. Browsing or duplicating a template can associate your email address with the creator's CRM.

**Workspace guest invitations.** When a colleague adds you to their workspace as a guest, your email address is now visible to the workspace owner and potentially to any admin-level integrations they have running. That workspace owner's tools — their Zapier, their HubSpot, their Notion-connected CRM — may now have your address.

**Third-party integrations via the Notion API.** Zapier, Make, Notion Calendar, and dozens of other services connect to Notion using OAuth. Each integration that handles your account data is a separate data processor with its own privacy practices and breach risk profile.

**Workspace transfers and sales.** Notion workspaces change hands. A startup using Notion for their internal wiki gets acquired. A freelancer's client workspace moves to a new owner. In both cases, the guest list — including your email address — moves with it.

**AI partner data flows.** Notion's AI features route content through model infrastructure. Your email address, embedded in account metadata, passes through these systems and their sub-processors.

Using an alias for your Notion signup severs the connection between your real address and all of these vectors.

### 2. Apple Hide My Email

Apple's iCloud+ feature creates random `@icloud.com` forwarding addresses. If you pay for any iCloud storage tier (50 GB and above), you already have access.

**For Notion users:** Works natively in Safari and on iPhone. When you sign up for Notion on a Mac or iOS device, "Hide My Email" appears as a suggestion in the email field. The alias is persistent — it stays active until you manually disable it in iCloud settings.

**Limitations:** Only available in Apple's ecosystem. If you sign up for Notion on a Windows machine, a browser that is not Safari, or an Android device, you cannot generate a Hide My Email alias inline. Managing your aliases requires the Settings app or iCloud.com, which is less convenient than a dedicated dashboard. No fake-identity generator — Apple gives you a masked email but not a masked name.

**Best for:** Apple-native Notion users who sign up and primarily work on Mac or iPhone.

### 3. SimpleLogin

An open-source alias service with a free tier of 15 aliases and an unlimited paid plan at around $4/month. Acquired by Proton in 2022.

**For Notion users:** The 15-alias free tier is generous enough for a single Notion account plus a handful of other services. Two-way alias support means you can reply to emails *from* your alias address, keeping your real address hidden in outgoing mail as well. Useful if you ever respond to a Notion workspace invitation email and do not want your real address in the Reply-To field.

**Limitations:** More technically involved to set up than Alt-ID or Apple's offering. No fake-identity generator. The free cap is finite, and heavy users of email aliases across multiple services will exhaust it.

**Best for:** Privacy-conscious users who want open-source tooling and value two-way alias support.

### 4. Firefox Relay

Mozilla's service offers five permanent forwarding aliases free, with unlimited on the Premium plan ($1.99/month). Browser extension for Firefox auto-suggests an alias when it detects an email input field.

**For Notion users:** Five aliases is a workable budget if you are protecting a small number of accounts. The tracker-stripping feature removes known tracking pixels from forwarded emails — useful if template creators embed tracking in the emails their forms trigger.

**Limitations:** Five-alias cap gets used up fast. Extension is Firefox-only. Chrome and Safari users get no inline alias generation. Deleted aliases cannot be reactivated.

**Best for:** Firefox users who only need a few aliases and want lightweight tracker blocking.

---

### 5. Gmail `+` Trick

Adding `+anything` to your Gmail address (e.g., `yourname+notion@gmail.com`) technically creates a variant that delivers to your real inbox.

**Why it does not work:** Most spam operations and marketers are aware of this pattern and strip the `+tag` before storing the address. Anyone who sees `yourname+notion@gmail.com` can trivially derive `yourname@gmail.com`. There is no deletion capability. The moment your real address is out, the filter is gone permanently.

**Verdict:** Not recommended. The illusion of privacy is worse than no protection, because it may give you false confidence.

## FAQ

**Can I change my existing Notion account to use an email alias instead of my real address?**

Yes. In Notion, go to **Settings → My account** and update your email address to your alias. Notion sends a verification email to the alias, which forwards to your real inbox. Confirm it and the alias becomes your active Notion email. [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-notion-account-signup-2026__faq) supports this fully — the alias is a persistent forwarding address, not a one-time inbox.

**Will an email alias affect Notion's account recovery and 2FA?**

Not as long as the alias is active and forwarding. Password reset emails, 2FA backup codes, and workspace re-invitation emails all arrive through the alias to your real inbox. The only risk is if you delete the alias without first updating your Notion account email — handle the alias like you would handle any email address change: update the account first, then retire the old address.

**Does Notion block forwarding or alias email domains?**

Notion does not currently block major alias providers like Alt-ID or SimpleLogin. Services that block alias domains typically target known disposable-mail domains (Guerrilla Mail, Temp Mail, and similar). Established alias providers use domains that are not on common blocklists, so Notion account creation and email verification succeed normally.

**Can I use a different alias for each Notion integration (Zapier, Calendar, etc.)?**

Yes, and for high-volume Notion users this is the recommended approach. Each integration gets its own alias. If spam starts arriving on the Zapier-linked alias, you know Zapier (or one of its connected apps) is the source — not Notion itself. Alt-ID's unlimited aliases make per-integration aliasing practical without tracking a free-tier cap.

**What happens to my Notion data if I delete my email alias?**

Nothing happens to your Notion workspace, pages, or data. Deleting the alias only closes the email channel — future emails sent to that address bounce. Your Notion account remains fully intact. Before deleting an alias you actively use for Notion, update your account email to a new alias or your real address so you retain access to password resets and workspace invitations.

**Is one alias enough for a Notion account, or do I need multiple?**

One alias is sufficient to protect your signup and basic account activity. If you connect multiple third-party integrations, share your workspace with external guests, or browse the public template gallery regularly, separate aliases per integration give you better traceability. When you can pinpoint exactly which service leaked, you respond faster and more precisely.
