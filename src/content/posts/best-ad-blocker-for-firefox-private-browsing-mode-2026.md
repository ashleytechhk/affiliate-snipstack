## What Is Firefox Private Browsing Mode, Really?

Private browsing mode (sometimes called "incognito mode" on other browsers) is a session type that stops Firefox from writing to your local browsing history, cookie jar, and cache once you close the tab. Open a private tab, browse a site, close the tab — and no record of that visit sits in your phone's storage afterward.

That's the entire feature. It's a **local storage setting**, not a content filter. Firefox private mode does not inspect, evaluate, or block any of the network requests a page makes while you're on it.

### What Private Browsing Does NOT Do

This is the part most mobile users get wrong, and it matters for anyone trying to reduce ads on their phone:

- It does **not** block ad networks from serving ads to the page
- It does **not** stop tracking scripts from loading and running during the session
- It does **not** hide your activity from your mobile carrier or Wi-Fi network
- It does **not** change your IP address or location
- It does **not** prevent the sites you visit from recognizing you via fingerprinting

Private mode only affects what Firefox stores on your device after the tab closes. Everything that happens *during* the session — including every ad request — behaves identically to a normal tab.

## How Ad Blocking Actually Works on Mobile Firefox

To fix this, you need to understand where ad blocking can actually happen on a phone — because the two approaches have very different reach inside private tabs.

### Browser-Level: Firefox Add-ons (Android Only)

Firefox for Android supports a limited catalog of extensions, including well-known content blockers. When installed and enabled, these add-ons filter ad requests inside Firefox — including in private tabs, since the extension runs as part of the browser regardless of session mode.

The catch: this only covers Firefox itself. Ads inside other apps — social feeds, games, email — are completely untouched. And Firefox for iOS has historically offered far more limited extension support than the Android version, which leaves iPhone users with fewer in-browser options.

Every request from every app, including Firefox in any session mode, passes through the same filter.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Firefox private browsing mode block ads by default?**

No. Private browsing only stops Firefox from saving history, cookies, and site data locally after you close the tab. It does not block ad servers, tracking scripts, or any network requests during the session itself.

**Why do I still see ads in Firefox incognito or private mode?**

Because private mode was built for local privacy — hiding your session from someone else who later uses your phone — not for filtering the content of the pages you visit. Ads load the same way in private tabs as they do in normal ones.

**Can I install an [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-that-stops-notification-permission-popups-2026/) extension in Firefox private tabs on mobile?**

On Android, yes — Firefox add-ons that are enabled in your browser settings also run inside private tabs, since the extension is part of the browser rather than the session mode. **Does a VPN-based [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-pluto-tv-and-free-streaming-services-2026/) like CleanWeb work in private browsing mode?**

Yes. Because CleanWeb filters requests at the DNS level before they reach your browser, it has no visibility into whether a tab is private or normal — it filters both identically.

**Is there a difference between "Enhanced Tracking Protection" and an ad blocker in Firefox?**

Yes, and it's an important one. Firefox's built-in Enhanced Tracking Protection focuses on blocking known trackers and fingerprinting scripts, not on hiding display ads or video ads themselves. It reduces some tracking-related network traffic but is not designed as a full ad blocker.

**Will blocking ads slow down my Firefox browsing?**

Generally the opposite. Removing ad and tracker requests before they load typically means fewer scripts competing for bandwidth and processing power, which tends to make pages feel faster to load, not slower.

**Do I need a separate [ad blocker for](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-podcast-app-with-dynamic-ad-insertion-2026/) every app, or just Firefox?**
