## The 7 Best NordVPN Mac Alternatives (and NordVPN Itself)

### 1. **Surfshark**

Surfshark's macOS app installs cleanly, supports both Intel and Apple Silicon Macs natively, and connects in under two seconds via its WireGuard implementation. The interface is minimal — you pick a server location and toggle on. No bloat, no confusing settings buried behind three menus.

For subscription arbitrage, Surfshark is the most cost-efficient option on this list. Its 2-year plan runs roughly $2.19/month, which is the lowest entry point among credible no-logs VPNs. Unlimited simultaneous devices means your Mac, iPhone, iPad, and Apple TV can all run the same subscription at the same time. That matters when you are managing several streaming accounts across devices.

Streaming unblocking covers Netflix, Disney+, HBO Max, Amazon Prime, and BBC iPlayer. The 30-day money-back window gives you enough time to verify it works with the services you care about before committing.

---

### 2. **NordVPN**

NordVPN's Mac client is one of the better-designed VPN apps on macOS — quick connect, a visual server map, and dedicated servers for P2P and double-VPN routing. It installs from the App Store or direct download, works on both Intel and M-series chips, and has never had a compatibility issue through major macOS updates.

The pricing is higher than Surfshark. The 2-year plan runs around $3.39/month, roughly $1.20 more per month. Over two years that is about $29 extra for equivalent streaming performance. NordVPN's NordLynx protocol does edge ahead on raw speed benchmarks — consistently among the fastest in independent tests — which matters if you do large downloads or use the VPN for more than just streaming.

If you want a premium feel and are already familiar with the product, NordVPN delivers. But for pure arbitrage ROI, the price premium is hard to justify against Surfshark's equivalent unblocking performance.

### 3. **ExpressVPN**

ExpressVPN is the premium-tier option: fast, polished, and consistent. Its Mac app is a benchmark for what a well-designed VPN client looks like — minimal chrome, fast server switching, and a status indicator that sits cleanly in the menu bar.

The cost is the sticking point. ExpressVPN is the most expensive mainstream VPN, typically $8–13/month even on annual plans. For a cost-first user, that price erodes streaming savings faster than any competing option. It remains the go-to recommendation for travelers and users who need reliability above all else, but it is harder to justify when Surfshark and NordVPN match its core streaming performance at a fraction of the cost.

ExpressVPN supports five simultaneous connections — lower than NordVPN's ten and well below Surfshark's unlimited. Unless you need its specific router firmware or have a reason to trust the brand over others, the arbitrage math does not work in its favor.

### 4. **[ProtonVPN](https://snipstack.io/vs-protonvpn-2026/)**

ProtonVPN is the privacy-first choice on this list, developed by the team behind ProtonMail and based in Switzerland. Its Mac app is well-maintained, open-source, and audited — which means the code that handles your traffic can be independently reviewed, not just vouched for by a marketing statement.

The free tier is genuinely useful for testing: no data cap, but limited to three server locations and one device. Paid plans unlock the full server network, streaming servers (called "Plus" servers), and up to ten simultaneous connections. Pricing for the Plus plan is mid-range, roughly $4–5/month on annual billing.

For subscription arbitrage, ProtonVPN's streaming server coverage is solid but not as consistently deep as NordVPN or Surfshark. If privacy credentials and an audited no-logs policy are your primary criteria alongside streaming access, ProtonVPN is the pick.

### 5. **Mullvad**

Mullvad is the outlier on this list — no accounts, no email required, payment accepted in cash or cryptocurrency. You get a randomly generated account number and nothing else ties your subscription to an identity. For privacy purists, it is hard to beat.

The Mac app is straightforward and fast. Mullvad supports WireGuard and OpenVPN, runs well on Apple Silicon, and is one of the few VPNs that has never experienced a verified data breach or audit failure. Pricing is flat: €5/month regardless of commitment length, which is honest but means no long-term discount.

The arbitrage case for Mullvad is moderate. Streaming unblocking is less reliable than Surfshark or NordVPN — it is not Mullvad's primary use case, and the company is transparent about that. It earns its place here for users who want a privacy-first Mac VPN and are willing to work a bit harder on server selection for streaming access.

### 6. **Private Internet Access (PIA)**

PIA has the largest server network of any VPN on this list — 35,000+ servers across 91 countries — which gives the most granular regional targeting for finding the cheapest pricing tier on a given streaming platform. If Turkey, Argentina, and India are the primary arbitrage markets, PIA will have multiple server options in each.

The Mac app is functional but visually dated compared to Surfshark or ExpressVPN. It works, connects reliably, and offers solid customization for protocol and encryption settings. Unlimited simultaneous connections match Surfshark's device policy, which is a meaningful advantage.

Long-term pricing on PIA is competitive — often under $2.50/month on a 3-year plan. It is a strong backup option for anyone who wants server variety above all else, or who finds Surfshark's regional options insufficient for a specific target market.

---

### 7. **CyberGhost**

CyberGhost rounds out this list with a feature that is genuinely useful for streaming arbitrageurs: dedicated streaming servers labeled by platform and region. Instead of trial-and-error on which server unblocks Netflix Japan, CyberGhost maintains a dedicated "Netflix Japan" server that is regularly tested and updated.

The Mac app is approachable — one of the better options for users who are new to VPNs and do not want to manage server selection manually. Pricing on the 2-year plan is roughly $2.03/month, which is slightly below even Surfshark's entry price. The 45-day money-back window is the longest on this list.

The downside: CyberGhost's privacy audit history is less rigorous than Surfshark, NordVPN, or ProtonVPN. It is not a red flag, but privacy-focused users will want to note it. For straightforward streaming arbitrage on Mac, it competes well on features and price.

## The Subscription Arbitrage Math on Mac

Running a VPN on Mac for streaming savings is a simple equation. Pick a low-cost region on a streaming platform, pay the local subscription price, and let the VPN handle the rest. The regions that consistently offer the steepest discounts versus US or UK pricing are Turkey, India, Argentina, and Egypt.

Take YouTube Premium as one example: US pricing runs $13.99/month; India pricing is approximately $1.89/month at current exchange rates. A Surfshark subscription at $2.19/month means your total cost is roughly $4/month versus nearly $14. That is over $100 saved per year from a single service.

Stack that across two or three subscriptions — Netflix, Spotify, and one other — and annual savings of $250–400 are realistic. The VPN pays for itself within the first month.

## FAQ

### Does NordVPN have a Mac app?

Yes. NordVPN has a dedicated macOS app available through the App Store or direct download. It supports both Intel and Apple Silicon Macs, includes WireGuard-based NordLynx protocol, and integrates with macOS system settings for split tunneling. Setup takes under five minutes.

### [Is Surfshark](https://snipstack.io/is-surfshark-one-worth-the-upgrade-2026/) better than NordVPN on Mac?

For most Mac users whose primary goal is streaming and subscription savings, Surfshark offers equivalent unblocking performance at a lower price with no device cap. NordVPN has a slight speed edge and a larger server network, but neither difference is meaningful at streaming bitrates.

### Can I use a free VPN on Mac for streaming?

[Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) impose data caps, limit server selection, and are frequently blocked by streaming platforms due to shared IP reputation. ProtonVPN's free tier is the most credible free option — no data cap — but restricts you to three server locations, which limits regional pricing access. For consistent arbitrage use, a paid plan is necessary.

### How do I install a VPN on Mac?

Download from the VPN provider's website or the Mac App Store, run the installer, and follow the setup prompt. Most modern VPNs (including Surfshark and NordVPN) install in under two minutes. You will need to grant a VPN configuration permission in macOS System Settings the first time. Apple Silicon and Intel Macs are both supported by every VPN on this list.

### Are VPNs legal on Mac?

VPNs are legal software in most countries and widely used for privacy, security, and regional access. Using a VPN to access lower-cost regional pricing on streaming services is a terms-of-service question, not a legal one — enforcement by platforms is typically limited to service restrictions rather than legal action. Review each platform's terms before use.
