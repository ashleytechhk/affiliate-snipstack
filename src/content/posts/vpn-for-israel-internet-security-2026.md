## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its core, it builds an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. Once connected, your internet traffic flows through that tunnel — your ISP, any network you are on, and every website you visit sees only the VPN server's IP address, not yours.

A simple analogy: imagine your internet traffic is a letter. Without a VPN, that letter travels on a public postal truck with your name and address clearly visible on the envelope. With a VPN, the letter goes into a locked courier bag, and only the courier's return address appears on the outside. No one along the route can read what is inside or trace it back to you.

For users in Israel, that shift matters on two levels:

- **Security.** On public Wi-Fi — in cafes, airports, hotels, shopping centres — your traffic is exposed to anyone else on the same network. Encryption seals it off completely.
- **Location.** Streaming services and other platforms determine what content and pricing to show you based on your IP address. Route your traffic through a different country's server and you appear to be located there — unlocking different libraries and, crucially, different price tiers.
