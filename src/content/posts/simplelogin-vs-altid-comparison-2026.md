## SimpleLogin: What It Does Well

SimpleLogin launched as an open-source [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-twitch-account-privacy-2026/) service and has built a loyal following among privacy-focused users who want full transparency into how their data is handled. Proton — the company behind ProtonMail and ProtonVPN — acquired it in 2022, which gave it a significant credibility boost.

### Open Source and Auditable

The entire SimpleLogin codebase is publicly available on GitHub. If you're technically inclined, you can verify exactly how aliases are processed and forwarded. You can even self-host the service on your own server if you want zero dependency on a third-party company.

For most spam-weary users, the open-source angle is more reassurance than utility. But it matters: independent security researchers can audit the code, and that audit history provides a level of trust that closed-source tools can't match on principle alone.

### Custom Domain Support

SimpleLogin lets you connect your own domain — say, `yourdomain.com` — so your aliases look like `shopping@yourdomain.com` instead of a randomised string at someone else's domain. This is useful for professionals who want branded aliases or for anyone who's concerned about alias domains being blocked by services that filter known privacy tool addresses.

### Free Tier With Real Functionality

SimpleLogin's free plan allows up to 10 active aliases. For a user who just wants category-level protection (one alias for shopping, one for newsletters, one for forums), 10 aliases is enough to get started without paying anything.

The catch: the free tier limits replies, and some advanced features like custom domains require a paid plan. At around $4/month or $30/year, the paid tier is reasonably priced as a standalone tool.

### Where SimpleLogin Falls Short

The interface is functional but not the most polished. If you're not comfortable with a settings-heavy dashboard, the configuration options can feel like overhead. More practically: SimpleLogin is a standalone tool. It doesn't come with a VPN, data removal, or any other privacy layer. You're buying exactly one feature.

## Alt-ID: What It Does Well

Surfshark's Alt-ID is the [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-gym-membership-signup-2026/) component of a broader [privacy suite](https://snipstack.io/privacy-suite-for-healthcare-professionals-2026/). It sits inside the Surfshark app alongside the VPN, data breach scanner, and personal data removal tool. For someone who's already dealing with spam *and* thinking about VPN protection, Alt-ID is the most efficient package.

### Built Into a Broader Privacy Stack

The most compelling thing about Alt-ID isn't the alias feature itself — it's the context around it. A spam problem is often a symptom of a broader data exposure issue. Your email got harvested; so did your IP address, your [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/), your browsing habits. Alt-ID addresses the email piece, and the rest of Surfshark's toolset addresses the other pieces.

If you're getting 50+ spam emails a day, there's a good chance your data is sitting on broker lists and breach databases. Surfshark's data removal tool requests removal from those lists directly. That's a combination SimpleLogin can't match because it's not trying to — it's a focused tool, not a platform.

### Simpler Setup for Non-Technical Users

Alt-ID lives inside the Surfshark app, which has one of the cleaner UIs in the privacy software space. Creating an alias takes about 30 seconds: open the app, find Alt-ID, click generate, label it, done. There's no separate account to manage, no additional login, and no decisions to make about custom domains or forwarding rules.

For a spam-exhausted user who just wants a solution that works, this matters. Less friction means higher adoption — and the strategy only works if you actually use the alias instead of your real address every time you sign up for something.

### Instant Delete and Reply Masking

Like SimpleLogin, Alt-ID supports two-way communication through the alias. When a forwarded email lands in your inbox and you reply, the reply goes out from your alias address, not your real one. The original sender never sees your actual email.

Deleting a compromised alias is one click. The spam stops immediately because the forwarding address no longer exists. No unsubscribing, no filters, no waiting.

### Where Alt-ID Falls Short

No custom domain support means your aliases will always use an Alt-ID–assigned domain. Some aggressive services block known privacy-tool domains — if an alias gets rejected at signup, you'll need your real address for that service or find a workaround.

There's also no open-source component. You're trusting Surfshark's infrastructure and privacy policy rather than an auditable codebase. Surfshark has published independent security audits, but that's different from a fully open codebase that anyone can inspect line by line.

Finally, Alt-ID requires a Surfshark subscription. If you already have one, it's free in the sense that no extra cost is added. If you're buying Surfshark specifically for Alt-ID and have no interest in the VPN, you're paying more for [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-real-estate-inquiries-2026/)ing than SimpleLogin's standalone plan would cost.

The difference is in the *surrounding system*.

If you're using Alt-ID inside a Surfshark subscription that also includes VPN and data removal, you're addressing spam from multiple angles simultaneously — blocking future exposure via aliases, removing existing exposure via the data removal tool, and masking your IP from trackers via the VPN. If you're using SimpleLogin as a standalone tool, you get excellent alias functionality and optionally a custom domain — but the spam coming in on your already-compromised real address isn't addressed by any other layer.

For someone starting fresh and willing to invest in a proper privacy setup, Alt-ID within Surfshark is the more complete solution. For someone who already has privacy tools elsewhere and just needs a best-in-class alias service with full configuration control, SimpleLogin is the right pick.

## FAQ

**Can I use both SimpleLogin and Alt-ID at the same time?**

Yes — there's no technical reason you can't use both. Some users run SimpleLogin for accounts where they want custom domain aliases and use Alt-ID for day-to-day signups within the Surfshark ecosystem. In practice, most people settle on one tool and use it consistently; splitting aliases across two services can create management overhead that defeats the purpose.

**Does Alt-ID work if I cancel my Surfshark subscription?**

No. Alt-ID is tied to your active Surfshark subscription. If you cancel, your aliases stop forwarding. Before cancelling, export your alias list and update any accounts that rely on those addresses. This is a meaningful lock-in consideration compared to SimpleLogin, which you own independently.

**Will SimpleLogin or Alt-ID stop spam that's already on my real address?**

Neither tool removes spam from an already-compromised real address. Both tools prevent *future* exposure by substituting the real address with an alias going forward. For spam arriving on your existing address, you'll need inbox filtering or — if the volume is severe enough — migrating to a fresh email address and using aliases exclusively for all new signups.

**Are alias domains blocked by banks and government services?**

High-security services like banks, government portals, and healthcare providers sometimes block known alias domains. This is more common with SimpleLogin's free-tier domains. If you're registering with a sensitive service, use your real address — those accounts warrant a direct relationship with your actual inbox. Reserve aliases for everything else.

**Does two-way reply via alias hide my identity completely?**

When you reply through an alias, the recipient sees your alias address, not your real one. However, your email client's display name, email headers, and writing style can still be identifying. For most spam-prevention purposes, alias-based reply masking is more than sufficient — your real address stays private. For strong anonymity, a dedicated [anonymous email](https://snipstack.io/anonymous-email-2026/) account adds an extra layer.

**Which tool is better for signing up to SaaS trials and newsletters?**

Both handle this use case well. The practical difference is volume: if you're spinning up five new aliases a week for various trials, Alt-ID's in-app creation is marginally faster. SimpleLogin's browser extension makes in-browser alias creation seamless if you're signed into the extension. For newsletters and trials specifically, either tool works — pick based on which broader ecosystem you're already in. **[Try Alt-ID with Surfshark](/blog/altid?sub=simplelogin-vs-altid-comparison-2026__faq)** if you want the fastest path to a clean, spam-free inbox.
