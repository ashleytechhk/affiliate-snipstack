## What "Disposable Email" Actually Means (Two Very Different Things)

The phrase covers two completely different tools. Mixing them up is why most people end up frustrated.

### Type 1 — Throwaway No-Inbox (Self-Destructing)

Services like 10-Minute Mail, Temp Mail, and Guerrilla Mail give you a randomly generated address. You paste it into a form, check a web page for the confirmation code, and that's it. The inbox disappears. You cannot reply from it. If the confirmation email arrives five minutes late and you've closed the tab, it's gone.

**When this works:** Forum registrations where you'll never log in again. Software trials that need a verified address to start. Anything where you want zero ongoing contact.

**When this fails:** When the service sends a password reset to that address a week later. When a delivery notification goes there. When you actually need to respond to the sender. When the inbox times out before the email arrives.

### Type 2 — Alias With Real Inbox (Persistent)

Services like Apple Hide My Email, Firefox Relay, and Surfshark Alt ID generate a unique forwarding address. Emails sent to that alias land in **your real inbox** — organized, searchable, and permanent. You can reply through the alias so the sender never sees your actual address. When the alias starts attracting spam, you delete it in one click and the flood stops instantly.

**When this works:** Online shopping. Newsletter signups you genuinely want. Account creation for services you'll use long-term. Anywhere you need a real correspondence address that isn't your personal one.

**When this fails:** If you need something that never routes to a real account at all (use Type 1 for that).

## Troubleshooting

**The confirmation email never arrived.**
Wait two minutes — forwarding adds a small delay. If nothing arrives after five minutes, check your spam folder in your real inbox. Some aggressive spam filters flag forwarded email. If it's consistently failing, check whether the site explicitly blocks forwarded addresses (a minority of sites do).

**The site rejected my alias address.**
A small number of services run alias-detection checks. If you hit one, this is unfortunately a site that insists on a real address. Use your actual email for that site, or consider whether you want an account with them at all.

**I can't find the Alt ID option in my dashboard.**
Alt ID is included in [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-mcafee-total-protection-2026/), not standalone VPN plans. Check which plan you're on. If you're on VPN-only, you'd need to upgrade to access Alt ID.

**Replies are bouncing.**
Make sure you're replying via your regular email client with the alias address in the "From" field — most Alt ID setups configure this automatically. If your client is overriding to your real address, check your reply-from settings.

**I deleted an alias by mistake.**
Deleted aliases cannot be restored. Any email sent to that address after deletion bounces. Generate a new one and update the signup on the relevant site.
