## What Is Fubo TV and Why Does It Block You Abroad?

Fubo TV is a US-based live TV streaming service built around sports. Its lineup includes the NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, MLS, Premier League, college football, and a wide range of regional sports networks — all streaming live, the same way cable once did. It also carries news and entertainment channels, but sports is the reason most people subscribe.

Fubo is available in the United States, Canada, and Spain. If you are outside those three territories, the service will block access at the IP level regardless of your account status. This is not a bug or a billing error — it is a deliberate licensing restriction. The leagues and broadcasters who supply Fubo's content sell international rights separately, often to competing platforms in each country. Fubo is contractually prohibited from streaming that content outside its licensed territories.

The result: the moment your device's IP address resolves to an unlicensed country, Fubo stops working. Your account and billing remain intact; only the access is gone.

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — solves this by routing your internet traffic through a server physically located inside the US. Fubo reads a US IP address and grants access. To its detection system, you appear to be streaming from Dallas or Chicago, even if you are actually in Tokyo or London.

## How a VPN Works for Fubo TV: The Technical Reality

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server in a location you select. When you connect to a US-based VPN server, all your outbound traffic appears to originate from that server's IP address — not your device's actual location.

![How a VPN routes your connection for Fubo TV live sports outside the US](/og/vpn-for-fubo-tv-outside-us-2026-inline-1.png)

For Fubo specifically, the process runs in three steps:

1. **You connect to a US VPN server** — the VPN app handles this in seconds once you select a US location.
2. **Your traffic routes through the server** — your real IP is masked; Fubo sees the server's US IP instead.
3. **Fubo grants access** — the platform reads a valid US address and loads your content normally.

The practical complication: Fubo, like most major streaming services, actively maintains blocklists of known VPN IP addresses. When a VPN server's IP gets flagged, streams through that server are blocked. Quality VPNs solve this by rotating their IP pools — when one address is flagged, traffic shifts to another. [Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) typically do not invest in this infrastructure, which is why they fail consistently against streaming platforms.
