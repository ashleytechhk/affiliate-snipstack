## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its core, a VPN is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server in a location you choose. Your internet traffic exits through that server — so websites and streaming platforms see the server's IP address and country, not yours.

Think of it like borrowing a friend's home address. The streaming service checks where you appear to be connecting from, finds the server's address instead of your real location, and serves you content as if you were a local subscriber.

There are two reasons F1 fans care about this:

1. **Geo-restriction bypass** — Broadcasting rights are sold territory by territory. A service licensed to show races in one country will block the stream for visitors from other countries.
2. **Regional price arbitrage** — The same streaming product often carries vastly different prices across markets. A Brazilian, Mexican, or Turkish subscription to an F1 streaming service can cost a fraction of the UK or US equivalent.
