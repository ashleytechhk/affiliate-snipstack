### SimpleLogin

SimpleLogin is the preferred choice for freelancers who want open-source software with auditable code. The free tier gives you 10 aliases, which is enough to get started. Paid plans at around $4 per month unlock unlimited aliases and custom domain support — useful if you operate under a personal brand and want aliases like `contact@yourfreelancerbrand.com`.

Reply-from-alias works well on SimpleLogin, and the interface is more feature-rich than Alt ID if you want granular control: per-alias notes, PGP encryption for forwarded mail, and detailed activity logs. The downside is that it is a dedicated tool requiring a separate account, and the setup process has a slightly steeper learning curve for users who are not technically inclined.

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple's Hide My Email generates random iCloud-backed aliases and is available to anyone paying for iCloud+ (starting at $1/month). It integrates seamlessly with Safari's autofill, which makes it genuinely effortless on Apple devices. The limitation is real: you can only use it properly within the Apple ecosystem. Replying from an alias requires using Apple Mail, which many freelancers do not use as their primary client.

For a Fiverr freelancer who works entirely on a Mac or iPhone and uses Apple Mail, Hide My Email is a perfectly adequate solution. For anyone on Windows, Android, or who prefers Gmail or Outlook, it quickly becomes frustrating.

### Gmail + Addressing

Gmail's dot trick (inserting dots anywhere in your username) and the plus alias system (`yourname+fiverr@gmail.com`) are well-known shortcuts. They are not true [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-veteran-benefit-portal-registration-2026/)es. Anyone receiving an email from a plus-addressed account can see your base Gmail address simply by looking at the From field. This offers no real privacy and no spam isolation — senders can trivially filter out the plus portion and reach your real address directly. Treat this as a sorting tool, not a privacy tool.

## FAQ

### Do I need a separate email account, or is an alias different?

An alias is different from a separate email account. You do not create a new inbox — you create a forwarding address that delivers mail to your existing inbox. You manage everything in one place. The alias is just the address the outside world sees.

### Can clients reply to my alias and have it work properly?

With Surfshark Alt ID and SimpleLogin, yes. When a client replies to your alias, the reply lands in your real inbox, and when you reply back, it sends from the alias address. The conversation looks seamless to the client. [Learn more about how Alt ID handles replies.](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-fiverr-freelancer-account-2026__faq)

### [Is using an](https://snipstack.io/is-using-an-ad-blocker-legal-2026/) alias against Fiverr's terms of service?

No. Fiverr requires a valid, working email address for your account — an alias qualifies because it delivers mail reliably. Fiverr does not require you to use a personal email address. As long as you can receive and respond to verification and notification emails, you are in compliance.

### [What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if I delete the alias?

Any future email sent to that alias simply bounces or is silently dropped, depending on the provider. Your real inbox receives nothing. This is the core advantage over trying to unsubscribe from spam — instead of hoping the sender respects your request, you eliminate the delivery path entirely.

### Should I use a different alias for each client or one alias for all of Fiverr?

It depends on your volume and preferences. Using one alias for Fiverr platform emails and separate aliases for individual high-value clients gives you the most control. If a specific client relationship generates noise after the project ends, you can disable that alias without affecting anything else. For most freelancers, one or two aliases — one for Fiverr itself and one for general professional use — is enough to see a dramatic improvement.

### Does an alias affect email deliverability when I send messages?

When you reply from an alias via Alt ID or SimpleLogin, the email is sent on your behalf through the alias service's servers. Deliverability is generally good, but you should avoid sending high-volume bulk emails through an alias service — those are not designed for newsletter distribution. For one-to-one client communication, deliverability is not an issue in practice.
