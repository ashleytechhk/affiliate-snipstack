## What a Dedicated IP Add-On Actually Gives You

A standard VPN connection puts you on a shared server IP alongside thousands of other users. That's fine for everyday browsing, but it causes friction the moment you need something to reliably recognize you: a banking portal, a whitelisted remote-access tool, or a subscription service that gets suspicious about "new location" logins every time you connect.

A dedicated IP address flips that. You're assigned one address that only you use, in a country you choose. Services see the same IP every session — no rotation, no repeated two-factor prompts, no sudden account lock because your traffic "moved" overnight. For someone switching away from Norton or McAfee specifically because of renewal fatigue and bloated background processes, this is the kind of quiet, practical upgrade that makes the switch feel worthwhile rather than just cheaper.

## FAQ

### Do Norton or McAfee offer a dedicated IP address add-on?

Not as a self-serve consumer option. Both companies focus their dedicated or static IP offerings on business and enterprise tiers, which typically require a sales conversation and a higher contract. Surfshark One offers it as an in-app add-on available to any subscriber.

### Is a dedicated IP the same as a static IP?

The terms are generally used to mean the same thing: one IP address assigned only to your account that doesn't change between sessions. Some providers say "static," others say "dedicated" — the underlying feature is the same.

### Will a dedicated IP stop every account lockout or verification prompt?

It significantly reduces them because your address stays consistent, but no single feature removes every security check a service runs. Results vary by platform and by how that service evaluates logins. [Compare current options →](/blog/one?sub=security-bundle-with-dedicated-ip-address-add-on-2026__faq)

### Does adding a dedicated IP slow down the VPN connection?

There's a small amount of overhead with any VPN connection, but a dedicated IP doesn't meaningfully change that. Modern protocols like WireGuard keep the difference small enough that most people don't notice it during normal browsing, calls, or file transfers.

### Can I cancel Norton or McAfee before the renewal date?

Both offer cancellation windows and prorated refunds in some regions, though the terms vary by plan and by country. Check your specific account settings, and time your cancellation before the renewal charge posts if you're planning to switch.

### Do I need the dedicated IP add-on on every device?

No. The dedicated IP is tied to your account and plan, not to an individual device. Once it's enabled, any device you connect from can use it, which is part of why it works well alongside an unlimited-device bundle.

### Is Surfshark One overkill if I only want the dedicated IP feature?

Not really — since the antivirus, VPN, and breach monitoring come in the same subscription at no extra cost beyond the add-on itself, you're not paying a premium just to get the dedicated IP. It's priced as one bundle, not stacked products.
