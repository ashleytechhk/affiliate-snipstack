## The Problem With Running Two Separate Products

Legacy suites like Norton and McAfee were built in a different era. Their antivirus engines are solid, but the products carry years of feature bloat: VPN add-ons bolted onto the side, password managers that overlap with what your browser already does, parental controls you never enabled, and PC-tune-up tools that slow down boot time while claiming to speed it up.

When you add a standalone VPN on top — because the bundled one has a data cap or subpar server count — you end up with:

- Two different renewal cycles to track
- Two separate apps competing for system resources
- Two support queues if something breaks
- A combined monthly cost that surprises you every time

This is the exact friction a combined VPN and antivirus subscription is designed to eliminate.
