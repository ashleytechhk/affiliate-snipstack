## What Is a VPN and Why Does It Matter for Accountants?

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a secure server. Any data leaving your laptop — whether it is a tax filing uploaded to a cloud portal, a payroll spreadsheet sent by email, or a video call with a client — passes through that tunnel in encrypted form.

Without a VPN, anyone on the same network as you can potentially capture that data in transit. Public Wi-Fi in hotels, airport lounges, and client conference rooms is a common attack surface. Even a home broadband connection exposes your activity to your ISP, which may log and sell browsing data in some jurisdictions.

For accountants specifically, the risks are compounded:

- **Regulatory exposure.** Many jurisdictions require that professionals handling financial or personal data implement reasonable technical safeguards. A VPN is one of the simplest to deploy.
- **Client trust.** A single breach can end a long-standing client relationship overnight.
- **Remote work reality.** Post-pandemic work patterns mean accountants routinely access cloud accounting platforms, payroll systems, and document management tools from locations other than their secure office network.

A VPN addresses all three concerns without requiring a large infrastructure investment.

## How a VPN Protects Client Data in Practice

Here is [what happens when](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) you connect through a VPN:

1. Your device establishes an encrypted connection to a VPN server.
2. All outgoing data is scrambled before it leaves your device.
3. The VPN server forwards your requests to the internet — cloud storage, email servers, accounting software APIs — on your behalf.
4. Responses come back through the same encrypted tunnel.

Anyone intercepting traffic between your device and the VPN server sees only encrypted gibberish. Your IP address is also masked, so third-party trackers cannot build a profile of your online activity.

### Kill Switch: The Safety Net You Did Not Know You Needed

A quality VPN includes a kill switch. If the VPN connection drops unexpectedly — due to a spotty Wi-Fi signal or a server hiccup — the kill switch immediately cuts your internet access entirely until the VPN reconnects. Without it, your device falls back to unencrypted browsing, potentially exposing data mid-session. Surfshark's kill switch is available on all major platforms and is enabled with a single toggle.

## Do Accountants Have a Legal Obligation to Use a VPN?

The short answer is: it depends on your jurisdiction and the type of client data you handle — but the trend is toward stricter data-handling requirements, not looser ones.

- **GDPR (European Union and UK):** Requires that personal data be protected by appropriate technical measures. Transmitting unencrypted financial data over public networks would be difficult to defend as "appropriate."
- **HIPAA (United States — if you handle healthcare-adjacent financials):** Requires encryption for electronic protected health information in transit.
- **GLBA (United States — financial services):** The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act requires financial institutions to protect client information with administrative, technical, and physical safeguards.
- **Local professional body rules:** Many accountancy institutes publish cybersecurity guidance that effectively recommends encrypted connections for remote work.

A VPN is not a compliance silver bullet on its own, but it is a credible and auditable technical control that demonstrates due diligence.
