## Why You Need Both Antivirus and a VPN in 2026

Many people have heard of [antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-for-iphone-2026/). Fewer have heard of a VPN. Here is what each one does — and why having both matters.

### What [antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-2026/) does

Antivirus software is like a security guard for your device. It checks every file, email, and website for anything dangerous, and blocks threats before they can cause harm. Without it, a single click on the wrong link can install a program that steals your passwords, wipes your photos, or demands money to unlock your computer.

**Antivirus protects you from:**
- Viruses and malware that slow or damage your device
- Ransomware that locks your files until you pay
- Spyware that records what you type — including passwords and bank details
- Fake websites designed to steal your information

### What a VPN does

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is like drawing the curtains on your internet connection. When you use public Wi-Fi — at a library, cafe, airport, or doctor's waiting room — strangers on the same network can sometimes see what you are doing online. A VPN hides your activity so that no one can see what you are browsing, and it masks your location so advertisers and data collectors cannot track you.

**A VPN protects you from:**
- Snooping on public Wi-Fi networks
- Your internet provider selling your browsing history
- Online tracking and targeted ads based on your activity
- Some types of [identity theft](https://snipstack.io/identity-theft-protection-software-2026/) tied to unsecured connections
