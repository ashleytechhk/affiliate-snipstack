## Windows Defender: The Sleeper Pick

Microsoft quietly turned Windows Defender into a genuinely capable antivirus. Independent lab tests from AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives now rate it alongside many paid products for malware detection rates. If your threat model is everyday phishing links and run-of-the-mill malware, Defender is a completely reasonable choice — and it is already running on your Windows 11 machine.

The problem is everything it does *not* do. There is no VPN. There is no identity monitoring beyond the basic Microsoft Account breach alerts (which most users never check). It covers one Windows device. If you use a Mac, Android phone, or a work laptop alongside your home PC, you are on your own.

For the former Norton or McAfee customer, the real comparison is not "Defender vs. paid antivirus" — it is "Defender + a VPN subscription + a breach monitoring service vs. a bundle." Once you add those up, free stops being free.

## Avast and AVG: Ad-Supported Free Tiers

Avast Free and its sister product AVG Free both score well in independent malware detection tests. They are genuinely capable scanners. The tradeoff is the business model: these products are free because you are the product. Both have faced scrutiny over data collection practices in previous years.

![Free antivirus workflow comparison](/og/vs-free-antivirus-2026-inline-1.png)

Avast does include a VPN in its free tier, but the 200 MB-per-day cap makes it useless for anything beyond a quick web search. Streaming, remote work, or even a single video call will burn through the daily limit in minutes. AVG Free does not include a VPN at all.

Both apps also push upsells hard. If you found Norton's constant upgrade prompts annoying, you will find Avast and AVG's popups no less intrusive — just without the price tag for the base install.

## Malwarebytes Free: The Cleanup Tool

Malwarebytes built its reputation as the go-to tool for removing infections that other scanners missed. The free version holds up that reputation for on-demand scans. What it does not offer is real-time protection — the free tier only scans when you manually trigger it, which means it catches malware after the fact rather than blocking it at the door.

For a second-opinion scanner sitting alongside Defender, Malwarebytes Free is useful. As a primary security solution, the gaps are too wide for anyone who wants genuine ongoing protection.
