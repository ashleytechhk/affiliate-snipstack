## What Is ERT and Why Is It Geo-Blocked?

ERT (Elliniki Radiofonia Tileorasi) is Greece's public broadcaster. It operates several TV channels (ERT1, ERT2, ERT3, ERT Sports) plus a streaming platform called ERT Play. ERT is publicly funded, which means Greek taxpayers essentially pay for it — but the licensing agreements ERT holds for certain sports, films, and international programmes are territory-specific.

When a rights holder sells ERT the licence to broadcast a football match or a foreign series, that licence typically covers Greece only. Broadcasting the same content to international IP addresses would breach the agreement. So ERT's servers check your IP address, determine that you are in, say, Melbourne or Toronto or Dubai, and block the stream.

This is not ERT being hostile to the diaspora. It is a legal constraint baked into how broadcast rights work globally.

## What Is a VPN?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in a location of your choice. When you connect to a VPN server in Athens, websites and streaming services see an Athens IP address — not your actual location.

Think of it like using a Greek PO box. Your letter (data request) goes out from your house in London, but it arrives at ERT's servers stamped with a Greek return address.

Beyond changing your apparent location, a VPN also encrypts your traffic, which means your internet service provider, your landlord's router, and the coffee-shop Wi-Fi cannot see what you are doing online. For expats, this is a useful side benefit when browsing on hotel or airport networks.

What a VPN does not do: it does not make you untraceable, it does not guarantee your ERT account will never be audited, and it does not bypass paywalls on its own. You still need an ERT Play account (free for Greek residents, though registration requirements vary).
