## What Is a VPN and Why Does It Matter for Smart TVs?

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in a location you choose. From the perspective of every website and streaming service you visit, you appear to be browsing from that server's location, not your own.

For laptops and phones, this is simple: install an app, tap connect, done. Smart TVs are more complicated because most TV operating systems — Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, and older Android TV builds — don't support VPN apps the way a phone or desktop does. That's why there are three distinct methods for getting a VPN onto your TV:

1. **Native app on Android TV / Google TV** — the easiest option if your TV or TV box supports it
2. **Router-level VPN** — works on any smart TV, covers your whole home
3. **SmartDNS** — fast geo-unblocking without encryption, good for TVs that can't run VPN apps

Each method is covered in detail below.

## Why Streaming Prices Vary by Region

Streaming platforms set prices in local currencies and adjust them based on purchasing power and market competition. A Netflix Standard plan might cost the equivalent of $15 in one country and $4–6 in another — for the same content and account features.

The regions with lower pricing tend to be emerging markets — Turkey, Argentina, India, and Brazil are commonly cited as markets where major streaming services are priced significantly lower than in the US, UK, or Australia.

A VPN lets you connect to a server in one of those regions so the streaming service sees you as a local subscriber. For anyone paying full regional price on multiple services, the annual savings can easily exceed $150.

### Method 2: Router-Level VPN (Works on Any Smart TV)

If your TV runs Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, or any other closed operating system, you can't install a VPN app directly on it. The solution is to set the VPN up on your home router instead. Every device connected to that router — including your TV — then routes through the VPN automatically.

![Router-level VPN setup diagram for smart TV connection](/og/vpn-for-smart-tv-setup-2026-inline-1.png)

**How to set it up:**

1. Log into your router admin panel (usually at `192.168.1.1` or `192.168.0.1`)
2. Find the VPN or OpenVPN/WireGuard section in the settings
3. Enter your Surfshark credentials and the server address for your chosen region
4. Save the settings and restart the router
5. Your TV and every other device on the network now connects through the VPN

Surfshark provides step-by-step setup guides with screenshots for over 30 router models, including ASUS, Netgear Nighthawk, GL.iNet, and Linksys WRT series. Most setups take 15–20 minutes. Note that some ISP-provided routers don't support VPN configuration — if yours is one of them, Method 3 is a good alternative.

**Works on:** Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, all smart TVs, game consoles, and any other networked device in your home

### Method 3: SmartDNS (Fast Geo-Unblocking, No Encryption)

SmartDNS is a different approach: instead of routing all your traffic through a VPN server, it only reroutes the DNS lookup — the part of your connection that identifies your location to streaming services. The actual video data still travels directly, which means no speed overhead.

The trade-off is that SmartDNS does not encrypt your traffic. It unblocks geo-restricted content and pricing but doesn't provide the [privacy protection](https://snipstack.io/privacy-protection-software-review-2026/) that a full VPN tunnel does. For viewers whose only goal is accessing regional streaming pricing, and whose TV doesn't support the other methods, SmartDNS is a practical tool.

**How to set it up on a Samsung or LG Smart TV:**

1. Log into your Surfshark account dashboard and find the SmartDNS section
2. Activate SmartDNS and note the two DNS server addresses provided
3. On your TV, go to Network Settings > Advanced > DNS Settings
4. Switch from Automatic to Manual and enter the Surfshark SmartDNS addresses
5. Restart your TV — it will now resolve streaming service locations through Surfshark's DNS

**Works on:** Samsung Tizen, LG webOS, Apple TV, and any TV where you can manually change DNS settings

**Will a VPN slow down my streaming quality?**

On a standard broadband connection of 25 Mbps or above, the impact on streaming quality is minimal for the router and native app methods. SmartDNS has essentially no speed overhead because your video data doesn't route through a VPN server. For 4K HDR content, most home broadband connections have enough headroom to absorb the slight VPN overhead without buffering.

**What is SmartDNS and how is it different from a VPN?**

SmartDNS only reroutes the DNS lookup — the step where a streaming service checks your location. Your actual video data travels directly, so there's no speed penalty. The difference is that SmartDNS does not encrypt your traffic, so it provides geo-unblocking without the privacy protection of a full VPN tunnel. It's a good fit for smart TVs that can't run VPN apps and for users whose main goal is accessing regional pricing rather than encrypted browsing.
