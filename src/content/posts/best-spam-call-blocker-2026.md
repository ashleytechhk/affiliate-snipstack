## 2. Google Voice — Best Free Option (US Only)

**The core idea:** a [free second](https://snipstack.io/free-second-phone-number-for-whatsapp-2026/)ary number from Google, forwarded to your real phone. You hand out the Voice number on dating apps, and real calls ring through to your device.

Google Voice has been around long enough that it handles spam filtering reasonably well. It automatically labels likely spam calls and lets you block individual numbers with one tap. The number itself is a genuine US area code, so it does not raise eyebrows when you share it.

The catch is the US-only limitation. If you date internationally or travel, coverage is patchy. Google Voice also requires a Google account in good standing and can occasionally flag your number as inactive if you go weeks without using it. For US-based daters on a tight budget, though, it is a capable free baseline.

## 3. Hiya — Best Dedicated Spam Detection App

**The core idea:** a call-screening layer over your existing number that identifies and blocks known spam sources in real time.

Hiya maintains one of the largest spam-number databases in the industry, which means it recognises robocallers, scammers, and phishing lines before your phone even rings. The caller ID display tells you what kind of call it is — telemarketer, fraud risk, suspected scam — so you can make an informed decision before picking up.

Where Hiya falls short for dating use is that it does nothing to hide your real number. A match you no longer want hearing from can still ring you; Hiya only helps if that person's number has already been flagged as spam by other users. It is reactive rather than preventive.

## 4. RoboKiller — Best for Answering Spam So You Do Not Have To

**The core idea:** AI-powered answer bots that pick up spam calls and waste the caller's time while you get on with your life.

RoboKiller is genuinely entertaining if you have ever wanted revenge on persistent callers. Its answer bots hold spammers in loop conversations, draining the time of automated diallers and eventually getting you removed from call lists. The spam-block list is community-sourced and updated continuously.

For dating scenarios, RoboKiller is most useful once a bad interaction has already occurred and someone is calling repeatedly from a number you recognise. It is less useful as a front-line privacy tool — your real number is still exposed before any blocking can happen.

## 7. Nomorobo — Best Carrier-Level Spam Blocking

**The core idea:** intercept robocalls at the carrier level before they reach your device.

Nomorobo partners with carriers to stop automated calls on the network itself, before your phone rings at all. It is the most transparent option on this list — when it works, blocked calls simply never arrive. There is no app cluttering your home screen and no setup beyond enabling it through your carrier settings.

The trade-off is limited control. You cannot fine-tune block lists, you cannot block individual numbers you know personally, and carrier support varies by country. For online daters who primarily want to reduce the background noise of robocalls while using their real number, Nomorobo is a clean, low-effort option.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a spam call blocker hide my real [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/)?**

Most dedicated spam blockers — Hiya, RoboKiller, Nomorobo — do not hide your real number. They filter incoming traffic after your number is already known. If you want to keep your real number private, you need a [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-lyft-driver-account-2026/) tool like [Surfshark Alt-Number](/blog/surfshark?sub=best-spam-call-blocker-2026__faq) or Google Voice that lets you share an alternative number instead.

**Is Google Voice good enough for online dating privacy?**

Google Voice is a workable free option for US-based daters. You get a real-looking local number that you can share on apps. Its spam filtering is decent but not comprehensive, and it does not work reliably outside the US. If your dating life extends beyond US matches or you want tighter privacy controls, a dedicated tool is worth the cost.

**Can I use a spam call blocker on both iPhone and Android?**

Most tools on this list support both platforms. Surfshark Alt-Number, Hiya, RoboKiller, Burner, and YouMail all have iOS and Android apps. Nomorobo works at the carrier level and is therefore device-agnostic. Google Voice requires the Google Voice app, available on both platforms.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if I block someone on a dating app — can they still call me?**

Blocking someone on a dating app (Tinder, Hinge, Bumble, etc.) prevents contact through that platform. If they already have your real [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-social-media-2026/), they can still call or text you from it. This is exactly why sharing a secondary number rather than your real one matters — you can retire the secondary number without affecting your actual line.

**Is it legal to use a [second phone number](https://snipstack.io/second-phone-number-2026/) for dating?**

Yes, using a secondary number for personal privacy is legal in most jurisdictions. It is a standard privacy practice, not deception. Apps like Alt-Number are designed precisely for situations where you want to control who has access to your direct contact information. The number you provide is a real, working number — calls and texts reach you — it simply is not your primary SIM number.
