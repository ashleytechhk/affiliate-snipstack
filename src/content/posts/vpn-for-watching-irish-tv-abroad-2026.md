## What Is a VPN and Why Does Irish TV Block You Abroad?

A VPN is software that encrypts your internet connection and tunnels it through a server in a location you choose. When you connect to an Irish VPN server, websites and streaming platforms see that server's Irish IP address — not the address assigned to your actual device in Tokyo, Toronto, or Dubai.

Irish broadcasters like RTE and TG4 operate under broadcast licensing agreements that are tied to geographic territories. These deals dictate where their content can legally be streamed. When you travel or move overseas, the platform detects your foreign IP address and blocks access — the infamous "this content is not available in your region" message. The platform is not being difficult; it is enforcing rights it is contractually required to enforce.

A VPN sidesteps this by making your connection appear to originate from within Ireland. The platform sees an Irish IP, assumes you are in Ireland, and grants access. This is the core mechanic behind every "watch Irish TV abroad" guide you will find in 2026.
