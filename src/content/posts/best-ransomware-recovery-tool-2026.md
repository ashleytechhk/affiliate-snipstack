## What Is Ransomware and Why Should Seniors Worry?

Ransomware is a type of virus that sneaks onto your computer — usually through a suspicious email, a fake link, or an unsafe website — and then quietly locks all your files. Your family photos, tax documents, medical records, everything. Then a message pops up on your screen demanding money, usually hundreds or even thousands of dollars, before the criminal will unlock your files.

Here is the hard truth: even if you pay, there is no guarantee you will get your files back. Criminals do not keep promises.

What makes ransomware so dangerous for older adults:

- **It spreads through ordinary-looking emails.** You might get what appears to be a message from your bank, the post office, or even Medicare. One wrong click is all it takes.
- **It works fast.** A ransomware attack can lock every file on your computer in minutes.
- **Recovery without protection is nearly impossible.** Without the right tool in place beforehand, recovering your files often requires expensive professional help — or they are gone for good.
- **Seniors are specifically targeted.** Cybercriminals buy lists of older adults' email addresses and run automated attacks. You are on those lists whether you know it or not.

## The Warning Signs You Must Not Ignore

![Common ransomware warning signs for seniors to watch for in 2026](/og/best-ransomware-recovery-tool-2026-inline-1.png)

Most people have no idea ransomware is on their computer until it is too late. Watch for these early warning signs:

**Your computer suddenly becomes very slow.** Ransomware works in the background before revealing itself, and this uses a lot of processing power.

**Files you try to open say they are corrupted.** This is often one of the first visible signs that ransomware is encrypting your files one by one.

**You see a pop-up message you have never seen before.** A sudden screen covering everything, demanding payment or showing a countdown timer, is the ransomware revealing itself.

**Your antivirus has been disabled.** Some advanced ransomware turns off your protection first. If your security software suddenly stops working, take action immediately.

**Unfamiliar file extensions on your documents.** If your photos suddenly end in ".locked" or a strange string of letters instead of ".jpg," ransomware has likely already started.

If you notice any of these signs, disconnect your computer from the internet immediately by unplugging the ethernet cable or turning off your Wi-Fi. Then contact a trusted person for help.
