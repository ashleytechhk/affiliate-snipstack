## What Is a VPN for South Korea Access?

A **VPN (Virtual Private Network)** is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in a location you choose. When you connect to a South Korean VPN server, websites and online services see a South Korean IP address — not your real one.

For South Korea access specifically, this means:

- **Streaming platforms** (Netflix, TVING, Wavve) see you as a Korean viewer and show you the Korean content library
- **Subscription services** display pricing in Korean Won at Korean local rates
- **Korean-only services** (platforms that block non-Korean IP addresses entirely) become accessible

The VPN does not alter your account or payment details — it only changes the apparent location of your connection. You remain the same subscriber with the same account; the platform simply serves you as a local customer.

### Key Terms Defined

**IP address:** A number that identifies your internet connection's location. Websites use this to determine where you are browsing from.

**VPN server:** A computer operated by the VPN company in a specific country. Your traffic routes through it, so you appear to be in that country.

**Geo-restriction:** When a platform limits access or pricing based on the user's detected country. Netflix's different catalogs by country is a common example.

**Subscription arbitrage:** Using a VPN to access the lower-priced version of a subscription service available in another country. South Korea has notably lower pricing on several major services compared to Western markets.

## How a VPN for South Korea Access Works

The technical process is straightforward, though the underlying mechanics are worth understanding to use it effectively.

### Step-by-Step: How Your Connection Routes

1. **You open the VPN app** and select a South Korean server location
2. **The VPN encrypts your traffic** and sends it to that Korean server
3. **The Korean server makes the request** to the streaming service or website on your behalf
4. **The streaming service sees a Korean IP address** and returns Korean-region content, pricing, and access
5. **The content returns to you** through the encrypted tunnel

From the streaming platform's perspective, the request came from South Korea. Your actual location — wherever you are browsing from — is not visible to the platform.

A VPN adds a routing step, which introduces some latency. For South Korean servers specifically, the speed impact depends on:
