## What Is a VPN? (Plain English Version)

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server somewhere else on the internet. All your network traffic travels through that tunnel before reaching its destination.

Two things happen when you connect to a VPN:

1. **Your traffic is encrypted.** Anyone watching your connection — your ISP, a coffee shop router, a client's network — sees scrambled data, not what you are actually doing.
2. **Your apparent location changes.** Websites and services see the IP address of the VPN server, not your real IP. If the VPN server is in a different country, you appear to be in that country.

For IT support workers, the first point is the professional one. For subscription savings, the second point is the one that matters.

## Why Remote IT Support Workers Need a VPN

Remote IT support is one of the job roles where a VPN is not optional — it is a baseline requirement. Here is why.

### Accessing Client Networks Securely

When you remote into a client's server, you are reaching into a private network over the public internet. Without a VPN, that connection is either exposed or relying entirely on the target system's own security. A VPN adds an encrypted layer at the network level so that your access session cannot be intercepted in transit.

Most enterprise remote support setups — RDP, SSH tunnels, TeamViewer with enterprise config — expect a VPN to be part of the stack. If your workflow sends any credentials or configuration data over the wire, a VPN is how you keep that safe.

### Protecting Your Traffic on Untrusted Networks

IT support workers often connect from unusual places. A client's office wifi. A hotel room. A co-working space. A venue where you have just been on-site for an emergency call.

These networks are not under your control. An operator can see unencrypted traffic. Another user on the same wifi can run a packet capture. Your ISP logs which services you connect to. A VPN encrypts your session so the network you are using sees only that you are connected to a VPN server — nothing else.

### Bypassing Geographic Restrictions on Work Tools

Some IT monitoring platforms, cloud management consoles, and vendor portals are geographically restricted. If you are managing infrastructure for clients in multiple regions or working from a location that does not match the vendor's expected territory, you may hit access blocks that have nothing to do with your credentials. A VPN server in the right region resolves this in seconds.
