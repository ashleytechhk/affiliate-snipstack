## What Is an iCloud [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-online-shopping-2026/)?

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/how-email-alias-2026/) is a secondary address that delivers mail to your primary inbox. The sender sees only the alias — they never learn the address behind it.

iCloud's version, **Hide My Email**, works the same way: Apple generates a random `@icloud.com` address, links it to your Apple ID, and forwards any incoming mail to your main inbox. You can create a new alias for every app, newsletter, or service you sign up for.

When a specific alias starts attracting junk, you deactivate it in Settings. No unsubscribe hoops. No hoping the sender complies. The address just stops receiving mail.

## Prerequisites

Before you start, confirm you have everything in place:

1. **An Apple ID** — required for all iCloud features
2. **An iCloud+ subscription** — Hide My Email is not available on the free iCloud tier. Any paid storage plan (starting at the entry-level tier) unlocks it
3. **A device running iOS 15 / iPadOS 15 / macOS Monterey or later** — or access to icloud.com in a browser
4. **Your primary Apple ID email address** — this is where forwarded mail lands

If you are not sure whether you have iCloud+, open **Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud**. If you see "Upgrade to iCloud+" or a storage plan label, you'll know your current status immediately.

## Step-by-Step: Create an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/) with iCloud Hide My Email

### Step 1 — Open Hide My Email on Your iPhone or iPad

1. Open the **Settings** app
2. Tap your name at the top to open Apple ID settings
3. Tap **iCloud**
4. Scroll down and tap **Hide My Email**

> *Screenshot placeholder: iPhone Settings showing the path from [Your Name] → iCloud → Hide My Email, with the Hide My Email row highlighted.*

You will see a list of any aliases you have already created. If this is your first time, the list will be empty.

### Step 2 — Create a New Address

Tap the **+** button or **Create New Address** at the bottom of the screen.

Apple immediately generates a random `@icloud.com` address for you — something like `teal.pine.4m7k@icloud.com`. You cannot choose your own username; the address is always randomised.

> *Screenshot placeholder: The "Create New Address" screen showing a randomly generated iCloud alias and the Label and Note fields below it.*

### Step 3 — Add a Label and Note (Optional but Recommended)

Below the generated address, you will see a **Label** field. Add a label that tells you where you used this alias — for example, "Amazon checkout" or "Newsletter signups." This makes it easy to find and disable the right alias later without guessing.

The **Note** field is free text for anything extra you want to remember. Not required, but useful if you are managing many aliases.

### Step 4 — Confirm the Forwarding Address

At the bottom of the screen, check that **Forward To** shows the correct Apple ID email address. By default it forwards to your primary iCloud email, but if you have multiple addresses linked to your Apple ID you can change the destination here.

Tap **Next**, then **Done**.

![Flowchart diagram: random iCloud alias generated → forwards to Apple ID inbox → real email address stays hidden from websites](/og/how-to-create-email-alias-icloud-2026-inline-1.png)

*Each alias acts as a one-way buffer. The website stores only the random address — your real inbox stays out of their database.*

### Step 5 — Use the Alias at Your Next Signup

Copy your new alias from the Hide My Email list and paste it into any website's email field. The site's confirmation email will arrive in your normal inbox within seconds, forwarded invisibly through the alias.

Good candidates for alias use:

- E-commerce checkouts
- Newsletter subscriptions
- Free trial registrations
- Loyalty cards and reward programs
- Any site you have never used before

### Step 6 — Create Aliases on iCloud.com (Desktop Path)

If you are on a Windows PC, a non-Apple browser, or simply prefer the web interface:

1. Go to **icloud.com** and sign in with your Apple ID
2. Click your name or the account icon in the top-right corner
3. Click **Hide My Email** from the menu
4. Click **Create New Address**
5. Follow the same Label and Forward-To steps as above

> *Screenshot placeholder: icloud.com showing the Hide My Email section under Account settings, with the Create New Address button visible.*

### Step 7 — Manage and Deactivate Aliases

When an alias starts generating spam:

1. Go back to **Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > Hide My Email**
2. Find the alias by its label
3. Tap it, then tap **Deactivate Email Address**

Deactivated aliases stop forwarding immediately. The address stays in your list (so you remember it existed) but any email sent to it is silently dropped.

## Where iCloud Hide My Email Falls Short

Hide My Email is a well-built feature for what it covers. But there are real limitations worth knowing before you rely on it exclusively.

**Requires iCloud+.** The free iCloud tier does not include Hide My Email. If your subscription lapses, your aliases stop forwarding without warning.

**Apple-ecosystem only by design.** The alias creation flow is built into Apple's Settings and Safari. On an Android phone, on Chrome on Windows, or on any non-Apple browser, you cannot easily create a new Hide My [Email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-generator-2026/) on the fly. You have to go to icloud.com or pull out an Apple device.

**`@icloud.com` domain is sometimes flagged.** Some websites run checks on email domains and flag or reject known Apple alias addresses. Not common, but it happens often enough to be an occasional nuisance.

**No alternative identity details.** Hide My Email gives you a masked address, nothing more. When a site also asks for your name or date of birth, iCloud does not offer a way to generate alternative personal details the way some privacy tools do.

## Troubleshooting Hide My Email

### Forwarded mail is landing in spam

Your mail client sometimes flags forwarded mail because the sending domain differs from the original sender. Open the message, mark it **Not Junk** or **Not Spam**, and add the forwarding address to your contacts. This trains your mail client to trust the source.

### I do not see the Hide My Email option

Confirm your iCloud+ subscription is active under **Settings > [Your Name] > Subscriptions**. If the subscription is paused or lapsed, Hide My Email will not appear. Also confirm you are running iOS 15 or later.

### A website rejected my iCloud alias

Some sites screen for known Apple alias domains. If `@icloud.com` gets rejected, try creating a new Alt-ID alias instead — the forwarding domain is different and clears most site-side filters.

### I cannot find which alias goes where

This is a labelling problem. Open Hide My Email, tap each alias, and add a descriptive label (you can edit labels after creation). Going forward, label every new alias the moment you create it before you paste it anywhere.

### My alias disappeared after I got a new device

Aliases are linked to your Apple ID, not your device. Sign in to the same Apple ID on your new device and your aliases will reappear in **Settings > iCloud > Hide My Email** automatically.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does creating an iCloud [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-outlook-2026/) cost anything?

Hide My Email requires an **iCloud+** subscription, which starts at the entry-level paid storage tier (currently 50 GB). If you already pay for iCloud storage, you have access to Hide My Email at no additional cost. The free iCloud tier does not include the feature.

### How many iCloud [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-gmail-2026/)es can I create?

Apple does not publish a fixed cap for Hide My [Email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-sign-2026/)es. In practice, users report creating dozens without hitting a limit. Create as many as you need — one per service is a sensible habit.

### Can I use an iCloud alias to reply to emails?

On Apple devices, yes. If an email arrives via your alias, you can reply from the same alias address using Apple Mail — the alias appears in the From field. On non-Apple mail clients, replies will come from your primary address unless you configure the alias in your email client's settings manually.

### What happens to my iCloud aliases if I cancel iCloud+?

Alias forwarding stops when iCloud+ lapses. Emails sent to your aliases during a lapsed period are not delivered and are not queued for later — they are dropped. Before cancelling, note your active aliases and update those accounts with a new address.

### Is Surfshark Alt-ID better than iCloud Hide My Email?

It depends on your setup. If you use only Apple devices and have iCloud+, Hide My Email is a clean built-in solution. If you use Android, Windows, or Chrome alongside Apple devices, Alt-ID's cross-platform support makes it more practical. Alt-ID also generates alternative personal details (name, date of birth) that Hide My Email does not. [See the current Alt-ID offer here.](/blog/altid?sub=how-to-create-email-alias-icloud-2026__faq)

### Can I use iCloud Hide My Email on Android?

You can access icloud.com on an Android browser and manage existing aliases, but the seamless in-app creation flow — where an alias pops up automatically in a sign-up form — is Apple-ecosystem only. For Android-native alias support, Alt-ID is the more practical choice.

### Do iCloud email aliases expire automatically?

No. iCloud Hide My Email aliases are permanent until you manually deactivate or delete them. Deactivated aliases stop forwarding but remain in your list. Deleted aliases cannot be recovered.
