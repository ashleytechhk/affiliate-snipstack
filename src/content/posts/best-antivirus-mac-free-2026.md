## Step 1 — Turn On Apple's Built-In Protection

Apple quietly runs two security tools in the background on every Mac. Most people never check whether they are switched on. Let us make sure yours are active.

**1.1 — Enable XProtect and Gatekeeper**

These tools scan files you download and block software from unknown sources. They update automatically and cost nothing.

1. Click the Apple logo (top-left corner of your screen).
2. Click **System Settings** (or **System Preferences** on older Macs).
3. Scroll down and click **Privacy & Security**.
4. Under **Security**, look for the line that says "Allow applications downloaded from."
5. Make sure **App Store and identified developers** is selected — not "Anywhere."

That single change blocks a wide category of dangerous software before it ever opens.

**1.2 — Turn On the Firewall**

The firewall stops unwanted network traffic from reaching your Mac.

1. Stay in **Privacy & Security**.
2. Click the **Firewall** tab.
3. If the firewall shows "Off," click **Turn On Firewall**.

You are done with Step 1. No restart needed.

## Step 5 — Keep Everything Updated

Outdated software is the number-one way attackers get into Macs. Updates patch the holes before anyone can use them.

**5.1 — Update macOS automatically**

1. Click the Apple logo, then **System Settings**.
2. Click **General**, then **Software Update**.
3. Turn on **Automatic Updates**.
4. Make sure all four sub-options are ticked (Download, Install, Security Responses, App Updates).

**5.2 — Update your apps**

1. Open the **App Store** from your Dock or Applications folder.
2. Click **Updates** in the left sidebar.
3. Click **Update All**.

Repeat this once a month if you want to be thorough, or just leave automatic updates on and let your Mac handle it.
