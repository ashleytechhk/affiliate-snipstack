## Method 1 — Real Email + Unsubscribe

**How it works:** Give your real address, click unsubscribe when spam starts.

**Why it fails for hotel programs:** Each partner sender requires a separate unsubscribe. Marriott Bonvoy, Marriott Vacations, Ritz-Carlton Rewards, and a co-branded Chase credit card are all technically separate senders. Unsubscribing from one does not remove you from the others. Hotel program terms allow re-opt-in when you stay at a property — so the next check-in reactivates the cycle.

**Verdict:** Only works if you never actually stay at a hotel again.

---

## Method 2 — Gmail `+tag` Alias

**How it works:** Sign up with `yourname+marriott@gmail.com`. Gmail delivers mail to your normal inbox. You can filter it by the tag.

**Why it fails for hotel programs:** Sophisticated email marketers — and hotel programs are exactly that — routinely strip everything after the `+` before storing the address. Your address becomes `yourname@gmail.com` in their database. When they share with a partner, they share the stripped version. Your tag is gone and you have no way to trace which program leaked to which partner.

**Verdict:** Useful for filtering, useless for privacy or traceability with hotel loyalty programs.

---

## Method 3 — Throwaway Address (10MinuteMail, Guerrilla Mail)

**How it works:** Use a temporary inbox for the sign-up confirmation, then let it expire.

**Why it fails for hotel programs:** You actually need to receive email from these programs — booking confirmations, stay receipts, points balance updates, redemption codes. A dead address means losing access to your own loyalty account. Throwaway addresses solve the spam problem by also blocking the legitimate mail.

**Verdict:** Works for zero-commitment sign-ups. Not viable for a loyalty program you intend to use.

## Method 4 — iCloud Hide My Email / SimpleLogin / Firefox Relay

**How it works:** These services generate persistent forwarding aliases. iCloud Hide My Email is built into Apple ID ($0.99/mo iCloud+ plan). SimpleLogin has a free tier. Firefox Relay offers five free aliases.

**Why it partially works:** These are real, sustained forwarding aliases. They do handle hotel programs better than the previous methods — they stay alive, they forward, and you can delete them.

**Limitations:**
- iCloud Hide My Email is Apple-only; Android users need a workaround
- SimpleLogin's free tier caps aliases at 10
- Firefox Relay's free tier limits you to 5 aliases and blocks reply-from-alias
- None of these bundle additional privacy features (VPN, identity masking) — you are paying separately for each tool

**Verdict:** Solid standalone option. Best fit if you are already in the Apple ecosystem and do not need VPN coverage.

## FAQ

**Can I use a masked alias for a hotel loyalty account I already have?**

Yes. Log into your existing loyalty account, go to profile settings, and update the email address to a new alias. The program will send a verification email to the alias, which forwards to your real inbox. Confirm it, and the switch is done. Your existing points and status carry over.

**Will hotel loyalty programs reject masked email addresses?**

Most do not. Hotel programs accept any valid-format email address at registration. The `@surfshark.id` domain used by [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=hide-email-from-hotel-loyalty-programs-2026__faq) is not on the public blocklists that target common throwaway services like 10MinuteMail. In the rare case a program rejects it, try a different alias domain if Alt-ID offers one, or contact the program's support to whitelist the address.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my points if I delete the alias and lose access to the account?**

Do not delete the alias before updating the account email. The correct sequence is: create a new alias, update the account to the new alias, confirm it works, then delete the old alias if it was a different address. Your points sit on the account, not the email address — changing email does not affect your balance or status.

**Does this prevent hotel programs from sharing my data with partners?**

Hiding your email address prevents partners from reaching you by email. It does not stop the loyalty program from sharing your profile data with partners — that is a separate data-rights issue governed by privacy law in your jurisdiction. For email spam specifically, a masked alias is the most effective tool available to individuals right now.

Alt-ID is not sold as a standalone product — it comes bundled with Surfshark. If you are already a Surfshark subscriber, you have it at no extra cost. **How many loyalty program aliases should I create?**
