## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect to one, your device creates an encrypted tunnel to a server operated by the VPN provider. Your internet traffic travels through that tunnel before reaching any website or streaming service.

The result is three simultaneous changes to how the internet sees you:

- **Your IP address changes.** Websites see the VPN server's IP, not yours. If the server is in Turkey, you appear to be browsing from Turkey.
- **Your traffic is encrypted.** Anyone on your local network — your ISP, a coffee shop router, a hotel Wi-Fi system — sees scrambled data instead of your actual activity.
- **Your apparent location shifts.** Streaming services, pricing engines, and geo-restriction systems all use IP address to determine where you are. The VPN moves that marker to wherever your chosen server sits.

These three effects work together. Privacy and savings come from the same mechanism.
