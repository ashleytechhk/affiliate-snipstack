## TextNow: Free, Functional, Limited

![Flat-style illustration of a smartphone showing a chat screen with a TextNow logo and a privacy shield icon blocking an incoming message request](/og/textnow-vs-burner-app-comparison-2026-inline-1.png)

TextNow is a free VoIP-based calling and messaging app. You get a real US phone number at no cost — it's subsidized by in-app ads. You can make calls and send texts from it just like a regular number.

### What TextNow Does Well

- **Free.** No subscription, no upfront cost. You get a working number immediately after download.
- **Functional for basic texting.** Most normal SMS and call scenarios work fine.
- **Ad-supported, not subscription-gated.** If you just need a number occasionally, the free tier is usable.

### Where TextNow Falls Short for Dating

**US and Canada only.** You can only get a North American number. If you are traveling, relocating, or prefer a number from another region, TextNow cannot help you.

**Heavy detection risk.** TextNow numbers are widely known VoIP numbers. Tinder, Bumble, and Hinge use carrier-detection layers on SMS verification. TextNow numbers fail this check more often than not — some users report being blocked from verification entirely, or getting an error that the number "cannot be used for verification." You may not be able to create or verify your account with a TextNow number.

**Ad exposure.** To keep the number free, TextNow runs ads throughout the app. For a privacy-focused use case, routing your dating communications through an ad-supported environment has an irony to it.

**Account tied to your details.** TextNow requires an email address to register, and the account links back to your identity. It is less anonymous than it first appears.

**Number recycling.** TextNow reclaims inactive numbers and reassigns them to other users. If you stop using the number, someone else may end up with it.

**Verdict on TextNow:** Good for occasional use when you need a free temporary number and detection risk is not a concern. Not reliable for consistent dating app use where SMS verification is required.

## Burner App: Better Concept, Real Restrictions

The Burner app is built specifically for the use case of disposable numbers. You get a temporary number, use it for however long you need it, then "burn" it — delete it and replace it with a new one. The concept is sound.

### What Burner Does Well

- **Purpose-built for disposable numbers.** The workflow is designed around the idea of using and discarding numbers, which maps directly onto online dating scenarios.
- **Easy to replace.** Burning a number and getting a new one is straightforward inside the app.
- **Call and text both work.** Unlike some VoIP apps that only handle texts, Burner supports voice calls too.
- **More reliable than free VoIP options** on some platforms, because Burner has made more effort on number quality than free services have.

### Where Burner Falls Short

**Paid subscription required.** Burner is not free. The standard plan runs around $4.99 per month for one line, with higher tiers for multiple numbers. For occasional dating use, the recurring cost adds up fast.

**US and Canada only.** Like TextNow, [Burner number](https://snipstack.io/burner-number-for-online-classified-ads-2026/)s are limited to North America. International dating, traveling while using apps, or just preferring a non-US number are all off the table.

**VoIP detection still applies.** [Burner number](https://snipstack.io/burner-number-for-facebook-marketplace-2026/)s are better than TextNow's in terms of detection rate, but they are not immune. High-verification platforms can still flag and reject [Burner number](https://snipstack.io/burner-number-for-offerup-listings-2026/)s during SMS confirmation. The success rate varies by platform and over time as detection systems update.

**Number availability is limited.** Depending on your area code preference, the specific number you want may not be available. Popular area codes run thin.

**Verdict on Burner:** A meaningful step up from TextNow for paid, recurring use. Still subject to geographic and detection limitations that matter when dating apps tighten their verification systems.
