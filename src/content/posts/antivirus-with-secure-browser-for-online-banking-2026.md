## Why Online Banking Logins Are a Prime Target

Think about how much sits behind one login screen: your checking account, your savings, sometimes your entire retirement fund. Criminals know this, which is why fake banking pages are one of the most common scams reported to consumer protection agencies every year.

Here is how it usually works. A scammer sends an email or text that looks like it came from your bank. It says there is a problem with your account and asks you to click a link to "verify" your information. The link leads to a page that looks identical to your real bank's website — same logo, same colors, same layout. You type in your username and password, and the criminal now has both.

Older adults are targeted more often for a few clear reasons:

- Retirement savings mean there is real money to steal
- Trust in official-looking emails and phone calls runs high
- Passwords are often reused across several accounts and years
- Devices may be running older software without the latest protections

None of this means you did something wrong. It means the scam was built to fool exactly the kind of trust that most people naturally have. That is precisely why a secure browser layer matters — it catches the fake page even when the email looks completely convincing.

Your everyday web browser — Chrome, Safari, Edge — was not designed with banking fraud in mind. It treats a bank login page the same way it treats any other website. That is exactly the gap criminals exploit.

### It Checks the Site Before You Log In

Before your login page even loads, the secure browser checks it against a database of known banking sites and fraud reports. If something is off — the wrong address, a copycat domain, a page flagged as suspicious — you get a warning before you type a single character.

### It Blocks Keyloggers and Screen Capture

Some malware works by silently recording every key you press or taking screenshots of your screen while you bank. A secure browser blocks these attempts specifically during banking sessions, so even if something malicious is hiding on your device, it cannot see what you type or view your screen.

### It Isolates Your Session

The secure browser runs your banking session in its own protected space, separate from other tabs, extensions, and programs running on your device. This means a bad browser extension or another compromised tab cannot reach into your banking session and grab your information.
