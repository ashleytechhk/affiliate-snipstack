## Why Your Phone Number Is the Biggest Dating App Risk Nobody Talks About

Most online dating advice focuses on obvious threats: catfishing, financial scams, fake profiles. Fewer people talk about the quieter danger — what happens after a match seems legitimate and you start texting.

The moment you share your real mobile number, you give someone the ability to:

**Reverse-lookup your address.** People-finder sites like BeenVerified, Spokeo, and Whitepages index phone numbers alongside home addresses. A motivated person can pay a few dollars and get your street address within minutes of receiving your number.

**Link your number to your other accounts.** Most social platforms — Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, LinkedIn — let users search by phone number unless you manually disable it. Many people never do. Your dating match types your number into the search bar and suddenly sees your full name, employer, and every photo you have ever posted publicly.

**Run SIM-swap attacks.** This is more technical but increasingly common. A bad actor who knows your number, carrier, and a few personal details can contact your carrier and impersonate you to port your number to a new SIM. Once they control your number, they own any account that uses SMS-based two-factor authentication — including your bank.

**Track you through WhatsApp last-seen metadata.** Even if you share nothing else, your WhatsApp "last seen" timestamp tells someone your daily routine. Leave for work at 8am every day? They can see that pattern.

The tragedy is that none of this requires hacking. It requires only your phone number and a few hours.

## The Bigger Picture: Layered Privacy for Online Dating

A virtual number is one layer. Used alone it handles the phone number risk well. But the threat model for online dating is broader, and it is worth understanding what else belongs in a solid privacy setup.

**Use a separate email address for dating apps.** Do not use the same email you use for work or banking. A match who knows your email can search for it across data breach databases and see what else you have registered with it.

**Do not use Facebook login.** Connecting your dating profile to Facebook creates a visible link between your dating activity and your social graph. Use email signup instead.

**Turn off photo location metadata.** Photos taken on modern smartphones embed GPS coordinates in the file metadata. Most dating apps strip this when they process uploads, but not all do. Disable location metadata in your phone camera settings as a baseline habit.

**Be slow with full name sharing.** First name only in the app. Wait until you have met in person and feel confident before sharing your surname.

The Alt Number slot into this stack as the phone-number layer — arguably the most critical one, because it is the identifier most likely to be requested early and to cause the most downstream exposure if misused.
