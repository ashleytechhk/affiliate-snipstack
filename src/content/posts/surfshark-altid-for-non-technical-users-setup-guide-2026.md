## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Alt-ID work with Gmail, Outlook, or any email provider?

Yes. Alt-ID forwards to whatever real email address you specify during setup. It works with Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, ProtonMail, and any other inbox. You set the forwarding destination once in the dashboard and it applies to all your aliases.

### Can I reply to emails from my alias address?

Currently, Alt-ID is primarily designed for receiving mail rather than two-way alias replies. For most spam-protection use cases — sign-up forms, newsletters, shopping confirmations — this covers everything you need. If you need full two-way alias replies, tools like SimpleLogin Pro support that feature.

### Will the alias look suspicious to websites?

No. The alias looks like a standard email address. Sites cannot easily detect that it is an alias unless they specifically check against known alias providers. For the vast majority of sign-up forms, coupon sites, and free trials, it works exactly like a real address.

### [What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to emails sent to a deleted alias?

Once you delete an alias in the Alt-ID dashboard, any emails sent to that address are rejected automatically. They never reach your real inbox. This is what makes it effective against spam — you are not just filtering junk, you are eliminating the target.
