## Why Free VoIP Numbers Fail Dating App Verification

Most dating apps use third-party number verification services — Twilio Verify, Telesign, and similar — that maintain blocklists of VoIP number ranges. When you enter a VoIP number, the verification service checks it against these ranges before sending an SMS code. If the number matches a known VoIP range, the code is never sent.

This is why Google Voice and TextNow fail at signup on Tinder and Hinge even when the number looks legitimate to you. The check happens before you see anything — no error message, no explanation, just silence.

Carrier-backed numbers — the kind Alt-Number provisions — are not in these blocklists because they are allocated from the same carrier pools as physical SIM numbers. The check passes, the code arrives, and the signup completes.
