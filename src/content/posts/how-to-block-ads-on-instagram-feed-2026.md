## What Are Instagram Feed Ads?

Instagram feed ads are paid promotional posts that Meta (Instagram's parent company) inserts into your home feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore tab. Advertisers target you based on your activity, interests, location, and data shared across Meta's ecosystem.

By 2026, Meta earns the vast majority of its revenue from advertising — which means the ad load is by design, not by accident. Instagram has no paid ad-free tier (at least not one available globally at a fair price), so the default experience for most users is a feed stuffed with sponsored content.

## How Network-Level Ad Blocking Works

Think of it like this: your phone sends out hundreds of tiny requests per second — some fetch your friend's photo, others ping ad servers to load a sponsored post. A network-level blocker sits between your phone and the internet, reads those request destinations, and silently drops the ones that match known ad-serving domains.

![Step-by-step: how CleanWeb blocks Instagram ads at the network layer](/og/how-to-block-ads-on-instagram-feed-2026-inline-1.png)

The result: the ad request never reaches Meta's ad server. Instagram sees a failed load and either shows a blank space (briefly) or skips to the next piece of organic content. You never see the ad.

There are two common implementations:

**DNS-based blocking** — Your DNS resolver refuses to resolve ad-serving domain names. It's lightweight and free (e.g., NextDNS, Pi-hole), but setup can be technical and it doesn't encrypt your traffic.

**VPN-based blocking** — A [VPN app on](https://snipstack.io/vpn-app-on-android-2026/) your phone routes traffic through a server that also strips ad requests. This combines privacy (encrypted connection) with ad filtering in a single toggle. No router configuration, no advanced setup — just install, connect, and done.

Surfshark's **CleanWeb** uses the VPN-based approach, which makes it the most beginner-friendly option for mobile users.

## Options for Blocking Instagram Ads on Mobile in 2026

Here's a practical comparison of the main approaches:

### Option 1: Instagram's Own Controls (Limited)

Instagram lets you tap the three dots on any ad and select "Hide ad." You can also mark ads as "not relevant." This doesn't block ads — it only tells the algorithm you disliked one particular ad. More ads follow immediately.

**Verdict:** Useless for actually reducing ad volume.

### Option 2: DNS Ad Blocking (Pi-hole / NextDNS)

Pi-hole requires a home server (Raspberry Pi or similar) acting as your local DNS resolver. NextDNS offers a cloud version with a free tier. Both can block a significant portion of Instagram's ad traffic.

**Pros:** Free or very cheap. Works across all devices on your network.  
**Cons:** Pi-hole requires technical setup. NextDNS works on mobile but needs a profile install. Neither encrypts your traffic. Effectiveness against Instagram's ad domains fluctuates — Meta rotates domain names to evade blockers.

### Option 3: Rooted Android + Ad-Blocking App

On a rooted Android device, apps like AdAway can block system-wide at the hosts file level. This is highly effective but voids warranties, breaks some banking apps, and is overkill for most users.
