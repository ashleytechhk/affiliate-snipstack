## Why People Are Ditching Norton and McAfee

Norton and McAfee are not bad products — but they have a packaging problem. Both companies built their businesses in an era when antivirus was a standalone purchase, and they have never fully shed that legacy.

Here is what users report most often:

- **Aggressive upsells.** Every login surfaces another tier upgrade or add-on pitch.
- **Bloatware installs.** Browser extensions, toolbars, and "optimizer" tools arrive alongside the core security scan.
- **Confusing renewal pricing.** First-year discounts sometimes drop to a quarter of the renewal rate, catching users off-guard.
- **The VPN is an afterthought.** Norton's bundled VPN has a data cap on some plans. McAfee's VPN is usable but lags behind dedicated VPN providers on server count and speeds.

None of this means the malware detection itself is weak — both have solid lab scores. But for a user who just wants clean, modern protection across their devices without a monthly settings battle, the bundled alternatives have gotten strong enough to make switching worth it.

## What to Look for in a Cybersecurity Subscription with VPN and Antivirus

Before the comparison table, here are the five criteria that actually matter:

**1. Antivirus coverage quality.** Does it catch modern threats — ransomware, phishing pages, zero-day exploits — not just signature-based malware from 2018?

**2. VPN performance.** Speed matters. A VPN that drops your connection from 300 Mbps to 40 Mbps is not usable for streaming or video calls.

**3. Device limits.** Some plans cap you at 5 devices. If you have a laptop, phone, tablet, and a partner's phone, you are already at the edge.

**4. Extra features.** Data breach monitoring, ad blocking, private search, password managers — are they genuinely useful or just checklist items?

**5. Renewal pricing transparency.** Does the second-year price stay manageable, or does it jump to three times the introductory rate?
