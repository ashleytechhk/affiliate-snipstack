## Why Encryption Matters for [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-car-dealership-contact-2026/)es

Standard email travels as plaintext across servers. TLS (Transport Layer Security) encrypts the connection between servers so the message cannot be read in transit — this is table stakes for any reputable service, and every tool on this list has it.

Where the tools diverge is **at-rest and end-to-end encryption**. If a service stores a copy of forwarded messages on its servers, and those servers are breached, your message content is exposed. End-to-end encrypted services like ProtonMail mean even the provider cannot read your messages. PGP-forwarding options like AnonAddy encrypt the message before it leaves the alias service, so only your recipient's key can decrypt it.

For the spam-exhausted inbox owner, this nuance matters less than you might think. Most spam and marketing email contains no sensitive content worth encrypting. The threat you are defending against is:

1. **Identity exposure** — your real name, address, or email leaking from a signup form
2. **Inbox overload** — commercial spam you cannot stop once your address is circulating
3. **Targeted phishing** — personalised attacks based on which services you use

All three are addressed more effectively by **not sharing your real address in the first place** than by encrypting the mail after the fact. This is where Alt-ID's identity generation feature is genuinely differentiated — it reduces the plaintext PII you hand over, not just the address.

## AnonAddy (Addy.io) — Best for PGP-Encrypted Forwarding

AnonAddy, rebranded as Addy.io, is the most technically capable alias tool for users who run GPG keys. The optional PGP encryption feature encrypts forwarded messages before they leave the AnonAddy servers — only your key can decrypt the content at the other end. This is genuine end-to-end protection for forwarded mail, not just TLS-in-transit.

The free tier is generous: unlimited aliases with a bandwidth cap. Paid plans start at $1/month for unlimited bandwidth, custom domains, and access to the full catch-all alias feature. The catch-all lets any email sent to `anythinghere@yoursubdomain.addy.io` land in your inbox automatically — useful for tracking which service leaked your alias without creating addresses in advance.

The usability ceiling is real. Configuring PGP requires a working key pair, a compatible mail client, and a willingness to manage key rotation. Most spam-exhausted users will never configure it. The interface is also more technical than Alt-ID or SimpleLogin.

**Who it is for:** Developers, journalists, and technically inclined users who actively use GPG and want maximum encryption on forwarded mail.

## SimpleLogin — Best Open-Source Option

SimpleLogin is open-source, audited, and since its acquisition by Proton, tightly integrated into the Proton ecosystem. If you use ProtonMail as your primary inbox, SimpleLogin aliases benefit from Proton's end-to-end encryption on the receiving end — the forwarded mail lands in an encrypted inbox.

The browser extension makes alias creation one-click during signups. The free tier covers 10 aliases. Paid plans add unlimited aliases, custom domain support, and auto-create rules.

SimpleLogin does not offer identity generation. It handles the [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-twitch-account-privacy-2026/), nothing else. If a signup form also asks for your name and date of birth, you are on your own — or you need to combine it with a separate identity generator.

**Who it is for:** ProtonMail users who want alias creation woven into their existing workflow.

## Apple Hide My Email — Best for iPhone-First Users

Apple's Hide My Email, included with iCloud+ from $0.99/month, creates random `@privaterelay.appleid.com` aliases inside Safari and supported apps. When you tap "Sign in with Apple," an alias is generated automatically before you have a chance to hand over your real address.

Encryption is TLS-in-transit standard. There is no PGP or E2EE forwarding. The privacy story is alias isolation — your real Apple ID email is never shared with the third-party app. Apple does not monetise mail content, which adds a layer of trust.

The limitation is ecosystem lock-in. On Android or Windows, this feature becomes awkward. Non-Apple browser support is minimal. If your life runs across multiple platforms, the convenience advantage disappears.

**Who it is for:** iPhone and Mac users already paying for iCloud+ who want zero-friction alias creation within Apple apps.

## FAQ

### What does "email alias with encryption support" actually mean?

It means the alias service encrypts data in some form — at minimum, TLS encryption of the forwarding connection between servers. Some tools, like AnonAddy, go further with optional PGP-encrypted forwarding where the message content is encrypted before leaving the alias service. Others, like ProtonMail, offer end-to-end encryption so no party except the intended recipient can read the message.

### Is Alt-ID encrypted?

Alt-ID forwards mail over TLS-encrypted connections, which is the standard for serious mail services. It does not offer PGP forwarding or a built-in end-to-end encrypted inbox. For most spam-prevention use cases, TLS-in-transit combined with not sharing your real address offers strong practical protection. If you need encrypted message content for sensitive correspondence, pair Alt-ID with a ProtonMail inbox as your receiving address.

### Can I use an email alias with encryption support to reply without revealing my real address?

Yes, with most tools on this list. [Alt-ID](https://snipstack.io/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-with-encryption-support-2026__faq), SimpleLogin, and AnonAddy all support reply-through-alias, meaning outgoing replies appear to come from the alias address, not your real inbox. The recipient never sees your actual email.

### Do email aliases protect against phishing?

They reduce exposure significantly. If each service gets a unique alias and a phishing attempt arrives on that alias, you immediately know which service was targeted or breached. You can kill the alias before the attacker can use it for further attempts. What an alias cannot do is stop a phishing email that has already landed — treat suspicious links with the same caution regardless.

### What is the difference between an email alias and a disposable address?

A disposable address is temporary — it expires after a set time or number of uses. An email alias is persistent: it stays active indefinitely until you delete it, and it forwards to your real inbox. For the spam-management strategy of one alias per service, you want persistent aliases you control, not throwaway addresses that expire mid-conversation.

### Is free email alias software good enough?

Free tiers — AnonAddy, SimpleLogin, Firefox Relay — are usable entry points. The limits kick in once you want unlimited aliases, custom domains, or GPG features. For a complete solution including identity generation and breach monitoring, Alt-ID's paid bundle is worth the cost over the free-tier alternatives.
