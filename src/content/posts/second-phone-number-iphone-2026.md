## Method 2: Use iPhone's Built-In Dual-SIM with an eSIM

If you want a fully separate carrier line — with its own voicemail, its own carrier plan, and its own iMessage account — iPhone's dual-SIM feature gives you that. This requires an eSIM-capable iPhone (iPhone XS or newer) and a carrier that offers eSIM activation.

### Step 1: Confirm eSIM Support

Go to **Settings → General → About** and scroll to find **Available SIM** or **eSIM**. If you see eSIM listed, your iPhone supports it. If not, skip to Method 1.

### Step 2: Get an eSIM Plan from a Carrier

Contact a carrier in your region and request an eSIM plan. Many carriers offer prepaid eSIM plans specifically for people who want a secondary number — these are often cheaper than adding a line to an existing plan and do not require a new contract. Some carriers let you activate an eSIM entirely online in minutes.

You will receive a QR code or activation code from the carrier.

### Step 3: Add the eSIM to Your iPhone

Go to **Settings → Mobile Service** (or **Cellular**) and tap **Add eSIM**. Choose **Use QR Code** and scan the code from your carrier, or enter the activation code manually if your carrier provided one instead. Follow the on-screen prompts. The new line activates within a few minutes.

### Step 4: Label Your Lines

Once both SIMs are active, iOS will ask you to label them. Give your new line a name like **Dating** or **Personal 2** and keep your existing line labeled **Primary**. This label appears when you make calls, so you always know which number you are calling from.

Go to **Settings → Mobile Service** and configure the following:
- **Default Voice Line**: set to your primary number for regular calls
- **iMessage and FaceTime**: confirm only your primary number is enabled for iMessage and FaceTime, unless you specifically want your second number linked there too
- **Allow Mobile Data Switching**: turn this off to prevent iOS from silently switching data lines

### Step 5: Make a Call or Send a Text from Your Dating Number

When composing a new message or making a call, iOS will show a line selector. Tap the line label to switch to your dating number before sending. Your second number appears as the caller ID on the recipient's device.

## Troubleshooting

**The Alt-Number section is not showing in the Surfshark app.**
Make sure you are subscribed to Surfshark One, not just the base VPN plan. Open the app, go to Account, and check your active plan. If Alt-Number is not listed, upgrade from within the app.

**My eSIM activation code is not working.**
eSIM codes expire. If more than 24–48 hours have passed since your carrier sent the code, contact them for a fresh one. Also confirm you are entering the code exactly as provided — some carriers use capital letters and hyphens that are easy to misread.

**iOS is making calls from the wrong number.**
Go to **Settings → Mobile Service → Default Voice Line** and confirm the correct line is selected. If you have Allow Mobile Data Switching on, iOS may have changed your active line automatically — disable that setting.

**Incoming texts to my Alt-Number are not appearing.**
Check that the Surfshark app has notification permissions: **Settings → Surfshark → Notifications**. If notifications are off, incoming texts arrive silently. Also confirm you have not enabled Do Not Disturb or Focus Mode in a way that filters the Surfshark app.

**I am being asked for my real number to verify on a dating app.**
Dating apps require a number for initial signup, but that number does not have to be your personal number. Use your Alt-Number or eSIM line for verification. If the app rejects a VoIP number, Alt-Number will work where VoIP fails because it provides a real local number, not a VoIP alias.
