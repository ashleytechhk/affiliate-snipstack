## Why Seniors Are the Number One Target

Cybercriminals are not random. They target the people most likely to fall for their tactics, and they know that many older adults:

- Manage retirement savings and Social Security online
- Are less likely to question a suspicious-looking pop-up
- Often use older devices with outdated software
- Tend to trust caller ID and official-looking emails

But here is the detail that surprises most people: the real money is not in smash-and-grab attacks. Sophisticated scammers install **remote access tools** on a victim's device and monitor activity quietly for weeks. They watch for banking logins, account numbers, and wire transfer moments. By the time you notice anything wrong, they are already gone with your money.

This kind of attack does not leave a suspicious file on your desktop. It lives entirely in your network traffic — which is why file-scanning antivirus alone won't catch it.

## What "Network Traffic Monitoring" Actually Means (Plain English)

Think of your internet connection like a water pipe. Data — websites, emails, login details, banking pages — flows through that pipe constantly. Network traffic monitoring is like having a filter on the pipe that can inspect everything passing through and flag anything that looks dangerous.

Specifically, good network traffic monitoring will:

- **Detect connections to known malicious servers** — If spyware on your device is quietly "calling home" to a hacker's server, the monitor catches that outbound call and blocks it.
- **Flag unusual data leaving your device** — If something is trying to send your saved passwords out of your computer, that shows up as abnormal outbound traffic.
- **Warn you about unsecured connections** — Websites that do not use proper encryption are flagged before you enter any personal information.
- **Block phishing sites in real time** — When you click a suspicious link in an email, the traffic monitor intercepts the connection before the malicious page loads.

## The Threats It Protects You From

### Remote Access Scams

You have likely heard of the "Microsoft support" phone scam. Someone calls claiming your computer has a problem. They walk you through installing a small program so they can "fix" it remotely. That program is a remote access tool — and once it is on your device, they can see everything you do, including every website you visit and every password you type.

Network traffic monitoring detects the unusual outbound connections these tools make and shuts them down.

### Banking Trojans

A banking trojan is a type of malware that hides on your device and waits for you to log into your bank. The moment you enter your username and password, it copies them and sends them to an attacker. This happens faster than you can blink.

Real-time traffic monitoring detects and blocks that outbound data transmission the instant it starts.

### Fake Wi-Fi Hotspots

At a coffee shop or airport, scammers sometimes set up a Wi-Fi hotspot with a convincing name — "Airport Free WiFi" or "CoffeeshopGuest." When you connect, all your traffic passes through their device. They can read everything that is not encrypted.

Network traffic monitoring, combined with a secure connection, protects your data even on public networks.

### Adware and Spyware

Some "free" apps and browser extensions secretly track your behavior and send browsing data to advertisers — or worse, to identity thieves. These programs do not behave like traditional viruses. File-scanning antivirus often misses them entirely. But network monitoring catches their suspicious phone-home behavior immediately.
