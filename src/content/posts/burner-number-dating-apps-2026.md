## Why Dating Apps Create a Privacy Problem

Most dating apps verify your identity with your real phone number during signup. That's fine — they need to reduce fake accounts. The problem is what happens *after* you match with someone.

Moving from in-app chat to texting feels natural. It signals trust. But it also means handing a stranger your actual phone number — the same one tied to your bank, your employer, your family. If the vibe turns bad, you can block them, but they still have your number stored in their phone forever.

People who've dealt with persistent texters, unexpected call-blocking battles, or straight-up harassment know how unsettling this is. A burner number sidesteps all of it. You give out a number that's real enough to receive texts and calls — but one you control entirely.

**Verdict:** Decent for US-based users with light needs. Falls apart if you travel, if the app rejects VoIP numbers, or if you'd rather not funnel more data through Google.

## The National Do Not Call Registry: What It Actually Does

Since "national do not call registry" brings people here, let's clear this up properly.

The Do Not Call Registry is maintained by the FTC and lets you opt out of unsolicited telemarketing calls from legitimate businesses. Register your number at donotcall.gov and telemarketers are legally required to stop calling you (with some exceptions for charities, political organizations, and survey companies).

It is genuinely useful for one thing: reducing cold calls from companies trying to sell you things.

It does absolutely nothing for:
- Someone you willingly gave your number to
- Texts or calls from individuals (as opposed to businesses)
- Harassment from someone you matched with online
- Spam texts from unknown numbers (those senders are already breaking the law and don't care about registries)

The registry has no enforcement mechanism for personal contacts. If a Hinge match turns toxic, the FTC is not going to help you. A burner number is the actual solution — because you never gave them your real number in the first place.
