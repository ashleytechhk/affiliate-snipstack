## What Is a Temporary Email Address?

A temporary email address is a disposable inbox you use in place of your real one. You give it to a website; mail arrives there instead of your personal address; your real inbox stays clean.

There are two distinct types:

**1. Truly temporary (throwaway)** — No account needed. You visit a site, get a random address, check messages for a few minutes, and it disappears. Great for: verifying a sign-up you'll never use again, downloading a PDF behind a gate, redeeming a one-time promo code.

**2. Permanent masked email (alias)** — You create an alias that *forwards* to your real inbox. The sender never learns your actual address. You keep the alias as long as it's useful, then delete it. Spam stops the moment you kill the alias. Great for: shopping sites, newsletters, any service you actually want long-term.

Most people who Googled "temporary email" today actually need the second type — they just don't know it yet.

## Why "Just Unsubscribe" Doesn't Work

The standard advice is to click unsubscribe. In theory, unsubscribing removes you from a list. In practice:

- **Unsubscribe links confirm your address is active.** Some senders use clicks to verify which addresses are worth targeting.
- **They only control their own list.** If your address was sold to a third party, that party's list is unaffected.
- **It takes up to 10 business days to take effect** under CAN-SPAM. You're still getting email.
- **Not all senders comply.** Offshore spam operations ignore opt-out requests entirely.

A masked alias sidesteps all of this. You never had to trust anyone — you just delete the alias and the spam channel is closed.
