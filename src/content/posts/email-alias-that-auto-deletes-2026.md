## What Is an Email Alias?

An email alias is a forwarding address — a separate email address that sends everything it receives directly to your real inbox. When you sign up for a website, you give them the alias, not your actual address. To the website, the alias looks like any normal email. To you, the emails appear in your existing Gmail, Outlook, or Apple Mail as usual.

The critical property is control. Because the alias is not your real address, you can delete or deactivate it at any time. When you do, the channel is closed. Any sender — including every spammer who bought the list your alias ended up on — gets a permanent bounce. Your real inbox is untouched.

This is fundamentally different from an unsubscribe button. Unsubscribing removes you from one sender's list. Deleting an alias closes the address for every sender at once, including the ones you don't even know have it yet.

## How On-Demand Delete Aliases Work

The mechanics are straightforward. When you create an alias with a service like Alt ID, a new forwarding address is generated — something like `random-string@altid-domain`. That address is registered in the service's mail system and pointed at your real inbox.

Every email sent to the alias gets forwarded. You see it in your inbox as normal. The sender never sees your real address.

When spam starts arriving, you open the alias dashboard, find the alias that's receiving the junk, and deactivate or delete it. Deactivating pauses forwarding — the alias still exists but bounces incoming mail. Deleting removes the address entirely. Either way, the spam channel is closed immediately.

The reason this works better than filtering is scope. A spam filter blocks one sender's domain. Spammers rotate domains constantly, so filters age out fast. Deleting the alias blocks every current and future sender that has that address — regardless of what domain they're sending from.

## FAQ

**Does Alt ID actually auto-delete aliases on a timer?**

No — and that is a feature, not a gap. Alt ID aliases are permanent until you deactivate or delete them manually. This means your confirmation emails, receipts, and login-related messages all arrive normally. When you're ready to close the spam channel, you do it in one click. You control the timing; a clock does not.

**What is the difference between deactivating and deleting an alias in Alt ID?**

Deactivating pauses forwarding — the alias still exists and can be reactivated later. Deleting removes the address permanently. For most spam situations, deactivating is enough. Delete if you're certain you'll never need that alias again. [Alt ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-that-auto-deletes-2026__faq) lets you do either from the same dashboard.

**Can I use a timed throwaway address for a site I actually shop at regularly?**

You can try, but you'll lose access to order confirmations, shipping updates, and password resets once the address expires. For any service where you expect ongoing correspondence, use a persistent on-demand-delete alias instead. Reserve timed throwaways for one-click registrations you'll never return to.

**What happens when I delete an Alt ID alias — does the sender know?**

The sender gets a bounce or permanent delivery failure, the same response any mail server returns for a non-existent address. They receive no notification that an alias was deleted. From their side, it looks the same as if the address never existed.

**Does using an email alias protect me from all spam?**

It protects the email address itself from being burned. If a service leaks your alias, you can revoke it and the spam stops. It does not protect against spam that arrives before you deactivate, and it doesn't filter spam that comes through your real address from older exposures. For your existing real address, spam filters and Gmail's Promotions tab still do useful work. The alias model is the structural fix for all future signups.

**Is Alt ID the same as a completely disposable email address?**

Not exactly. A disposable address typically expires automatically and has no forwarding. An Alt ID alias is persistent and forwards to your real inbox — it behaves like a normal email address for legitimate purposes. The "disposable" quality is that you can destroy it instantly, on your schedule, rather than having it disappear on its own.
