## Why Your Android Phone Needs Antivirus Protection

You might have heard that Android phones "don't get viruses." That is only half true.

Android can be infected by:

- **Fake apps** — look like real banking or delivery apps but steal your login details
- **[Phishing link](https://snipstack.io/phishing-link-checker-free-2026/)s** — sent via SMS, WhatsApp, or email; tap one and your details can be stolen
- **Adware** — unwanted software that slows your phone and floods it with pop-up ads
- **Spyware** — secretly reads your texts or watches your camera

Seniors are frequently targeted because scammers assume older users are less likely to spot a fake. A good antivirus app watches your downloads, scans links, and alerts you before damage is done.

## Step 1 — Turn On Google Play Protect (It Is Already on Your Phone)

Play Protect is Google's built-in scanner. Many people do not know it exists. It scans every app you install and runs regular background checks.

**How to turn it on:**

1. Open the **Play Store** app (the colourful triangle icon).
2. Tap your **profile picture** (top-right corner).
3. Tap **Play Protect**.
4. Tap the **Settings gear** (top right).
5. Make sure **Scan apps with Play Protect** is switched ON (the toggle should be blue/green).
6. Tap **Scan** to run an immediate check.

If Play Protect finds a problem it will show a red warning and tell you exactly which app to remove.

*This costs nothing and requires no download.*

## Step 4 — Run Your First Scan

Once the app opens, it will usually ask permission to access your files. Allow this — the app needs it to scan for threats.

1. Look for a button that says **Scan**, **Smart Scan**, or **Run Full Scan**.
2. Tap it.
3. Wait. A first scan can take two to five minutes.
4. Review the results. If the app says "No threats found," your phone is clean. If it finds something, follow the on-screen steps to remove it.

Do not panic if the app also flags a setting like "unknown sources allowed" or "screen lock not set." These are warnings about your phone's security settings, not viruses. Fix them if you can.

## Step 5 — Set Up Ongoing Protection

A one-time scan is useful but not enough. You want the app watching your phone every day.

1. In your antivirus app, find **Settings** or **Protection**.

## Troubleshooting: Common Questions

**The app keeps asking me to upgrade. Can I ignore those?**
Yes. Tap the X or "Not now" button. The core scanning feature still works without upgrading.

**My phone is slow after installing antivirus. What do I do?**
Try Bitdefender Free — it is the lightest option and least likely to slow older phones. If slowness continues, uninstall via *Settings → Apps → [App name] → Uninstall* and try a different one.

**Can I have two antivirus apps running at once?**
No. Running two at the same time causes conflicts and slows your phone. Pick one and stick with it.

**Play Protect says my phone is clean but I still see strange pop-ups. Why?**
Pop-up ads often come from a browser extension or a misbehaving app — not a traditional virus. Try Malwarebytes for a deeper scan that catches adware.

**Someone texted me a link to download antivirus for free. Should I click it?**
No. This is almost certainly a scam. Only download apps from the Play Store.
