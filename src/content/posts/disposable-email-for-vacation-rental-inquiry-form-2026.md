## Why Vacation Rental Inquiry Forms Are a Spam Magnet

Not every sign-up form treats your data the same way. Rental inquiries sit in an unusually aggressive category, for a few clear reasons.

**1. Lead-gen business model.** Small property managers often run booking pages through third-party form tools that treat every inquiry as a captured lead, feeding it into marketing platforms whether or not a booking ever happens.

**2. Aggregator sprawl.** A single listing frequently appears across multiple comparison sites and price-alert trackers. One inquiry form can quietly end up syndicated to several secondary sites at once.

**3. Low unsubscribe compliance.** Large booking platforms tend to honour opt-outs; individual hosts and boutique agencies frequently don't have that infrastructure, so requests get ignored.

**4. Long-tail trail.** Every trip means a new round of inquiries across different rental sites, leaving your real email on file with dozens of unrelated hosts over the years — each one a point of potential breach exposure.

Put those together and the pattern is predictable: give a rental inquiry form your real email, and it's compromised within weeks.

### SimpleLogin (Free Tier) — Workable for a Single Trip

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service with a free tier capped at ten active aliases. It forwards reliably and lets you delete aliases on demand — fine for a single rental inquiry.

The ceiling shows up fast once you're comparing multiple listings for the same trip, since each ideally gets its own alias. It also doesn't generate fake identity details, so you'd still hand over your real name and [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-social-media-2026/) on the form.

**Best for:** Travellers already on SimpleLogin with alias slots to spare.

### Guerrilla Mail / Temp Mail — Wrong Tool for Booking Correspondence

Guerrilla Mail and similar temp-mail services hand you a working address instantly with zero sign-up — great for a single confirmation click, wrong for an ongoing booking.

You need to receive the host's reply, booking confirmation, check-in codes and Wi-Fi passwords, and any last-minute messages. Temp inboxes typically expire within about an hour of inactivity, so a reply that arrives a day later never reaches you, and the domain is often blocked by rental sign-up forms outright.

**Best for:** A single instant verification click. Not an ongoing booking conversation.

---

### Gmail + Addressing — Free but Transparent

Gmail's plus-addressing lets you append a tag: `yourname+rental@gmail.com` still delivers to `yourname@gmail.com`. It's free and works with Gmail's filters.

The catch: it doesn't hide your real address at all, since anyone can strip the tag to reveal it. Many marketing platforms do exactly that before storing the address, routing around your filters. There's no kill-switch either — you can't revoke an address that was never separate to begin with.

**Best for:** Light inbox organisation, not for keeping a host from ever seeing your real email.
