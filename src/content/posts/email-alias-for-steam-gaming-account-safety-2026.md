## SimpleLogin: Best Open-Source Alternative

SimpleLogin is a well-regarded open-source email alias service. It was acquired by Proton in 2022 and integrates with Proton Mail, which gives it strong privacy credibility.

The free tier allows 10 aliases — enough for casual use but limiting if you sign up for a lot of gaming platforms. Paid plans unlock unlimited aliases, custom domain support, and priority email delivery.

**What SimpleLogin does better than Alt-ID:** custom domain support and open-source transparency. If you want to audit the code or host the service yourself, SimpleLogin supports both. It also integrates cleanly with Proton Mail for a fully private email stack.

**What it lacks compared to Alt-ID:** no fake identity profile generation, no VPN bundle, and the interface is more technical. For a gamer who just wants a clean inbox without configuration overhead, it is a heavier lift.

## Apple Hide My Email: Tight but Narrow

Apple Hide My Email is built into iCloud+ subscriptions and Safari. It generates random Apple-managed aliases and forwards to your Apple ID email. The setup is seamless on Apple devices — it appears as an autofill option when signing up for services in Safari.

**The problem for gamers:** Steam's desktop client is not a Safari website. Third-party launchers, tournament platforms, and skin trading sites often run outside the browser contexts where Hide My Email integrates smoothly. You can copy-paste an alias manually, but the experience degrades outside the Apple ecosystem. If you use Windows as your primary gaming machine, Apple Hide My Email is a poor fit.

## DuckDuckGo Email Protection: Lightweight but Limited

DuckDuckGo's email privacy feature strips trackers from emails and forwards them to your real inbox via a @duck.com alias. It is free and browser-extension-based.

**The hard limitation:** you cannot reply from your DuckDuckGo alias. For Steam, this matters — account recovery emails, trade notifications, and support responses all need a two-way address. DuckDuckGo Email is better suited as a throwaway address for one-time signups, not as your primary gaming account email.

## How to Use an Email Alias Specifically for Steam

![Setting up an email alias on Steam account security settings](/og/email-alias-for-steam-gaming-account-safety-2026-inline-2.png)

Here is the practical setup flow for Steam:

1. **Generate your alias** in whichever tool you choose (Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, etc.)
2. **Log into Steam** and go to Account Details under your profile
3. **Change your contact email** to the alias address
4. **Confirm the change** — Steam will send a verification email to the alias, which forwards to your real inbox
5. **Verify** that you can receive the confirmation, then approve it
6. **Keep your real email** as the recovery option in your password manager, but not anywhere public

Once set up, your real email never touches Steam's servers again. If Steam's data is ever involved in a breach, attackers get your alias — not your primary inbox.

**Pro tip:** Create a separate alias for each major gaming platform. That way a breach on one platform does not contaminate your alias for others, and you can trace exactly which platform leaked your data when the spam starts.

## FAQ

**What is an email alias and how does it protect my Steam account?**
An email alias is a forwarding address that routes mail to your real inbox. You register it with Steam instead of your primary email. If that alias is exposed in a data breach, your real email address stays private — attackers cannot use it to target you elsewhere or attempt account takeovers via your inbox. [Learn more about Alt-ID here.](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-steam-gaming-account-safety-2026__faq)

**Can I use an email alias to recover a Steam account?**
Yes, as long as the alias is active and still forwarding to your real inbox. If your alias gets spammed and you delete it before removing it from Steam, account recovery emails will bounce. Best practice: before deleting a compromised alias, update Steam's contact email to a new alias first.

**Will Steam accept an email alias as an account email?**
Steam accepts any valid email address. It does not block alias domains. Both Alt-ID and SimpleLogin aliases work with Steam's email verification process. The alias receives the verification email, forwards it to your real inbox, and you confirm from there.

**Is Surfshark Alt-ID safe to use as a long-term Steam email?**
As long as your Surfshark subscription is active, your Alt-ID alias remains active and forwards mail. The main risk is if your subscription lapses — in that case the alias may stop forwarding. Keep your subscription current or have a plan to migrate your Steam email before any renewal gaps.

**How many aliases should I create for gaming?**
A sensible minimum is one per major platform: Steam, Epic Games Store, GOG, battle.net, and any launcher you use regularly. Add one for gaming-adjacent services like Discord, key reseller sites, and tournament platforms. Alt-ID makes alias generation fast enough that a per-service approach is practical.

**Does using an email alias make me completely anonymous?**
No. An alias hides your real email address, but your Steam account is still tied to your payment method, IP address history, and account activity. For stronger privacy, combine an email alias with a VPN. Surfshark bundles both, which is part of why it sits at the top of this comparison for gamers.
