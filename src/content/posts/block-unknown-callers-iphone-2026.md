## What Is "Block Unknown Callers" on iPhone?

"Block Unknown Callers" — officially labeled **Silence Unknown Callers** in iOS Settings — is a built-in iPhone feature that automatically silences incoming calls from phone numbers not saved in your contacts, Mail, or recent outgoing calls.

When it's active:
- Calls from unknown numbers go directly to voicemail
- Your iPhone does not ring or vibrate
- The call appears in your recent calls list, but it never interrupts you
- Known contacts ring through normally

It is not a true "block" — the caller can still leave a voicemail. Think of it as a noise gate, not a wall.

**Where to find it:**

Go to **Settings → Phone → Silence Unknown Callers** and toggle it on. That's it. No app download, no account needed.

## How "Silence Unknown Callers" Actually Works

Understanding the mechanics helps you set realistic expectations.

iOS matches incoming calls against three sources before deciding to ring or silence:

1. **Your Contacts app** — anyone stored there rings through
2. **Your Mail app** — email addresses associated with phone numbers in recent messages
3. **Recent outgoing calls** — anyone you called in the past few days

A number that appears in none of those sources gets silenced and routed to voicemail.

**What it catches well:**
- Spam robocalls
- Telemarketing calls
- Random numbers you've never interacted with

**What it misses:**
- Anyone you've already given your number to (they're not "unknown")
- Anyone calling from a number you recognize (even if you'd prefer not to hear from them)
- Anyone who calls from multiple numbers to bypass the silence

## What About Blocking Individual Numbers?

If you need to block a specific person who already has your number, iPhone's individual block feature is straightforward:

- Open **Phone** app → **Recents**
- Tap the info icon (i) next to the number
- Scroll down and tap **Block this Caller**

Or from a text message: open the conversation → tap the contact name at the top → **Block this Caller**.

**The limitation:** blocking doesn't stop someone from contacting you from a different number. Anyone determined to reach you can call from a new number and your block list won't help. This is why the second-number approach is more robust — there's no connection to your real number to begin with.
