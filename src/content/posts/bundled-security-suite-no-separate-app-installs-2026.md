## Why "No Separate App Installs" Actually Matters

This is not a cosmetic preference. Every additional installer in a security stack is a surface for:

- **Conflicting real-time scan engines** — [Norton Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/norton-antivirus-software-2026/) and [Windows Defender](https://snipstack.io/windows-defender-download-2026/) will fight. So will two VPN clients.
- **Redundant background processes** — four security apps means four update daemons, four notification channels, and four things slowing your cold boot.
- **Credential sprawl** — separate app = separate account = another email and password to manage (or reuse, which defeats the point).
- **Renewal chaos** — products on different billing cycles means you will inevitably let one lapse.

A true bundled suite collapses all of this into a single point of management. You install once, authenticate once, and the dashboard surfaces everything.

## Norton 360 Deluxe: Broad but Fragmented

Norton 360 Deluxe remains one of the most complete security packages money can buy — if you are willing to accept its install model.

### What It Bundles

At the Deluxe tier: antivirus, Secure VPN, Dark Web Monitoring, a password manager (Norton Password Manager), parental controls, and 50 GB cloud backup. On paper, this beats [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-eset-smart-security-premium-2026/) on raw feature count.

### The Install Problem

Here is what actually happens when you subscribe: you download the Norton 360 installer, which handles antivirus and the VPN. The password manager requires a separate download or the browser extension. Parental controls (Family Premier) push you to a separate app on mobile. Cloud backup installs its own agent.

By the time you are done, you have three to four distinct processes running. The Norton VPN client is based on a different engine than the antivirus, with its own settings panel. Users migrating from "everything in one place" setups find Norton's per-device activation flow particularly irritating — each device requires its own setup wizard.

### Performance

Norton's antivirus detection rates are excellent (consistently 99%+ in independent lab tests). The VPN is functional but limited to five simultaneous connections on the Deluxe plan. Speeds tested lower than Surfshark on the same connection — UK hop averaged 340 Mbps.

## McAfee+: Portal-First, Device-Second

McAfee redesigned its product around a web portal (protection.mcafee.com) rather than a fat desktop client. This is conceptually appealing — your security status is visible from any browser.

In practice, the portal manages your devices but does not replace the local apps. You still download McAfee Security for your Windows PC, McAfee Mobile Security for Android, and a separate VPN client if you want encrypted tunneling. The portal just tells you whether those apps are installed and up to date.

For users who want no separate app installs, McAfee+ trades one frustration (bloated clients) for another (constant browser-to-app context switching). Useful if you manage security for a household — less useful if you want a single place to do everything.
