## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you turn one on, your device connects to a server operated by the VPN provider before it connects to the rest of the internet. Websites and streaming services then see the server's IP address — not your home address in Johannesburg, Cape Town, or Durban.

Picture it like calling a business through a local number that forwards to you elsewhere. The business sees the local number. They have no idea where you actually are.

For streaming, that shift in perceived location changes three things:

- **Content libraries** are region-specific. Shows and films licensed for South African Netflix may not exist on US Netflix, and vice versa. By switching server locations, you browse whichever library you want.
- **Subscription prices** are pegged to local markets. Netflix charges based on what the local economy will bear. Connecting through a lower-income market server means you see — and can subscribe at — that market's price.
- **Geo-blocked platforms** become accessible. Services that simply refuse South African IP addresses (certain live sports feeds, US-only free tiers, regional streaming portals) open up when your traffic appears to come from an eligible country.
