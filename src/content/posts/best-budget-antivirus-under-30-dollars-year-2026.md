## What to Look For in a Budget Antivirus

Not all cheap antivirus products are equal. Some underprice themselves because they sell your browsing data to advertisers. Others offer a low first-year price and then renew at three or four times the cost. Here is what genuinely matters:

### Real-Time Protection (Not Just Scanning When You Ask)

The most important feature is protection that works automatically, all the time — not only when you remember to run a scan. Real-time protection catches threats the moment they try to enter your computer. Without it, you are only as safe as your last manual check.

### Phishing and Scam Website Blocking

Viruses are one threat. Fake websites are another. A good antivirus checks every website you visit against a database of known scam pages and blocks access before you can accidentally enter your password or card details. This single feature prevents more financial harm to seniors than almost anything else.

### Simple Interface

Security software that is hard to understand does not get used. Look for large, clear buttons, plain-English alerts (not technical error codes), and a layout that does not require reading a manual.

### Honest Renewal Pricing

The introductory offer is not the problem — the year-two renewal is where many providers quietly double or triple the price. Always check what the renewal rate is before you subscribe.

### Covers Multiple Devices

If you have a tablet or a second computer, a plan that covers multiple devices at no extra cost is considerably better value than paying separately for each one.

### What is the difference between a virus and a scam?

A virus is software designed to damage your computer or steal information once it gets inside. A scam is an attempt to trick you into handing over information or money voluntarily — like clicking a fake bank link and typing in your password. Good antivirus software protects against both: it blocks viruses from entering and warns you before you land on a scam page.

### How do I know if my computer already has a virus?

Common signs include: the computer running much slower than usual; pop-up windows appearing even when you have not opened a browser; programmes opening or closing on their own; your browser home page changing to something unfamiliar; or unexpected charges appearing on your bank statement. If you notice any of these, running a scan immediately is a sensible first step.

### Is it safe to buy antivirus software online?

Yes, as long as you buy directly from the company's official website or a trusted retailer. Avoid clicking links in unsolicited emails claiming your computer needs protection — these are often scams themselves. Bookmark the official site from a trusted search result and return to it directly when you are ready to buy.

### Do I need antivirus on a tablet or iPad as well?

Android tablets face similar risks to Windows computers — malware, [phishing link](https://snipstack.io/phishing-link-checker-free-2026/)s, and scam apps. iPads (which run Apple's operating system) have a more restricted environment, similar to iPhones, so the biggest risk there is phishing links and scam websites rather than traditional viruses. [Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-avast-free-2026/) covers Android tablets.
