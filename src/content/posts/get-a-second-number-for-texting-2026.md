### Step 4 — Choose Your Area Code

This is where most people make a mistake: they pick a random area code because it looks cool, then their match notices they're claiming to be local but their number says a different city.

**Pick an area code that:**
- Matches the city you actually use dating apps in, OR
- Is generic enough that nobody will question it (major metro codes like 212, 310, 415, 312 read as normal)
- Is available — popular area codes fill up fast on free tiers

**Google Voice:** After verification, search by area code or city. Type your target area code into the search box. Available numbers show in a list — pick one you like.

**TextNow:** You get a number assigned automatically. You can pay a small fee to pick a specific area code if the default doesn't work for you.

**Hushed:** During setup you're prompted to choose country → state → area code. The inventory is solid — most major US area codes are available.

**Alt Number:** Select your country (US or UK currently supported) then your area code from the available pool. Real numbers, not recycled VOIP.

## Troubleshooting

**My match says the number looks fake or VOIP.**
This happens most often with Google Voice numbers on older dating apps. Switch to a paid provider (Hushed or Alt Number) for a cleaner number reputation.

**I'm not receiving texts in the app.**
Check: (1) notifications are enabled for the app, (2) you haven't accidentally enabled Do Not Disturb, (3) on iOS, confirm the app has permission to send notifications under Settings → [App Name] → Notifications.

**The app is asking me to verify with MY real number again.**
This is normal — providers confirm you're a real person during setup. Your real number is not visible to anyone you text. It's used only for initial account verification.

**I can't pick the area code I want.**
Popular area codes (212 New York, 310 LA) fill up fast on free tiers. Try off-peak times or move to a paid tier which has wider number inventory.

**Dating app rejected my second number during verification.**
Some apps specifically block known VOIP ranges. Alt Number or Hushed (paid) are your best options here — their numbers pass more verification checks than Google Voice or TextNow free tier.

**I lost access to the app and now I can't receive texts.**
This is the main risk with app-based numbers. Keep the app installed and logged in. If you switch phones, reinstall the app and log in with the same account before your match tries to reach you.

**Will the person I text know it's a second number?**
Not from the number alone. A real US number from Alt Number or Hushed looks identical to a carrier-issued number in most apps. Reverse-lookup sites may flag some VOIP numbers — another reason to use a provider with real number inventory.

**Can I use a second number to verify dating apps like Tinder or Bumble?**
Usually yes, but results vary by provider. Paid providers with real number inventory (Alt Number, Hushed paid tier) have better success rates than Google Voice or TextNow free tier. The number is tied to your account, not the app install. Delete and reinstall is fine as long as you log back into the same account. If you cancel a paid subscription (Hushed, Alt Number), the number is released after a grace period.

**Can my second number receive calls, not just texts?**
All four providers support calls through the app (VOIP calling). Alt Number and Hushed forward calls directly through the app. Google Voice also supports voicemail. For most dating scenarios, texting is all you need initially — but the call option is there when you're ready.

**Is there a truly free option with no ads?**
Google Voice is free and ad-free, but requires a Google account and has limitations with some VOIP-blocking platforms. It's the best genuinely free option, with the caveat that reliability on dating app verifications is inconsistent.
