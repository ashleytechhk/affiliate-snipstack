## SimpleLogin: The Best Free Alternative

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service (now owned by Proton) that offers 10 free aliases on its free tier — more than enough for a single Roblox account. If your child plays multiple games across platforms and you want separate aliases for each service, the paid plan at around $30 per year unlocks unlimited aliases.

### Strengths

- True per-alias forwarding control (pause, delete, change destination)
- Works reliably with Roblox's email verification system
- Open-source and audited — you can self-host if you want full control
- Integration with Proton Mail if your family already uses that ecosystem

### Weaknesses

- The dashboard requires a bit more setup than Alt-ID
- Free tier limits you to 10 aliases and one custom domain

SimpleLogin is the right choice if you want a dedicated standalone alias service and do not need or want a VPN bundled in. For parents who are already paying for Surfshark, Alt-ID is the better value since there is no additional subscription.

## Apple Hide My Email: Easiest for Apple Families

If every device in your home runs iOS, iPadOS, or macOS, Apple Hide My Email is nearly frictionless. You generate an alias directly from Safari's autofill suggestions or from iCloud Settings, and Apple handles the forwarding to your Apple ID email.

### Strengths

- Built into every Apple device — no separate app or account
- Per-alias disable is a tap away in iCloud Settings
- Reliable forwarding with no extra cost beyond iCloud+ (50 GB plan starts at $0.99/month in the US)

### Weaknesses

- Ties you to Apple's ecosystem — if you switch to Android, managing aliases becomes awkward
- No web dashboard on non-Apple devices
- Aliases are linked to your Apple ID, so a family sharing a single Apple ID can create conflicts

If your household is Apple-exclusive and you already pay for iCloud+, Hide My Email is the path of least resistance. Otherwise, Alt-ID or SimpleLogin give you more flexibility.

DuckDuckGo's Email Protection service gives you a free @duck.com address that strips trackers from forwarded emails before they reach your inbox. You can use it as a Roblox alias with no account required beyond a DuckDuckGo browser sign-in.

## What to Avoid: Temporary and Public Inboxes

Guerrilla Mail, Mailinator, and similar services generate inboxes that expire in minutes or hours and are publicly accessible to anyone who guesses the address. They have two fatal problems for Roblox:

1. Roblox occasionally detects these domains and blocks account creation outright.
2. Even when they work, the inbox expires before your child ever needs a password reset, locking the account permanently.

Do not use these for any account your child plans to keep.

## FAQ

### Can I use any [email alias for](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-privacy-without-creating-new-account-2026/) Roblox, or does Roblox block certain providers?

Roblox blocks some well-known temporary mail domains (Guerrilla Mail, Mailinator, and similar). Established alias providers like Surfshark Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, Apple Hide My Email, and DuckDuckGo Email Protection use custom domains that pass Roblox's checks without issue.

### [What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if I lose access to the [alias email](https://snipstack.io/alias-email-for-linkedin-signup-without-inbox-spam-2026/)?

If the alias is paused or deleted and Roblox needs to send a verification or password reset, the email will not reach you. Always keep the alias active for any account your child actively uses. With Alt-ID, you can re-activate forwarding instantly from the dashboard. **[Manage your aliases with Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-roblox-kids-account-setup-2026__faq)**

### Is it against Roblox's terms of service to use an alias?

No. Roblox's terms require a valid, working email address — not a specific type of email address. An alias that correctly receives and forwards email satisfies this requirement. The alias is the email of record from Roblox's perspective.

### Can my child's Roblox friends see the alias address?

No. Roblox does not display a user's email address to other players. The email field is private, used only for account recovery and notifications. Your alias address is invisible to anyone other than Roblox's own systems.

### Do I need a separate alias for each child?

It is good practice to use one alias per Roblox account. This way, if one account generates spam, you can kill that specific alias without affecting other accounts. Alt-ID makes it easy to generate multiple aliases and label them clearly — one per child, one per game platform if you want to go further.

### Will the alias work for Robux billing confirmations?

Yes. Billing confirmation emails from Roblox route through the alias just like any other message. If you have forwarding enabled, they arrive in your real inbox. If you have forwarding paused for promotional content, keep transactional forwarding on to ensure you receive payment receipts.
