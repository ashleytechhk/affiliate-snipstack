## What Is a VPN, and Why Do Real Estate Agents Need One?

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet. Instead of your data traveling in the open — readable by anyone on the same Wi-Fi network — it is wrapped in a layer of encryption and routed through a server in a location you choose.

For a real estate agent, that matters in three concrete ways:

**1. You work on public Wi-Fi constantly.**
Open houses, coffee meetings, hotel lobbies, coworking spaces — the networks you use are often shared with dozens of strangers. Without a VPN, anyone on that network with basic tools can intercept unencrypted traffic. Client details, contract attachments, login credentials for your CRM or MLS portal — all of it can be exposed.

**2. Some property platforms are region-restricted.**
International MLS platforms, certain virtual tour software subscriptions, and property data feeds sometimes restrict access by geography. If you are traveling abroad and your platform detects a foreign IP address, it may block your login or flag your account. A VPN lets you appear to connect from your home country regardless of where you physically are.

**3. Subscription costs vary by region — sometimes dramatically.**
Software tools used in real estate — video conferencing, cloud storage, project management, even some CRM platforms — price their subscriptions differently across markets. A VPN that lets you switch your apparent location can let you access lower regional pricing on annual plans. For an agent carrying five or six SaaS subscriptions, the savings add up fast.

## How a VPN Works Step by Step

Understanding the mechanics helps you use it more effectively.

1. **You install the VPN app** on your laptop, phone, or tablet. Surfshark, for example, has native apps for Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android.
2. **You choose a server location.** Want to appear as if you are connecting from the UK? Select a UK server. Want to access a US-only platform while abroad? Pick a US server.
3. **The VPN encrypts your traffic** before it leaves your device, using AES-256 encryption — the same standard used by financial institutions.
4. **Your request goes to the VPN server first**, which strips your real IP and forwards the request using its own IP address.
5. **The website or platform sees the VPN server's IP**, not yours. It responds to the VPN server, which decrypts and forwards the response back to you.

The entire process happens in milliseconds. From your perspective, it feels like a normal internet connection — just more private and flexible.
