## What Exactly Is a VPN and Why Do Dizis Get Blocked?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its simplest, it is a piece of software that encrypts your internet traffic and sends it through a server in a country of your choice before it reaches its destination.

When you try to watch a Turkish TV platform from, say, the United Kingdom or the United States, the platform checks your IP address. Your IP address is a numerical label assigned by your internet provider that reveals your approximate location. If the platform only has a licence to broadcast in Turkey, it blocks any IP address that does not appear to be Turkish.

A VPN solves this by giving you a Turkish IP address. You connect to a VPN server physically located in Turkey, and from that point forward every streaming platform you visit sees a Turkish address — not yours.

This is not a technical trick that requires expertise. It takes about three minutes to set up and works on every device you own: laptop, phone, smart TV, tablet, or router.
