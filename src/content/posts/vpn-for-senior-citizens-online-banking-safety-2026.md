## What Exactly is a VPN?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. The word "virtual" means it exists in software, not a physical cable. The word "private" is the key point — it creates a private, encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet.

Think of it like this: when you send a letter, it travels in an envelope. Without a VPN, your internet activity travels like a postcard — anyone handling it along the way can read it. A VPN puts everything into a sealed, locked envelope that only you and your destination (your bank's website, for example) can open.

When you connect to a VPN, your internet traffic is routed through a secure server run by the VPN provider. Websites see the VPN server's address, not yours. Your internet service provider and anyone on the same Wi-Fi network can no longer read what you are doing.

## Why Seniors Face Higher Online Banking Risk

Seniors are disproportionately targeted by online financial fraud for several reasons that have nothing to do with intelligence or caution:

**Public Wi-Fi is everywhere and mostly unsafe.** Coffee shops, libraries, doctors' waiting rooms, and airports offer free Wi-Fi. Most of these networks are unencrypted, meaning anyone with basic tools can intercept your login credentials or session data. Seniors who bank while waiting for an appointment are particularly exposed.

**Older devices may lack security updates.** Tablets and laptops bought five or more years ago may no longer receive software updates, leaving known security gaps unfixed. A VPN adds a layer of protection on top of the device itself.

**Phishing attacks are increasingly sophisticated.** Scammers send emails and text messages that look like real messages from banks. While a VPN does not block phishing emails, it does prevent your banking session data from being captured by a third party monitoring your network.

**Data brokers sell your browsing behavior.** Every time you visit your bank's website without a VPN, your internet service provider and data brokers can see that activity. Some services sell this data. A VPN keeps your financial browsing habits private.
