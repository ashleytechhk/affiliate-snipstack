## Step 1: Disable App Tracking on iPhone

**What this fixes:** Apps on iPhone request permission to track you across other apps and websites using Apple's Advertising Identifier (IDFA). When you grant this permission, advertisers can link your activity in one app to your activity in another — building a cross-app profile that powers "following" ads.

**How to disable it:**

1. Open **Settings**
2. Tap **Privacy & Security**
3. Tap **Tracking**
4. Toggle **Allow Apps to Request to Track** to **off**

When this is off, apps cannot ask for tracking permission — the request is automatically denied. Any app that was previously granted tracking permission stops receiving your advertising ID.

**Also worth doing:** While you are in Privacy & Security, tap **Apple Advertising** and toggle **Personalized Ads** off. This stops Apple's own ad network from targeting you based on your App Store and Apple service activity.

## Step 2: Reset Your Advertising ID on Android

**What this fixes:** Android phones have a Google Advertising ID (GAID) — a unique identifier tied to your device that ad networks use to link your activity across different apps. Resetting it severs the connection between your past browsing behavior and your device. Deleting it entirely is even better.

**How to reset or delete it:**

1. Open **Settings**
2. Tap **Privacy** (or **Google** on some devices, then **Ads**)
3. Tap **Ads**
4. Tap **Delete advertising ID** — confirm the deletion

On Android 12 and later, you can delete the advertising ID entirely rather than just resetting it. Deleting is better than resetting: resetting gives you a new ID that advertisers will rebuild a profile on over time, while deleting removes the ID so apps receive a string of zeros.

**If your Android version only shows "Reset":** Reset it now, then revisit every 30 days to keep the profile fragmented.

## Step 3: Switch to a Privacy Browser on Mobile

**What this fixes:** Your default mobile browser — Chrome or Safari — sends referrer information, accepts third-party cookies, and does not block tracker scripts by default. Every website you visit can embed invisible tracking pixels and JavaScript that report your visit to dozens of ad networks simultaneously.

**Recommended browsers for mobile:**

- **Firefox for Android** — supports extensions including uBlock Origin, which gives you the most granular tracker blocking available on mobile
- **Brave** (iOS and Android) — blocks trackers and ads by default, no configuration needed, built-in Shields

**To switch your default browser:**

On iPhone: Settings → General → Default Browser App → select Firefox or Brave

On Android: Settings → Apps → Default Apps → Browser → select Firefox or Brave

**Specific setting to enable in Brave:** Tap the lion icon on any page → make sure Shields is set to Aggressive. This upgrades Brave's default blocking from standard to cross-site tracker blocking.

## Step 4: Audit and Revoke App Permissions

**What this fixes:** Many apps request permissions — location, contacts, clipboard — that they use for advertising purposes beyond their stated function. A weather app with "always on" location access is feeding your location to an ad data broker in real time. This is legal and extremely common.

**On iPhone:**

1. Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services
2. Review every app — set apps that do not need location to **Never**, apps that need it occasionally to **While Using the App**
3. Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking — review which apps have been granted tracking access and revoke any you do not recognize

**On Android:**

1. Settings → Privacy → Permission Manager
2. Tap **Location** — switch any app that doesn't genuinely need location from "Allow all the time" to "Allow only while using"
3. Tap through other sensitive categories: Camera, Microphone, Contacts

**Rule of thumb:** If you cannot explain why a free app needs always-on location, revoke it.

## Step 6: Opt Out of Personalized Ads in Individual Apps

**What this fixes:** Even with Steps 1–5 done, some platforms run their own internal ad targeting systems that do not rely on your device's advertising ID. Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Google, and YouTube all have in-app ad personalization settings that need to be turned off separately.

**Facebook and Instagram:**

1. Settings & Privacy → Settings → Ad Preferences → Ad Settings
2. Tap **Data About Your Activity From Partners** → toggle off
3. Tap **Ads Based on Data From Partners** → toggle off
4. Tap **Ads Based on Your Activity on Facebook Company Products** → toggle off

**Google (covers YouTube and Search):**

1. Visit myaccount.google.com → **Data & Privacy**
2. Under "Things you've done and places you've been" → **My Ad Center**
3. Toggle **Personalized ads** off

**TikTok:**

1. Profile → Settings and Privacy → Privacy → Ads
2. Toggle off **Personalized Ads**

These opt-outs reduce cross-platform ad targeting within each platform's own system. They do not affect third-party ad networks — that is what Steps 1, 2, and 5 handle.

## Step 7: Use a Private DNS (No VPN Required)

**What this fixes:** If you want DNS-level tracker blocking without installing a VPN app, private DNS is an alternative for Android users. It routes your DNS queries through a privacy-respecting resolver that blocks known tracker domains.

**On Android (Android 9+):**

1. Settings → Network & Internet → Advanced → Private DNS
2. Select **Private DNS provider hostname**
3. Enter: `dns.adguard-dns.com`
4. Tap Save

This gives you free DNS-level tracker blocking with no app required. The trade-off compared to CleanWeb: the blocklist is less frequently updated, it does not cover VPN benefits, and it only works while connected to mobile data or Wi-Fi with the DNS setting active.

**On iPhone:** iOS does not have a native private DNS setting — you need a configuration profile or an app like Surfshark to achieve the same effect.

## Troubleshooting

**Tracking ads are still appearing after all these steps**

Check whether the platform is using first-party ad serving. Some publishers serve ads from their own domains (e.g., ads.example.com) — DNS blocking cannot block these without blocking the site itself. This is less common but exists.

Also verify CleanWeb is actually active — open the Surfshark app and confirm the CleanWeb toggle is on and the app shows it is running.

**CleanWeb is toggled on but I don't see a difference**

Make sure you are connected (or that CleanWeb's standalone DNS blocking is active). On iOS, the app needs VPN permission to function — approve the VPN configuration request when prompted. Check Settings → VPN to confirm the Surfshark profile is installed.

**Brave Shields is blocking content I need to see**

Tap the lion icon in Brave's address bar and lower Shields to Standard for that specific site. Brave applies Shields per-site — changing it for one site does not affect others.

**After resetting my Android ad ID, targeting ads came back quickly**

Ad networks can rebuild a probabilistic profile using other signals: IP address, device type, language, and behavioral patterns. This is called fingerprinting. DNS-level blocking (Step 5) reduces fingerprinting data significantly by cutting off the tracking scripts that collect these signals.

**My social media ads are still personalized**

Platform-internal personalization (Facebook, Google, TikTok) operates on data already collected. Opting out (Step 6) stops future data collection for targeting purposes, but it does not delete historical data the platform already holds. Targeting gradually becomes less precise over 2–4 weeks after opting out.

## FAQ

### Why do ads keep following me even in incognito mode?

Incognito mode does not block ad trackers — it only prevents your browser from saving your local history. Third-party tracker scripts still fire, your IP address is still visible, and in-app trackers are completely unaffected by incognito mode. To actually stop tracking, you need DNS-level blocking (Step 5) and device-level changes (Steps 1–2). [Surfshark CleanWeb](/blog/adblock?sub=how-to-stop-tracking-ads-follow-me-2026__faq) handles the network-level blocking across all apps.

### Does turning off ad personalization mean I see fewer ads?

No — you will see the same number of ads, just less targeted ones. The ads become random or contextual (based on the page you are on) rather than behavioral (based on your tracked activity). Some people find random ads less intrusive because they are not eerily accurate; others find contextual ads just as annoying. The goal of these steps is to stop the surveillance, not eliminate advertising entirely.

### Is this completely private after following all these steps?

No privacy measure is absolute. These steps cut off the primary tracking mechanisms — device IDs, cross-app tracking permissions, tracker domain requests, and platform-level profiling. However, IP-based geolocation, device fingerprinting, and behavioral inference still exist as techniques. For a significantly stronger privacy posture, combining these steps with a VPN (which masks your IP address) and using Surfshark CleanWeb together addresses most tracking vectors.

### Does CleanWeb work on both iPhone and Android?

Yes. Surfshark CleanWeb is available in the Surfshark app for both iOS and Android. On iPhone, it works across Safari, Chrome, Firefox, and all apps. On Android, it works across all browsers and apps. Setup takes about two minutes on either platform.

### How long does it take before tracking ads stop following me?

Device-level changes (Steps 1 and 2) take effect immediately. App permission changes (Step 4) take effect immediately. DNS-level blocking (Step 5) takes effect as soon as CleanWeb is active. The lag you will notice is in the ad delivery systems themselves: advertisers bid on your profile in real time, and even after your tracking data stops updating, some ad networks cache old targeting data for 24–72 hours before it expires. Expect a noticeable reduction within a day, with full effect within a week.

### Can I do all of this for free?

Steps 1, 2, 3, 4, and 6 are free — they use built-in phone settings and free browsers. Step 7 is also free.
