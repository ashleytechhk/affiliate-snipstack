## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. At its core, it creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. When you connect through that tunnel, websites and streaming platforms see the server's IP address — not yours.

Think of it like forwarding mail through a friend's address in another city. To anyone looking at the envelope, the letter came from your friend's location, not from you.

For streaming purposes, that single shift in perceived location changes everything:

- **Content libraries** differ by country. A show available on Netflix Germany may not exist on Netflix US, and vice versa.
- **Subscription prices** vary dramatically. The same Netflix Standard plan can cost 40–60% less in markets like Turkey or Argentina compared to Germany.
- **Geo-blocked platforms** become accessible — services that simply refuse connections from German IP addresses open up when you connect through a different country's server.
