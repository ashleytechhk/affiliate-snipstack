## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect through a VPN, your internet traffic is routed through a server in the location you choose before reaching its destination. Every website and app you connect to sees the VPN server's IP address — not yours, and not your real location.

For streaming, this matters because platforms like Netflix, Disney+, Spotify, and most other subscription services price their plans differently depending on which country your account is registered in. The price difference reflects local purchasing power, and the gaps are substantial. A plan priced at $15.49 per month in the United States might cost the equivalent of $4–6 per month in Turkey or Argentina.

A VPN for Chromebook is a VPN app that runs natively on ChromeOS. Because most Chromebooks released since 2019 support Android apps through the Google Play Store, [VPN providers](/vpn-providers-best-2026/) have built full-featured apps that install in seconds, exactly as they would on an Android phone.

## How a Chromebook VPN Actually Works

You do not need to understand networking to use a Chromebook VPN, but the basics help you make smarter choices.

![Diagram showing how VPN traffic routes from Chromebook through server to streaming service](/og/vpn-for-chromebook-setup-guide-2026-inline-1.png)

When your Chromebook connects to the internet without a VPN, every service you visit sees your real IP address. That IP address reveals your approximate location with enough accuracy for streaming platforms to determine which pricing tier applies to your account.

When a VPN is active:

- Your Chromebook connects to a VPN server in the country you chose
- All traffic between your Chromebook and that server is encrypted
- The streaming service only sees the VPN server's IP address, not yours
- The service treats your session as coming from wherever that server is located

Pick a server in Turkey, and Turkish Netflix pricing applies. Pick Argentina, and you see Argentine rates for the platforms that price aggressively there.

**Kill switch** — worth understanding before you set this up. If the VPN connection drops unexpectedly, a kill switch instantly blocks your internet traffic until the VPN reconnects. Without it, your real IP could briefly appear to a streaming service during the gap, which is how accounts get flagged for location inconsistency. Surfshark includes a kill switch in its ChromeOS app.
