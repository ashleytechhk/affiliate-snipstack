## Why Seniors Are a Top Target for Online Threats

Scammers know that older adults often spend more time online and may be less familiar with the tricks being used. The most common threats in 2026 include:

- **Fake virus alerts** — pop-up windows that say your computer is infected and ask you to call a number or click a link
- **Phishing emails** — messages pretending to be from your bank, Medicare, or a parcel delivery service, asking you to enter your password or card number
- **Ransomware** — software that locks all your files and demands payment to get them back
- **Spyware** — hidden programs that watch what you type, including passwords and card numbers

A good antivirus catches all of these automatically, before they cause harm. Think of it as a smoke detector for your computer — you install it once, and it watches over you around the clock.
