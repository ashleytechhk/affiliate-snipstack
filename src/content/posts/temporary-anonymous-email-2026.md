## Understanding the Two Types of Temporary Anonymous Email

Before jumping into setup, it helps to know which tool fits which situation.

**Throwaway email (truly temporary)** — A one-time inbox that requires no account. You visit a site, get a random address, check your messages for a few minutes, and the address vanishes. Good for: redeeming a single-use promo code, downloading a gated PDF, verifying a trial signup you will never revisit.

**Permanent masked alias** — You generate an alias that forwards to your real inbox indefinitely. The sender never sees your actual address. You keep the alias as long as it is useful, then delete it. Good for: shopping sites, newsletters, any service where you actually need ongoing emails.

Most people searching for a temporary anonymous email today genuinely need the second type. The free throwaway services feel simpler, but they fail the moment you need to receive a password reset, track an order, or manage a subscription.

## Troubleshooting

**The site is rejecting my alias address.**
Some services check submitted addresses against databases of known alias or [disposable email](https://snipstack.io/disposable-email-that-receives-messages-2026/) domains and block them. If your Alt-ID alias is rejected, try generating a new one — Alt-ID uses multiple alias domains, and different domains have different acceptance rates across sites. If a specific site consistently rejects all aliases, you may need to use your real address for that one service.

**Forwarded emails are landing in my spam folder.**
Add your Alt-ID forwarding address to your real inbox's contacts or safe-sender list. Once your email provider trusts the forwarding source, this stops happening.

**The confirmation email never arrived.**
First, check that the "Forward to" address in your Alt-ID settings is correct and has no typo. Second, check your real inbox's spam or junk folder — some providers flag forwarded messages on first contact.

**I deleted an alias by mistake.**
Alt-ID aliases cannot be recovered once deleted. Create a new alias and update the affected accounts with the new address. This is rare, but it is worth keeping a note of which alias is used where before deleting anything.

**I am receiving emails I want alongside ones I do not want from the same alias.**
Create separate aliases for different services or categories going forward. Delete the mixed alias, create two clean ones, and update your accounts.

## Is a Temporary Anonymous Email Actually Anonymous?

Practically speaking, an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-sign-2026/) provides strong pseudonymity — marketers and spammers cannot connect your alias to your real identity through the email address alone. But "anonymous" in an absolute sense has limits worth understanding:

- Your internet service provider can see network-level traffic regardless of what email address you use.
- Payment records at Surfshark are associated with a real account.
- If you reply to emails, metadata in message headers can occasionally carry information.
- Services can still identify you via cookies, device fingerprinting, or the account details you provide alongside the alias.

For the most common use cases — stopping spam, protecting your name from marketing lists, keeping signups separate from your personal identity — Alt-ID is more than sufficient. For high-stakes scenarios, the requirements go well beyond any email tool.
