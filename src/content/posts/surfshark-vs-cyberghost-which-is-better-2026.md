## Price: Nearly Identical, With a Catch

On the headline 2-year plan, CyberGhost often edges out Surfshark by a few cents per month — roughly $2.03 versus $2.19. That difference is real but small. The catch is the device limit.

CyberGhost caps simultaneous connections at seven. Surfshark has no cap. For a single-user setup, seven devices is probably fine. For a household running the arbitrage play across multiple people, phones, TVs, and laptops, the cap bites. Running out of connection slots is not a hypothetical — it happens when two people are streaming different regions at the same time while a third device is connected for a cheaper Spotify plan.

When you factor in the device math, Surfshark's slightly higher per-month price covers more ground. The effective cost per device falls fast once you go past three or four simultaneous users.

Both services should be purchased on long-term plans only. Monthly pricing on either is not worth it for anyone serious about arbitrage — the annual savings evaporate quickly.

## Smart DNS for Non-App Devices

Both Surfshark and CyberGhost offer Smart DNS — a feature that routes your DNS queries through a specific region without installing the full VPN app. This matters for devices that cannot run [VPN software](https://snipstack.io/vpn-software-hotspot-shield-2026/) directly: older smart TVs, gaming consoles, Blu-ray players, and some streaming sticks.

Smart DNS cannot encrypt your traffic the way a full VPN does, but for streaming arbitrage on a home network, that trade-off is acceptable. You just need the platform to see a regional IP address when you log in or access pricing pages — Smart DNS handles that.

Surfshark's Smart DNS documentation is well-maintained and covers the major TV platforms with step-by-step setup guides. CyberGhost's Smart DNS is functional but has historically had more limited regional coverage in independent reviews. Either way, if you have a smart TV as part of your setup, Smart DNS makes the whole arbitrage workflow cleaner.
