## Why Beta Testing Wrecks Your Inbox Specifically

Regular online shopping generates spam from maybe a dozen retailers a year. Active beta testing is worse, and for a specific reason: the sign-up forms are often built by solo developers or small teams using free-tier email marketing tools, not enterprise-grade systems with real data hygiene. Your address doesn't just go into "did you like the app" surveys — it often gets synced into a mailing list by default, sometimes without a clear opt-out, and occasionally sold onward when the app shuts down and the developer offloads their user list.

Add in third-party beta recruitment platforms like Betabound, uTest, or various Discord-based tester communities, and you're handing your real email to a dozen new parties a month. Each one is a potential future spam source, and unsubscribing from all of them individually isn't realistic once you're testing regularly.
