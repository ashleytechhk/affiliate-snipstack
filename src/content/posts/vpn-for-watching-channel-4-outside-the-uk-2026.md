## What Is Channel 4 (and What Do You Lose Outside the UK)?

Channel 4 is the on-demand streaming service run by Channel Four Television Corporation, the UK's public-service broadcaster. It carries live simulcasts and catch-up libraries for Channel 4, E4, More4, Film4, and their spin-off channels — panel shows, reality formats, acquired dramas, and Film4's movie catalogue. The service was previously branded All 4, and before that 4oD.

The core tier is entirely free, funded by advertising, with no signup fee and no library behind a paywall. An optional ad-free tier, Channel 4+, costs roughly £3.99 a month or £39.99 a year and removes advertising outside live broadcasts. Neither tier requires a UK TV licence — that only applies to live television and BBC iPlayer.

The catch: both tiers are locked to UK and Ireland IP addresses. Leave those countries and Channel 4 reads your new location and blocks the stream outright. Your account and any Channel 4+ subscription stay active — you simply cannot reach any of it without a connection that presents as UK-based.

## Why Channel 4 Blocks Viewers Outside the UK

The restriction is a licensing requirement, not a technical accident. Channel 4 negotiates broadcast and streaming rights on a territory basis, and most of that content is licensed for the UK and Ireland only. Extending access globally would breach those agreements.

Every request to Channel 4's servers triggers an IP-to-location check. A UK or Irish address passes; anything else returns the geo-block. Like most broadcasters, Channel 4 also maintains blocklists of known VPN and proxy ranges, so the VPN needs a large pool of clean, actively rotated UK addresses to stay ahead of detection.
