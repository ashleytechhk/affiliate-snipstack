## Why Blocklists Fail and the Calls Keep Coming

You have probably tried the standard advice. Block the number. Use your phone's built-in [spam call blocker](https://snipstack.io/best-spam-call-blocker-2026/). Enable silence unknown callers on iPhone. Report them to the carrier.

And the calls kept coming anyway.

This is not a coincidence. The tools built into your phone are designed to filter out automated robocalls and known spam ranges — they are not designed to stop a specific person who is motivated to reach you. When you block a number, a determined caller picks up a burner or uses a spoofing service and calls from a different one. Caller ID spoofing is cheap, legal in many contexts, and trivially easy to execute. Your blocklist grows by one. Nothing changes.

The fundamental problem is structural: a **call blocking app** that operates on a blocklist is always reacting to calls that have already happened. You add a number to the block. They call from a new one. You add that number. This loop has no exit — not as long as they still have access to your real number.

A call blocking app for small business solves this differently. The business owner does not add the ex-client's number to a blocklist and hope. They retire the number the client had and issue a new one. The ex-client's number — spoofed or not — can no longer reach them, because the number being called no longer exists.

This is the mechanic online daters need.
