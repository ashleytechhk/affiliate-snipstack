## The Three Biggest Gaps in Windows Defender on Your Surface Pro

Windows Defender is not bad software. Microsoft built it, it ships free with every Surface Pro, and it blocks the most common viruses reliably. The problem is what it does not do.

**Gap 1: Browser protection is Edge-only**

Windows Defender's web filtering works inside Microsoft Edge. Most people use Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox instead. When you browse in Chrome — checking your email through Gmail, reading news, shopping on Amazon — Defender's web shield is effectively switched off. A dangerous website loads without any warning.

**Gap 2: No email link scanning**

When a scam email lands in your inbox and you click the link inside it, Defender does not intercept that click. It only scans files that are downloaded and saved to your computer. A link that takes you to a fake login page — one that never downloads anything — slips straight past.

**Gap 3: No data breach monitoring**

If your email address or password appears in a leaked database — which happens when a company you use gets hacked — Defender will never tell you. You may not find out for months, during which time criminals have access to your accounts.

These three gaps are precisely where targeted attacks against seniors land. A dedicated antivirus like [Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-avast-free-2026/) closes all three.
