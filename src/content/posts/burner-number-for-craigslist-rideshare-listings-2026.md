## The Risk Nobody Talks About With Craigslist and Rideshare Listings

Most privacy advice focuses on dating apps themselves. But for a significant number of online daters, the exposure happens one step upstream — in the classifieds and gig platforms where real phone numbers are essentially required.

Here is why this matters specifically for online daters:

**Your number is the bridge.** A phone number handed out on Craigslist or during a rideshare is all someone needs to find you on WhatsApp, Telegram, or iMessage — platforms you may be actively using for dating conversations. The volume of unsolicited contact, scam attempts, and harassment reports from Craigslist interactions is disproportionately high relative to any mainstream dating app.

**Rideshare ratings are not anonymous.** Both Uber and Lyft allow passengers to rate drivers and vice versa. Those ratings are tied to real accounts. A passenger who has your masked ride number and knows your first name has more than enough to try to locate you on dating apps or social platforms, especially in smaller cities where the overlap between rideshare users and dating app users is concentrated.
