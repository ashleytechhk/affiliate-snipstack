## Why Antivirus Alone Is No Longer Enough

Most people picture a virus as something that sneaks onto your computer and breaks it. That still happens. But the threats seniors face in 2026 have expanded far beyond that.

Here is what actually happens in a typical scam targeting older adults:

1. You connect to public Wi-Fi at the pharmacy, library, or airport.
2. A stranger on the same network — or a device the scammer planted nearby — can silently read the information you send and receive. This includes passwords, banking details, and anything you type into a form.
3. Or you click a link in what looks like an email from your doctor's office. The page looks real but is a copy. When you enter your login details, the scammer has them.
4. Or a file you downloaded runs quietly in the background, recording every key you press.

Standard antivirus stops step 4 well. It does almost nothing about steps 2 and 3. A VPN encrypts your connection so that even if someone intercepts it, they cannot read what is inside. Phishing protection blocks the fake websites before they even load.

Getting all three — virus scanning, connection encryption, and website protection — in a single app means you do not have to manage separate programs, separate subscriptions, or remember which one covers what.

## What to Look for in an Antivirus + VPN Bundle

Before comparing specific products, here is what matters most for seniors:

**Real-time protection — not just manual scans.** The antivirus component should run continuously in the background. If it only scans when you tell it to, you could be infected for days before you notice.

**The VPN should switch on easily.** Look for one large button that says "Connect" — not a list of servers, protocols, and settings. You should not need to know what a VPN server is to use one.

**Phishing and scam-website blocking.** This is the layer that stops fake bank pages, fake government sites, and fake parcel-tracking pages from loading in the first place. Not all bundles include this — check before you buy.

**Covers more than one device.** If you have a phone, tablet, and a computer, your protection should follow you across all of them under one plan.

**Plain-English alerts.** Security software that fills your screen with technical terms and red warnings you cannot interpret causes anxiety without helping. Good software tells you in simple words what happened and what, if anything, to do.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I really need a VPN if I already have antivirus?

Yes, if you ever use Wi-Fi outside your home. Antivirus protects the device. A VPN protects the connection. If you connect to Wi-Fi at a pharmacy, library, hotel, or any public place, your information can be read by others on the same network — antivirus alone does not prevent this. An antivirus + VPN bundle covers both risks.

### Will this slow my computer or phone down?

Modern antivirus + VPN bundles are designed to run quietly in the background with minimal impact. Surfshark, Norton, and Bitdefender all have low performance footprints on reasonably recent devices. Older computers (more than 8 years old) may notice a small slowdown during full scans, but everyday use — email, browsing, video calls — should not be affected.

### What happens if I get a scary virus warning on my screen?

Do not click anything inside the popup — not even "Close" or "Cancel." Many of these warnings are fake, designed to trick you into calling a number or installing real malware. Press **Alt + F4** on your keyboard to force the window closed. Then open your antivirus app and run a scan. If you are on a tablet or phone, press the home button and close the browser. A legitimate antivirus with real-time protection, like Surfshark, would have blocked the threat before that popup appeared — which is why having always-on protection matters.

### Is it safe to enter my credit card details to buy antivirus online?

Yes, when you are on the official website of a reputable company and you see a padlock icon in the browser bar next to the web address. The padlock means the connection is encrypted. Surfshark, Norton, and Bitdefender all use standard secure payment processing. Never buy security software from a popup or an unfamiliar website that appeared unexpectedly.

### How do I know if the VPN is working?

With Surfshark, the app shows a clear green indicator when the VPN is connected. You do not need to test it or verify anything technical — if the indicator is green, you are protected. The app also tells you plainly when the VPN is off, so there is no guessing.

### Can one subscription cover my phone and my computer?

That means your phone, your computer, and a tablet are all protected under one plan and one price. Norton's entry plan covers only one device; you need a higher-tier plan for multiple devices. Bitdefender covers up to 10 devices depending on the plan.

### What is a "data breach alert" and why does it matter?

A data breach alert notifies you when your email address or password appears in a leaked database — for example, if a pharmacy, airline loyalty program, or online shop suffers a security breach and your details are exposed. Without this alert, you would not know your password had been stolen until something went wrong. With it, you can change the affected password immediately before a scammer uses it.
