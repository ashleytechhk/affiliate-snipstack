## The Three Risks a Good Antivirus Stops

### Risk 1: Malware and Ransomware

Malware is software designed to damage your computer or steal your data. Ransomware is a particularly cruel version: it locks all your files — your photos, your documents, your saved passwords — and demands payment to unlock them.

Ransomware attacks on individuals have surged. And older adults are frequently targeted because they are more likely to have savings and less likely to have recent backups.

A real-time antivirus scanner intercepts ransomware before it can encrypt a single file.

### Risk 2: Phishing and [Fake Website](https://snipstack.io/fake-website-detector-tool-2026/)s

You receive an email. It looks exactly like a message from your bank. The logo is right, the wording is right, even the layout matches what your bank normally sends. You click the link. You enter your password. Except that website was a perfect fake, and your password just went to a criminal.

Web protection in a good antivirus checks the destination of every link against a constantly updated list of known scam sites. If the site is dangerous, the antivirus blocks the page and shows you a warning before any information can be stolen.

### Risk 3: Spyware and Keyloggers

This is the threat most people do not think about. Spyware hides silently on your computer and records everything you type — your usernames, your passwords, your Social Security number, your credit card details — then sends that information out over the internet.

You would never know it was there. Your computer might run fine for months while a criminal collects your financial data.

An [antivirus with](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-with-best-malware-removal-rate-2026/) continuous background scanning catches spyware on arrival, before it has a chance to record anything.
