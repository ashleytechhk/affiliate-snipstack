**Number quality:** VoIP. This is the key limitation — Google Voice numbers are well-known to spam filters, SMS verification services, and dating platforms. Some apps (WhatsApp, certain dating apps, two-factor authentication systems) will flag or reject these numbers during setup.

**Cost:** Free for personal use. Calls within the US are free. International calling costs extra.

**Privacy design:** It's Google. Your number, contacts, and call logs are associated with your Google account. If you're trying to separate a work alias from your personal identity, a Google-tied service adds a layer of cross-referencing risk.

**Limitations:** Getting increasingly blocked by apps and platforms that screen VOIP numbers. US-only. Tied to Google's data ecosystem.

**Bottom line:** The right choice if you're an existing Google user with light work alias needs and you don't care about platform compatibility. Not the right choice if reliability across SMS verification is important to you.

## Why Number Type Matters More Than Most Guides Admit

The VoIP vs. real mobile distinction comes up repeatedly above. Here's why it's not just a technical footnote.

When you give a client a new number for work contact, or when you exchange numbers after a dating app match, that number often needs to:

1. **Pass SMS verification** if they want to add you to a messaging app like WhatsApp or Signal
2. **Not trigger spam detection** — many phones now auto-label calls from known VoIP ranges as "Potential Spam" before your contact even picks up
3. **Look normal under reverse lookup** — services like Truecaller, Spokeo, and BeenVerified categorize VoIP numbers differently. A number flagged as "VoIP — unverified" looks suspicious next to a standard mobile number

Real mobile numbers from Alt Number and Hushed avoid these issues. VoIP numbers from Google Voice and TextNow do not.

If your use case involves giving the number to people who are meeting you for the first time — whether that's a new client, a date, or a marketplace buyer — number quality is the most important variable in this decision.

## FAQ

**Is using a phone number alias for work legal?**

Yes. Virtual and alias phone numbers are used by businesses and individuals across industries for exactly this purpose: maintaining a professional contact point separate from a personal number. There is nothing deceptive about giving clients a dedicated work number rather than your personal cell. The legal line is using a number for fraud or harassment — the tool itself is neutral and widely used.

**Will clients know my work number is an alias?**

No, not from the number itself. A real mobile number from Alt Number or Hushed is indistinguishable from a carrier-issued number to the person receiving it. Reverse lookup services may identify VoIP numbers (Google Voice, TextNow) as virtual — another reason the number type distinction matters for work credibility.

**Can I use a phone number alias for two-factor authentication at work?**

Generally yes, but with caveats. Most 2FA SMS systems accept real mobile numbers without issue. VoIP numbers are inconsistently supported — some providers accept them, some reject them. If 2FA is a primary use case, use a service that provides real mobile numbers (Alt Number or Hushed paid tier).

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my alias if I cancel the subscription?**

With Alt Number, the number is released after a grace period when you cancel or downgrade your Surfshark subscription. With Hushed, the number expires when your paid period ends. With Google Voice, the number stays active as long as your Google account is active (though it gets reclaimed after 6 months of inactivity). Plan your handoff before canceling if the number is in active use with clients.

**Can I port my existing number to an alias service?**

Google Voice supports US number port-in for a one-time fee. Alt Number and Hushed do not support inbound porting — you get a number from their pool. If keeping your existing number is essential, Google Voice is currently the only option here, despite its other limitations.

**How do I handle voicemail on an alias number?**

All four providers include voicemail within their apps. Alt Number and Hushed let you record a custom greeting. Google Voice has robust voicemail with transcription. If you're using the alias professionally, set a voicemail greeting that sounds like a normal work line — clients who reach voicemail shouldn't experience anything that signals they've reached a [virtual number](https://snipstack.io/virtual-number-for-hinge-account-registration-2026/).
