## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect through a VPN, your device routes its internet traffic through an encrypted tunnel to a server in a location you choose. From the perspective of any website or app you visit, your connection appears to originate from that server's location — not your actual physical location.

Two practical outcomes matter here:

1. **Your traffic is encrypted** — even on a public WiFi network, your data is scrambled in transit, making it much harder for anyone on the same network to intercept it.
2. **Your apparent location changes** — if you connect to a VPN server back home, services see your home country's IP address, not a European one.

That is the entire mechanic. Everything else — streaming, banking, privacy — flows from those two properties.

## Why European Travel Specifically Creates Problems

### Hotel and Café WiFi Is a Real Risk

Public WiFi networks — in hotels, airport lounges, cafés, train stations — are shared environments. On an unencrypted connection, a knowledgeable person on the same network could potentially read traffic that is not already protected by HTTPS. Most modern websites use HTTPS, which adds a layer of protection, but not every app or service does. A VPN encrypts your connection before it leaves your device, adding a meaningful layer of protection regardless of what the destination service uses.

![Step-by-step guide: how a VPN encrypts your connection on hotel WiFi](/og/best-vpn-for-travel-europe-2026-inline-1.png)

### Your Streaming Library Disappears at the Border

Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, and most other streaming platforms license content by territory. The library available in Germany is different from the one available in the US, the UK, or Australia. When you land in Europe, your streaming service detects your European IP address and switches to the local catalogue — which may be missing entire series or films you planned to watch.

Connecting your VPN to a server in your home country before opening the streaming app restores the library your subscription is built around. You are accessing content your subscription already covers, from a location where you have a valid account — you are simply tunneling through your home server to get there.

### Banking Apps Often Block Unfamiliar Locations

Many banks flag logins from a foreign country as a potential security event. Depending on the bank, this can trigger a multi-step verification, temporarily lock the account, or refuse the login entirely until you contact support. Connecting through a VPN server back home can reduce the likelihood of these flags because the bank sees your usual location.

One important caveat: some banks also detect and block known VPN IP ranges. Results vary by bank and by VPN provider. A VPN reduces the friction in many cases, but it is not a universal fix — if you know a trip is coming, notify your bank in advance as a parallel step.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is using a VPN legal in Europe?

Yes. VPN use is legal throughout the European Union and in virtually all European countries popular with tourists — France, Germany, Spain, Italy, the Netherlands, Portugal, and others. Some countries outside the EU have restrictions, but for standard Western European travel destinations, you will not encounter legal issues.

### Will a VPN slow down my internet connection?

Some speed reduction is normal, since your traffic is being routed through an additional server. With a quality provider like those listed above, the reduction is usually small enough that HD streaming remains smooth. The biggest variable is the quality of your base connection — a fast hotel WiFi connection through a VPN will still feel fast. A weak connection will feel weak regardless.

### Can I use a free VPN for travel in Europe?

[Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) are generally not recommended for this use case. Most free options impose data caps (often 500MB-1GB per month — not enough for streaming), have slower speeds, and may monetize your usage data in ways that undermine the privacy benefit. For actual travel use, a paid monthly subscription is more reliable and typically costs less than a single meal out.

### Will a VPN definitely fix my banking app abroad?

A VPN reduces the likelihood of location-based flags by presenting your home IP address to your bank. In many cases this helps. However, some banks also block IP ranges associated with known [VPN providers](https://snipstack.io/vpn-providers-best-2026/), which can create different complications. The most reliable approach is to use a VPN and notify your bank of your travel dates before departing — use both together rather than relying on either alone.

### How many devices can I connect at once with Surfshark?

Surfshark allows unlimited simultaneous device connections on a single subscription. This means you can have your laptop, phone, tablet, and any other devices all connected to the VPN at the same time under one account — a practical advantage over competitors that cap connections at five or six devices.

### Do I need a VPN if most sites use HTTPS already?

HTTPS encrypts the content of your connections, but your device still broadcasts metadata — which sites you are visiting, when, and for how long — to anyone monitoring the network. A VPN encrypts this layer too, and additionally protects traffic from apps that do not use HTTPS. On a shared public network, the additional layer a VPN provides is meaningful.
