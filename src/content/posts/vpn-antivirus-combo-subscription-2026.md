## Step 1 — Cancel Your Norton or McAfee Subscription

Do this *before* anything else, so you are not charged for another year.

**For Norton:**
1. Go to norton.com and log into your account.
2. Click your name in the top-right corner, then go to **My Subscriptions**.
3. Find the active subscription and click **Turn Off Auto-Renew**.
4. Confirm the cancellation. You will see a confirmation screen — screenshot it.

**For McAfee:**
1. Go to my.mcafee.com and sign in.
2. Under **My Account**, click **Subscriptions**.
3. Click **Cancel** next to the active plan and follow the prompts.
4. McAfee will often try to offer a discount at this step. Decline and complete the cancellation.

Note: canceling auto-renewal lets your current coverage run until the expiry date. You do not lose protection immediately — you are just stopping the next charge.

## Step 2 — Uninstall Your Existing [Security Suite](https://snipstack.io/security-suite-for-unlimited-devices-2026/)

This is the step most people skip or do badly. A partial uninstall leaves registry entries and background processes that conflict with new security software. Do it properly.

**Windows — using the dedicated removal tool (recommended):**

Norton and McAfee both publish official removal tools that do a cleaner job than the standard Add/Remove Programs uninstall.

1. Search for **"Norton Remove and Reinstall tool"** or **"McAfee Consumer Product Removal tool"** and download from the official support site.
2. Close all browser windows and running programs.
3. Run the removal tool as Administrator (right-click the file, select "Run as administrator").
4. Follow the on-screen steps. The tool will restart your computer once complete.
5. After restarting, check **Task Manager** (Ctrl + Shift + Esc) under the Processes tab — you should not see Norton or McAfee processes listed.

**Mac:**
1. Open Finder → Applications.
2. Drag the Norton or McAfee app to Trash.
3. Empty the Trash.
4. For a thorough cleanup, also check **Library/Application Support** for leftover folders.

**Why this matters:** Running two antivirus programs simultaneously causes conflicts, false alerts, and significant slowdowns. A clean removal takes five extra minutes and prevents headaches later.

## Troubleshooting — Common Issues After Switching

**"The antivirus flagged a file that seems legitimate."**
This is called a false positive. Right-click the flagged file in the [Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-kaspersky-comparison-2026/) results and choose **Mark as safe** or **Exclude from scan**. This happens occasionally with older software installers or developer tools.

**"VPN slows down my internet."**
Connect to a server closer to your geographic location — use the **Fastest server** option in the VPN menu rather than picking manually. VPN overhead is typically 10–20ms of added latency, which does not affect streaming or browsing for most connections.

**"I still see Norton/McAfee prompts after uninstalling."**
Reboot your computer and check Programs and Features (Windows) or Applications (Mac) again. If entries remain, run the dedicated removal tool again as described in Step 2. Browser toolbars from these suites sometimes require manual removal via your browser's Extensions settings.

**"Alert is not finding my email in breaches — is it working?"**
Alert checks against known breach databases. If your email has not appeared in any documented breach, Alert will show clear — that is a good result. The service sends notifications when new breaches are discovered; it monitors ongoing, not just at setup.

**"My phone is not covered yet."**
Download the Surfshark app on your phone from the App Store or Google Play and log in with the same email. One [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-plan-comparison-2026/) subscription covers unlimited devices simultaneously.
