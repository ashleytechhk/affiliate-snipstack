## SimpleLogin: The Best Free Alternative

SimpleLogin (now owned by Proton) is the strongest standalone free option. You get up to 10 aliases on the free plan, with unlimited aliases on the paid Pro tier (~$4/month). It supports two-way replies, has browser extensions for all major browsers, and integrates natively with Proton Mail.

For light users who only need a few aliases for occasional price comparison shopping, SimpleLogin free is perfectly adequate. Power users who register on dozens of sites every month will hit the 10-alias ceiling quickly and need to upgrade or pay.

**Limitation:** No identity masking. SimpleLogin handles the email layer only — it does not generate a fake name or date of birth to pair with the alias.

## Apple Hide My Email: Good, But Locked In

If you use an iPhone and pay for iCloud+, Hide My Email is already available to you. It generates a random `@privaterelay.appleid.com` address that forwards to your Apple ID email. Setup is seamless on Safari and iOS.

The catch is the Apple ecosystem lock-in. It only works reliably in Safari and on Apple devices. If you switch to Android or use Chrome to browse a price comparison site, Hide My Email is awkward to use. It also offers no reply-from-alias feature and no identity masking layer.

For Apple-only households who browse on iPhone and Mac, it is a solid no-cost option. For everyone else, the cross-platform tools win.
