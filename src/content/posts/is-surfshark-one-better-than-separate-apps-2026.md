## Renewal Pricing: The Norton and McAfee Problem

Both Norton and McAfee rely heavily on introductory pricing that doubles or triples at renewal. This is not a secret — it is their documented business model.

Norton 360 Standard is often advertised at $39.99/yr. Year two: commonly $99.99 or more. McAfee Total Protection follows a similar pattern — introductory rates in the $30–50 range, renewals at $80–120.

These products count on inertia. Switching security software feels like effort. Reinstalling, reconfiguring, double-checking nothing slipped through during the transition — it is enough friction that most people just pay the renewal and quietly resent it.

If you have been a Norton or McAfee customer for more than one year and have not checked your actual renewal price recently, do that now. The number in your billing portal may be the push you needed.

Surfshark uses introductory pricing too, but the gap between intro and renewal rates is narrower and more clearly communicated. There are no in-app upsell banners nudging you to upgrade between renewals.
