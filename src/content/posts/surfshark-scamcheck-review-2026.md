## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Email Address Checking

**Surfshark Scamcheck** checks the sender's email address against breach records and phishing databases. This catches two types of risk: addresses that have been used in known scam campaigns, and addresses that have appeared in data breaches — meaning a real account may have been compromised and taken over by a scammer.

**HaveIBeenPwned** checks whether an email address appears in known data breaches, which is useful for checking your own address. It does not flag addresses used in phishing campaigns, and it does not check URLs.

**VirusTotal** accepts email addresses but the output is designed for security analysts — you will see a series of vendor scan results with pass/fail codes. There is no plain-language summary.

**Winner for non-technical users: Surfshark Scamcheck.**

### URL and Link Checking

**Surfshark Scamcheck** checks web links against phishing and malware databases and reports the result simply. You can check a full URL or a shortened link — it follows the redirect to the real destination before checking.

**VirusTotal** checks URLs against more than 70 security engines, which gives it broader coverage than any single-database tool. The trade-off is the output: VirusTotal presents all 70+ results as individual rows, which is genuinely useful if you know what you are looking at and overwhelming if you do not.

**ScamAdviser** specialises in checking website domains rather than full URLs. It assesses site age, hosting location, and trust ratings. It is strong for checking whether a whole website is legitimate, but less focused on checking a specific link in a suspicious email.

**Google Safe Browsing** runs in the background of Chrome, Safari, and Firefox — it warns you when you are about to visit a flagged site. But it is not a tool you interact with directly to check a link before clicking. You cannot paste a URL into it.

**Winner for checking a specific suspicious link: Surfshark Scamcheck** for ease of use; VirusTotal if you want the broadest possible scan and are comfortable reading technical output.

## Limitations Worth Knowing

No tool catches everything. Here is what to keep in mind:

**New scam sites may not yet be indexed.** A phishing page created yesterday may not appear in any database yet. If you receive an unexpected email from a brand you use and something feels wrong, contact that organisation directly regardless of what the checker says.

**Display names can be spoofed.** Surfshark Scamcheck works on the real email address, not the display name. Make sure you are checking the actual address shown after the @ symbol, not the friendly name the scammer chose to display.

**It is a checker, not a filter.** Scamcheck tells you about messages you actively submit. It does not sit between your inbox and incoming mail to block things automatically — that is your email provider's spam filter. Think of it as a second opinion tool for the messages that get through.
