## Why Figma Accounts Attract Spam

Figma is not just a design tool — it is a platform with an ecosystem, and that ecosystem is what feeds your inbox.

**Plugin and resource marketplaces.** The Figma Community hosts thousands of plugins, UI kits, and templates. Many require you to provide an email to download, unlock a premium version, or access a creator's asset library. Each of those is a separate email list with its own policies.

**Team and organization invites.** When you are added to a Figma team or organization, your email is shared with workspace administrators and potentially with any third-party integrations the team has enabled — Slack, Jira, Notion, and others. The chain of exposure grows with each integration.

**Figma's own marketing.** Figma sends product update emails, release notes, webinar invitations, and "tips" digests. These are legitimate communications, but they add to volume — and opt-out preferences tend to reset with major account or plan changes.

**Third-party design tools that integrate with Figma.** Prototyping tools, handoff platforms, design system managers, and feedback tools all offer "connect with Figma" OAuth flows. Each platform that accesses your Figma account through OAuth may capture your registered email address separately.

**Data broker scraping.** If your address appears in a breach tied to any of these services, it ends up on broker lists that circulate for years. By the time you are receiving 50+ unwanted emails a day, the address has passed through dozens of resale cycles.

An alias stops that chain at the source: Figma never sees your real address, so none of its ecosystem partners do either.
