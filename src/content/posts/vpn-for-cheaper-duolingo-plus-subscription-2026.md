## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in a location you choose before it reaches the website or app you are using. Instead of that site seeing your real IP address (and the country it is tied to), it sees the VPN server's IP address instead. Connect to a server in Argentina, and most websites will treat you as if you are browsing from Argentina — even if you are actually sitting in your kitchen in another country entirely.

This is the mechanism behind almost every "VPN for cheaper subscription" tip circulating online: streaming price arbitrage, regional game pricing, and yes, app subscriptions like Duolingo Plus. The VPN itself does not talk to Duolingo's billing system or negotiate a discount. It simply changes what location gets reported at the moment you connect.

## Why Does Duolingo Plus Cost Different Amounts in Different Countries?

Duolingo Plus — rebranded Duolingo Super in 2023, though a lot of people still search the old name out of habit — removes ads, unlocks unlimited hearts, and adds progress tracking on top of the free app. Like Spotify, Netflix, and YouTube Premium before it, Duolingo prices that tier using a strategy commonly called purchasing power parity, or PPP pricing.

The idea behind PPP pricing is straightforward: a flat global price would be unaffordable in lower-income markets and would leave money on the table in high-income ones. So instead, the same subscription is priced relative to local income levels and market conditions. A subscriber in a market with lower average income and a weaker currency might pay a fraction of what a subscriber in a wealthier market pays, for identical access to the app.

This is not a pricing bug or an exploit Duolingo is unaware of — it is a deliberate, common practice across the subscription app industry. The side effect is that forums and social media are full of screenshots comparing what people pay for the same app in different countries, which is exactly what fuels searches for a VPN workaround.

## How Region-Switching Is Supposed to Work

In theory, the process looks simple:

1. Connect your VPN to a server in a lower-priced country.
2. Go to Duolingo's website (not the app) and start a new Plus/Super subscription.
3. If the checkout page prices based on your detected IP location, you see that country's rate.
4. Complete the purchase with a payment method that will process the transaction.

![Diagram showing how a VPN reroutes a connection through a lower-priced country before checkout](/og/vpn-for-cheaper-duolingo-plus-subscription-2026-inline-1.png)

Some web checkouts genuinely do work this way — pricing is calculated from the IP address at the moment of purchase, with no further verification. Others check your IP alongside your billing address, your card's issuing country, or your account's language and locale settings, and will flag a mismatch. Which behavior applies can change without notice, since pricing logic is not something companies publish or hold constant.

## Where to Actually Try This: Website Checkout, Not the App

The detail that determines whether region-switching has a shot at working is where you complete the purchase. Do it through Duolingo's website with your VPN connected to the target country, not through the iOS or Android app.

That distinction matters because Apple and Google price in-app subscriptions by the country registered on your App Store or Google Play account, not by your IP address at checkout — a VPN has no effect on that account-level setting. Duolingo's own web checkout is a different system, and in cases where it prices by IP address at signup, a VPN pointed at a lower-cost region is the piece that can actually influence the number you see.

So the practical takeaway is simple: run the web checkout, not the app, if a lower regional price is the goal. Trying to force the same result inside the mobile app is the version of this that reliably goes nowhere, since the account-region setting overrides whatever your VPN reports.
