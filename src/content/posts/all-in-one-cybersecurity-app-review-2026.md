## The Problem With Legacy [Security Suite](https://snipstack.io/security-suite-for-android-and-windows-together-2026/)s

### Bloatware That Slows You Down

Norton and McAfee were built in an era when security software's job was to scan files and quarantine viruses. They did that job and expanded — adding password managers, parental controls, identity services, cloud backup — without rethinking the underlying architecture. The result is software with a heavy installation footprint, multiple background services competing for CPU and memory, and a notification tray that never goes quiet.

Users on machines with 8 GB of RAM or less — which still describes a significant share of Windows laptops in daily use — report noticeably slower boot times and background sluggishness during scan cycles. McAfee is the more aggressive offender, but Norton 360 is not lightweight either. For a tool whose purpose is to stay invisible while protecting you, that kind of friction is a genuine cost.

### Renewal Pricing Designed to Catch You Off Guard

The introductory pricing model used by both Norton and McAfee is built on one assumption: most users will not comparison-shop at renewal time. A Norton 360 plan at $35 in year one commonly renews at $80–110. McAfee runs a similar playbook. The terms are disclosed in small print, but the gap between promotional and renewal rates is wide enough that users routinely express surprise when the invoice arrives.

[Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-pricing-changes-2026/) uses a multi-year pricing model that is more honest about what you will pay after the first term. You are buying two years upfront at a lower rate — and the renewal rate, while higher per month than the introductory rate, is not designed to triple without warning.

### The VPN Gap

Norton 360 includes an unlimited VPN on all current plans, which is a genuine upgrade from its earlier offering. McAfee Total Protection on standard tiers caps the VPN at 5 GB per day — which is roughly one HD streaming session before the cap hits. Neither VPN has been independently audited for its no-logs policy the way [Surfshark's has](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-mcafee-total-protection-2026/).

For users who plan to use the VPN for anything beyond occasional light browsing — streaming, remote work, public Wi-Fi on a regular basis — this matters.

A privacy-respecting search engine that does not log your queries or build an advertising profile. Not every user needs a default search engine change, but for privacy-focused users who want to reduce their data footprint, having it included rather than priced separately is a clear extra.
