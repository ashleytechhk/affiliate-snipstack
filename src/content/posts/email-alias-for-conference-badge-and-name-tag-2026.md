## Why Your Real Email Address Does Not Belong on a Name Tag

When you print your email on a conference badge you are effectively publishing it to everyone in the venue — and to every partner, sponsor, and exhibitor who gets access to the attendee list.

Event organizers routinely share attendee data with sponsors as part of their revenue model. Badge-scanning apps capture your details the moment a vendor waves a phone at your lanyard. That data gets entered into CRMs, enriched with third-party data brokers, and then you are in a sequence for the next 18 months.

The solution is not to skip networking. It is to use a different address on the badge — one that forwards to your real inbox but that you can terminate whenever you choose.

### What Makes a Good Conference Alias?

Not all alias tools are equal for this specific use case. A good conference badge alias needs:

- **Instant creation** — you should be able to generate it in under 60 seconds at the registration desk
- **Reliable forwarding** — misdelivered follow-ups mean missed genuine connections
- **Easy disabling** — kill-switch after the event without touching your real email settings
- **Optional custom domain** — looks professional on a badge (`hello@yourdomain.com` vs. `abc123@noreply.net`)
- **No personal data stored at the alias provider** — the whole point is privacy

## SimpleLogin: Best Free Option With Custom Domains

SimpleLogin is open-source, respected in privacy circles, and free for up to 10 aliases. The paid tier ($4/month) unlocks unlimited aliases and custom domain support — useful if you want `contact@yourprofessionaldomain.com` on your badge rather than a system-generated address.

The dashboard is clean. Creating a new alias takes about 30 seconds. Forwarding is reliable. Where SimpleLogin falls short for conference use is the identity layer — it handles email only, so if a form asks for your name, you are on your own for deciding what to enter.

SimpleLogin was acquired by Proton in 2022 and remains a solid, trusted service. For budget-conscious professionals who only need [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-healthcare-appointment-reminders-2026/)ing (no alt identity), it is a strong choice.

## DuckDuckGo Email Protection: Zero-Cost, Zero-Friction

DuckDuckGo Email Protection gives every user a free `@duck.com` forwarding address and also strips email trackers (open-pixel tracking beacons) before delivering messages to your real inbox. There is no paid tier — it is entirely free.

The catch for conference use: `@duck.com` addresses are not customizable, so your badge reads something like `j.smith.7q2x@duck.com`. That works fine if privacy is the goal and professional polish is secondary. If you are at a sales or exec-level event where your email address signals personal brand, the format may feel too anonymous.

Forwarding is reliable and the tracker-stripping feature is a genuine bonus for post-conference follow-up emails.

## Apple Hide My Email: Seamless for iPhone Users

If you live in the Apple ecosystem, iCloud+ ($0.99/month or bundled with higher iCloud storage tiers) gives you Hide My Email — one-tap alias generation that integrates directly into Safari autofill and the Mail app on iOS and macOS.

Creating a badge alias is fast: go to iCloud Settings, tap Hide My Email, create a new address. It generates something like `abc.xyz@privaterelay.appleid.com`. You can toggle each alias on or off individually.

The limitation for conference professionals: no custom domain, no identity layer, and the service is Apple-only. Android users cannot use it, and managing aliases requires staying within the Apple ecosystem. It is excellent if you are already paying for iCloud+, but it is not a reason to start.

## AnonAddy: Power Users Who Want Full Control

AnonAddy (now rebranded as addy.io) is another open-source option with a generous free tier: unlimited aliases on shared domains, with 10MB/month of forwarding bandwidth. The paid tiers start at $1/month and unlock higher bandwidth, custom domains, and unlimited recipients.

For conference use it is capable but slightly technical — the interface is less polished than SimpleLogin or Alt-ID. It suits privacy power users who want to self-host or who need very high alias volume. For most conference-goers it is overkill.

## FAQ

### Can I use an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-investment-account-notifications-2026/) on a printed conference badge?

Yes. Any [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-separate-shopping-from-work-email-2026/) that forwards to your real inbox works on a printed badge. The alias receives emails just like a standard address — the alias provider forwards them to your actual inbox. The only difference is you can disable the alias after the event to stop unwanted follow-ups.

### Will senders know they are emailing an alias?

In most cases, no. The alias address looks like a normal email address. Some alias providers do use recognizable domains (like `@duck.com` or `@privaterelay.appleid.com`) which may signal to technically savvy senders that it is a relay, but the forwarding itself is transparent.

### [Does using](https://snipstack.io/does-using-email-alias-protect-from-spam-2026/) an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-service-that-works-with-apple-mail-2026/) affect email deliverability?

Occasionally. Some senders' spam filters flag forwarded email because the originating server differs from the alias domain. This can cause legitimate emails to land in your spam folder. The major providers (SimpleLogin, Alt-ID, DuckDuckGo) manage their sender reputations to minimize this, but it is worth checking your spam folder for the first week after an event.

### [Is Surfshark](https://snipstack.io/is-surfshark-one-good-for-streaming-and-privacy-2026/) Alt-ID available as a standalone product?

Alt-ID is currently bundled with [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-senior-tech-novice-easy-to-use-2026/) and [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-two-year-plan-pricing-breakdown-2026/)+ subscription tiers. It is not sold as a standalone product. If you only need an alias tool, SimpleLogin or DuckDuckGo may be more cost-efficient. If you want a broader privacy suite, the bundle often works out cheaper than subscribing to each tool separately. [See current Alt-ID plans here](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-conference-badge-and-name-tag-2026__faq).

### How quickly can I disable an alias after a conference?

Most alias tools let you disable or delete an address immediately from a web dashboard or mobile app. With Alt-ID, you toggle forwarding off in the Surfshark app. With SimpleLogin, you flip a switch in the alias list. Disabling typically takes effect within minutes — any mail sent to the alias after that point is either bounced or silently dropped, depending on the provider's setting.

### Should I use a different alias for every conference?

It depends on your threat model. Using one dedicated "events" alias for all conferences means the alias becomes linkable across events over time — a data broker who sees the same email on two attendee lists can still build a profile. Creating a fresh alias per event provides stronger separation. Alt-ID and SimpleLogin both make per-event alias creation fast enough to be practical.
