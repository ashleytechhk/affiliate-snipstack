## What Is a VPN for PS5 Gaming?

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in another location. When you connect to a VPN server in, say, the United Kingdom or Australia, websites and online services see that server's IP address instead of yours. To them, you appear to be browsing from that country.

For PS5 gaming, this means two things:

1. **Overseas server access** — You can connect to game servers in other regions, which may have shorter queues, different player pools, or exclusive regional events.
2. **Regional PlayStation Store pricing** — Some countries have significantly lower prices for the same games and PlayStation Plus subscriptions, and a VPN can let you browse and buy from those storefronts.

The PS5 itself does not have a native VPN app. Sony does not allow VPN clients to be installed directly on the console. The workaround is to run the VPN on your router (so all devices on your home network use it), or to share a VPN-protected connection from a PC or Mac to your PS5 via a wired or wireless hotspot.

## Does a VPN Improve Gaming Lag?

This is the question every PS5 gamer asks first, and the honest answer is: sometimes, not always.

A VPN adds an extra step to your traffic — your data travels to the VPN server before reaching the game server. This typically adds a small amount of latency. However, in certain cases, a VPN can actually reduce ping:

- If your ISP is throttling gaming traffic, a VPN can bypass that throttling
- If there is a more efficient network route available through the VPN server, you may see lower latency to specific game servers
- Connecting to a regional server that is geographically closer to a game's server cluster can help

The practical takeaway: do not expect a VPN to magically cut your ping in half. Use it primarily for the server access and pricing benefits, and test different VPN server locations if you want to optimise for speed.
