## Why Canal+ Is Geo-Locked to France

Canal+'s content licensing is sold territory by territory, the same way most broadcast rights work globally. Ligue 1 rights, Champions League rights, and film distribution deals are all negotiated country by country, and letting a French-licensed platform stream freely worldwide would undercut every one of those separate regional deals. So myCANAL checks the IP address behind every playback request, and only French IPs pass.

There's one wrinkle worth knowing: under EU cross-border portability rules, subscribers who normally live in France can access their Canal+ subscription while *temporarily* travelling elsewhere in the EU — a work trip to Berlin or a holiday in Spain, for example. That exemption is built for short-term travel, not for someone who has relocated permanently or who is outside the EU altogether. For anyone in that second group — expats, long-term travellers, or viewers outside Europe entirely — myCANAL simply reads a non-French IP and blocks the stream.

That's the gap a VPN closes: it makes your connection look like it's coming from inside France, regardless of where the portability rule would or wouldn't apply.

## How a VPN Works for Canal+

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — routes your internet traffic through an encrypted tunnel to a server operated by the VPN provider. Connect to a server in France and:

1. Your real IP address is hidden from myCANAL's servers.
2. Your traffic appears to originate from the VPN's French IP.
3. myCANAL's geo-check reads "France" and grants playback.

This isn't a workaround exploiting a bug — it's presenting a French IP address, which is the only thing the geo-check actually verifies. The rest of your connection also gets encrypted while the VPN is active, a useful bonus if you're streaming over hotel or airport Wi-Fi.
