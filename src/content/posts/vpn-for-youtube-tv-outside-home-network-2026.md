## What Does "Outside Home Network" Mean for YouTube TV?

YouTube TV uses your IP address to determine your home area. When you first sign up, it locks your account to a specific local market — usually the metro area where you activated the service. That market determines which local broadcast channels (ABC, NBC, CBS, FOX affiliates) appear in your guide.

The moment your IP address moves outside that home area — because you are on a hotel Wi-Fi in another state, traveling abroad, or working remotely in a different city — YouTube TV detects the mismatch. It does not immediately cancel your account, but it will:

- Remove local channels from your guide within a short period.
- Warn you to update your home area if the system detects a prolonged location change.
- Restrict certain regional sports blackouts tied to your original market.

YouTube TV's terms allow for two home area updates per year. If you exceed that, or if your IP location consistently contradicts your declared home, the service may restrict your local content.

A VPN solves this by keeping your apparent IP address pinned to your original home region, regardless of where you are physically sitting.

## How a VPN for YouTube TV Outside Home Network Actually Works

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. All your traffic exits through that server, so websites and apps see the server's IP address — not your real one.

Here is the sequence for YouTube TV:

1. You open your VPN app and connect to a server located in your home city or state.
2. YouTube TV receives a connection request from that server's IP address.
3. Because that IP is in your home region, YouTube TV serves your local channels and live content normally.
4. Your actual location — hotel room in Tokyo, apartment in Toronto, wherever — is invisible to YouTube TV.
