## What Is an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/how-email-alias-2026/) (and Why You Need One)?

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-sign-2026/) is a forwarding address — it looks like a real email, accepts messages, and routes them to your actual inbox. The sender never sees your real address. When that alias starts attracting spam, you turn it off. Your real address stays untouched.

**Common use cases:**

- Signing up for e-commerce sites you don't fully trust
- Entering sweepstakes or giveaways
- Downloading free software that requires registration
- Trying out a new app or SaaS product
- Submitting your address to a forum or community

Without aliases, every sign-up is a permanent handover of your real email to a third party. With aliases, it's a temporary, revocable loan.

## Troubleshooting

**I created an alias but emails are not arriving.**
Check Step 3. The forwarding email may not be confirmed. Go to Alt-ID settings, verify the address shown is correct, and look for a verification email in that inbox (including spam folder).

**The alias exists but I can't find it in the dashboard.**
Make sure you are logged in to the correct Surfshark account. If you have multiple accounts, it is easy to check the wrong one.

**I'm getting spam on my real address even though I used an alias.**
The alias is forwarding to your real address, which is expected behavior. The alias itself is clean — the spam count tells you which service is the problem. Deactivate or delete that alias to stop further forwarding.

**I accidentally deleted an alias I needed.**
Deleted aliases cannot be recovered. You will need to create a new one and update your account with the service that used the old address.

**The browser extension is not appearing on certain sites.**
Some sites block browser extensions from injecting into input fields. In those cases, copy the alias from the dashboard manually.

**Can I reply from an alias so my real email stays hidden?**
Yes, but it requires a small extra step. Most desktop email clients support adding a "send as" alias in account settings. Gmail calls this "Send mail as" under Settings → Accounts. Once configured, replies appear to come from the alias address.

**Will my real email address ever be visible to senders?**
Not through the alias itself. However, if you reply from your real inbox without configuring the "send as" feature, the reply will show your real address. Always reply through the alias if privacy matters for that conversation.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to emails sent to a deactivated alias?**
They are silently discarded. The sender will not receive a bounce or error message — the email simply disappears. This is intentional: it avoids confirming to spammers that the address was ever active.
