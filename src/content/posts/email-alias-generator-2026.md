## How an Email Alias Generator Works

Before the steps, a quick mental model. An email alias generator creates a secondary address — sometimes called a masked email or forwarding alias — that is entirely separate from your real inbox. When someone sends email to that alias, it arrives in your real inbox as normal. The sender never sees your actual address.

The key difference from a [temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-and-password-free-2026/) is permanence. Throwaway services (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail) give you an inbox that expires in minutes. An alias generator gives you a permanent address you control — one you can keep for years or delete the second it starts attracting spam.

## Method 1: Use a Free Browser-Based Generator for One-Off Sign-Ups

![Email alias generator dashboard showing a masked address being created with one click](/og/email-alias-generator-2026-inline-1.png)

Free generators are useful when you need an address right now for something you will use exactly once.

### Step 1 — Open a Free Generator in Your Browser

Navigate to one of these well-known options:

- **SimpleLogin** (simplelogin.io) — free tier gives 10 aliases; aliases forward to your real inbox
- **AnonAddy** (addy.io) — free tier includes up to 20 aliases with forwarding
- **Guerrilla Mail** (guerrillamail.com) — instant throwaway, no sign-up, no forwarding, expires in ~1 hour

### Step 2 — Generate an Alias

For SimpleLogin or AnonAddy, create a free account, then click **Create alias** or **New alias**. The service generates a random address (e.g., `forest.echo.749@simplelogin.com`). Copy it.

For Guerrilla Mail, an address appears on the homepage with no action required. Copy the address shown at the top.

### Step 3 — Paste It Into the Sign-Up Form

Use the generated alias anywhere a site asks for your email. Submit the form as normal.

### Step 4 — Check Your Real Inbox

For SimpleLogin and AnonAddy: the confirmation email arrives in your real inbox via forwarding, usually within seconds. For Guerrilla Mail: return to the Guerrilla Mail tab to find the confirmation email — it does not forward anywhere.

### Step 5 — Confirm Your Registration

Click the confirmation link in the email. You are registered. Your real address was never shared.

**Where free generators fall short:** The free tiers cap you at 10–20 aliases. Hit the ceiling and you either pay for a plan or start juggling multiple accounts. Guerrilla Mail does not forward at all — you must babysit the tab. And many sites now block known alias domains, so your sign-up attempt fails before it starts.

For people receiving 50+ spam emails a day, a 10-alias cap and a blocklist problem are not solutions — they are just different constraints.

## Troubleshooting

![Surfshark Alt-ID alias management dashboard showing active and disabled aliases side by side](/og/email-alias-generator-2026-inline-2.png)

**The site is rejecting my alias address.**
Sites increasingly block known [temporary email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-and-password-2026/) domains. If a free generator's domain (simplelogin.com, addy.io) is on the site's blocklist, try Surfshark Alt-ID — the `surfshark.id` domain is not on most public blocklists and passes the majority of validation checks.

**Forwarded mail is landing in my spam folder.**
Add the forwarding domain to your safe senders list. In Gmail: open the misfiled email, click the three-dot menu, select **Filter messages like these**, then **Never send to spam**. In Outlook: **Settings → Mail → Junk email → Safe senders → Add**.

**I am not receiving forwarded mail at all.**
First, check your spam folder. If nothing is there, verify the email address you entered during Surfshark account setup — that is the delivery address for all forwarded mail. Log out of the Surfshark app and back in, then test with a fresh alias.

**I hit the alias limit on a free service.**
SimpleLogin and AnonAddy cap free accounts at 10 and 20 aliases respectively. If you need more, either upgrade their paid plans or switch to Surfshark Alt-ID, which does not publish a hard cap for standard subscribers.

**I forgot which alias I used for a particular service.**
This is why labelling aliases at creation time matters. If you forgot, check the "To:" header on any email from that service — it will show the alias address, not your real one. Match it against your Alt-ID dashboard to find and manage it.

**The auto-fill feature is not triggering.**
Make sure the Surfshark extension is enabled and you are signed in. Some sign-up forms use non-standard field names that auto-fill does not detect. In that case, open the extension manually, copy the alias, and paste it yourself.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is an email alias generator?**
An email alias generator creates a secondary email address that forwards messages to your real inbox. You use the alias on sign-up forms so websites and apps never see your actual address. If the alias starts attracting spam, you delete it without touching your real account.

**Are email aliases legal to use?**
Yes. Using an alias or masked email address is legal. You are simply choosing which address to share, the same way you might use a work number instead of your personal mobile. Some services prohibit disposable email addresses in their terms of service, but using one is not a legal violation.
