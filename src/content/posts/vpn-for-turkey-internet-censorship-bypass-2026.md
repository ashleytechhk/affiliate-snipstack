## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. It creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. Once connected, all your traffic flows through that tunnel — your ISP, your government's monitoring infrastructure, and any website you visit sees the VPN server's IP address, not yours.

Think of it like having a private courier service that picks up your letters, repacks them in a different envelope from a different city, and delivers them on your behalf. The recipient sees the courier's return address. The local post office — your ISP — only sees that you sent a package to the courier.

For users dealing with internet censorship, this means:

- **Blocked websites become accessible.** The block is applied to IP addresses and domain names associated with your country's traffic. Traffic flowing out of a VPN server in another country bypasses the block entirely.
- **Throttling disappears.** ISPs throttle certain types of traffic — like video streaming or social media — by identifying it. Encryption makes traffic type undetectable, so throttling rules cannot apply.
- **Your activity stays private.** Surveillance tools monitoring internet traffic at the ISP or network level cannot read encrypted VPN traffic.
