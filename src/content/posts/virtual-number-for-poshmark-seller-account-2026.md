## How a Real Phone Number Becomes a Privacy Problem

Most people think of their phone number as a minor detail — one step up from an email address in terms of sensitivity. That instinct is badly out of date.

Your phone number is now the key that unlocks your entire digital identity. Data broker sites like Spokeo, WhitePages, and BeenVerified index phone numbers alongside names, home addresses, and social media profiles. Anyone with $5 and five minutes can type your number into one of those sites and walk away with your full name and the city block where you live.

Poshmark, to its credit, does not publicly display your phone number on your seller profile. But the number is still attached to your account in ways that create risk:

- **Buyer disputes** sometimes escalate outside the app. A buyer who has your username and some persistence can cross-reference it with other platforms.
- **Data breaches** happen. Poshmark itself was breached in 2018, exposing usernames, hashed passwords, and email addresses. The next breach could include phone numbers.
- **Third-party integrations** — shipping apps, payment processors — may store your contact details with weaker security than you assume.

None of these risks are unique to Poshmark. The point is that every account you create with your real number adds one more surface where that number can leak.
