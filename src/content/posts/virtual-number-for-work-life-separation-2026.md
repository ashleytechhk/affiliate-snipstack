## The Data Broker Chain Most People Don't See

The risk isn't just from the match themselves. It's from the infrastructure behind the app.

Dating apps verify your number at signup — a reasonable anti-fraud measure. But once that number is in their database, its journey doesn't end there.

Dating apps are funded by advertising. They embed analytics and ad network SDKs that collect device and contact signals. Data brokers purchase, license, and aggregate this data across thousands of apps and services. Your dating app number gets matched to your public records, your employer information, your home zip code, and your social media presence. The resulting profile is sold to marketers, people-search sites, and anyone willing to pay.

The person who Googled your number three weeks after a bad first date didn't need to be a hacker. They just needed to know where to look — and data brokers made sure the answer was waiting.
