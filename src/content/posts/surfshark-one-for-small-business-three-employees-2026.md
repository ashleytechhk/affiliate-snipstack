## Why Three-Employee Teams Outgrow Norton and McAfee

The standard pitch for [Norton 360](https://snipstack.io/norton-360-antivirus-2026/) for Business or McAfee Business Protection is reasonable enough when you first sign up. You get antivirus, maybe a VPN add-on, and a management console. The problems surface at renewal time and during day-to-day use.

**The per-seat pricing trap.** Legacy business antivirus products charge per device or per seat. With three employees each running a laptop and a phone, you are already at six devices. Some plans cap at five. Upgrading to the next tier adds cost without adding features you actually need.

**The bloatware reality.** Norton's installation footprint on Windows is substantial. McAfee has a long reputation for slowing down older business laptops at startup. Neither is trivial to uninstall cleanly. For a small team without a dedicated IT person, this becomes a time drain.

**Fragmented renewals.** If you bought different products at different times — an antivirus here, a password manager there, a VPN bolt-on somewhere else — you are managing multiple billing dates, multiple support channels, and multiple sets of credentials. That is unnecessary friction for a three-person operation.

## What [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-black-friday-deal-prediction-2026/) Actually Includes

[Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-two-year-plan-pricing-breakdown-2026/) bundles four products into one subscription. Here is what matters for a small team.

### VPN — the workhorse feature

For a three-person team, VPN use cases include: working from coffee shops, connecting to business tools over public Wi-Fi, accessing region-restricted services for research, and keeping client communications off unsecured networks. Surfshark's VPN covers all of these with WireGuard protocol by default (fast, low overhead), a kill switch to prevent accidental unprotected connections, and split tunneling so employees can route only business traffic through the VPN while keeping personal browsing on their regular connection.

There is no bandwidth cap and no limit on simultaneous connections. All three employees plus their phones are covered under one account at no extra cost.

### Antivirus — lighter than it sounds

Surfshark Antivirus runs real-time protection on Windows and macOS. It is not as feature-heavy as Norton's full suite, but for a team whose primary threat model is phishing emails, malicious downloads, and infected USB drives, it covers the meaningful bases without the system slowdown associated with legacy products.

It does not include a firewall (Windows Firewall handles that natively) or endpoint management features like remote wipe — if those are hard requirements, [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-mcafee-plus-ultra-comparison-2026/) is not a complete replacement for a dedicated endpoint solution.

### Surfshark Alert — data breach monitoring

This is genuinely useful for small businesses. Alert monitors your registered email addresses against known data breaches and notifies you when credentials appear in leaked databases. For a team sharing business email addresses across multiple SaaS tools, early breach notification can be the difference between a contained incident and a compromised client account.

### Surfshark Search — clean research

Private, ad-free search that does not build a profile on your queries. Not essential, but useful for employees doing competitive research or sourcing vendor options without every search being fed back into an ad profile.

## Setting Up [Surfshark One for](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-college-student-laptop-and-phone-2026/) Three Employees

1. Purchase a single [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-digital-nomad-travel-setup-2026/) subscription (one account, unlimited devices).
2. Install the app on each device — Windows, macOS, iOS, Android are all supported.
3. Each employee logs in with the shared account credentials, or you can set up individual accounts depending on your preference for usage separation.
4. Configure the kill switch and auto-connect on startup for each device so protection is always on.

## What [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-pricing-per-month-breakdown-2026/) Does Not Replace

It is worth being honest about the gaps.

**Password management.** [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-eset-smart-security-premium-2026/) does not include a password manager. If your team currently uses Norton's password manager or McAfee's equivalent, you will need a separate tool — Bitwarden (free tier is generous) or 1Password for Teams are the common alternatives.

**Centralized device management.** No remote lock, remote wipe, or device compliance dashboards. For a team handling sensitive client data under compliance requirements, this matters. For a typical service business or freelance team, it is not a gap you will feel.

**Email filtering / spam gateway.** [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-intego-mac-security-bundle-2026/) does not sit in front of your email. For phishing protection at the email layer, you are relying on your email provider (Google Workspace and Microsoft 365 both include reasonable anti-phishing) rather than a dedicated gateway.

## FAQ

### Can all three employees use [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-total-av-internet-security-2026/) simultaneously?

Yes. [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-cheapest-price-strategy-2026/) allows unlimited simultaneous connections on a single subscription. All three employees can be connected at the same time on as many devices as they use.

### Do we need one account per employee or one shared account?

Surfshark's model is one account per subscription. In practice, most small teams share the account credentials for simplicity. If you want separate login histories or usage logs per employee, you would need separate subscriptions — though for most three-person teams the single-account approach works fine.

### [Is Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/is-surfshark-one-worth-it-for-a-single-device-user-2026/) compliant with GDPR or HIPAA requirements?

Surfshark operates under a no-logs VPN policy and is subject to Dutch/EU law. This can support GDPR compliance posture, but [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-avast-one-comparison-2026/) alone does not constitute a complete compliance program for HIPAA-regulated businesses or PCI-DSS requirements. Consult your compliance requirements separately.

### How [does Surfshark](https://snipstack.io/does-surfshark-one-include-email-protection-2026/) Antivirus compare to Norton for real-time protection?

Independent AV testing labs (AV-Test, AV-Comparatives) have historically given Norton higher marks for malware detection rates. Surfshark Antivirus is newer to the field and has solid scores, but if absolute detection rate is your primary concern and budget is not, Norton's antivirus engine is more battle-tested. For most small business threat environments the practical difference is minimal.

### What happens if we grow beyond three employees?

Nothing changes mechanically — [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-vs-kaspersky-plus-comparison-2026/)'s unlimited device policy means adding a fourth or fifth employee costs nothing extra. The constraint is the single account credential, which becomes harder to manage as teams grow. At around 8–10 employees, investing in a dedicated endpoint management platform typically makes more sense.
