## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

A **VPN** creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet. Without one, your internet traffic travels across public roads where anyone nearby can observe it. With a VPN, that same traffic moves through a private, locked tube that third parties cannot read.

When you connect to a VPN server — say, one located in Canada or Japan — your real IP address (the unique number that identifies your device online) is replaced with the server's IP address. Websites and databases see the VPN server's identity, not yours. Your actual location and the network you're sitting on stay hidden.

For church volunteers, this creates two practical benefits:

1. **Security**: Encrypted traffic means your login credentials and the donor data you're accessing cannot be intercepted, even on a coffee shop or library Wi-Fi network.
2. **Cost savings**: Connecting through a server in a lower-cost region — such as Turkey, Brazil, or Argentina — can unlock significantly lower pricing on software subscriptions. More on this below.

Both benefits matter for a volunteer-run organization watching a tight budget.

## Why Remote Donor Database Access Is Riskier Than You Think

Most churches rely on CRM platforms, spreadsheets stored in cloud drives, or specialized donor management software. These tools are accessed through a browser or app — over whatever internet connection the volunteer happens to be using.

If that connection is a trusted home network, the risk is relatively low. But volunteers frequently work from:

- Coffee shops and cafes
- Library public Wi-Fi
- Hotel lobbies during conferences
- Church campus networks shared with guests

Public Wi-Fi networks are easy to exploit. Attackers can position themselves between your device and the router — a technique called a man-in-the-middle attack — and capture login credentials or session tokens as they travel. Even HTTPS-protected websites carry risk if a volunteer accidentally accepts a certificate warning or unknowingly connects to a spoofed network with a similar name.

Donor data is personal data. A breach does not just damage trust — in jurisdictions like the UK and Canada, data protection regulations mean organizations (including nonprofits and religious groups) can face formal complaints if they fail to take reasonable precautions when handling personal information.

The fix is not complicated. An active VPN connection on every volunteer device closes the most common attack vectors instantly.
