## What Is a Trojan Horse Virus?

The name comes from an old Greek story. A giant wooden horse was given as a "gift" to the city of Troy — but soldiers were hiding inside. Once the horse was brought through the city gates, the soldiers crept out and attacked.

A computer Trojan works exactly the same way. It disguises itself as something harmless — a greeting card email attachment, a free game download, or even a "Windows update" notification. Once you click on it and it gets inside your computer, it secretly opens a door for criminals to access your files, passwords, and banking details.

Unlike a regular virus, a Trojan does not spread on its own. It needs you to invite it in. That's why they are so effective — and so dangerous for seniors, who may not recognise the warning signs.

**Common ways Trojans get onto your computer:**
- An email attachment that claims to be a photo or document from a friend
- A fake "Your computer has a virus!" warning that asks you to call a [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/)
- A free software download from an unofficial website
- A link in a text message or social media post

## Warning Signs: Is Your Windows 11 PC Infected?

You do not need to be a computer expert to spot a Trojan. Look for these red flags:

**Your computer is suddenly very slow.** If it used to start up in one minute and now it takes ten, something may be running in the background without your knowledge.

**Strange programmes appear.** If you see software installed that you don't remember downloading, that is a serious warning sign.

**Pop-up windows keep appearing.** Legitimate programmes rarely create non-stop pop-ups. If your screen is filling with alerts, ads, or warnings — especially ones that ask for your [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-social-media-2026/) or credit card — stop and do not click anything.

**Your antivirus has been turned off.** Trojans often try to disable your security software first so they can operate freely. If [Windows Security](https://snipstack.io/windows-security-2026/) shows as "off" and you didn't turn it off, take action immediately.

**You've been locked out of accounts.** If your email or bank login suddenly doesn't work, a Trojan may have already captured your password.

If any of these sound familiar, keep reading.

## [How to Remove](https://snipstack.io/how-to-remove-malware-from-windows-11-2026/) a Trojan From Windows 11: Step by Step

Follow these steps carefully. Take your time — there is no rush, and each step is written to be as simple as possible.

### Step 1: Disconnect From the Internet

Before you do anything else, unplug your internet cable or turn off your Wi-Fi. Go to the bottom-right corner of your screen, click the Wi-Fi symbol, and turn it off. This stops the Trojan from sending any more of your information to criminals while you work on removing it.

### Step 2: Run Windows Security (the Built-In Scanner)

Windows 11 comes with a free built-in scanner called Windows Security. Here's [how to use](https://snipstack.io/how-to-use-email-aliases-for-security-2026/) it:

1. Click the **Start** button (the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner)
2. Type **Windows Security** and press Enter
3. Click **Virus & threat protection**
4. Click **Scan options**
5. Select **Full scan**
6. Click **Scan now**

This will take 20–60 minutes. Let it run without using your computer. When it finishes, follow any instructions it gives you to remove threats it finds.

### Step 3: Use a Dedicated Antivirus Programme

Windows Security is a good start, but it does not catch everything — especially newer, more sophisticated Trojans. A dedicated antivirus programme with real-time protection is far more thorough.

### Step 4: Remove Suspicious Programmes

Even after a scan, it's worth checking your installed programmes manually.

1. Click **Start**, then click the gear icon (**Settings**)
2. Click **Apps**, then **Installed apps**
3. Scroll through the list. If you see anything you don't recognise or didn't install yourself, click on it and select **Uninstall**

If you're not sure whether a programme belongs there, type its name into a search engine before removing it.

### Step 5: Change Your Passwords

Once your computer is clean, change the passwords for your email, bank accounts, and any other important accounts — from a different device if possible, such as a phone or tablet. Use a different password for each account. A Trojan may have already recorded your old passwords, so this step is essential.

### Step 6: Turn Internet Back On and Update Windows

Reconnect to the internet, then make sure Windows 11 is fully up to date:

1. Click **Start** > **Settings** > **Windows Update**
2. Click **Check for updates**
3. Install any updates that appear

Updates often contain security fixes that prevent future infections.

## Why Senior Computer Users Are Targeted More Often

This is not meant to frighten you — it's important information. Criminals specifically design Trojans to fool people who may be less familiar with modern technology. Common tactics aimed at seniors include:

- **[Tech support scam](https://snipstack.io/tech-support-scam-phone-call-identify-2026/)s:** A pop-up or phone call claiming your computer has a virus and asking you to pay for remote help. Legitimate companies like Microsoft never contact you this way.
- **Fake grandchild emails:** A message pretending to be from a grandchild who needs money urgently — and asks you to click a link.
- **Fake prize notifications:** "You've won!" messages that ask you to download a file to claim your prize.

The single most effective protection is a good antivirus programme running at all times. You can't monitor everything yourself — but software can.
