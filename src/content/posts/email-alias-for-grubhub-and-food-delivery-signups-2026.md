**Strengths:** Full transparency via open-source code, Proton's privacy reputation, browser extension for one-click alias creation, works with any email provider.

**Weaknesses:** The free tier's ten-alias limit can feel tight if you are privacy-conscious across multiple services. The interface is slightly more technical than Alt-ID. No bundled VPN or breach monitoring.

**Verdict for food delivery:** Excellent if you are already in the Proton ecosystem or prefer open-source tools. Requires a bit more setup than Alt-ID but delivers comparable alias functionality.

## Apple Hide My Email: Seamless But Locked In

Apple's Hide My Email feature, available to iCloud+ subscribers starting at $0.99 per month, generates random Apple-domain aliases on the fly. It integrates natively into Safari autofill and iOS's share sheet, making it frictionless for iPhone users.

**Strengths:** Zero extra apps needed, deep iOS and macOS integration, simple alias management in Settings.

**Weaknesses:** Completely Apple ecosystem dependent. If you switch to an Android device or use a non-Safari browser, alias creation becomes cumbersome. No Android app. Aliases are tied to your Apple ID.

**Verdict for food delivery:** The smoothest experience if you live inside the Apple ecosystem. A poor choice if you use Android or Windows primarily.

## Firefox Relay: Decent for Casual Use

Mozilla's Firefox Relay offers five free aliases with forwarding. The Premium tier ($1.99/month) removes the cap and adds phone masking. It works as a browser extension, making alias creation fast within Firefox.

**Strengths:** Trusted brand (Mozilla), free tier is generous enough for most food delivery users, phone number masking on Premium is a useful extra.

**Weaknesses:** Firefox-centric experience. The five-alias free limit is a real constraint if you use more than five delivery services. No mobile app equivalent for Android or iOS alias creation on the go.

**Verdict for food delivery:** A solid free-tier pick if you only use one or two delivery apps and mainly order via desktop.

## Disposable Address Services: Why They Fail for Food Delivery

Guerrilla Mail, Temp Mail, and similar throwaway services generate an inbox at a random address that anyone who knows the address can read, and that typically expires within hours. For food delivery, this creates real problems:

- **Order confirmation may arrive after the address expires.** Many services have a 10–60 minute TTL. If your delivery runs long, the receipt is gone.
- **Tracking links require a live inbox.** If the alias is dead, you cannot click through to live order tracking.
- **Account recovery is impossible.** If Grubhub ever locks your account for any reason, you cannot receive the password reset because the address no longer exists.

Disposable services are fine for a one-off newsletter signup. They are a bad fit for any service that needs to communicate with you over a multi-hour window or that you plan to use repeatedly.

## FAQ

**Q: Will an email alias work for Grubhub account verification?**

Yes, as long as you use a persistent alias that supports forwarding. The verification email Grubhub sends will be forwarded to your real inbox, where you can click the link normally. Avoid single-use disposable addresses for this step — they may expire before the email arrives.

**Q: Can I use the same alias across multiple food delivery apps?**

You can, but it defeats part of the purpose. Using one alias per service means you can identify which app is leaking or sharing your address — and delete only that alias without affecting the others. Most services offer unlimited alias creation. [Alt-ID makes this easy to manage](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-grubhub-and-food-delivery-signups-2026__faq).

**Q: What happens to my existing Grubhub orders if I delete the alias?**

Active orders that have already been confirmed will have sent their tracking and confirmation emails before you delete the alias, so those should be fine. Future emails — including receipts for new orders — will not be delivered once the alias is deleted. If you want to keep ordering on the same account, either keep the alias active or update your account email to a new alias before deleting the old one.

**Q: Is Surfshark Alt-ID available on Android and iOS?**

Yes. Alt-ID is accessible through the Surfshark app on both Android and iOS, as well as via the web dashboard. You can create and manage aliases from any device.

**Q: Does an email alias protect my phone number on food delivery apps?**

An email alias protects only your email address. Food delivery apps also collect your phone number, delivery address, and payment details. For broader privacy coverage, consider pairing an alias with a virtual card service for payments. Alt-ID is part of Surfshark One, which bundles additional privacy tools alongside the alias feature.

**Q: Will Grubhub ban accounts using alias email addresses?**

Grubhub's terms of service do not prohibit alias addresses, and aliases from reputable services (Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, Apple Hide My Email) are not distinguishable from regular email addresses at the platform level. There is no widespread report of accounts being banned for using an alias.
