## What "Fastest VPN for Streaming" Actually Means

Speed in VPN marketing is frequently a marketing number — a best-case throughput measured from a single server under ideal conditions. The number you see in a press release is not the number that matters when you are in London trying to stream a US Netflix library on a Tuesday evening at peak traffic.

Here is what actually determines streaming speed through a VPN:

**Protocol.** VPN protocols handle how your traffic is encrypted and routed. WireGuard, the newest major protocol, is significantly faster than older options like OpenVPN because it uses fewer lines of code and a more efficient encryption approach. A VPN running WireGuard will reliably outperform one still defaulting to OpenVPN, even on the same server hardware.

**Server density.** A VPN provider with 3,000+ servers can spread load across many exit points. One with 300 servers packs users onto shared infrastructure — and during peak hours, that congestion shows up as dropped frames and stuttering playback.

**Distance to server.** Latency increases with physical distance. Connecting to a server in Amsterdam when you are in Toronto adds 60–80ms of round-trip time. For streaming this matters less than for gaming, but it still contributes to slower initial load times and more re-buffering during peak demand.

**Streaming server maintenance.** Platforms like Netflix, Disney+, and Max actively detect and block VPN IP addresses. A fast VPN that gets blocked is worthless for streaming. The providers that stay unblocked are the ones that actively rotate IPs and maintain dedicated streaming infrastructure — which requires ongoing investment.

The minimum speed benchmarks for different streaming quality levels:

| Quality | Required Speed |
|---|---|
| SD (480p) | 3 Mbps |
| HD (1080p) | 5–10 Mbps |
| 4K Ultra HD | 15–25 Mbps |

A good streaming VPN should retain 70–85% of your base connection speed. On a typical home broadband connection of 100 Mbps, that leaves 70–85 Mbps — more than enough headroom for multiple simultaneous 4K streams.
