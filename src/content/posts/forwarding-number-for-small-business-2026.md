## Why Your Personal Number Is a Liability

When your real number is your business number, you give up more than privacy. You give up control.

Clients text you at 11pm because there's no signal that you're off the clock. Spam callers from old directory listings chew up your voicemail. And when you eventually sell the business, hand it to a partner, or just want to change your personal number, you're facing the nightmare of updating every listing, every invoice template, and every client contact who has your number saved.

A dedicated business forwarding number breaks that dependency. Calls from clients hit a number you control — not your SIM. You can forward it, silence it after hours, or replace it without touching your personal line.

## Google Voice — The Free Baseline

**What it is:** Google Voice gives you a US [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/) that forwards calls and texts to your real number. Free for personal use, with a paid Workspace version for business.

**What it does well:** Zero cost for basic use. Works with existing Google accounts. Decent voicemail transcription. Web-based portal for managing calls on a desktop.

**Where it falls short:** US-only — if you travel or have international clients, you're stuck. Numbers are VoIP, which means some services reject them for SMS verification. Call quality depends on your internet connection. The free tier's business features are minimal — no auto-attendant, no extensions, no hold music.

For a sole trader who makes and receives calls within the US and wants to stop handing out their personal number, Google Voice is a workable starting point. For anyone beyond that baseline, it runs out of headroom quickly.

## Grasshopper — Built for Small Business Call Routing

**What it is:** Grasshopper is a [virtual phone](https://snipstack.io/virtual-phone-number-for-customer-service-line-2026/) system designed explicitly for small businesses. You get a local or toll-free number, an auto-attendant, call forwarding rules, and extensions for multiple team members.

**What it does well:** The call routing logic is genuinely useful for small teams. You can set an auto-attendant that routes "press 1 for sales, 2 for support" — professional presentation without enterprise infrastructure. Extensions mean each person on the team gets their own forwarding rule from a single number.

**Where it falls short:** Mid-range monthly pricing for features that many solo operators will never use. US and Canada only. VoIP-based, so the same verification limitations as Google Voice apply. The mobile app has historically received mixed reviews for reliability on Android.

Grasshopper is the right fit if your primary goal is appearing more established — a professional auto-attendant and team extensions matter when you're pitching clients. It's overkill for a solo business owner who just wants to stop giving out their personal number.

## OpenPhone — CRM-Adjacent for Growing Teams

**What it is:** OpenPhone positions itself as a business phone system with a CRM layer built in. You get local or toll-free numbers, team inboxes, shared contact notes, call recording, and integrations with tools like HubSpot and Slack.

**What it does well:** If you're managing client relationships across a team and want call notes, shared history, and integration with your existing workflow tools, OpenPhone's feature depth is real. The shared inbox model works well for small customer-facing teams where multiple people handle inbound calls.

**Where it falls short:** Pricing scales with team size and can climb quickly once you add users. Like Grasshopper, it's US and Canada only. The CRM-adjacent features are only useful if you have the team and the workflow to use them — for a solo operator, you're paying for complexity you don't need.

OpenPhone makes most sense at the transition point where you have two to five people handling calls and you want a single business number with routing and shared notes. Before that point, it's expensive overhead.

## FAQ

**Can I use a forwarding number for my business without buying a [second phone](https://snipstack.io/second-phone-number-for-ebay-selling-2026/)?**

Yes. Services like Google Voice, Grasshopper, OpenPhone, and [Surfshark Alt-Number](/blog/altnumber?sub=forwarding-number-for-small-business-2026__faq) all run on your existing phone. You receive calls and texts through an app, not a second SIM. Alt-Number requires no additional hardware at all — it operates entirely within the Surfshark app.

**Will a VoIP business number work for SMS verification on banking and payment platforms?**

Not always. Many financial services, payment processors, and supplier portals detect VoIP numbers and reject them during SMS verification. This is a known limitation of Google Voice, Grasshopper, and Open[Phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-social-media-2026/)s. Surfshark Alt-Number provides real mobile numbers, which pass these checks reliably.

**Can I choose a local area code for my business forwarding number?**

Google Voice lets you pick from available US area codes. Grasshopper and OpenPhone offer local and toll-free US numbers. Alt-Number lets you choose the country your number is registered in, which determines the country code — useful if you want a number that looks local to your client base.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to client calls if I travel internationally?**

Google Voice, Grasshopper, and OpenPhone are primarily US-and-Canada services. Functionality varies or disappears once you leave North America. [Surfshark Alt-Number](/blog/altnumber?sub=forwarding-number-for-small-business-2026__faq) works globally — the same number and the same app function wherever you are.

**Can I transfer my existing business number to one of these services?**

Google Voice, Grasshopper, and OpenPhone support number porting (transferring an existing number in) under most circumstances. Surfshark Alt-Number assigns a new number rather than supporting porting — it's better suited to setting up a fresh business line than inheriting an existing one.

**Is it legal to use a [virtual number](https://snipstack.io/virtual-number-for-paypal-setup-2026/) as my primary business contact number?**

Yes, in most jurisdictions. There are no legal restrictions on using a virtual or forwarding number for business purposes. The number is real and functional — calls and texts route normally. As with any business contact number, you remain responsible for complying with applicable communication regulations in your industry.

**What if I need multiple extensions for different departments?**

Alt-Number is optimised for individual use — it's a single number for a single operator. If you need extension routing (e.g., press 1 for sales, 2 for support), Grasshopper or OpenPhone's infrastructure is built for that. Choose based on whether your priority is privacy and simplicity or team routing logic.
