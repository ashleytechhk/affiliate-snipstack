## Privacy and Security: Both Pass the Baseline Test

Neither service will let you down on the fundamentals. Both operate a verified no-logs policy — meaning they do not record your browsing activity, connection timestamps, or IP addresses in identifiable form. Both have undergone independent third-party security audits.

Key security features on both platforms:
- AES-256 encryption (industry standard)
- Kill switch (cuts internet if VPN drops, preventing IP exposure)
- DNS leak protection
- Split tunneling (route some traffic outside the VPN)

Surfshark adds CleanWeb, a built-in ad and tracker blocker that works at the DNS level. NordVPN's equivalent is Threat Protection. Both work well; neither replaces a dedicated browser-level blocker but both reduce ad load noticeably.

If you are running the VPN purely for region-switching on streaming platforms, neither provider's privacy posture gives you cause for concern. Both are registered in privacy-friendly jurisdictions and outside the 14 Eyes surveillance framework.
