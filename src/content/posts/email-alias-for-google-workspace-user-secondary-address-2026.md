## What Is an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-receiving-government-benefit-update-notices-2026/) — and Why Google Workspace Users Need One

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-app-store-refund-request-correspondence-2026/) is an additional address that delivers mail to your main inbox without being your main inbox. When someone emails your alias, the message lands in your primary account. When you reply, you can choose which address shows in the "From" field.

For Google Workspace users, the use case is straightforward: you want a secondary address to use for sign-ups, vendor contacts, or public-facing forms — so your real Workspace address stays clean and professional.

The problem is that "alias" means two very different things depending on the tool:

- **A routing alias** (what Google Workspace provides natively) forwards mail to you from a different address at the same or related domain.
- **A masked alias** (what Alt-ID and similar services provide) generates a completely random or custom address that hides your real email from the sender entirely.

Only one of these actually stops spam at the source.

## How Google Workspace Secondary Addresses Work — and Where They Fall Short

In Google Workspace admin settings, you can add secondary email addresses (aliases) to any user account. For example, if your primary address is `sarah@company.com`, you can add `hello@company.com` as an alias. Mail to either address lands in Sarah's inbox.

This is legitimate and useful for certain things: catch-all addresses, role-based addresses (`support@`, `billing@`), and name variations after a rebrand.

But for spam protection, it fails on three counts:

1. **Your domain is still visible.** Any alias you create still ends in `@company.com` or `@yourdomain.com`. A data broker or spam list vendor can still identify and target your organization.
2. **You can't generate aliases on demand.** Adding or removing aliases requires going into Workspace admin settings. There's no "create a throwaway address in 10 seconds" option.
3. **There's no masking.** Senders know they're reaching a real company domain. If the alias gets compromised, you rename it — but you can't burn and replace it quickly.

Google Workspace's secondary address feature is an admin tool, not an inbox hygiene tool.
