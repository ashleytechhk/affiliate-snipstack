## What Is RTBF and Why Does It Block You Abroad?

RTBF stands for Radio-Television Belge de la Francophone — Belgium's French-language public broadcaster. It runs Auvio, its streaming platform, alongside live channels and catch-up content. France's public broadcaster operates under a similar model with its own free streaming services.

Both RTBF (Belgium) and France's public TV services are funded partly by domestic license fees and advertising deals tied to specific regions. As a result, they are legally required to restrict access to viewers located within their broadcast territories. When you're in the UK, Spain, Canada, or anywhere outside Belgium or France, their servers detect your IP address, confirm you're not in the target region, and block playback.

This is called geo-blocking — it's a standard content licensing practice. It has nothing to do with your account or subscription status. It's purely about where your internet traffic appears to originate.

### What Exactly Is a VPN?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is software that encrypts your internet connection and routes it through a server in a country of your choice. When you connect to a VPN server in Belgium, your traffic exits the internet from a Belgian IP address. RTBF's geo-check sees Belgium, clears you for access, and streams normally.

The VPN server acts as a middleman. Your actual physical location stays private; the streaming service only sees the VPN server's location.
