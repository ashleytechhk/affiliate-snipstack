## What Is a VPN, and Why Should NFT Artists Care?

A Virtual Private Network creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and the internet. When you connect through a VPN, your traffic exits through a server in a location of your choice. Websites, marketplaces, and other services see the VPN server's IP address — not yours.

For most people, that's a privacy win. For digital artists selling NFTs, it's considerably more important.

### Your IP Address Is a Breadcrumb Trail

Every time you log into OpenSea, Foundation, Rarible, or any NFT marketplace, your device sends its IP address as part of the connection. That IP address ties your session to a rough geographic location and a specific internet service provider. Sophisticated buyers, competitors, or bad actors can aggregate this data over time.

If you're a pseudonymous artist who sells under a creative alias, your IP address is one of the cleaner ways someone can begin connecting your pseudonym back to your real identity. A VPN severs that link.

### NFT Transactions Are Public — But Your Identity Doesn't Have to Be

Blockchain transactions are transparent by design. Your wallet address, transaction history, and holdings are visible to anyone with a block explorer. This is not something a VPN fixes — the blockchain remains public. What a VPN does fix is the metadata layer above the blockchain: which IP address initiated a marketplace login, which location uploaded your artwork files, which connection signed into your creator profile.

Protecting that metadata layer is the practical value of a VPN for NFT artists.

## Common Misconceptions About VPNs and NFT Privacy

### "A VPN makes my transactions untraceable on the blockchain."

No. A VPN protects your IP address and the metadata surrounding your marketplace activity. It does not alter or obscure [what happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) on-chain. Your wallet address, your minting transactions, and your sale history remain visible on the blockchain regardless of whether you use a VPN. For blockchain-level privacy, separate tools (like privacy-focused wallets or mixing services) are different discussions entirely.

### "I need a VPN to stay legal when selling NFTs."

VPN use is legal in most countries and is not required to comply with NFT marketplace terms of service. A VPN is a privacy and security tool, not a compliance tool.

### "[Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) work just as well."

Free VPNs typically fund themselves by logging and selling user data — the exact opposite of what you want. Many have bandwidth caps that make uploading artwork files impractical. For serious NFT artists, a paid, audited VPN is worth the cost.
