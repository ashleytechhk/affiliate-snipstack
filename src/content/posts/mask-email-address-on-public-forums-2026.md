## The Threat Model: Why Public Forums Are a Special Problem

Most [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/) guides focus on form fills and data breaches. Public forums are different, and the threat is more aggressive.

When you post your email address on a public forum, it is:
- **Indexed by search engines** — visible to anyone, including automated crawlers
- **Scraped by bots 24/7** — forum pages are high-value harvesting targets precisely because users leave contact details
- **Archived indefinitely** — even deleted posts can persist in cached versions and third-party archives

The harvested address is then sold to spam operations, phishing kits, and data brokers. You cannot unsubscribe from this pipeline. The only way to break it is to give the forum an address that you control — and can discard.

### Gmail Plus-Addressing

The `yourname+tag@gmail.com` trick is widely used but poorly suited for public forum posting. The fundamental problem: **it does not hide your real email domain.** Anyone reading `yourname+forum@gmail.com` knows your real address is `yourname@gmail.com`. Scrapers strip the `+tag` suffix automatically — many spammers explicitly target the base address after seeing plus-addressed variants.

There is also no revoke mechanism. Blocking senders from a plus address requires filters, which are reactive and require maintenance. You cannot kill the address itself. Plus-addressing is a minor convenience for personal inbox organization, not a privacy tool.

---

### Disposable Inbox Services (Temp-Mail, 10MinuteMail, Guerrilla Mail)

These services give you a throwaway address with a built-in inbox, typically active for 10 minutes to a few hours. They are fine for single-use confirmations — a trial signup, a one-time verification code. They are not useful for public forum activity.

The problems for forums:
- **Not persistent.** If someone replies to your post two days later, the disposable address is gone and you never receive it.
- **Not your inbox.** You have to check the disposable service's website, not your normal email client.
- **Shared domains.** Popular disposable services use well-known domains that most forum platforms and some services block outright.

For ongoing forum participation where you expect legitimate replies, disposable inboxes are the wrong tool.

### SimpleLogin

SimpleLogin is a dedicated alias service with a strong reputation in the privacy community. It offers persistent aliases, full forwarding, reply support, and instant revoke — feature parity with Alt-ID for the core use case.

The differences: SimpleLogin is a standalone product requiring its own subscription for unlimited aliases. The free tier is capped at a small number of aliases, which may be limiting if you post across many forums. It lacks the bundling advantage: if you already use Surfshark, Alt-ID is no extra cost.

For someone who does not use Surfshark and wants a dedicated alias service, SimpleLogin is a credible alternative.

---

### Firefox Relay

Firefox Relay is Mozilla's alias service, integrated with Firefox accounts. The free tier provides five aliases — enough to get started. It forwards to your real inbox and supports basic reply masking.

The limitations for power users: five aliases is a hard cap on the free tier, and paid expansion requires a Mozilla subscription. Forum-active users who post in many communities will hit that ceiling quickly. It also works best when you are already in the Firefox / Mozilla ecosystem.

---

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple Hide My Email is the most polished option in this category — clean UI, native iOS and macOS integration, one-tap alias creation from Safari autofill. It generates genuinely random addresses (nothing traceable to your Apple ID), supports forwarding, and lets you disable aliases instantly from Settings.

The restriction is ecosystem lock-in. You need an iCloud+ subscription (starting at $0.99/month) and an Apple device to manage aliases. There is no web dashboard for non-Apple devices. If your forum posting happens on an Android phone or Windows PC, the workflow gets clunky.

## FAQ

**Does masking my email address on public forums actually stop spam?**

It stops spam *from that source*. If a scraper harvests your masked alias and spam starts arriving, you delete the alias and the spam stops immediately. Your real address is never exposed, so it stays clean regardless of how many forums you post on. What it cannot fix is spam that already reaches your real address from before you started using aliases. For existing spam on your real address, see our guide on [email aliases for stopping existing spam](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/).

**Will forum members be able to reply to my masked email normally?**

Yes, with tools that support two-way reply (Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, Apple Hide My Email, Firefox Relay paid). When someone replies to your forum post, the email arrives in your real inbox. When you reply, it goes out from the alias address. The other person never sees your real email. Basic disposable services and Gmail plus-addressing do not offer this — they are one-way or reveal your real domain.

**Can I create a different alias for each forum I post on?**

Yes, and this is strongly recommended. Alt-ID and SimpleLogin support unlimited (or high-limit) alias creation. Having a per-forum alias means when spam arrives, you know which specific forum's address was harvested. You delete that alias, create a new one for that forum, and continue. Per-forum aliases give you forensic visibility that a single "forum alias" does not. See our [email alias generator guide](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-generator-2026/) for setup tips.

**What if a forum rejects my alias address during registration?**

Some forums block known alias domains. Alt-ID uses a domain that is less likely to be on block lists than well-known disposable services. If you encounter a block, you have a few options: use a different alias from a different domain (if your service supports multiple), or register with your real address and apply inbox filters manually for that specific forum. Fortunately this is uncommon for the domains used by services like Alt-ID and SimpleLogin.
