## Streaming Performance and Server Network

![Surfshark vs ProtonVPN streaming server comparison](/og/vs-protonvpn-2026-inline-1.png)

Streaming is the use case that separates these two services most visibly.

### Server Reach

Surfshark covers 100+ countries with servers in markets across Europe, Asia-Pacific, Latin America, and Africa. More countries means more regional pricing opportunities — you can connect to a country where Netflix, Disney+, or Spotify prices are lower, then subscribe or renew from there. Surfshark regularly updates its server network to maintain access as streaming platforms adjust their geo-detection.

ProtonVPN's network spans 90+ countries on the Proton VPN Plus plan. That is a solid footprint, but it trails Surfshark in coverage. Proton's Secure Core feature routes traffic through privacy-hardened servers in Switzerland, Iceland, and Sweden — useful for threat models that require extra hops, but it adds latency and is not optimized for streaming.

### Smart DNS: Surfshark's Practical Edge

Surfshark includes Smart DNS, which lets you configure regional access directly on devices that do not support VPN apps natively — older smart TVs, certain gaming consoles, or media players running firmware-locked software. For a reader trying to lock in a regional streaming price across an entire household, Smart DNS is often the easiest path. ProtonVPN does not offer Smart DNS.

### Streaming Consistency

Both services maintain dedicated streaming servers that rotate IPs to stay ahead of VPN-detection systems. Neither can guarantee permanent access to any specific streaming library — platforms update detection methods regularly — but Surfshark's dedicated streaming server pool and active IP rotation have kept it reliable for most major platforms throughout 2025 and into 2026.

## Speed, Security, and Simultaneous Devices

### Speed

Both services use WireGuard and IKEv2/IPsec as default protocols. In independent speed benchmarks across 2025, Surfshark and ProtonVPN both performed within acceptable margins for 4K streaming (typically requiring 25 Mbps). Proton's Secure Core servers showed higher latency, which is expected given the multi-hop architecture. For straightforward streaming use, neither will be your bottleneck if your underlying connection is 50 Mbps or faster.

### Devices

Surfshark's unlimited simultaneous connections policy is its most underrated feature for multi-device households. One subscription, zero counting. ProtonVPN Plus allows up to 10 devices — more than enough for most individuals, but a household sharing an account could brush up against that limit with TVs, tablets, and guest devices in the mix.

### Security Features

Both services offer:
- AES-256 encryption
- Kill switch
- No-logs policy (both independently audited)
- DNS leak protection
- Split tunneling

ProtonVPN adds Secure Core (multi-hop routing) and Tor over VPN on selected servers. Surfshark adds CleanWeb (ad and tracker blocking) and NoBorders mode for use in restrictive network environments.

## Privacy and Jurisdiction

![VPN privacy features comparison chart](/og/vs-protonvpn-2026-inline-2.png)

This is ProtonVPN's strongest territory, and it deserves an honest acknowledgment.

**ProtonVPN** is headquartered in Switzerland, which sits outside the EU and US legal frameworks and has strong statutory privacy protections. Its no-logs policy has been independently audited by Securitum, and ProtonVPN publishes transparency reports. For journalists, activists, or users whose threat model involves state-level surveillance, ProtonVPN's Swiss jurisdiction is a meaningful advantage.

**Surfshark** is based in the Netherlands, which is an EU member state and participates in various intelligence-sharing arrangements. Surfshark has also undergone independent no-logs audits (Deloitte, 2023) and published transparency reports showing it received government data requests and complied where legally required — disclosing no user connection data because it does not log it. For the typical streaming-focused user, this distinction matters less than it does for high-risk users.

If your primary concern is accessing regional streaming prices and saving money on subscriptions, both services are reasonable choices from a privacy standpoint. If your threat model is more serious, ProtonVPN's Swiss home base is a structural advantage that Surfshark cannot replicate from the Netherlands.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Surfshark cheaper than ProtonVPN?

Yes, significantly on long-term plans. Surfshark's two-year plan runs around $2.19/month, compared to ProtonVPN Plus at approximately $4.99/month annually. Monthly pricing on both is higher — Surfshark at $15.45, ProtonVPN at $9.99. For anyone committed to a VPN for more than six months, Surfshark delivers a lower total cost of ownership.

### Can both VPNs unblock Netflix in different countries?

Both services maintain streaming-optimized servers designed to access regional Netflix libraries. Neither can provide a permanent guarantee — streaming platforms update their detection regularly — but both Surfshark and ProtonVPN have remained effective across most major markets through 2025. Surfshark's Smart DNS adds flexibility for devices that do not support VPN apps natively.

### How many devices can I use at once?

Surfshark allows unlimited simultaneous connections on a single subscription. ProtonVPN Plus allows up to 10 devices. For individuals or couples, 10 is plenty. For larger households or users who want to cover every device without counting, Surfshark's unlimited policy is a practical advantage.

### Does ProtonVPN have a free plan?

Yes. ProtonVPN's free tier is one of the most generous in the industry — no data caps and no speed throttling beyond what the free server load allows. The catch is that free servers are limited to a small number of countries and are slower during peak hours, making them unsuitable for reliably accessing specific regional streaming prices. [Surfshark's paid plan](/blog/surfshark?sub=vs-protonvpn-2026__faq) often undercuts ProtonVPN Plus so significantly on the two-year plan that the free-vs-paid comparison is worth running yourself.

### Is it legal to use a VPN to access regional streaming pricing?

VPN usage is legal in most countries. However, using a VPN to access content or pricing in a region where you do not reside may conflict with a streaming platform's terms of service. Each user should review the terms of the services they subscribe to and make their own informed decision. This article does not constitute legal advice.

### Which is faster — Surfshark or ProtonVPN?

Both support WireGuard, currently the fastest widely available VPN protocol. On standard streaming and browsing tasks, speed differences between the two services are negligible for most users. ProtonVPN's Secure Core servers are noticeably slower due to the multi-hop routing — this is by design and expected. For streaming-focused use, disable Secure Core and speeds are competitive.
