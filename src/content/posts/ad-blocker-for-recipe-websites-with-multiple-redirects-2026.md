## What Is an [Ad Blocker for](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-senior-online-shoppers-coupon-sites-2026/) Recipe Websites With Multiple Redirects?

An [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-that-removes-youtube-pre-roll-ads-2026/) is software that filters out advertising content before it loads in your browser or app. On most websites, this means banner ads and autoplay videos. On recipe websites, however, the problem runs deeper: **multiple redirects**.

A redirect is when a website automatically sends you to a different URL before landing on the final page. Recipe sites use these for several reasons:

- **Affiliate tracking** — They earn a commission when you click through a chain of partner links.
- **Ad network handoffs** — Your browser pings three or four ad servers before the page content loads.
- **Email capture gates** — A landing page intercepts your visit to push a newsletter signup before releasing you to the recipe.
- **Sponsored content funnels** — A "sponsored" recipe link actually routes you through a brand's promotional server first.

Each redirect adds load time. On a desktop with a fast connection, you might barely notice. On mobile — especially on LTE with variable signal — every extra hop can add one to three seconds of delay. String together five redirects and you're waiting fifteen seconds before you can even see if this is the right carbonara recipe.

An [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-that-removes-sponsored-search-results-2026/) for recipe websites with multiple redirects intercepts these hops at the network level, blocking the known ad-serving and redirect domains before your phone ever contacts them.

## Why Recipe Sites Are Particularly Aggressive

Recipe content is one of the most heavily monetized niches in publishing. A single "easy chocolate chip cookies" article might earn the publisher:

- Revenue from banner ads shown during your visit
- Affiliate commissions from links to ingredients on grocery delivery apps
- Revenue from sponsored placement in their recipe index
- Data broker fees from selling your browsing behavior to food brands

To maximize all of these, recipe sites have evolved a layered ad strategy. You'll commonly encounter:

**Pop-over ads** — Full-screen overlays that appear two seconds after page load, requiring you to find and tap a tiny X.

**Sticky video players** — An autoplay video that follows you down the page as you scroll toward the ingredients.

**Redirect chains on "Jump to Recipe" buttons** — Even when a site offers a "Jump to Recipe" button, tapping it on mobile sometimes routes through an ad impression URL first.

**Pre-interstitial pages** — An intermediate page that shows an ad for three to five seconds before forwarding you to the recipe.

These patterns are particularly punishing on mobile because:

1. Small screens make close buttons hard to find.
2. Accidental taps on ads are more common with touch input.
3. Mobile browsers have less RAM, so extra tabs opened by redirect chains can crash the original tab entirely.
