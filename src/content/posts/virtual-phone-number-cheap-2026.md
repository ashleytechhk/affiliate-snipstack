## **2. Google Voice**

**Best free option — US users only**

Google Voice has been around long enough that most people have heard of it. Sign up with a Google account, pick a US number, and you get free calls and texts forwarded to your real phone. For a certain kind of user — US-based, light usage, already deep in the Google ecosystem — it's hard to beat at zero cost.

The problem for dating apps: Google Voice numbers are widely recognized as VoIP lines, and some platforms reject them during SMS verification. Tinder in particular has flagged Google Voice numbers intermittently. It's not a guaranteed failure, but it's not a guaranteed pass either.

The other limitation is geographic. Google Voice numbers are US-only. If you're outside the US — or want a non-US number for any reason — this option disappears entirely.

**Cost:** Free. Requires a Google account.

**Works for:** US users who want zero spend and can tolerate occasional verification rejections.

## **3. TextNow**

**Best free option with a real-ish number**

TextNow gives you a free US or Canadian number that works for calls and texts through its app. The catch: it's ad-supported, and the number is classified as a VoIP line. Some dating apps will reject it during signup.

That said, TextNow has a large user base and its numbers are less consistently flagged than Google Voice. It's worth trying if you want a free option and don't mind the ad experience. The paid tier ($1.99–$2.99/month) removes ads and adds a few features but doesn't change the underlying number classification.

TextNow works best as a short-term burner — use it to verify an account, then monitor it through the app. It's not ideal as a long-term dating number because number availability isn't guaranteed and the app has to stay installed for the number to function.

**Cost:** Free (ad-supported) or ~$2/month for ad-free.

**Works for:** Free burner use, app verification attempts, short-term needs.

## **4. Hushed**

**Best for temporary numbers by the week**

Hushed is a purpose-built second number app that sells numbers by usage — you can get a number for three days, a week, a month, or ongoing. Prices start around $1.99 for a short-term number. It supports US, Canadian, and UK numbers.

For online dating specifically, Hushed's short-term option is genuinely useful. You match with someone, things move to texting, you use a Hushed number for a week or two, and if it doesn't work out you let it expire. No cancellation friction, no lingering number.

The downside: Hushed numbers are VoIP and get flagged by some verification systems. They're better suited for the texting phase of dating than the initial account setup. Also, costs add up if you're cycling through numbers regularly.

**Cost:** ~$1.99 for 3 days, ~$3.99/month ongoing. Lifetime plan available (~$25 one-time).

**Works for:** Short-term privacy between matches, temporary numbers during the early texting phase.

## **5. Burner App**

**Best for US/Canada users who cycle through matches**

The Burner app is one of the older players in this space and is built specifically for temporary numbers. The core concept: you get a number, use it, then "burn" it and get a new one. It's a clean mental model that fits dating well.

Burner supports US and Canadian numbers. Subscription starts at $4.99/month for one line. You can add more lines for additional cost. Numbers work for calls and texts through the app.

The limitation is the same as most VoIP-based services: some dating apps and verification systems flag and reject Burner numbers. It works well once you're past account setup and into the direct messaging phase. Geographic restriction (US/Canada only) is a hard stop for international users.

**Cost:** ~$4.99/month per line.

**Works for:** US/Canada users who want a number they can deliberately retire and replace.

## **6. Talkatone**

**Best for calls over Wi-Fi**

Talkatone gives you a free US phone number that works over Wi-Fi or cellular data — no carrier plan required. It's useful if you want to make actual calls without burning your real number, especially on Wi-Fi at home.

The service is ad-supported on the free tier. Paid plans start at $2.99/month and remove ads. Talkatone numbers are VoIP and carry the same verification risks as the others in this tier. Where it shines is the call quality on Wi-Fi — for people who actually want to talk (not just text) before meeting, it's a decent choice.

For pure SMS verification on dating apps, it's hit or miss. For ongoing use after you've matched, it's functional.

**Cost:** Free (ad-supported) or ~$2.99/month.

**Works for:** Voice calls over Wi-Fi, privacy-conscious users who want to talk before meeting.

## **7. Second Number from Your Carrier**

**Most reliable, least private**

Most major carriers — T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T, and their equivalents outside the US — offer a secondary number as an add-on to your existing plan. T-Mobile's Digits service, for example, lets you add a line for around $10/month. It shows up on your same phone, same SIM.

The obvious advantage: it's a real mobile number with zero verification issues. Dating apps, banking apps, any SMS-based system — they all accept it without question. There's no VoIP flag risk.

The downside is privacy. A carrier-assigned number is still attached to your real account. If something went very wrong, a subpoena could connect that number to your identity. For most people this is theoretical — but it's the reason this option sits at the bottom of a privacy-focused list. It's also the most expensive option here.

**Cost:** ~$10–15/month add-on, varies by carrier.

**Works for:** Users who prioritize reliability over privacy, or who have high-volume dating activity and need a number that always passes verification.
