## How a VPN Works for NFL Streaming: The Technical Reality

A VPN creates an encrypted connection between your device and a server in a location you choose. When you connect to a US-based server, all outbound traffic from your device appears to originate from that server's IP address. Streaming platforms — including NFL services — see the server's IP rather than your real location, and grant or deny access based on that.

![How a VPN routes your connection for NFL Game Pass outside the US](/og/vpn-for-nfl-game-pass-outside-us-2026-inline-1.png)

The mechanism for NFL streaming works in three steps:

1. **Connect to a VPN server in the target country** — the VPN app handles this automatically once you select the right location.
2. **Your traffic routes through the server** — the NFL platform sees the server's IP address and reads your location as that country.
3. **The streaming service grants access** — content that was previously blocked loads normally.

The complication: major streaming services maintain IP blocklists of known VPN addresses. When a server's IP gets flagged, connections through it get blocked. Premium VPNs address this with large, regularly rotated IP pools — when one address is detected, traffic shifts to another. Free and budget VPNs typically have a small, static pool of IPs that end up on blocklists quickly and stay there.
