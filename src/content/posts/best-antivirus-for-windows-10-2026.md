## Why Windows 10 Seniors Are Targeted More Often

Scammers specifically look for older computer users. Why? Because surveys consistently show that people over 60 lose more money to online fraud than any other age group. Windows 10 remains one of the most popular operating systems on the planet, which makes it the biggest target for viruses and malware.

Here are the four most common threats seniors face:

- **Phishing emails** — messages that look like they are from your bank, Medicare, or a family member, designed to trick you into entering a password.
- **Tech-support scams** — fake pop-ups that say "Your computer is infected, call this number." The number connects to a scammer.
- **Ransomware** — a type of virus that locks all your photos and documents until you pay money to strangers.
- **Spyware** — software that secretly watches what you type, including passwords and credit card numbers.

A good antivirus stops all four of these automatically, before you even see them.
