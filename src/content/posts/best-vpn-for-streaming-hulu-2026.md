## Why Hulu Only Works in the United States

Hulu is a US-only streaming service. Unlike Netflix, which operates globally with regional libraries, Hulu has not licensed its content for international distribution. The reason is straightforward: the TV studios and networks that supply Hulu's catalog — ABC, NBC, Fox, FX, and others — sell international streaming rights separately, often to different platforms in different countries.

When you log in from outside the US, Hulu detects your IP address. That address reveals your approximate location to their servers. If the IP does not resolve to a US location, Hulu blocks playback — even if you are a paying subscriber with a perfectly valid US account.

This is a licensing enforcement mechanism, not a judgment about you as a customer. The practical effect is identical: no Hulu outside the US without a workaround.

### Who Actually Needs a VPN for Hulu

- **US residents traveling internationally** — business trips, holidays, study abroad semesters. Your subscription keeps charging; Hulu stops working the moment your flight lands.
- **American expats** — living abroad long-term but keeping a US Hulu account and billing address.
- **International students in the US** — if you return home during breaks, your access cuts off.
- **Cord-cutters on the Live TV tier** — Hulu + Live TV includes ESPN, CNN, and local channels. Losing access abroad means missing live sports and news you are already paying for.

If you fall into any of these categories, a VPN is the practical fix. It routes your connection through a US-based server, so Hulu's detection system sees a US IP address and grants access.

## How Server Quality Determines Your Streaming Experience

Not all VPN servers are equal — and for Hulu, the difference between a good server and a bad one is the difference between seamless 1080p playback and a buffering nightmare.

![How VPN server quality affects Hulu streaming — illustrated guide](/og/best-vpn-for-streaming-hulu-2026-inline-1.png)

### IP Reputation and Detection

Hulu, like other major streaming platforms, maintains updated blocklists of known VPN IP addresses. When a VPN server's IP gets flagged, connections through that server fail. Quality VPNs maintain large, rotating pools of US server IPs — when one gets blocked, another is available. Cheap or [free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) operate a handful of shared IPs that get flagged within hours of Hulu updating its blocklist.

This is why "does this VPN unblock Hulu?" is not a yes-or-no question. The honest answer is "it depends on whether that specific server's IP is currently on Hulu's blocklist." Premium VPNs invest in maintaining clean IPs as part of their core infrastructure.

### Speed and Latency

Hulu requires at least 3 Mbps for standard definition, 8 Mbps for 1080p, and 16 Mbps for 4K content (on plans that support it). A VPN adds overhead — your traffic is encrypted and routed through an extra server. Quality VPNs using the WireGuard protocol typically lose 10–20% of your base connection speed. A poor VPN on congested servers can halve your speed or worse.

If your home internet runs at 100 Mbps, losing 20% still leaves you with 80 Mbps — more than enough for 4K. If you are on hotel Wi-Fi at 15 Mbps, a VPN that cuts that to 8 Mbps creates real problems.

### Protocol Matters

WireGuard is the fastest modern VPN protocol and the best default for streaming. Both Surfshark and ExpressVPN offer WireGuard (Surfshark calls its implementation WireGuard; ExpressVPN uses its proprietary Lightway protocol, which performs similarly). NordVPN also offers WireGuard under the name NordLynx.
