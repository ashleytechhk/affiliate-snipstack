### SimpleLogin

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service acquired by Proton in 2022. The free tier gives you 10 aliases, which is enough for casual cashback app use. The paid plan at around $4 per month unlocks unlimited aliases plus custom domain support.

It is solid, privacy-respecting, and has strong community trust given its open-source roots. What it lacks is the identity profile layer. You still need to decide what name and personal details to enter on signup forms yourself.

Best for: Users who want a standalone alias tool and are comfortable stopping at email-only protection.

### Apple Hide My Email

If you are on an Apple device and pay for any iCloud+ tier (starting at $0.99/month), Hide My Email is built in. You generate random Apple-managed addresses that forward to your real iCloud account.

The limitations are real though. Managing aliases requires Apple devices. Aliases are created through Safari or iOS apps — not a universal web dashboard. And there is no identity profile support, no Android app, and the alias system is tightly coupled to iCloud, making it awkward if you ever want to move off Apple.

Best for: iPhone-only users who already pay for iCloud+ and want zero-friction alias creation in Safari.

### DuckDuckGo Email Protection

DuckDuckGo's [free email](https://snipstack.io/free-email-alias-2026/) service gives you a `@duck.com` alias that strips email trackers before forwarding. It is genuinely free and easy to set up. The trade-off is that it is a single address, not infinite aliases. You get one `yourname@duck.com` alias, not per-app aliases.

For cashback app signups, a single alias means you cannot tell which app caused the spam, and deleting it kills forwarding from every app at once. That makes it less useful for the multi-app deal-hunter this article is aimed at.

Best for: Users who want basic tracker removal and do not need per-app alias control.

## How to Set Up an [Email Alias for](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-legal-document-service-signup-2026/) Cashback App Signups

The workflow is the same regardless of which tool you choose.

**Step 1 — Create a new alias before signing up.** Do not enter your real email first and try to change it later. Create the alias, copy it, then paste it into the cashback app's signup field.

**Step 2 — Use a fake but consistent name if the app asks.** Tools like Alt ID generate a name for you. Write it down somewhere so if the app ever asks for account verification, you remember what you used.

**Step 3 — Confirm the welcome email comes through.** Most cashback apps send a verification link. Your alias forwards it. Click the link and you are in.

**Step 4 — Monitor the alias for 30 days.** If the app is well-behaved, you might keep the alias long-term. If spam starts flowing, delete the alias. Done.
