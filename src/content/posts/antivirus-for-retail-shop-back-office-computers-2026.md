## Why Your Back Office Computer Is a Bigger Target Than You Think

Most shop owners assume hackers only go after big chains with millions of customers. The opposite is often true. Large companies have full-time IT teams and expensive security systems. A small retail shop usually has one computer, one owner juggling a dozen jobs, and no dedicated technical support at all. That combination makes small shops an easier and more attractive target.

Your back office computer typically stores:

- Point-of-sale software with customer names, card details, and purchase history
- Supplier and vendor invoices with bank account information
- Payroll records for any employees you have
- Saved passwords for your business bank account and merchant services

A single successful attack can freeze your point-of-sale system during business hours, expose customer payment data, or drain your business checking account before you even notice something is wrong. For a shop already running on thin margins, that kind of disruption can be devastating.

## The Risk Explained: How Shops Actually Get Hit

Cybercriminals rarely need to be sophisticated to succeed against a small shop. Here is the pattern that plays out again and again:

### The Fake Invoice Email

A scammer sends an email that looks like it came from a real supplier you already work with. It has a familiar logo and a normal-looking invoice attached. The moment someone in the back office opens that attachment, malware installs itself quietly in the background. No pop-up warning. No obvious sign anything happened.

### The "Update Your Payment System" Message

Another common trick is an email or text claiming your point-of-sale provider needs you to "verify" your account or "update" your payment terminal software. The link leads to a fake website designed to steal your login credentials. Once a scammer has those, they can access your merchant account directly.

### The Old, Unpatched Computer

Many back office computers run for years without updates because nobody has time to think about it during a busy retail day. Older, unpatched systems have known security gaps that criminals actively search for using automated tools — they do not even need to target your shop specifically. The scan finds you.
