## What Is Netflix Regional Pricing?

Netflix, like most global subscription services, sets prices country by country based on local currency strength, purchasing power, and competitive pressure. This is called regional or differential pricing, and it's standard practice across streaming, software, and app store platforms. The service is identical everywhere — only the price tag shifts depending on which country your account is tied to.

Here's roughly how Netflix's monthly plans compare across a few markets as of 2026:

- **United States**: Standard with Ads $7.99, Standard $17.99, Premium $24.99
- **United Kingdom**: Standard with Ads ~£4.99, Standard ~£12.99, Premium ~£17.99
- **Argentina**: Standard with Ads ~ARS $8,999 (~$6 USD), Standard ~ARS $14,999 (~$10 USD), Premium ~ARS $19,999 (~$13 USD)

On the Premium tier, that's close to a 45–50% saving compared to the US price — over $140 a year for identical streaming. The ad-supported entry tier shows a smaller gap, since it's already priced low everywhere.

### What Is a VPN and How Does It Relate to This?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) routes your internet traffic through a server in another country and swaps your visible IP address for one from that server's location. Connect through an Argentine VPN server, and Netflix's systems read your connection as coming from Argentina. That location signal is what determines which storefront pricing you see and, in most cases, which price your new account gets billed at.
