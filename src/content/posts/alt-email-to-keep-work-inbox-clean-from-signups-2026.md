## How to Set Up an Alt Email System That Keeps Signups Out of Your Work Inbox

### Step 1 — Create a Default Work Alias

Start with one general-purpose alias for anything you're not sure about — a demo request, a newsletter you half-want, a form a vendor asked you to fill in. This becomes your default answer any time a form asks for "your work email" and you're not planning a long-term relationship with the sender.

### Step 2 — Reserve Category Aliases for Recurring Situations

For situations that repeat often, a dedicated alias per category gives you faster identification when spam shows up:

## Why the Gmail "+" Trick and Temp Mail Don't Hold Up for Work Signups

Gmail plus-addressing — signing up as `yourname+vendor@gmail.com` — is a common first attempt at organizing work signups, and it falls apart quickly in a professional context. Marketing platforms routinely strip everything after the `+` before storing the address, so the base account is what actually ends up on a resold list. Some enterprise signup and procurement forms reject the plus-tag format outright. And if a plus-tagged address starts attracting spam, the only fix is a permanent filter rule or abandoning the Gmail account — there's no clean way to revoke just that one tag.

Temporary or throwaway inboxes solve a different problem. They're fine for grabbing a one-time confirmation link, but a work signup rarely stays a one-time event. The moment a vendor needs to send an invoice or a renewal notice weeks later, the throwaway address is already gone and the message bounces. A persistent alias you control — one that doesn't expire on its own schedule — is what survives an ongoing vendor relationship.

## FAQ

**Will an alt email break a SaaS trial signup or onboarding flow?**

No. Alt-ID aliases behave like a normal email address to the receiving service — verification links, onboarding emails, and in-app notifications all arrive normally, just forwarded to your real inbox. The vendor never sees anything unusual on their end.

**What if a coworker needs to reach me at the alias by mistake?**

Mail sent to an active alias forwards straight to your real inbox, so a misdirected message from a coworker still reaches you. If you've already deleted that alias, forwarding simply stops — which is exactly the point once it's no longer needed.

**Can I use this instead of asking IT for a second work email address?**

For most signup-related use cases, yes. An alias handles the problem of handing your address to external tools and vendors. It's not a replacement for your company's primary work account, but it removes the need to expose that account to every trial and form you fill out. **Does this work with Outlook and Google Workspace, or only Gmail?**

Alt-ID is provider-agnostic. Aliases forward to whichever inbox you designate, whether that's Gmail, Outlook, Google Workspace, or any other provider your company uses. You keep working from your existing mail client exactly as before.

**How many aliases do I actually need for a work inbox?**

Most people start with one default alias plus three or four category aliases — trials, events, vendors, and recruiter outreach covers the bulk of signup traffic. Add more as specific high-volume categories emerge.

**Is this just a fancier version of a Gmail filter?**

No. A filter sorts mail you're already receiving at one exposed address. An alias prevents the exposure in the first place, and lets you revoke access entirely when a source turns into noise — something a filter rule can never do.
