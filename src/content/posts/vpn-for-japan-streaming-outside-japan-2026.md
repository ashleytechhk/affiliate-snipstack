## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect to a VPN, your device creates an encrypted tunnel to a server operated by the VPN provider. From that point on, every streaming service or website you visit sees the server's IP address — not your actual location.

Think of it as borrowing a friend's home address in Tokyo. When a streaming platform checks where you are, it reads your friend's Tokyo address and shows you the Japanese version of the site, with Japanese pricing and the full Japanese content library.

For Japan streaming specifically, this matters in two ways:

- **Exclusive content libraries.** Netflix Japan carries a large catalogue of anime, J-dramas, and Japanese originals that are not available on Netflix in other regions. The same applies to Japanese-specific services like Abema TV, NHK World streaming, and domestic sports platforms.
- **Regional pricing.** Japan's subscription prices for major platforms are set for a market with different purchasing patterns than the US, UK, or Australia. The same Netflix Standard plan can cost 30–50% less in Japan compared to those markets.
