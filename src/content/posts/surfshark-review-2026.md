## What Is a VPN — and Why Does It Matter for Subscriptions?

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in another location. When you connect through a VPN server in Turkey, websites and apps see a Turkish IP address. As far as any streaming service can tell, you are in Turkey.

This matters for one straightforward reason: streaming platforms price their subscriptions differently by country. The same Netflix plan that costs $15.49 in the US costs the equivalent of roughly $3–4 in Turkey or India. The content library differs by region, but the core streaming service is the same product.

A VPN does not modify what streaming services offer — it changes your apparent location so you can access the pricing tier of the region you connect to. That is the entire mechanism behind subscription arbitrage.

What Surfshark specifically brings to this:

- **Server coverage in arbitrage-friendly regions:** Turkey, India, Argentina, and others where pricing is significantly lower than the US or UK
- **Consistent unblocking:** Streaming platforms actively block known VPN IP ranges. Surfshark invests in rotating server infrastructure to stay ahead of detection
- **No device cap:** Run it on every screen in the house without counting connections

## How Surfshark Works: The Basics

Surfshark runs as an app on your device — available for Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, Android TV, and as a [browser extension](https://snipstack.io/browser-extension-to-check-scam-sites-2026/). You open the app, choose a server location, and connect. Your internet traffic now routes through that server.

The key protocols Surfshark uses:

- **WireGuard:** The current speed standard. Fast, modern, and what Surfshark defaults to on most devices. Rarely more than 5–10% slower than your unprotected connection on a nearby server.
- **IKEv2/IPSec:** Strong performance on mobile. Good for networks where WireGuard gets throttled.
- **OpenVPN:** The long-standing reliable option. Slightly slower than WireGuard but battle-tested.

For streaming arbitrage, protocol choice rarely matters — any of these will handle 4K streaming comfortably. What matters more is server selection: pick a server in the region whose pricing you want to access, and keep a note of which server reliably works for each platform.

### CleanWeb — Ad and Tracker Blocking

Included with all Surfshark plans. CleanWeb is DNS-level blocking that removes ads and trackers across all devices and apps on the connection — not just in a browser. For users running ad-supported free streaming services alongside their arbitrage setup, this is a useful bonus. Our dedicated [Surfshark CleanWeb review](/surfshark-cleanweb-review-2026/) goes into the detail if you want it.

### Smart DNS

Surfshark includes Smart DNS for devices that cannot run the VPN app directly — smart TVs and gaming consoles where the operating system does not support VPN clients. Smart DNS changes your apparent DNS location without encrypting traffic, which means slightly simpler setup on constrained devices. Useful for streaming arbitrage on a TV that lacks a native Surfshark app.

### Unlimited Simultaneous Connections

No device cap. This is not standard — NordVPN caps at 10 connections, ExpressVPN at 8. With Surfshark, everyone in the household can be connected at the same time without kicking each other off. For a family running multiple streaming subscriptions at arbitrage pricing, this matters operationally.
