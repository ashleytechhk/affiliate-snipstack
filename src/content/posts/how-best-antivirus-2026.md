## FAQ

### Is [free antivirus](https://snipstack.io/free-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026/) software good enough?

### Will antivirus software slow down my computer?

Modern antivirus programs are designed to run quietly without noticeable impact. [Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-avg-comparison-2026/) is particularly lightweight. You may notice a brief slowdown during a full scan, but normal day-to-day use — browsing, email, video calls — should feel the same.

### What is the difference between antivirus and a VPN?

Antivirus software protects your device from viruses, scams, and malware. A VPN (Virtual Private Network) protects your internet connection by hiding your online activity and location — useful on public Wi-Fi like at a cafe or airport. Both are useful tools, and Surfshark offers both in one package.

### Do I need antivirus on my iPhone or iPad?

iPhones and iPads have strong built-in protections. However, no device is completely immune to scam websites, phishing emails, or unsafe app downloads. Security software can add an extra layer of protection even on Apple devices, especially for monitoring dangerous links in emails and messages.

### How do I know if my device already has a virus?

Common signs include: your computer is very slow for no reason, you see pop-ups you did not open, your browser home page has changed, or programs open and close on their own. If any of these are happening, run a scan immediately. [Surfshark Antivirus](/blog/surfshark?sub=how-best-antivirus-2026__faq) can detect and remove existing threats as well as prevent new ones.

### Can one subscription protect all my family's devices?

Yes. Surfshark's plans allow you to protect multiple devices — your computer, tablet, and phone — under one subscription. This is great if you want to set up protection for a spouse or adult child as well.

### How often will I need to do anything after installation?

Almost never. Once installed and set to update automatically, antivirus software handles everything itself. The only time you need to take action is if the program alerts you to a real threat — which happens rarely — or when your annual subscription renews.
