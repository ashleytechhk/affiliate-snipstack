## The Hotel WiFi Problem Nobody Talks About Enough

Frequent travelers — the kind logging four or more trips per month — are not worried about the same threats as someone who only travels twice a year. The risks compound. More hotel networks means more exposure windows. More devices (laptop, tablet, phone, AirPods case with chip) means a wider attack surface.

The most common threats on hotel WiFi are not the dramatic Hollywood ones. They are mundane: man-in-the-middle attacks where traffic is intercepted between your device and the router, evil twin access points that mimic the hotel's network name, and session hijacking on unencrypted connections. Standard antivirus — the kind Norton and McAfee built their reputations on — does essentially nothing to stop these. They scan files. Hotel WiFi attacks happen at the network layer, before a file ever lands on your device.

What you actually need is a VPN that encrypts your tunnel at the connection level, plus a lightweight security layer that does not eat your laptop's CPU while you are on a video call in a business center.
