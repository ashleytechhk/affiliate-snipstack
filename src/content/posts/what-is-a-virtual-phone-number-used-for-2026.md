## The Specific Risks for Online Daters in 2026

### Data Brokers Pick Up Your Number Automatically

Dating apps require phone number verification at signup. The app itself may share your number with advertising and analytics partners as a matter of routine data practice. Those partners feed data broker pipelines. Once your number is in a broker's database, it gets packaged with your name, address history, and linked profiles — and sold.

This is how a number you used once on a dating app starts receiving robocalls from sources you have never interacted with. The number was never private once it left your device.

### SIM-Swap Attacks Start With a Phone Number

SIM-swapping is an attack where someone convinces your carrier to transfer your number to a SIM they control. With your number, they intercept every SMS two-factor code sent to it — bypassing the security on your email, your bank, and your other accounts.

Dating apps are a documented source of phone numbers used in SIM-swap targeting. Matches who seem unusually interested in your carrier, your phone plan, or your job at a financial institution are sometimes building the profile they need to execute the attack. Your phone number is the lever.
