## Intego Mac Security Bundle: What You Get

Intego has been building Mac security software since 1997. That heritage matters because macOS security is genuinely different from Windows security — the attack vectors, the permission models, the way apps are sandboxed. Intego's VirusBarrier engine understands this natively.

The Mac Premium Bundle X9 includes:

- **VirusBarrier X9** — real-time antivirus with scheduled scans
- **NetBarrier X9** — application-level firewall that monitors which apps make outbound connections
- **ContentBarrier** — parental controls for web filtering
- **Personal Backup** — local and network backup scheduling
- **Washing Machine** — disk cleaner for removing junk files

On pure malware detection, independent lab tests consistently give Intego high marks for Mac-specific threats. If someone in your household regularly downloads software from outside the App Store or clicks sketchy email attachments, Intego's VirusBarrier will catch most of it.

The catch: it is Mac-only. If you also run Windows for work or have an Android phone, Intego does not protect those. And there is no VPN, no data breach monitoring, and no private search. You are getting a clean antivirus + firewall suite, which is exactly what it says on the box — but nothing more.

## Surfshark One: What You Get

Surfshark One is a multi-layer security bundle built around four products working together.

### VPN — The Layer Intego Skips Entirely

The VPN is where Surfshark One immediately pulls ahead. With servers in over 100 countries, you can route your traffic through locations including Japan, the UK, Canada, Brazil, Turkey, and Argentina. This matters for two reasons:

1. **Privacy on public Wi-Fi** — coffee shops, airports, hotel networks. An antivirus won't protect you on an untrusted network; a VPN will encrypt the traffic before it leaves your device.
2. **Bypassing geo-restrictions** — whether that's a streaming catalogue that differs by country or accessing content while travelling overseas.

Surfshark's VPN allows unlimited simultaneous connections under one account. If you have a Mac, an iPhone, an iPad, and a partner with a Windows laptop — all covered, no extra seat required. Intego allows 3–5 devices depending on which tier you choose, and they must all be Macs.

### Antivirus — Competitive, Not Intego-Level, But Close

Surfshark's antivirus uses real-time scanning and scheduled scans. It performs well in independent testing for cross-platform threats. For Mac-specific malware, Intego has a small edge due to its longer history on the platform. That said, for most home users the practical difference is minor — both catch the common threats.

### Surfshark Alert — Data Breach Monitoring

This is something Intego has no equivalent for. Alert monitors your email addresses, passwords, and credit card numbers against known data breach databases. If your credentials show up in a breach, you get notified so you can change your passwords before damage is done. With the average person having 200+ online accounts, this is increasingly not optional.

### Surfshark Search — Private Browsing Without Tracking

Search is a private search engine that returns organic results without tracking your queries or building an ad profile. It won't change your life, but it is a clean replacement for using Google when you don't want searches tied to your identity.

## Performance on Mac: Does It Slow Things Down?

This is the number one concern for anyone migrating away from a bloated suite. Both products are meaningfully lighter than Norton or McAfee, but there are differences worth noting.

**Intego** runs as background daemons that are well-optimised for macOS. On Apple Silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, M4), performance impact is genuinely minimal. The Washing Machine and ContentBarrier components are only active when you open them, so idle resource use is low.

**Surfshark One** requires running both the VPN client and the antivirus background service. When the VPN is connected, you will add a small amount of latency to network requests — typically 5–15ms depending on server distance, which is imperceptible for normal browsing. The antivirus component's CPU footprint is modest. On modern Macs the day-to-day experience is smooth; on older Intel Macs with 8GB RAM you may notice a more pronounced impact when running a full scan.

Neither product auto-renews at a hidden higher price the way Norton does, and neither floods your notification centre with upsell pop-ups.
