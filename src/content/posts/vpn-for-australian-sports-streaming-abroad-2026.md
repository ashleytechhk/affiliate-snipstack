For Australian streaming services, the check is straightforward: is your IP address registered in Australia? If yes, the stream loads. If not, you hit a geo-block error.

## Why Geo-Blocks Exist — and Why a VPN Works

Streaming platforms do not block overseas users out of hostility. They block them because broadcast rights agreements are territorial. A broadcaster that buys rights to show AFL in Australia has typically bought those rights only for Australian audiences — they do not have the legal clearance to deliver those streams internationally.

The platform's enforcement mechanism is simple: check the IP address of every incoming connection against a database of IP ranges assigned to Australian internet service providers. If the IP is Australian, access is granted. If it is not, the stream is blocked.

A VPN defeats this check by routing your traffic through a server whose IP address is Australian. The platform checks the server's IP — it reads as Australian — and delivers the stream. Your actual location is invisible to the platform.

This is not a vulnerability being exploited. It is the platform seeing an Australian IP address and doing exactly what it is designed to do: serve Australian content to an IP registered in Australia.
