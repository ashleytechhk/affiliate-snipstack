## What is a VPN? (And Why Healthcare Workers Need One)

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. In practical terms, it is software that wraps your internet traffic in a layer of encryption before it leaves your device. Think of it as sealing your data inside a locked courier pouch before handing it to a delivery driver — anyone who intercepts the pouch mid-route cannot read what is inside.

For nursing home staff, this matters because healthcare data carries legal weight. In the UK, patient information is protected under GDPR. In Canada, it falls under PIPEDA. In Japan, the Act on the Protection of Personal Information applies. Across all these frameworks, the core expectation is the same: sensitive personal data should not travel over unprotected connections.

When you log into an electronic health record (EHR) system, a remote desktop, or a resident management portal from home or a café, that connection uses the public internet. Without a VPN, login credentials and session data can be intercepted by anyone on the same network who has basic tools and bad intentions. The facility's IT team may not know this is happening until after a breach.

A VPN closes that gap. It routes your connection through an encrypted tunnel, so even on a shared home router or public Wi-Fi hotspot, the data your work systems send and receive stays protected.

## How a VPN Works: No Technical Background Required

You do not need to understand encryption algorithms to use a VPN. Here is what actually happens when you connect:

1. **Open the VPN app** on your laptop, phone, or tablet and tap Connect.
2. **Your device links to a VPN server** — a computer operated by the VPN provider, located in a country you select.
3. **All outgoing and incoming traffic is encrypted** before it leaves your device, then transmitted through that server.
4. **The websites and systems you visit** see the VPN server's address, not your home connection's address.

From the perspective of your work system, the connection appears to come from the VPN server's location. From the perspective of anyone monitoring the same Wi-Fi network, your traffic is unreadable.
