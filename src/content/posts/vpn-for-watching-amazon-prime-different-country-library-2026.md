## What Does "Country Library" Actually Mean on Amazon Prime?

Amazon Prime Video licenses content on a country-by-country basis. When a studio sells streaming rights to Amazon, it typically sells them separately for North America, Europe, Asia, and Latin America. That means a series available on Amazon Prime in the UK may not appear at all for a subscriber in Canada — and vice versa.

The result: there is no single global Amazon Prime catalog. What you see depends entirely on the IP address your device uses when it connects to Amazon's servers. Change that IP address to one from another country, and you see that country's catalog instead.

This is not a loophole or a bug. It is how the global rights system works. And a VPN is the standard tool people use to navigate it.
