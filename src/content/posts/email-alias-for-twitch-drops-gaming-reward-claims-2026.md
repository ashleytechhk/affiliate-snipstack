## Why Twitch Drops Turn Your Inbox Into a Warzone

Twitch Drops work through a partnership model. Streamers run campaigns, viewers watch and earn in-game rewards, but to claim those rewards you must connect a third-party game account — and that game account logs your email. The moment you verify a claim, you are on that publisher's marketing list. Legally. You opted in when you accepted the terms.

Publishers then share that data with ad networks and regional distribution partners. What started as one email address claiming a cosmetic item in a battle royale can cascade into dozens of sender domains within weeks. Multiply this across a year of gaming — seasonal events, new title launches, beta access windows, limited-time reward campaigns — and a single email address used for gaming can receive over 200 promotional messages per month.

The fix is not unsubscribing (that often just confirms your address is active). The fix is using a different email address for every gaming context, one you can switch off without touching your real inbox.

## What Is an Email Alias and How Does It Work?

An email alias is a fake address that forwards messages to your real inbox. You give out the alias, not your actual email. When messages arrive at the alias, they pass through to you. If the alias starts generating spam, you pause or delete it — the sender's messages stop, your real address stays untouched.

Most alias services also let you reply from the alias address, so the sender never sees your real email in the reply-to header.

## FAQ

**Can I use an email alias for all Twitch Drop claims, or do some publishers reject them?**

Most publishers accept any valid forwarding address. A small number of publishers use email domain filtering to block obvious disposable email services (like mailinator.com). Alt-ID aliases use legitimate-looking domains including custom domains you set up yourself, so they pass these checks in nearly all cases. If a publisher rejects a specific alias domain, you can create a new alias under a different custom domain.

**Will using an alias break my Twitch account's linked email?**

No. Your Twitch account email is separate from the publisher accounts you link for Drop claims. You can keep your real email on your Twitch profile and use an alias only when the publisher redirect asks you to register or verify a game account. [Learn more about Alt-ID's compatibility](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-twitch-drops-gaming-reward-claims-2026__faq).

**What happens to my claimed rewards if I delete the alias?**

Your in-game rewards are already credited to your game account before you delete the alias. Deleting the alias only stops future email from that sender — it does not affect items already added to your account.

**Does Alt-ID work on consoles like PS5 or Xbox?**

Consoles do not run the Surfshark app natively, but you do not need the app to use an alias. You create the alias on your phone or PC, then type the alias address into any console form field that asks for an email. The alias works regardless of which device generated the email request.

**How is Alt-ID different from just creating a throwaway Gmail account?**

A throwaway Gmail account requires you to check a separate inbox, log into a separate account, and manage credentials for an address that Gmail may suspend for inactivity. An alias forwards everything to your real inbox, requires zero separate login, and can be deleted permanently in seconds. You also cannot easily create hundreds of Gmail accounts without running into Google's phone verification requirements.

**Is Surfshark Alt-ID safe to use with sensitive gaming accounts?**

Alt-ID is a forwarding service — it reads the subject and recipient of forwarded messages to route them but does not store email content long-term. For gaming accounts the privacy risk is low. If you need maximum privacy for high-value accounts, combine Alt-ID with a strong unique password and two-factor authentication on the game publisher's side.
