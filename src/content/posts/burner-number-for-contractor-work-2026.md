## The Problem Nobody Talks About: When Your Work Number Meets Dating Apps

Most privacy advice treats work and personal life as separate problems. They're not — especially for contractors.

When you list your business number publicly (which you have to, because that's how you get clients), you're also handing anyone with Google a path to your full professional footprint. A match from Tinder who also happens to be in your city can search your number and find your company name, your Yelp reviews, your Facebook business page, and sometimes your home neighborhood if you list a service area.

The reverse is also true: a client who calls your number for a quote could, if they're determined, find you on dating apps that are linked to the same number. This is how people get harassed across contexts — the work-to-personal bridge is just a number lookup away.

The solution isn't to stop advertising your number. You can't run a contracting business without a contactable number. The solution is to use a different number in each context, so neither side can cross-reference the other.

## Why Contractors Are More Exposed Than They Think

Most people who use dating apps are operating with some layer of social obscurity — their number isn't publicly indexed, it's just in a few apps. For contractors, that's not the case.

Here's where your number typically gets indexed:

**Business directory listings.** Google Business Profile, Yelp, Thumbtack, Angi, HomeAdvisor — these platforms encourage contractors to display contact numbers prominently. Once indexed, they stay in search results for years.

**Facebook groups and marketplace.** Contractors regularly post in local "Recommend a Trades Person" groups, often with their direct number in the post. Facebook groups are indexed by Google.

**Truck and yard sign advertising.** Your number on a truck gets photographed. Sometimes those photos get uploaded to Google Maps street view or Yelp photo streams.

**Word-of-mouth referrals.** Clients screenshot your number and share it in neighborhood apps (Nextdoor) and WhatsApp groups. You have no visibility into where it goes from there.

This is normal and unavoidable for the business. But it means your work number has a much larger public footprint than a typical private individual's number — and you probably underestimate how many people have it.
