## Why Globo Play Blocks So Many VPNs

Grupo Globo faces the same contractual pressure every regional broadcaster does: rights holders who licensed content to Globo Play in Brazil often restrict distribution elsewhere, and Globo has to enforce it. Detection typically works in layers:

**IP reputation databases** flag incoming IPs against known VPN and datacenter ranges before your session even loads.

**Shared-IP overload** — too many users on one exit node, a common free-VPN trait — gets that IP burned and blacklisted quickly.

**DNS leak detection** catches devices that leak real DNS queries while the VPN is active, exposing a mismatch with your apparent location.

[Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) struggle hardest here — their small, reused IP pools are usually already flagged. Cheap paid VPNs with only a handful of Brazilian servers face the same ceiling: not enough IPs to rotate around a block.
