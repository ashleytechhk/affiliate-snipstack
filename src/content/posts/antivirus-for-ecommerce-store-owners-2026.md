## What Can Actually Go Wrong

Let us talk plainly about the threats that hit ecommerce sellers every day.

**Ransomware** is the most financially devastating attack. A criminal sneaks malicious software onto your computer, and it quietly encrypts every file — your customer list, your order history, your accounting records, your supplier details — so you cannot open any of them. Then you get a message demanding payment, usually hundreds to thousands of dollars in cryptocurrency, with a deadline. Many victims pay and still never recover their files.

**Credential theft** targets your store login. Once a hacker has your username and password for your selling platform, your PayPal, or your email account, they can redirect payments to their own bank, cancel orders, message your customers with scam links using your name, or simply lock you out of your own business.

**Spyware** sits invisibly on your computer and records everything you type. Your banking password. Your store admin login. Your email. You would never know it was there until your accounts are emptied.

**Fake invoice malware** is particularly common among small business owners. You receive what looks like a legitimate supplier invoice by email. You open the attachment. The file installs malware automatically. This one trick costs small business owners hundreds of millions of dollars every year.
