## Why Real Estate Agent Alerts Are an Inbox Nightmare

Property portals are lead-generation machines first and consumer tools second. When you register for alerts on Domain, Zillow, Rightmove, or any local portal, your email address moves into a database that is often shared with: listing agents, developer marketing teams, mortgage brokers, conveyancing firms, and third-party advertisers.

Each party has a different standard for "opt-in." Some honour unsubscribe requests promptly. Many do not. Industry data consistently shows real estate among the top five sectors for unsolicited commercial email.

The irony is that you cannot simply ignore all of it. Genuine property alerts — a new listing matching your criteria, a price drop, an inspection time — are time-sensitive. Missing them because they drowned in noise has real financial consequences in a fast-moving market.

An alias address draws a clean line. The alias receives everything from a given portal or agent. You monitor it when actively searching. You silence or delete it when you step back. Your real address stays clean throughout.

## SimpleLogin: The Power-User Option

SimpleLogin (now owned by Proton) is the most flexible alias service available. You can create unlimited aliases, attach a custom domain, reply from aliases, and inspect all forwarding rules from a clean dashboard.

For real estate specifically, the ability to reply from the alias matters more than you might expect. Arranging inspection times, asking for a floor plan, negotiating with an agent — all of that can stay behind the alias if you use SimpleLogin. Alt-ID does not offer this.

The downside is friction. SimpleLogin is a tool built for technically comfortable users. The dashboard is detailed, which is a plus for power users and mildly overwhelming for someone who just wants to stop getting spam from a portal they browsed three times.

Pricing is higher than Alt-ID at the entry level and there are no bundled extras — no VPN, no antivirus.

## Apple Hide My Email: Convenient but Walled

If you live entirely in the Apple ecosystem — iPhone, iPad, Mac, Safari — Hide My Email is frictionless. It integrates directly into Safari's autofill and iOS's Mail app. Sign up for a property portal, Safari offers a masked email, done.

The problems appear when you step outside Apple's garden. Accessing your alias management on an Android or Windows machine is awkward. There is no web dashboard worth speaking of. If your household is mixed-device, or if you do property research on a work Windows laptop, Hide My Email creates gaps.

It is also worth noting that iCloud+ pricing bundles storage you may not need just to unlock the alias feature.

## Firefox Relay: The Free-Tier Starter

Firefox Relay's free tier gives you five aliases with no expiry. For a buyer who is only active on two or three portals at once, five is enough. The paid tier ($1.99/month) is competitive, though it adds a phone mask feature you probably do not need for property alerts.

The limitation is volume and flexibility. There is no alt-identity layer, no breach monitoring, and replies from aliases require the paid plan. It is the right tool for someone who wants to dip their toe in alias use before committing to a paid service.

## How to Set Up an Alias Strategy for Property Alerts

![Alias strategy for real estate agent property alerts — device mockup comparison](/og/email-alias-for-real-estate-agent-property-alerts-2026-inline-2.png)

A simple three-alias structure covers most property searches:

1. **Portal alias** — one alias per major listing site. When portal emails turn spammy, deactivate the alias without touching the others.
2. **Agent alias** — a separate alias you hand to individual agents. If an agent sells your details to a mortgage broker, you know exactly who did it.
3. **Developer / project alias** — new developments often have aggressive marketing teams. Isolate these behind a dedicated alias.

This structure takes about ten minutes to set up with Alt-ID. Review it every ninety days: deactivate aliases that are no longer useful, create new ones when you engage with new portals or agents.

## FAQ

### Can I use an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-healthcare-appointment-reminders-2026/) to sign up for property portal alerts without getting spammed?

Yes. Most portals accept alias addresses the same as any other email address. You receive the alerts you want, and if the portal starts sending unwanted marketing you deactivate the alias rather than unsubscribing repeatedly.

### Does Surfshark Alt-ID work with Australian, UK, and US property portals?

Alt-ID generates a standard email address that any portal can send to. It works with Domain, REA, Rightmove, Zoopla, Zillow, Realtor.com, and other portals regardless of their country of operation.

### Will agents know I am using a masked email?

The alias looks like an ordinary email address to the recipient. Agents and portals cannot tell the difference between an alias and a primary inbox address.

### [What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my property alerts if I cancel Surfshark?

If you cancel the Surfshark One plan that includes Alt-ID, your aliases stop forwarding. Before cancelling, update any alert subscriptions you want to keep to your real or alternative address. This is standard for any subscription alias service.

### Is an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-investment-account-notifications-2026/) the same as a temporary or disposable email?

No, and this distinction matters for property alerts. Disposable email services (Mailinator-style) generate addresses that expire quickly and are often blocked by legitimate portals. An alias service like Alt-ID or SimpleLogin forwards indefinitely until you deactivate it, which is exactly what you need for ongoing alerts.

### Can I reply to agents from my alias address?

With Alt-ID, replies are not sent from the alias — it is designed for receiving.
