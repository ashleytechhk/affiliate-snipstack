## Malware Detection: Is Avast Free Actually Good?

The honest answer: yes, better than nothing. Avast consistently scores in the 99–100% range in AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives evaluations for known malware. For zero-day threats (brand new, never-seen attacks), free tiers typically lag paid products by a few percentage points because behavior-based analysis and machine-learning updates are either throttled or saved for paying customers.

Surfshark's antivirus engine, powered by independent lab-tested tech, performs comparably on known threats. The real difference shows up in what surrounds the scanner.

### What Avast Free Does Not Tell You Up Front

Three things buried in Avast's privacy documentation that matter:

1. **Data collection for advertising.** Avast's free product historically shared anonymized (but often re-identifiable) browsing data with third-party advertisers. After a 2020 FTC settlement, Avast updated its practices — but the opt-out remains non-obvious.
2. **Feature gating.** Ransomware protection, advanced firewall, and webcam shield all sit behind Avast Premium or Avast One paid plans. You will see prompts for them constantly.
3. **One device only.** The free license covers a single device. Family coverage costs extra.

If you are running a household with a few machines, "free" stops being free very quickly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Avast Free Antivirus actually safe to use in 2026?

Avast Free provides genuine malware protection and scores well in independent lab tests. The concern is not detection quality — it is the data-sharing model behind the free product. Avast has historically monetized user data through its subsidiary Jumpshot (later shut down after public pressure and regulatory scrutiny). The company updated its practices, but the free tier remains ad-supported and data-informed. For basic protection on a budget, it works. For privacy-conscious users, the trade-off warrants scrutiny.

### Does Avast Free slow down your computer?

In performance benchmarks, Avast Free has a moderate system footprint — heavier than ultra-light competitors but lighter than [Norton 360](https://snipstack.io/norton-360-antivirus-2026/)'s full suite. Startup impact and full-scan overhead are the main performance hits. If system speed is a priority, look at products built on lighter scanning engines, or schedule scans for off-hours.
