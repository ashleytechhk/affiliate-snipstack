**AdGuard** releases dedicated mobile apps for both Android and iOS that go well beyond what a browser extension can do. ### 5. Blokada — Best Free DNS-Level Blocker for Android

**Blokada** has been a favorite in the Android privacy community for years. Its core approach is a local VPN that intercepts DNS queries and checks them against community-maintained block lists. Requests to known ad, tracker, and malware domains are dropped; everything else goes through normally.

The free version (Blokada 5 on Android) supports multiple filter lists, custom host files, and a simple toggle to pause blocking when needed. There is no browser dependency — it covers every app on your phone. Blokada Cloud, the paid tier, moves filtering to Blokada's DNS servers and adds iOS support with a cleaner setup experience.

Performance is solid for a free tool, though the filter lists are less frequently updated than commercial alternatives like AdGuard or CleanWeb. Power users who are comfortable managing their own filter subscriptions will get the most out of it. Casual users may find occasional false positives requiring manual allowlist additions.

### 6. NextDNS — Best for Customizable DNS-Level Blocking Across All Devices

**NextDNS** is a cloud DNS resolver that lets you build a [custom filter](https://snipstack.io/custom-filter-list-for-ublock-origin-2026/)ing profile, then apply it to any device — including your phone — by pointing your DNS settings at NextDNS servers. On iOS 14+ and Android 9+, you can set a private DNS provider without any extra app, so setup takes about two minutes.

The blocking engine supports over 50 curated filter lists, parental controls, per-device analytics showing exactly which domains were blocked, and security threat feeds. The free tier allows 300,000 queries per month, which is enough for moderate single-device use. After that, a low-cost subscription removes the cap.

NextDNS's limitation on mobile is the same as any pure DNS solution: it does not handle in-app ads served from the same domain as the app's content (a practice common in social media feeds). For those cases, a VPN-level solution like CleanWeb catches what DNS filtering misses. NextDNS works best as a complement to a browser-level blocker, or as a whole-home solution when configured on your router.

### 7. Pi-hole with Wireguard — Best for Tech-Savvy Users Who Want Full Control

**Pi-hole** is a self-hosted DNS sinkhole that runs on a Raspberry Pi (or any Linux machine) and blocks ad domains across every device on your network. Pair it with a WireGuard VPN server and you can route your phone's traffic back through your home network while out and about — getting Pi-hole's filtering on mobile even when you are not home.

This is the most powerful and most customizable option on this list. You choose your filter lists, you own your data, and there are no subscription fees beyond the hardware. Community filter lists are extensive and updated frequently. Dashboards show exactly which domains are being blocked across all devices.

The cost is time and technical comfort. Installing Pi-hole, configuring WireGuard, managing certificate updates, and troubleshooting DNS leaks requires Linux experience. For mobile power users who are also homelab enthusiasts, this is a rewarding setup. For everyone else, a managed solution like Surfshark CleanWeb delivers comparable mobile coverage without the maintenance burden.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Adblock Plus work on mobile?

Adblock Plus works in Firefox for Android and Samsung Internet as a browser extension. It does not [block ads in](https://snipstack.io/block-ads-in-free-games-ios-2026/) other browsers or inside apps. For full-phone coverage, a DNS-level or VPN-level tool is required.

### What is the best free [ad blocker for Android](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-for-android-2026/)?

Blokada (free on Android) and Brave Browser are both strong free options. Blokada covers in-app ads via DNS; Brave covers browser ads only. For the most complete free mobile protection, Brave in the browser plus Blokada for apps is a common pairing.

### Can Surfshark CleanWeb block in-app ads?

Yes — because CleanWeb operates at the VPN tunnel level, it intercepts DNS requests from every app on your device, not just browsers. This is how it blocks ads in apps where browser extensions cannot reach. [Learn more about Surfshark CleanWeb →](/blog/surfshark?sub=top-adblock-plus-2026__faq)

### Is AdGuard better than Adblock Plus on mobile?

For mobile, AdGuard is significantly more capable. Its Android app provides system-wide blocking via a local VPN, and its iOS app uses both the native content-blocking API and DNS filtering. Adblock Plus on mobile is limited to supported browsers. AdGuard has a free tier; more advanced features require a paid plan.

### Does 1Blocker work on Android?

No. 1Blocker is built for Apple platforms only — iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Android users should consider AdGuard, Blokada, or Brave as alternatives.

### How does DNS-level ad blocking differ from browser extension blocking?

A browser extension intercepts requests made inside a specific browser. DNS-level blocking intercepts domain lookups before any app or browser even attempts a connection — if the domain resolves to a blocked ad server, the request is dropped regardless of which app made it. VPN-level blocking (like Surfshark CleanWeb) works similarly but with additional flexibility and security since all traffic is also encrypted and routed through a private server.

### Will using an [ad blocker](https://snipstack.io/ad-blocker-firefox-2026/) break websites?

Occasionally. Aggressive filter lists can block assets that websites need to function — like login scripts or video players hosted on CDN domains that overlap with tracking infrastructure. All tools on this list include an easy allowlist or pause feature for when this happens.
