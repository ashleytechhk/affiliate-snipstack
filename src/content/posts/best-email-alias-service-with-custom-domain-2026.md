## What Is an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-crypto-exchange-registration-2026/) Service?

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-pharmacy-prescription-alerts-2026/) is a forwarding address that sits in front of your real inbox. When you sign up on a website, you give them the alias. Emails sent to that alias arrive in your real inbox as normal — but you never expose your actual email address. If that alias starts receiving spam, you delete it. Problem solved, no account changes needed.

Custom domain support takes this further. Instead of aliases that end in `@aliasprovider.com`, you can create addresses like `orders@yourname.com` or `newsletters@myprivacydomain.org`. This looks professional, helps you organize email by category, and does not signal to senders that you are using an alias service.

For someone getting 50 or more spam emails per day, an alias strategy with custom domains is often the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade they can make to their digital life.

## AnonAddy: Lowest Cost, Highest Control

AnonAddy (now rebranded as addy.io) is an open-source alias service with plans starting around $1/month for custom domain support. It is highly configurable — you can set rules for which aliases forward, which bounce, and which are silently discarded.

The trade-off is a steeper learning curve. The dashboard is functional rather than polished. Custom domain setup requires more DNS know-how than Alt-ID or SimpleLogin. But for a technical user who wants maximum control at minimal cost, AnonAddy is hard to beat.

**Best for:** Technical users, developers, anyone who wants self-hosting or extremely low price.

## Apple Hide My Email: Great for iPhone Users, Limited Everywhere Else

Apple's Hide My Email generates unique `@privaterelay.appleid.com` addresses that forward to your iCloud email. It works seamlessly on Apple devices and requires no setup beyond having an iCloud+ subscription.

The major limitation: no custom domains. All your aliases end in Apple's domain, which some websites block or treat with suspicion. You also cannot use aliases generated on Apple devices easily on Android or Windows without workarounds.

**Best for:** iPhone-only households who do not need custom domains.

## Setting Up Custom Domain Aliases: What to Expect

The process is broadly similar across services. You add the alias provider's DNS records (MX records for receiving mail, and sometimes SPF/DKIM records for sending) to your domain registrar. Most providers give you a step-by-step checklist. DNS propagation typically takes 5–30 minutes.

Once your domain is verified, you create aliases through the provider's dashboard or [browser extension](https://snipstack.io/browser-extension-to-check-scam-sites-2026/). Alt-ID and SimpleLogin both offer browser extensions that auto-generate an alias whenever you encounter an email field on a webpage — this is the workflow that most heavy users end up relying on.

Receiving email from an alias feels identical to receiving it from your real address. Replying through an alias depends on the service — Alt-ID supports reply-from-alias so recipients see your alias address, not your real one.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-webinar-registration-privacy-2026/) and a [disposable email](https://snipstack.io/disposable-email-that-receives-messages-2026/)?**
[Disposable email](https://snipstack.io/disposable-email-address-for-trials-2026/)s (like Guerrilla Mail) are temporary and expire after a short time. Aliases are persistent — they forward to your inbox indefinitely until you delete them. For anything requiring ongoing access (account logins, receipts, order tracking), aliases are the right tool. Disposable emails are best for one-time signups you do not care about.

**Can I reply to emails using an alias address?**
It depends on the service. Surfshark Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, and AnonAddy all support reply-from-alias, meaning your reply appears to come from the alias address. This is essential for maintaining privacy in two-way email conversations. [See how Alt-ID handles reply routing here.](/blog/altid?sub=best-email-alias-service-with-custom-domain-2026__faq)

**Will websites block alias email addresses?**
Some websites attempt to block known alias domains. Using a custom domain alias sidesteps this almost entirely, because the domain is yours and not associated with any alias provider in public blocklists. This is one of the strongest reasons to invest in custom domain aliases over shared-provider ones.

**Is it legal to use a fake name or alternate identity when signing up for services?**
For optional profile fields, yes. For services that legally require your real identity (banking, government services, age verification), you must provide accurate information. Alias services and identity protection tools are designed for protecting your privacy on sites that do not require verified identity — not for bypassing legal obligations.

**[Does Surfshark](https://snipstack.io/does-surfshark-one-include-email-protection-2026/) Alt-ID work with any email provider?**
Yes. Your real inbox can be Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, ProtonMail, or any other provider. Alt-ID simply forwards incoming mail to whichever address you register. Your existing email workflow stays exactly the same.
