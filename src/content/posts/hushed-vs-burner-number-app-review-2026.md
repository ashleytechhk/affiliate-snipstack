Moving from in-app chat to texting feels like a natural step in online dating. What most people do not realize is that sharing your real number creates a permanent trail.

A [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-alias-for-work-2026/) is not just a way to receive texts. With a real number, someone can:

Blocking a number does not erase the problem — they still have it. The only real protection is never giving out your actual number in the first place.

## Hushed: What You Get

![Hushed app interface showing a secondary number for dating privacy](/og/hushed-vs-burner-number-app-review-2026-inline-1.png)

Hushed is a VoIP [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-online-selling-2026/) app that has been around since 2013. It gives you a secondary number for calls and texts, accessible from the app on any internet-connected device.

### What Works

**Area code selection.** Hushed lets you pick from 300+ area codes across the US, Canada, and UK. For someone who wants a number that matches their city, this is a genuine feature.

**Multiple numbers on one account.** You can run several Hushed lines simultaneously, which matters if you use different numbers for different apps or different stages of dating.

**Pay-as-you-go option.** For occasional use, Hushed's credit-based plan avoids locking you into a monthly subscription when you barely need the number.

**Disappearing messages.** Hushed includes a chat feature with optional auto-delete, which limits what someone could screenshot or preserve.

### What Falls Short

**Pricing is not cheap.** The unlimited plan starts at $6.99/month. The prepaid plan starts at $7.99/month. The pay-as-you-go plan costs $8.99/month plus credits. For a secondary number you might only use for dating, that adds up.

**US, Canada, and UK only.** If you are outside those three countries — traveling, living abroad, or dating internationally — Hushed has no number available for you.

**It is a VoIP number.** Dating apps and verification services increasingly screen for VoIP numbers. Hushed numbers are detectable. Some platforms reject them outright at signup. Others display flags or show the carrier as a VoIP provider, which looks suspicious to matches who check.

**No broader privacy features.** Hushed is a phone number tool, nothing more. There is no VPN, no data breach monitoring, and no [identity protection](https://snipstack.io/identity-protection-vpn-bundle-affordable-2026/) bundled in.

## Burner: What You Get

Burner, from Ad Hoc Labs, is the original "throwaway number" app. The name itself signals the intended use: get a number, use it, burn it when you are done.

### What Works

**Easy number swapping.** The core mechanic is straightforward: if a contact becomes a problem, burn the number and replace it. For serial daters who cycle through matches quickly, this workflow makes sense.

**Spam filtering.** Burner includes call and spam blocking tools, which reduce the noise from anyone you have already burned or blocked.

**Auto-reply.** You can set auto-reply messages for when you are unavailable, which some users find useful for managing early-stage matches before a relationship gets off the ground.

**Reasonable annual pricing.** At $47.99/year (roughly $4/month), the annual plan is among the more affordable dedicated second-number apps.

### What Falls Short

**US and Canada only.** Burner does not offer numbers outside North America. Full stop.

**It is a VoIP number.** Like Hushed, Burner operates on VoIP infrastructure. The same detection problem applies — some platforms flag or reject [Burner number](https://snipstack.io/burner-number-for-online-classified-ads-2026/)s, and technically savvy matches can identify them as non-standard lines.

**Paid subscription for basic features.** There is no meaningful free tier. The monthly plan is $7.99/month, and the annual plan requires a full year's commitment upfront.

**Single-purpose tool.** Like Hushed, Burner covers only the phone number layer. Everything else — connection security, [email privacy](https://snipstack.io/email-privacy-after-facebook-data-leak-2026/), breach monitoring — requires separate apps and subscriptions.

## The VoIP Problem Explained

Both Hushed and Burner use VoIP technology. That means your secondary number runs over the internet rather than a standard cellular network — and that matters more than most people realize.

Dating apps have invested heavily in fraud prevention. Part of that investment is carrier screening: identifying whether a phone number belongs to a real mobile carrier or a VoIP provider. When an app flags your number as VoIP, two things can happen:

1. The app rejects the number and will not let you verify your account.
2. The app accepts it, but a match who runs a reverse lookup sees "VoIP / Non-fixed" as the carrier — which looks fake.

Neither outcome is ideal. The first blocks you from using the app at all. The second undermines the whole point of having a credible alternative number.
