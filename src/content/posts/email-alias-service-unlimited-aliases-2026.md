## What Is an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-investment-account-notifications-2026/) Service?

An [email alias service](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-service-that-works-with-apple-mail-2026/) is a tool that generates alternative email addresses — aliases — and forwards all mail sent to those addresses into one real inbox of your choosing.

The alias is not a second inbox you have to check. It is a forwarding layer. Mail arrives at `shopping-alias@altid.domain`, the service forwards it to `you@gmail.com`, and you read it in Gmail exactly as you would any other email. The sender never knows your Gmail address exists.

The key mechanics:

- **Forwarding is transparent.** Replies from your inbox go back through the alias so the sender always sees the alias, never your real address.
- **Aliases are disposable on demand.** Delete an alias and all future mail to that address is silently dropped.
- **You can create as many as you need.** The "unlimited" part means one alias per service, not one alias per category of service.

This is fundamentally different from Gmail's "+" trick (`yourname+tag@gmail.com`), which does not hide your real address and cannot be deleted. It is also different from a second email account, which creates a separate inbox you would have to check separately.

## Why "Unlimited" Is the Feature That Actually Matters

When people first hear about [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-legal-document-service-signup-2026/)ing, they think in terms of categories: one alias for shopping, one for newsletters, one for social apps. Five aliases total. That seems manageable even with a limit.

The problem is that category-level aliasing only tells you *which category* of service leaked your address — not *which specific service*. When `shopping-alias@altid.domain` starts attracting pharmacy spam, you know a shopping site sold your data. You do not know if it was the large retailer, the boutique clothing shop, or the flash-sale app you signed up for three years ago.

Per-service aliasing solves this. Instead of one shopping alias, you create:

- `amazon-yourname@altid.domain`
- `ebay-yourname@altid.domain`
- `asos-yourname@altid.domain`

When pharmacy spam arrives at the Asos alias, you know exactly where your data went. You delete that alias. The spam stops. You may even decide to close that account.

**This forensic precision requires unlimited aliases.** Most people who use online services regularly have accounts on 50 to 200 sites. A plan capped at 10 or 20 aliases either forces you back to category-level grouping — losing the forensic benefit — or runs out before you have covered your exposure.

Unlimited aliases also remove the mental overhead of deciding whether something is "worth" an alias. When there is no cap, the answer is always yes. Use an alias for everything. The system only improves as you do.

## What to Look for in an Email Alias Service

Not all alias tools are equal. Here are the features that matter:

### Delete-on-Demand

The entire spam-killing mechanism depends on being able to permanently delete a compromised alias. A service that only lets you deactivate an alias — or that gives you no delete option at all — breaks the core value proposition. Verify this before committing to any tool.

### True Address Masking

The alias address must be opaque — it should not be guessable back to your real address. Gmail's "+" trick fails here: `yourname+shopping@gmail.com` exposes `yourname@gmail.com` to anyone who looks. A proper alias service generates an address with no connection to your real one.

### Reply-From-Alias Support

When you reply to a forwarded email, the reply should leave from the alias address, not your real address. Without this, any reply you send exposes your real account. Alt ID and comparable dedicated tools route replies through the alias by default.

### Works With Any Inbox

Provider-specific alias features (Outlook's built-in aliases, iCloud Hide My Email) tie you to that provider's ecosystem. If you ever switch providers, those aliases stop working. A dedicated alias service like Alt ID works with any inbox — Gmail, Outlook, ProtonMail, Fastmail, anything — regardless of which one you happen to be using now.

### Reasonable Pricing

Unlimited aliases should not cost more than a solid VPN subscription. Alt ID is bundled inside Surfshark's privacy suite, which means you get the alias service alongside a VPN and data removal tool at a combined price that undercuts buying each component separately.

## FAQ

**What exactly is an email alias service?**

It is a tool that creates forwarding email addresses — aliases — that route incoming mail to your real inbox. You give websites and services an alias instead of your real address. All mail still arrives in your existing inbox. The alias is a privacy layer between your real address and the outside world.

**Why do I need unlimited aliases — isn't five enough?**

Five aliases covers five categories of service. Unlimited aliases means one alias per service, which gives you forensic-level identification when spam hits. When `amazon-alias@altid.domain` starts receiving junk, you know Amazon (or a site you thought was Amazon) leaked your data. With only five category aliases, you know it was "a shopping site" — and cannot narrow it further without more investigation.

**How is this different from a throwaway email address?**

A throwaway address is typically temporary and expires. An alias from a dedicated service is persistent — it stays active until you delete it. This matters because you want an alias to keep working for months or years (your active Amazon account needs a permanent address) but disappear permanently the moment it is compromised. That combination — persistent until killed, then gone forever — is what alias services provide.

**Will using an alias address cause problems with account recovery?**

Potentially, if you later delete the alias. Before deleting a compromised alias, update the email on file for any accounts you want to keep. If you delete an alias tied to an active account and then get locked out, you may lose access to that account's recovery flow. The fix is to migrate the account to a fresh alias before deleting the old one.

**Do alias emails look different in my inbox?**

No. Forwarded alias emails appear in your inbox exactly like direct emails. The only difference you see is in the "To:" field — instead of your real address, it shows the alias. This is also how you identify which alias an email arrived on, which is how you trace spam back to its source.

**Can I send emails from an alias address?**

With Alt ID, replies to forwarded emails go out from the alias address, keeping your real address hidden. For composing new emails from an alias address (not replies), check the current feature set in the Surfshark app — this capability varies by tool and plan.

**Is an email alias service worth it if I am already using a good spam filter?**

Spam filters are reactive. They catch spam that has already been sent to your real address, from senders that have already been identified. New spam domains, novel phishing attempts, and carefully crafted marketing emails regularly bypass filters. An alias service is proactive — it prevents your real address from reaching spammers at all. The two approaches complement each other, but the alias strategy addresses the root cause rather than managing the symptom.
