## Why Seniors Are the Primary Target for Cyber Criminals

The FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center reported that adults aged 60 and older lost more than **$3.4 billion to online fraud in a single year** — a figure that grows year after year. Criminals know that many older adults are less familiar with digital warning signs, making them easier to deceive.

The most common attacks targeting seniors include:

- **Phishing emails** that pretend to be from a bank, the government, or a tech company
- **Fake virus alerts** that pop up on the screen and pressure you to call a phone number
- **Malware hidden in email attachments**, such as fake invoices or delivery notices
- **Ransomware**, which locks your files and demands payment to get them back

What makes these attacks so dangerous is that they constantly evolve. Criminals release new versions of their malware tools every week. If your antivirus software is running on old definitions — even definitions that are just a few days old — it may not recognise the latest threats.

This is exactly where automatic updates make the difference.

## What "Off Peak Hours" Means and Why It Matters

"Off peak hours" refers to quiet times — typically late at night or early in the morning — when your internet connection is less busy and you are not actively using your computer.

A smart antivirus program uses these quiet windows to:

1. **Download the latest virus definition files** — the database of known threats
2. **Run a full system scan** — checking every file on your computer
3. **Install program updates** — keeping the software itself current

Why does timing matter? Downloading large update files during the day can slow your internet connection and interrupt video calls, streaming, or browsing. By scheduling updates for off peak hours, the software does its heavy lifting overnight, leaving daytime performance completely unaffected.

For seniors, this is ideal. You wake up in the morning and your protection is already refreshed — no alerts asking you to update, no pop-ups interrupting your day.

## The Risk of Outdated Antivirus Protection

Many people install antivirus software once and assume it will protect them forever. Unfortunately, that is not how it works.

Antivirus software relies on a virus definition database — a constantly-updated list of known malicious software. When a new piece of malware is created, security researchers identify it, analyse it, and add its signature to the database. If your software has not downloaded that update, it will not know to block the new threat.

Think of it like a security guard working from an old photo album. If a criminal changes their appearance, the guard will not recognise them.

Here is how quickly threats evolve:

- Security researchers discover an average of **450,000 new malicious programs** every single day
- A zero-day vulnerability — an attack brand new to the security world — can spread to millions of computers within hours
- Out-of-date antivirus software can miss up to **40% of new threats**, according to independent testing labs

For someone who is not checking technical news or monitoring update logs, the only reliable safety net is software that updates itself, on its own, every day.
