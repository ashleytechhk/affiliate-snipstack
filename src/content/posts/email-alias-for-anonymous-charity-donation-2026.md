## Why Anonymous Donations Are More Complicated Than They Sound

Most donors assume that choosing to remain anonymous in the donation form is enough. It rarely is. The form anonymises your name on the charity's public donor wall — it does not prevent the organisation from storing your email address, adding it to their CRM, or selling it as part of a data-sharing agreement with partner organisations.

This is entirely legal in many jurisdictions. Charities rely on donor data to fund future campaigns, and data partnerships are a standard revenue stream. Some organisations are transparent about it in their privacy policy; many are not.

The only reliable way to keep your real email out of a charity's database is to never give it to them in the first place. That is where email aliases come in.

## SimpleLogin: Best for Power Users

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service with a generous free tier (10 aliases) and an unlimited paid plan. It is the go-to choice for users who want granular control: you can set different reply-from addresses per alias, add custom domains, and even self-host the entire service on your own server.

For charity donations specifically, SimpleLogin is excellent because you can label each alias clearly (for example, `donation-redcross@yourdomain.com`) and track which charities are generating the most email. When a particular alias becomes a spam magnet, you deactivate it without affecting any other alias.

The downside: setup is more involved than Alt-ID, and the free tier's 10-alias limit fills up quickly if you donate to multiple organisations.

## Apple Hide My Email: The Zero-Friction Option for Apple Users

If you use an iPhone, iPad, or Mac and already pay for an iCloud+ subscription, Hide My Email is already in your toolkit. Safari autofills a generated address whenever a site asks for your email — no separate account, no configuration.

The limitation for charity donations is flexibility. Apple generates the alias automatically; you cannot customise it or easily track which alias maps to which charity. If you want to delete a specific alias later, you need to hunt through a list in Settings. It works, but it is less organised than Alt-ID or SimpleLogin.

## Guerrilla Mail: When You Need a Burner Right Now

Guerrilla Mail generates a throwaway inbox in seconds with no account required. Visit the site, copy the address, paste it into the donation form, receive the confirmation email in the browser tab, and walk away.

The catch: there is no forwarding. If you need the tax receipt delivered to your real inbox, Guerrilla Mail is not the answer. It is useful only for one-time confirmation emails where you do not need the receipt long-term. Most donors who need tax documentation for filing purposes will find this option too limited.

## Firefox Relay: The Browser-Integrated Middle Ground

Mozilla's Firefox Relay integrates directly into the Firefox browser, similar to Apple's approach. The free tier allows five aliases, and the paid plan removes the cap. It works well for casual donors who use Firefox as their primary browser and want a lightweight solution without a separate subscription.

Like Apple Hide My Email, it lacks the identity-profile feature that makes Alt-ID stand out for donors who want full separation between their real name and the charity's records.

## FAQ

### Is using an [email alias for](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-veteran-benefit-portal-registration-2026/) charity donations legal?

Yes. You are not misrepresenting your identity for financial gain; you are simply choosing to limit how much personal data you share. Charities are not entitled to your real email address. Using a forwarding alias is no different from using a P.O. box for postal mail.

### Will I still get my donation tax receipt?

Yes, as long as you use a forwarding alias. Tools like Alt-ID, SimpleLogin, Firefox Relay, and Apple Hide My Email all forward incoming messages to your real inbox. Your tax documentation arrives normally. Guerrilla Mail does not forward, so avoid it if you need the receipt.

### Can charities detect that I am using an alias?

Most cannot. An alias address looks like any other email address. Some sophisticated CRM systems flag unusual domains, but forwarding aliases from services like Alt-ID use standard-looking domains that do not trigger filters. Your donation will be processed normally.

### Does an email alias hide my payment details?

No. The alias protects your email address and, with Alt-ID, your name. Your payment method — credit card, bank transfer, digital wallet — still creates a financial record. If payment-level privacy is your goal, that requires different tools entirely.

### Can I use Alt-ID for multiple charities at once?

Yes. Alt-ID allows unlimited alias generation, so you can create a separate alias for each charity. This makes it easy to identify which organisation is sending excessive email and deactivate that specific alias without affecting the others. [Start generating aliases with Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-anonymous-charity-donation-2026__faq) to get organised before your next donation.

### What happens to forwarded emails if I delete an alias?

Once you delete or deactivate an alias, forwarding stops immediately. Any emails sent to that alias after deletion bounce or are discarded, depending on the service. Your real inbox receives nothing further from that address.
