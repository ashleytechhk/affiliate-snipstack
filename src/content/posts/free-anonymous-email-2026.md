## Troubleshooting

**I am not receiving forwarded emails.**
Check the "Forwarding address" setting inside Alt-ID. Confirm the real address you entered is correct and does not contain a typo. Also check your spam folder — some email providers flag forwarded messages during initial setup.

**The site rejected my alias address.**
A small number of sites block non-standard email domains. In that case, create a separate free Gmail or ProtonMail account to use as the forwarding destination, and pair it with the alias. Your primary inbox stays clean.

**I accidentally deleted an alias I still need.**
Deleted aliases cannot be restored. Create a new alias, update the forwarding address in your account settings for that service, and label it clearly to avoid the same issue.

**My alias is receiving a lot of spam already.**
This usually means the service you gave the alias to sold or leaked its email list. Delete the alias — the spam stops immediately. Create a fresh alias for any legitimate communications from that service if needed.

**I have too many aliases and cannot keep track.**
Use the label field consistently from the start. Brief, descriptive labels like "Amazon-orders" or "LinkedIn-signups" are far easier to audit than generic names. A monthly five-minute review keeps the list manageable.
