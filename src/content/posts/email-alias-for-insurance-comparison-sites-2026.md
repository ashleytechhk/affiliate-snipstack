### Gmail's `+tag` Trick

Gmail lets you append `+anything` to your address: `yourname+insurance@gmail.com`. Mail still arrives in your inbox. In theory you could filter by tag and bulk-delete later.

In practice, insurance lead systems strip the `+tag` suffix before storing or reselling your address. Your `+insurance` tag disappears, and the brokers have your base address — the permanent one you can never revoke. This approach provides the illusion of protection without the substance.

It also doesn't help with calls. Your phone number is typically on the same form, and email aliases do nothing about the phone-based part of the broker follow-up campaign.

---

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple's service (available with iCloud+ from $0.99/month) generates aliases that forward to your iCloud address. It's clean, integrated into Safari autofill, and you can delete aliases directly from iOS Settings.

The limitation is platform lock-in. If you use Gmail, Outlook, or any non-iCloud address as your primary inbox, setup is awkward — you have to add the iCloud address as a forwarding destination, adding a step and a dependency. It also doesn't generate alternative identity details, so your real name still enters the insurance form's lead data.

For Apple-ecosystem users who already pay for iCloud+, Hide My Email is a workable option. For everyone else, the friction is real.

### SimpleLogin and Firefox Relay

Neither generates alternative personal data alongside the alias, and neither is specifically optimized for the insurance use case. For general email hygiene, either is a reasonable choice. ### Free Throwaway Services (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail)

Throwaway services give you a temporary address you check in a separate browser tab. They cost nothing and require no account.

For insurance comparison forms, they fail on two counts:

1. **Blocked at the form level.** Major comparison sites check submitted addresses against known disposable email domains. Guerrilla Mail and 10 Minute Mail domains are on most of these lists. Your form submission either gets rejected outright or flagged, affecting quote quality.

2. **They don't forward.** You have to keep the browser tab open to receive emails. Insurance follow-ups arrive over 48–72 hours. You will miss quotes.

Throwaway services are useful for single-use sign-ups with immediate confirmation. They are the wrong tool for insurance comparison.
