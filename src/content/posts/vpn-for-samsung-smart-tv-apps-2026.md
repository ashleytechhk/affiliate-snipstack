## What Is a VPN, and Why Does It Matter for Smart TVs?

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is software that routes your internet traffic through a server in another country. To every website and streaming service you visit, it looks like you're browsing from that country, not from your actual location.

For regular laptops and phones, this is straightforward: install the app, tap connect. Smart TVs are trickier because Samsung's Tizen OS doesn't natively support VPN apps the way Android does. That's why most guides recommend one of two approaches:

1. **Router-level VPN** — install Surfshark on your home router so every device, including your TV, connects through it automatically.
2. **Smart DNS / Virtual Router** — share a VPN connection from a Windows or Mac computer to your TV over Wi-Fi.

Both methods work. We'll cover both below.

## How to Set Up a VPN on Your Samsung Smart TV

### Method 1: Router-Level VPN (Recommended)

This is the cleanest solution. Once set up, every device in your home — including your Samsung TV — routes through the VPN without any extra steps.

1. Log into your router admin panel (typically at `192.168.1.1` or `192.168.0.1`)
2. Find the VPN or OpenVPN settings section
3. Enter your Surfshark credentials and select your preferred server location
4. Save and restart the router

Surfshark provides step-by-step guides for over 30 popular router models. Most users complete the setup in under 20 minutes.

**Routers that support this:** ASUS, Netgear Nighthawk, GL.iNet, and Linksys WRT models work well. Some ISP-provided routers do not support VPN firmware — in that case, use Method 2.

### Method 2: Share VPN from Your Computer (Windows or Mac)

1. Install Surfshark on your Windows PC or Mac
2. Connect to your chosen server
3. On Windows: go to Network Settings > Mobile Hotspot, enable hotspot sharing from your VPN-connected adapter
4. Connect your Samsung TV to that Wi-Fi hotspot
5. Done — your TV now streams through the VPN

This method requires your computer to stay on while streaming, but it costs nothing extra and works on any Samsung Smart TV model.

### Method 3: Native App on Samsung Smart TV (Select Models)

Surfshark has a native Tizen app available for Samsung Smart TVs running Tizen 5.5 and above (generally 2020 models and newer). Search "Surfshark" in the Samsung Smart TV app store. If your model supports it, this is the most seamless option — open the app, log in, pick a region, and stream.
