## Step 3 — Generate Your First [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-outlook-2026/)

1. Click **Create new Alt-ID**.
2. Alt-ID auto-generates a random alias like `dusty.harbor.29@altid.email`. You can accept this or click the refresh icon for a different one.
3. In the **Forward to** field, enter the real email address where you want messages to land.
4. Add a note (optional but recommended): e.g., "Used for: newsletter signup / [site name]". You will thank yourself later.
5. Click **Save**.

That alias is now live. Any email sent to it gets forwarded to your real inbox. The sender sees only the alias — never your real address.

**Pro tip:** Create a separate alias for every service or category. One for shopping, one for newsletters, one for forums. When one starts attracting spam, you delete just that alias — your real inbox stays untouched.

## Troubleshooting

**Forwarded emails are landing in spam.**
Add your Alt-ID forwarding address to your real inbox's contacts or safe-sender list. Most providers trust mail from known senders.

**The alias confirmation email never arrived.**
Check that the "Forward to" address is correct in Alt-ID settings. Also check your spam folder — some providers flag forwarded mail.

**A site refuses to accept the alias.**
Some sites block known alias domains. Try using a different alias from a different domain (Alt-ID offers multiple). Alternatively, use your real address for that site and accept the trade-off.

**Replies are not going through.**
Ensure reply-forwarding is enabled in your Alt-ID settings. Some corporate email servers strip forwarding headers — test with a personal account first.

**I deleted an alias by accident.**
Alt-ID aliases cannot be restored once deleted. Create a new one with a fresh address and update the affected accounts.

## Is Anonymous Email Actually Anonymous?

Practically speaking, an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-generator-2026/) gives you strong pseudonymity — marketers and spammers cannot connect your alias to your real identity. But "anonymous" in the absolute sense has limits:

- Your ISP can still see network-level traffic.
- Payment records at Surfshark link to a real account.
- If you are replying to emails, metadata in headers can sometimes carry information.

For the majority of use cases — stopping spam, protecting your name from data brokers, keeping signups separate — Alt-ID is more than sufficient. For high-stakes operational security scenarios, the requirements go well beyond an alias tool.
