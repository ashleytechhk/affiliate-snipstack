## Norton 360: Still Reputable, But the Value Has Slipped

Norton is a household name for good reason. Its malware detection rates are consistently high — independent labs like AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives routinely score it above 99% for protection. For pure antivirus efficacy, it remains among the top tier.

The problem is everything around the antivirus itself. Norton 360 Deluxe has become a layered product: the base plan is reasonably priced in year one, then renews at roughly double. The VPN data allowance varies significantly by tier — lower Norton plans cap it, and the unlimited VPN is not included in every plan. Dark web monitoring is included, but private search is not. The dashboard itself is heavy — on older machines, users frequently report the Norton background processes adding 2–4% constant CPU load.

If your machine is powerful, your year-one discount made it attractive, and you do not care about VPN caps, Norton is still a sound choice. If you are on year two pricing and shopping around, there are leaner alternatives.

### McAfee Total Protection: Unlimited Devices, But Watch the Upsells

McAfee's positioning in 2026 is "unlimited devices, one price" — and that is genuinely useful for families or anyone with four or more devices. Protection rates are solid. The VPN is now included with most plans and is not bandwidth-capped, which puts it ahead of Norton on that dimension.

Where McAfee loses points: the interface feels dated, the renewal prices jump significantly in year two, and the product is prone to surfacing upsell prompts for identity protection tiers that arguably should be part of the base product. There is also no private search feature — your search activity is not insulated from ad tracking just because McAfee is scanning your files.

## Bitdefender and Malwarebytes: The Specialist Picks

Bitdefender Total Security is often the recommendation for users who want minimal system impact. It runs lean, has strong detection, and is relatively quiet about upselling. Its weak point in this comparison is the VPN: the included VPN limits you to 200 MB per day unless you buy Bitdefender Premium Security or add the Premium VPN separately. If VPN matters to you, the total cost lands close to or above the competition.

Malwarebytes Premium has a loyal following, particularly among users who want fast, manual scans without a persistent background process. The catch: real-time protection at the Premium level is solid for malware, but the VPN is a completely separate product (Malwarebytes Privacy) with its own subscription. Breach monitoring is not included. If you piece it together, you can build a stack that matches Surfshark One — at higher total cost and with two separate apps to manage.
