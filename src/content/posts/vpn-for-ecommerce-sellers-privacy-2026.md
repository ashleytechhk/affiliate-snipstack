## What Is a VPN? A Plain-English Definition

A VPN, or Virtual Private Network, is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a secure server operated by the VPN provider. Every piece of data leaving your device — browser requests, app traffic, login credentials, API calls — travels through this tunnel before reaching the open internet.

Two things happen immediately when you connect:

1. **Your traffic is encrypted.** Anyone trying to intercept your connection — whether on a hotel wifi network at a trade show or a shared coworking space — sees only scrambled data. Your supplier negotiations, customer order data, and payment processor logins become unreadable to outsiders.
2. **Your apparent location changes.** Websites and platforms see the VPN server's IP address and country, not your actual device location. This is what enables region-based pricing for subscriptions — more on that shortly.

For ecommerce sellers, these two functions address two very different but equally valuable needs: protecting business data, and reducing the cost of the tools you use every day.

## Why Privacy Matters Specifically for Ecommerce Sellers

Most online privacy advice is written for general consumers. Ecommerce sellers face a different and higher-stakes set of risks.

### Supplier Negotiations Are a Target

When you research suppliers, negotiate pricing, or communicate order terms on an unsecured network, that information has real commercial value. Competitors who know your supplier relationships, your margin structures, or your cost-per-unit have a direct business advantage. Without encryption, a man-in-the-middle attack on a cafe or conference wifi can expose these conversations.

### Store Backend Access Is Sensitive

Logging into Shopify, WooCommerce, Amazon Seller Central, or any third-party fulfillment platform over an unprotected connection creates risk. Session tokens can be hijacked on open networks, giving an attacker temporary access to your store backend without needing your password. A VPN encrypts the entire connection, including these session tokens.

### Payment Processor and Ad Account Credentials

Google Ads, Meta Ads, and payment processor dashboards are high-value targets. Sellers who access these from shared networks — trade shows, ecommerce conferences, co-working spaces — expose credentials that could result in unauthorized ad spend or fraudulent transactions. Encryption eliminates this attack surface.

### Price Surveillance from Platforms

Some ecommerce platforms and supplier marketplaces adjust displayed pricing or availability based on detected location. A VPN lets you browse without broadcasting your exact location, giving you a more neutral view of what competitors and suppliers are actually offering in different markets.

### Do ecommerce sellers really need a VPN, or is it overkill?

If you ever access your store backend, supplier accounts, or payment dashboards on a network outside your home — a co-working space, a conference, a hotel — a VPN is not overkill. The additional subscription savings make it pay for itself whether or not you ever face a security incident.

### Will a VPN slow down my store operations?

The encryption overhead from a modern VPN is small — typically 5–15% on an ideal connection. Surfshark's WireGuard protocol minimizes this. On a fast home or office connection, most sellers report no noticeable difference in day-to-day tasks like updating listings, pulling ad reports, or uploading product images. On slower conference or hotel wifi, network congestion is typically the bottleneck, not the VPN.

### Is it legal to use a [VPN to get](https://snipstack.io/vpn-to-get-cheaper-spotify-premium-regional-pricing-2026/) cheaper streaming subscriptions?

A VPN protects your network traffic — it encrypts data in transit and prevents interception on unsecured networks. It does not protect against phishing attacks, malware installed on your device, or vulnerabilities in your store platform itself.
