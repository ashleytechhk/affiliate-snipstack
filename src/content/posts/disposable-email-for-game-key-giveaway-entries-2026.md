## Why Giveaway Entries Wreck Your Inbox

Game key giveaways are a different beast than a normal newsletter signup, and that's exactly why disposable inboxes need different rules here.

**Entry requires a real, working address.** Most giveaway tools (Gleam, Rafflecopter, KingSumo, Woobox) email you a confirmation link or the actual key. If your address bounces or the inbox has already expired, you lose the entry — or worse, you win and never receive the key.

**The timeline is unpredictable.** Some giveaways deliver instantly. Others run for two weeks, draw winners after the deadline, then take another few days to actually send codes. A disposable address with a 10-minute or 24-hour lifespan is dead well before any of that resolves.

**Entry data gets resold.** Smaller giveaway sites and key-reseller "bonus entry" pages routinely add your email to marketing lists as a condition of entry, often buried in fine print. One giveaway entry can trigger months of unrelated spam.

**You enter a lot of them.** Anyone chasing free keys isn't entering once — they're in dozens of giveaways across Reddit threads, Discord bots, YouTube description links, and Twitter/X raffles. Each one is a separate leak vector. Multiply that by fifty entries a month and your inbox becomes unmanageable fast.

The fix isn't filtering harder after the fact — it's giving giveaway platforms an address that was never connected to your real identity in the first place.

## Why Gmail `+tag` and Free Throwaways Keep Failing You

Gmail's plus-addressing trick (`yourname+giveaway@gmail.com`) is a common recommendation, but it solves the wrong problem. Mail still lands in your normal inbox — fine for organization — but your base address `yourname@gmail.com` is sitting in plain sight for anyone who strips the tag. Automated spam-list tools do this by default, which means a single leaked `+tag` address exposes your real one instantly. There's also no way to revoke it without disabling your whole Gmail account.

Free throwaway inboxes solve the opposite problem badly: they're genuinely anonymous, but they self-destruct in minutes to hours. Giveaway platforms that verify entries with a delayed confirmation link, or that draw winners after a multi-week window, will simply never reach you. Several major giveaway tools also maintain blocklists of known temp-mail domains — bad actors abuse them to enter the same giveaway multiple times, so your legitimate entry can get flagged and discarded before a human sees it.
