## What Is an Email Alias (and Why Gmail's Built-in Trick Falls Short)?

An email alias is a secondary address that delivers mail to your primary inbox. When a website asks for your email, you give it the alias instead of your real address. If that alias starts attracting spam, you delete it — your real Gmail address stays untouched.

Gmail has a built-in workaround: add a `+tag` after your username (e.g., `yourname+shopsite@gmail.com`). It works, barely. The problem is that anyone who sees that address can strip the `+tag` and recover your real address in two seconds. Spammers and data brokers figured this out years ago.

A proper alias tool gives you a completely different address — something like `frog42rain@altid.net` — with zero connection to your real Gmail. That is the meaningful difference.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### Forwarded emails are landing in Gmail's spam folder

Gmail sometimes flags forwarded mail because the sending domain changed. Open the forwarded message, click **Not spam**, and then add the Alt-ID forwarding address to your Gmail contacts. This trains Gmail's filter to trust the forwarding source over time.

### I stopped receiving forwarded emails

Check the Alt-ID dashboard to confirm the alias is still active and the forwarding address is correct. Also verify that your Surfshark subscription is current — a lapsed subscription pauses alias forwarding without deleting your aliases.

### A site rejected my alias address

Some sites run checks against known disposable email domains. If this happens, try a different alias or, as a last resort, use your real Gmail address for that specific site. The goal is to minimize exposure, not achieve perfection.

### Can I reply from my alias?

Not directly through Gmail. Alt-ID is a forwarding service, not a full two-way inbox. To reply without exposing your real address, you would need to configure Gmail's "Send mail as" feature (Settings > Accounts and Import > Send mail as) using an SMTP relay — this is an advanced setup most users don't need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Gmail natively support email aliases?

Gmail has a `+tag` workaround (e.g., `you+alias@gmail.com`) that routes tagged mail to your inbox and allows filtering rules. However, it is not a true alias — your real Gmail address is exposed once the tag is stripped, which takes about two seconds.
