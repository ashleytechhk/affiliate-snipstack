## Why Telemarketers Target Dating App Users

Dating apps are a rich source of phone numbers for data brokers. Here's how your number travels:

- **App permissions:** Many dating apps request contact access. Some share anonymized data with advertising partners — and "anonymized" rarely stays that way for long.
- **Third-party integrations:** Apps use analytics SDKs, ad networks, and login services. Each is a potential leak point.
- **Data breaches:** Dating platforms are high-value targets. When a breach happens, phone numbers go straight to the gray market.
- **Mutual contacts:** When a match saves your number, their phone's contact sync can expose it to services that harvest contact metadata.

None of this requires the app to do anything malicious. It's the predictable result of sharing personal data across multiple services. The only reliable fix is not sharing your real number.

## How to Block Telemarketers Permanently: Step-by-Step

### Step 1. Register with the National Do Not Call Registry

Go to [donotcall.gov](https://donotcall.gov) and register your real number. This is free and takes two minutes.

**What it does:** Legitimate US telemarketers are legally required to check this list and skip registered numbers. It reduces calls from compliant businesses.

**What it doesn't do:** Stop illegal robocall operations, international callers, political organizations, charities, or anyone who simply ignores the law. This step is worthwhile but insufficient on its own.

**Do this now, then continue to the steps below for actual permanent protection.**

---

### Step 2. Enable Your Carrier's Built-In Call Blocking

Every major US carrier offers a free spam-blocking service:

| Carrier | Service | How to enable |
|---------|---------|---------------|
| AT&T | ActiveArmor | AT&T app → More → ActiveArmor |
| Verizon | Call Filter | Download the Call Filter app |
| T-Mobile | Scam Shield | T-Mobile app → Scam Shield |

Turn this on. It uses network-level pattern detection to flag and block known spam numbers before your phone rings.

**Limitation:** Robocallers rotate through thousands of numbers. By the time a number is flagged, it's been retired. You'll still get calls — just fewer.

---

### Step 3. Turn On "Silence Unknown Callers" on Your Phone

**On iPhone:**
Settings → Phone → Silence Unknown Callers → toggle ON

**On Android (varies by manufacturer):**
Phone app → Settings → Blocked Numbers → Block Unknown/Private Numbers → toggle ON

This sends any call from a number not in your contacts straight to voicemail. Telemarketers rarely leave messages. Legitimate people who aren't in your contacts can leave a voicemail and you call them back.

**Note:** This affects all unknown numbers, including legitimate ones. If your job requires taking calls from new contacts, use this selectively.

## Troubleshooting

**"I'm still getting calls even after registering with Do Not Call."**
The registry only covers compliant US-based businesses. Illegal robocall operations and offshore callers ignore it. Steps 3–7 above address these.

**"The Silence Unknown Callers setting is blocking calls I actually want."**
This is a common trade-off. If you're expecting calls from doctors, delivery services, or recruiters — people who might not be in your contacts — keep this off and rely on your carrier's spam detection instead. Use the Alt-Number strategy to contain the damage from dating app exposure.

Some services use SMS verification and block VoIP number ranges. Alt-Number uses real carrier numbers in most cases, so this is rare — but if it happens, contact Surfshark support to request a different number from their pool.

You can keep the same Alt-Number and use your carrier's call filter to block individual spammers who have it. Only delete the number if the spam volume makes it unusable — most users find that spam targeting their Alt-Number is manageable compared to real-number exposure.

## Why Blocklists Alone Don't Work

![Illustration showing a revolving door of phone numbers being blocked and replaced — old spam numbers crossed out, new ones appearing — versus a second phone number sitting protected](/og/how-to-block-telemarketers-permanently-2026-inline-2.png)

Phone number blocking is a cat-and-mouse game. When a number gets flagged:

1. The robocall operation retires it
2. They activate a new number — one that's completely clean on every blocklist
3. The calls resume

This is why the Do Not Call registry and carrier blocking reduce calls but don't eliminate them. The blocklist is always 24–72 hours behind. Robocallers know this and exploit it.

The only approach that breaks this cycle is controlling what number you expose. If your real number isn't circulating, it can't end up in a dialer. That's what Alt-Number does — it creates a disposable layer between you and everyone who doesn't strictly need your real contact.
