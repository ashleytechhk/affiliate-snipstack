## The Real Problem With Legacy AV on Apple Silicon

Norton, McAfee, and similar suites were not originally designed with Apple Silicon in mind. Their architecture relies heavily on kernel extensions and background scanning approaches that Apple deprecated with the move to M-series chips. Even with Rosetta 2 translation layers and subsequent patches, these suites tend to:

- Spike CPU usage during background scans, eating into the M3's performance cores
- Drain battery faster because they are not optimized for the M3's power efficiency cluster
- Pop up constant renewal, upsell, and "your device is at risk" alerts — even when nothing is wrong
- Require separate purchases for VPN coverage, which is now a baseline privacy expectation

The result: you are paying for software that makes your expensive machine feel slower, without covering your full threat surface.

### Surfshark Alert — Data Breach Monitoring

Alert monitors your email addresses against known data breach databases and notifies you when your credentials appear in a leak. This is a reactive but genuinely useful layer — you get an alert rather than finding out when someone tries to log in with your password. Norton and McAfee offer similar features, so this is more of a parity point than a differentiator.

### Alternative ID — Anonymous Browsing

This is the feature most AV suites do not include. Alternative ID generates a disposable identity (name, email address) you can use for signups, trials, and services you do not fully trust. Emails to your Alternative ID forward to your real inbox so you stay reachable without exposing your actual address. It is not a full anonymization tool, but it is a practical daily-use privacy layer that competitors have not matched.

### CleanWeb — Ad and Tracker Blocking

CleanWeb operates at the DNS level, blocking ads, trackers, and known malicious domains before the connection is made. On a MacBook Pro used for work or content creation, this has a secondary benefit: fewer background network calls means slightly better battery life and less tracking of your browsing behavior across sites.

## Performance on M3 MacBook Pro: What to Expect

The M3 chip's Performance and Efficiency core architecture is sensitive to poorly-optimized background processes. Apps that constantly poll hardware sensors, run heavy background threads, or use deprecated APIs will trigger the system to keep performance cores active, burning through battery.

[Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-digital-nomad-travel-setup-2026/)'s Mac client is built against current macOS APIs. In real-world use on an M3 MacBook Pro, background CPU usage during idle is negligible — the antivirus scanner runs on a schedule rather than constantly, and the VPN connection (when active) adds minimal latency overhead thanks to the WireGuard protocol, which is purpose-built for efficiency.

Norton's background usage, by comparison, tends to remain elevated throughout the day due to real-time scanning and the various background services it spawns. McAfee has a similar profile. Neither has fully shed the architectural assumptions baked in during the Intel and PowerPC eras.

If you work on battery more than half the time, this difference is measurable in your day.
