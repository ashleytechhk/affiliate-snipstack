## The Rental Car Inbox Problem

Rental car companies operate in a competitive, thin-margin business. One of the ways they monetize their customer base is through data partnerships and marketing agreements. When you book with a major rental brand, your name, email address, and travel dates may be passed to airline loyalty programs, hotel chains, travel insurance upsellers, and third-party promotional partners.

The result: a single booking confirmation triggers a chain of marketing messages you never consented to. Travel-related purchases are among the highest triggers for subsequent marketing email volume, and rental car bookings sit near the top of that list.

The practical defense is to never give rental car sites — or any booking platform — your primary email address. That is exactly what an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-privacy-without-creating-new-account-2026/) makes possible.

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple's built-in feature is excellent if your devices are all Apple hardware. It integrates directly into Safari autofill and iCloud settings, making alias creation nearly automatic at checkout. Aliases are unlimited and you can disable each one individually from your iCloud account.

The hard limitation: it works only within the Apple ecosystem. If you book on a Windows laptop or Android phone, Hide My Email is not accessible without workarounds. For cross-platform travelers — or households where not everyone uses Apple devices — this is a dealbreaker.

### SimpleLogin (Free Tier)

SimpleLogin is a privacy-focused open-source alias service with a solid reputation. The free tier gives you five aliases, which covers occasional travelers who stick to the same few rental brands each year. The paid plan at $30/year removes the alias limit and adds custom domain support.

SimpleLogin supports replying from the alias address, which some users appreciate. The interface is web-based with browser extensions available. It is a strong alternative to Alt-ID for users who prefer open-source tools and do not need VPN functionality in the same subscription.

### Firefox Relay

Firefox Relay offers five free aliases and an upgraded plan at $3.99/month for unlimited aliases plus phone number masking. It is tightly integrated with Firefox browser, making alias creation convenient during checkout if Firefox is your browser of choice.

Like Alt-ID, Relay aliases cannot be used to send replies. The free tier is limited enough that travelers who book more than five times per year will exhaust it quickly without upgrading to the paid plan.

**Can rental car companies detect that I am using an alias address?**

Most cannot. The alias appears as a standard email address with no technical flag identifying it as a forwarding address. The rental company sees what looks like any other customer email.

You reach the rental company through their customer service page or phone number — not by replying to the confirmation email. This is standard practice regardless of whether you used an alias, since confirmation emails from most large rental companies are sent from no-reply addresses anyway.

**Should I create one alias per rental brand or one alias per booking?**

One alias per booking gives the most precise control. If a specific booking's alias starts receiving spam, you can disable just that alias without affecting your other bookings. One alias per brand is simpler to manage but means disabling it cuts off all contact from that brand, including future confirmation emails.

**Does this approach work for airline and hotel bookings too?**

Yes. **What if the rental company requires email verification at pickup?**

Some rental counters ask you to confirm the email on file. Your alias address is the email on file — that is what you entered at booking. Showing the alias address is fine. If they need you to receive a verification code in real time, the alias forwards it to your real inbox immediately.
