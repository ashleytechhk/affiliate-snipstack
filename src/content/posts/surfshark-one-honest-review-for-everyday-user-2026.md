## VPN: The Part Norton and McAfee Treat as an Afterthought

Surfshark's VPN is the same product sold standalone, not a stripped-down bundle version. It runs on WireGuard, covers unlimited simultaneous devices, and includes a kill switch, split tunneling, and multi-hop routing for people who want an extra layer between them and their ISP.

Norton's Secure VPN is capped at 50GB/month on its lower Deluxe tier — fine for casual browsing, not enough for streaming or daily video calls. McAfee's VPN is uncapped on most plans but has drawn past criticism over its logging practices.

For an everyday user who just wants "on" to mean "on" without checking a data meter, Surfshark's unlimited approach removes a whole category of friction.

## CleanWeb, Alert, and Search: The Quiet Advantages

These three features are where [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-senior-tech-novice-easy-to-use-2026/) pulls ahead of the "just antivirus plus VPN" model Norton and McAfee still lean on.

### CleanWeb (Ad and Tracker Blocking)

CleanWeb blocks ads, trackers, and known [malicious URL](https://snipstack.io/malicious-url-scanner-free-2026/)s at the network level, before they load — faster browsing, and fewer malvertising infections (malware served through legitimate-looking ad networks). Norton and McAfee offer partial browser-extension coverage, but neither has a comparable system-wide blocker in the base plan.

### Alert (Breach Monitoring)

Alert watches your email addresses, credit card numbers, and ID details against known breach databases and notifies you in real time when something surfaces — most people otherwise find out about a breach from a news headline weeks later, not from their own security software.

### Search (Private Search)

Search is a no-log, no-ad search tool built into the bundle. It won't replace your daily search engine for everyone, but for anyone who wants fewer queries feeding into ad-profiling systems, it's a bonus neither Norton nor McAfee offers at all.

## The Honest Limitations

Every switch involves a trade-off, and this review wouldn't be honest without naming them clearly.

**No password manager.** Norton 360 and McAfee+ both bundle one by default. [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-black-friday-deal-prediction-2026/) does not. If you currently rely on Norton's or McAfee's built-in password manager, switching means picking up a separate app — plenty of solid free and low-cost options exist, but it's an extra step, not a wash.

**No parental controls.** If you're a parent using Norton's or McAfee's screen-time and content-filtering tools to manage a kid's device, [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-for-parents-monitoring-teen-phone-2026/) won't replace that function. You'd need a dedicated parental control app alongside it.

**A shorter antivirus track record.** As covered above, Surfshark's malware detection is solid in current testing, but it doesn't carry the same multi-decade dataset that Norton's engine does.

None of these are dealbreakers for most everyday users, but they're real gaps, and pretending otherwise wouldn't be an honest review.
