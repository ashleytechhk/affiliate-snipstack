A few scenarios that happen constantly:

**Local pickup listings.** The moment you arrange an in-person handoff, most buyers want to coordinate by text or call. "I'm five minutes away" is a lot smoother than navigating eBay's message thread on a phone screen.

**High-volume sellers.** If you flip items regularly, your number gets stored in dozens of contact lists. Former buyers follow up months later about similar items. Some do not respect that the transaction is over.

**Disputes and pressure.** Difficult buyers occasionally escalate outside eBay's platform — calling directly to pressure you on returns or feedback. If they have your personal number, you can't end that call cleanly.

**Resellers with a business presence.** If you list on eBay alongside Craigslist, [Facebook Marketplace](https://snipstack.io/facebook-marketplace-scam-how-to-avoid-2026/), or Depop, and use the same number across platforms, that number becomes searchable. A motivated buyer can find your other listings, your approximate location, and your selling patterns.

None of this requires a malicious actor. The exposure comes from normal selling behavior. A [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-reddit-account-verification-2026/) keeps your personal line out of it entirely.

## Google Voice — Best Free Option for US Sellers

**Google Voice** remains the most functional free option if you're in the US and selling casually. Sign in with a Google account, pick a US number from available area codes, and you have a working line for calls and texts at no cost.

For occasional eBay sellers — a few listings a month, no local pickup — Google Voice covers the basics. You can forward calls to your real number or answer them in the app. Voicemail is included. Number blocking works.

The limits are real for higher-volume sellers. Google Voice numbers are widely known as VoIP, and some verification systems flag or reject them. If you're using the number for SMS verification on eBay's own two-factor checks, you may run into issues. The number is also tied to your Google account, which is its own privacy consideration. And Google Voice is US only — sellers outside the US have no access.

**Best for:** US-based casual sellers who want to keep costs at zero and aren't doing local pickup.

**Is it against eBay's rules to use a [second phone number](https://snipstack.io/second-phone-number-for-verification-2026/)?**

No. eBay does not require you to use your primary personal number. Using a number you control for buyer communication is standard practice among power sellers. The requirement is that you provide a working contact number — not that it be your main personal line.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my number when I'm done selling?**

With Alt-Number or Hushed, you can disable the number immediately when you're done. Any future calls or texts to that number will not reach you. With Google Voice, you can delete the number from your account. With Burner, you hit "Burn" and the number is released. None of these options leave a trace back to your real number.

**Can buyers find my personal details if they have my [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-instacart-shopper-account-2026/)?**

A real mobile number from Alt-Number or Hushed does not have public registry information attached to it. VoIP numbers may show the service provider (e.g., "Google Voice — Mountain View, CA") on reverse lookup sites, which can look suspicious to buyers checking your legitimacy.

**Should I use a different number for each platform (eBay, Craigslist, Facebook Marketplace)?**

It depends on your exposure tolerance. If you sell across multiple platforms, using the same [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-whatsapp-business-account-2026/) across all of them means a determined buyer can correlate your listings. If that concerns you, Alt-Number makes it easy to run more than one number simultaneously — different numbers for different platforms, managed from one app.

**What if a buyer tries to scam me and I've given them my [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-calling-overseas-cheaply-2026/)?**

A [second number](https://snipstack.io/second-number-for-test-driving-car-privacy-2026/) helps with harassment and unwanted contact, not with scam prevention — eBay's buyer and seller protection handles that.
