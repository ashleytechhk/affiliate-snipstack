## Why Your Real Number Is More Dangerous Than You Think

Most people treat their phone number like a username. It is not. It is closer to a home address.

Your phone number is linked to:

- Your bank accounts (SMS two-factor authentication)
- Your social media profiles (account recovery)
- Your government ID in many countries (SIM registration laws)
- Your physical location via cell tower data
- Your full name via reverse lookup services

Public data broker databases — services like Whitepages, Spokeo, and dozens of smaller operators — can take a phone number and return a full profile: name, address, email, relatives, estimated income, social media handles. Most of these lookups cost less than two dollars.

Anyone who gets your real number, whether that is an Uber driver or a date who turned sour, can find out more about you than you would ever voluntarily share.
