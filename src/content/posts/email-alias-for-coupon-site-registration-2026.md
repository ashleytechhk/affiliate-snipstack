## What About Burner Email Services Like Guerrilla Mail?

Temporary "burner" addresses like those from Guerrilla Mail or Temp-Mail work for one-time confirmation emails but are a poor fit for coupon sites. Here is why:

- **No persistent inbox.** Most burner services delete the inbox after 10-60 minutes. Coupon sites sometimes send codes hours later, or you need to retrieve your account later for cashback claims.
- **Blocked by many retailers.** Popular burner domains are blocklisted by major platforms. You may find the registration form rejects the address entirely.
- **No reply capability.** If the site needs you to reply to verify your account, a burner address will not work.

Alias services like Alt-ID give you a persistent address you control, not a ticking clock.

## FAQ

**Does using an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-hotel-chain-points-account-2026/) on coupon sites violate their terms of service?**

Generally no. Coupon sites require a working email address for verification, and alias addresses receive email and can reply — they fulfil that requirement. Terms vary by platform, but [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-insurance-comparison-sites-2026/)ing is a standard privacy practice, not a form of fraud or circumvention.

**Can I still receive cashback payments if I use an alias?**

Yes. Cashback platforms like Rakuten pay out to your account, which is linked to your alias email. As long as the alias stays active and forwarding, you receive payment notifications and can log in normally. If you disable the alias, remember to re-enable it before any payout period.

**Will the coupon site notice I am using an alias?**

They see a valid email address that accepts mail. Most alias domains are not blocklisted the way burner domains are. Alt-ID uses dedicated domains that behave like regular email, so standard validation checks pass without issue.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my coupon account if I delete the alias?**

The account still exists on the coupon site's end, but you lose the ability to receive email to it. If you need to log in for password resets or cashback claims, you would need to update the account email first — or keep the alias active but pause forwarding to reduce spam without losing account access. [Alt-ID lets you toggle forwarding off without deleting the alias](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-coupon-site-registration-2026__faq), which is the cleanest option.

**Is this different from having a separate Gmail account for shopping?**

Functionally similar, but an alias is far lower friction. A second Gmail account requires logging in and out, checking a second inbox, and managing two sets of credentials. An alias forwards everything to your existing inbox. One inbox, one login, full control over which senders can reach you.

**How many aliases do I realistically need for coupon sites?**

Most active coupon hunters use 5-15 different platforms regularly. Add store-specific loyalty programmes and seasonal sign-ups, and 20-30 active aliases is not unusual. That is why a service with no alias cap matters — you want room to grow without auditing and deleting old ones just to create new ones.
