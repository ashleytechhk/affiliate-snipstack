## What Free VPNs Actually Cost You

### Data Caps That Kill Your Session

The most popular free VPNs cap your bandwidth hard. [ProtonVPN](https://snipstack.io/vs-protonvpn-2026/)'s free tier has no data cap but restricts you to three countries and one device — which is why you can't access most streaming libraries with it. Most other free services cap you at 500 MB to 2 GB per month. One HD movie is roughly 4–7 GB. You'll burn through your monthly free allowance before you finish the first season of anything.

The math is ruthless: at 500 MB/month, you're not streaming. You're previewing.

### Speed Throttling That Ruins Streaming Quality

Free VPN servers are overcrowded. When thousands of users share the same three servers, speeds crater. You're looking at 2–5 Mbps on a good day, when Netflix recommends at least 15 Mbps for HD and 25 Mbps for 4K. The result: constant buffering, automatic quality downgrade, or the dreaded "are you still watching?" prompt after your stream stalls for the fourth time.

Paid VPNs like Surfshark maintain dedicated infrastructure. In independent speed tests, Surfshark consistently delivers 400+ Mbps on nearby servers — fast enough for 4K streaming with room to spare.

### Streaming Platforms Have Blocked Free VPNs

Netflix, Disney+, Hulu, and BBC iPlayer actively detect and block VPN traffic. They maintain blocklists updated in near real-time. Free VPN IP ranges are among the first to get flagged because they rotate infrequently and are used by enormous volumes of traffic. If you've ever seen the "you seem to be using an unblocker or proxy" error on Netflix, you know exactly what this looks like.

Surfshark invests heavily in staying ahead of these blocks — rotating IP pools, obfuscated servers, and NoBorders mode for restrictive networks. It's why the service actually works for region-switching when free VPNs can't get past the login screen.

### The Privacy Problem Nobody Talks About

A VPN is supposed to protect your data. Here's the dark irony: many free VPNs are the privacy threat. Running servers costs money. If you're not paying, the product is your data.

Hola VPN sold user bandwidth to third parties. SuperVPN was found to contain malware. Betternet logged user data and sold it to advertisers. These aren't edge cases — they're how the free VPN business model has worked for years. An independent audit in 2021 found that 72% of free VPN apps on Google Play had at least one privacy or security issue. A zero-cost VPN often costs more than you think — just in a currency you can't see.
