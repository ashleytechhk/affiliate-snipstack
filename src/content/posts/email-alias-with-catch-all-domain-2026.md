## What "Catch-All Domain" Actually Means

Before the breakdown, a definitions check — because these terms get used loosely.

**Catch-all domain** means: any email sent to `@yourdomain.com`, regardless of the prefix, lands in your inbox. You do not pre-create aliases. You sign up for a new site using `thatsite@yourdomain.com` on the spot, no dashboard required. Spam starts arriving on that address? You kill just that alias (or route it to trash). Your domain is the alias namespace.

**Managed-domain alias** means: you create aliases at a shared domain the provider controls — something like `randomword.randomword1234@altid.surfshark.net` or `yourusername.abc123@simplelogin.com`. You still get full control: create, delete, pause. But the domain is theirs, not yours.

Both approaches stop spam. The difference is setup overhead and whether the receiving address is branded to your domain or to the provider's.

## FAQ

**Does Alt-ID support catch-all domain or custom domain?**

No. Alt-ID generates randomised aliases at a Surfshark-managed domain. It does not support bringing your own domain or enabling catch-all across a custom domain. What it does offer is unlimited aliases, instant delete, and reply masking — all without DNS setup. For custom-domain catch-all, addy.io or SimpleLogin Premium are the right tools. For spam-free simplicity, [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-with-catch-all-domain-2026__faq) is faster to get running.

**Can I use a catch-all domain alias service for free?**

Yes. addy.io's free plan includes catch-all on a shared addy.io subdomain (e.g., `anything@username.addy.io`). SimpleLogin and Alt-ID's free tiers do not include catch-all. For custom-domain catch-all, addy.io Lite starts at $12/year.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to emails sent to a deleted alias?**

Once you delete an alias, the address stops accepting mail. Senders receive a bounce or a non-delivery notification depending on the provider's configuration. Any mail already in transit at the moment of deletion may still deliver briefly — typically within minutes.

**Will email services accept aliases instead of real addresses?**

Most services accept alias addresses without issue. A minority of services attempt to block known alias domains. If a service rejects your alias, you can try a different alias variant, contact the provider, or use your real address only for that service while keeping everything else protected.

**Is there a risk that the alias provider shuts down?**

Any provider could close. For Alt-ID, the alias infrastructure is tied to Surfshark's broader service. For addy.io, it is an independent privacy-focused project. For SimpleLogin, the Proton acquisition provides institutional backing. In all cases, keeping a note of which service uses which alias lets you update accounts quickly if you ever need to migrate.

**[Does using](https://snipstack.io/does-using-email-alias-protect-from-spam-2026/) a catch-all domain make me more vulnerable to spam?**

Potentially, yes. Because a catch-all domain accepts mail sent to any address, including guesses like `info@yourdomain.com` or `admin@yourdomain.com`, you may receive spam on addresses you never shared. Most catch-all users mitigate this with filters or by using addy.io's regex-routing options to accept only recognised patterns.
