## What "Identity Theft Protection" Actually Means

The term gets thrown around on product pages without much explanation. In practice, ID theft protection breaks into four distinct capabilities. A product can offer one, some, or all of them — and the difference matters.

### Dark Web Monitoring

Your email address, [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-for-facebook-verification-2026/), or password may already be sitting in a hacker forum or paste site right now. Dark web monitoring services scan these sources continuously and alert you when your data shows up. The scan covers breach databases, criminal marketplaces, and paste sites — places that aren't indexed by Google and that you'd never find manually.

This is the most commonly included feature at the budget end of the market. It's also the most immediately actionable: you get an alert, you change your password, you move on.

### Breach Alerts

Closely related to dark web monitoring but distinct. Breach alerts notify you when a specific company you have an account with suffers a data breach — even before your personal data surfaces publicly. Services like Have I Been Pwned popularized this concept; premium tools go further by cross-referencing your stored accounts against new breach disclosures in near-real-time.

### Credit Monitoring

This is where most budget tools stop short. Credit monitoring watches your credit file at one or more of the major bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) and alerts you when new accounts are opened, hard inquiries are made, or your score changes significantly. It's the layer that catches identity theft after someone has already used your data — opening a credit card or taking out a loan in your name.

Full credit monitoring with all three bureaus is usually only included in premium or standalone ID protection plans. Many VPN bundles offer single-bureau monitoring, which catches most fraud but not everything.

### ID Recovery Assistance

If theft has already happened, recovery assistance gives you access to specialists who can help you file disputes, notify creditors, and navigate the restoration process. Some plans include insurance coverage for losses and recovery costs. This is the layer most rarely included in VPN-adjacent bundles, and it's worth checking the fine print if this matters to you.

## Step-by-Step: Setting Up Dark Web Monitoring in Surfshark Alert

### Step 1 — Log into Your Surfshark Account

Open your browser and go to your Surfshark account dashboard. Log in with your credentials. Once inside, look for the **Alert** section in the left-hand navigation. This is where dark web monitoring lives — separate from the VPN client itself.

### Step 2 — Add Your Email Addresses

Click **Add email** in the Alert dashboard. Enter your primary email address first. Surfshark will send a verification email — click the link inside to confirm monitoring has started for that address.

Repeat this for every email address you actively use. Most people have two to four: personal, work, an old address they still get mail on, and possibly one tied to a bank or financial account. Add all of them. The scan runs continuously across all monitored addresses.

### Step 3 — Add Phone Numbers and Other Personal Data

In the same Alert section, you can also add phone numbers and, in some regions, additional personal identifiers. These get cross-referenced against breach databases the same way email addresses do. If your phone number shows up in a leaked marketing database or a phishing list, you'll get an alert.

### Step 4 — Review Your Initial Scan Results

After verification, the initial scan runs automatically and usually completes within a few minutes. Your dashboard will show a list of breaches where your data was found — the breach name, date, and what type of data was exposed (email, password hash, IP address, etc.).

Work through the list from newest to oldest. For any breach involving a password, go change that password immediately if you haven't already. For breaches involving only your email address, the risk is lower — mostly spam and phishing exposure.

### Step 5 — Enable Alerts for New Breaches

Make sure notifications are turned on so you're alerted in real time when new breaches surface. In the Alert dashboard, check that email notifications are enabled for your account. You can also enable push notifications through the Surfshark mobile app.

Going forward, you don't need to do anything actively — the monitoring runs in the background and pushes alerts when something new appears.

### Step 6 — Turn On the VPN

Dark web monitoring tells you when your data has leaked. The VPN prevents further exposure on unsecured networks. Open the Surfshark app on your device, connect to a server, and verify your connection is active. The combination of alert monitoring plus encrypted traffic is the baseline setup.

For most users, connecting to the nearest server is fine. If you're on a public network — a café, hotel, or airport — connect before opening any apps or browsers.

### Step 7 — Optional: Set Up Alternative ID

Inside your Surfshark One account, you'll also find Alternative ID — a tool that generates a masked email address and a fictional identity profile you can use when signing up for services you don't fully trust. Links still forward to your real inbox, but your actual email address isn't exposed.

This won't prevent a breach at a site you've already signed up on, but going forward it limits the surface area — fewer places have your real email, so fewer breaches can expose it.

## Troubleshooting

**I'm not receiving the verification email.**
Check your spam folder first. If it's not there, wait two minutes and try resending. Some corporate email servers strip automated messages — try adding the address from a personal inbox instead.

**My scan shows dozens of old breaches. Do I need to panic?**
Old breaches (3+ years ago) where you've already changed your password are low risk. Focus on anything in the last 12 months or anything involving passwords you might still be using. Use a password manager to identify reused passwords and rotate them.

**The VPN is slowing down my connection.**
Try switching to a closer server. If you're using the OpenVPN protocol, switch to WireGuard in the app settings — it's faster and more efficient on most devices.

**Alert isn't available in my region for certain data types.**
Surfshark Alert's availability for phone number monitoring and additional identifiers varies by country. Email monitoring is available globally. Check the in-app options for what's supported in your region.

**I want credit monitoring too. What should I add?**
Surfshark Alert does not include credit monitoring. For credit file watching, add Credit Karma (free, Equifax and TransUnion) or Experian's free monitoring tier. These pair cleanly alongside Surfshark Alert without overlap.

## FAQ

### Does a VPN prevent identity theft?

A VPN encrypts your internet traffic and masks your IP address, which reduces your exposure on unsecured networks and makes it harder for third parties to intercept data in transit.
