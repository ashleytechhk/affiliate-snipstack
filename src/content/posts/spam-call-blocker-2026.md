### 2. Google Call Screen (Pixel / Android) — Best Free Built-In Option

**Google's Call Screen feature uses Google Assistant to intercept unknown calls before your phone even rings.** The caller hears "The person you're calling is using a screening service" and is asked to state their name and reason for calling. Their response appears as live transcript on your screen. You decide whether to answer, decline silently, or mark as spam.

For Android users — especially Pixel owners — this is completely free and requires no app install. It handles robocalls and telemarketing reasonably well. ### 4. Apple Silence Unknown Callers — Best Passive iOS Option

**iOS's Silence Unknown Callers setting sends any number not in your contacts, recent calls, or Siri Suggestions straight to voicemail without ringing.** Setup takes about five seconds: Settings > Phone > Silence Unknown Callers.

This is blunt but effective. Spam callers never get a ring. Legitimate unknown callers (delivery drivers, doctors' offices) leave voicemail instead. If you're giving out a dating-specific number rather than your real one, this pairs perfectly — your main line stays silent while your Alt-Number handles dating traffic.

The downside is that you miss plenty of legitimate calls. It's best suited to people whose real number is well-protected and who don't expect unexpected calls from unknown numbers.
