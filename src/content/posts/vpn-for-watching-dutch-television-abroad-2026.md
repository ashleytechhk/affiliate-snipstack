## What Exactly Is a VPN and What Does "Geo-Restriction" Mean?

A **Virtual Private Network (VPN)** is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server in another country. When you connect through a Dutch VPN server, websites and streaming platforms see that server's Dutch IP address — not your real location in Osaka or São Paulo or Istanbul.

**Geo-restriction** is how streaming services enforce their broadcast licences. NPO has a licence to broadcast certain content only within the Netherlands. The moment your IP address falls outside a Dutch range, the platform blocks playback. This is the same reason BBC iPlayer blocks viewers outside the UK, or why certain US Netflix titles are not available in Europe.

A VPN solves this by giving you a Dutch IP address on demand. To NPO Start, you look like a viewer in Rotterdam. Your ISP and the local network you are on see only encrypted data going to a VPN server — they cannot see which streaming service you are using or what you are watching.
