A VPN — Virtual Private Network — routes your internet connection through a server physically located in Australia. Stan sees an Australian IP address and grants access as normal. From the platform's perspective, you appear to be browsing from Sydney or Melbourne, regardless of where you actually are.

## How a VPN Works for Stan: The Technical Reality

A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server you choose. When you connect to an Australian VPN server, all your outbound traffic appears to originate from that server's Australian IP address — not your actual location.

![How a VPN routes your connection for Stan streaming outside Australia](/og/vpn-for-stan-streaming-australia-overseas-2026-inline-1.png)

For Stan specifically, the process works in three steps:

1. **You connect to an Australian VPN server** — the VPN app handles this automatically once you select Australia as your location.
2. **Your traffic routes through that server** — Stan sees the server's Australian IP address, not yours.
3. **Stan grants access** — the platform's geo-detection reads a valid Australian address and allows playback.

The practical complication: Stan, like most major streaming platforms, actively maintains blocklists of known VPN IP addresses. When a VPN server's IP gets flagged, connections through that server are denied. Quality VPNs solve this by rotating their IP pools — when one IP gets blocked, traffic shifts to another. [Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) do not have this infrastructure, which is why they fail consistently on services like Stan.
