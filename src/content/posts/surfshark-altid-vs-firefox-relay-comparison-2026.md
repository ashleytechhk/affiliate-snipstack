## Firefox Relay: The Free Option With a Ceiling

Firefox Relay launched as Mozilla's answer to the spam problem and it does the basics well. The free plan gives you five [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-webinar-registration-privacy-2026/)es that forward to your real inbox. Setup is fast if you already have a Mozilla account, and the Firefox browser extension makes generating aliases on sign-up forms nearly automatic.

### What Firefox Relay Does Well

**Free for light use.** Five aliases covers casual use — a shopping account here, a trial sign-up there. If you only need occasional spam protection, the free plan works fine.

**Mozilla trust.** Mozilla is a non-profit with a genuine privacy track record. Relay isn't a data grab.

**Tight Firefox integration.** The browser extension is smooth. When you hit a sign-up form, Relay offers to generate an alias in one click without leaving the page.

**Paid plan is affordable.** At $1.99/month, the premium tier removes the five-alias cap and adds reply-from-alias functionality.

### Firefox Relay Limitations

**Five aliases on the free plan is a hard cap.** If you sign up for more than five services, you're forced to reuse aliases — which defeats the purpose.

**No identity masking.** Relay only masks your email. Your name, profile, and other submitted data go to sites as-is. You're half-protected.

**Firefox-first design.** The extension is built for Firefox. It works in other browsers but the experience is noticeably less integrated.

**No phone masking.** Spam calls and texts require a separate solution entirely.

## Privacy and Data Handling

Both tools forward your emails rather than storing them long-term, but the details differ.

Surfshark operates under a strict no-logs policy across its product suite. Alt ID aliases and forwarded messages pass through Surfshark's infrastructure and the company has published independent audits covering its VPN service. Surfshark is incorporated in the Netherlands, subject to EU data protection regulations.

Mozilla's Relay privacy policy states that relay email content is not stored after forwarding. Mozilla is a US-based non-profit. Its privacy reputation is strong, but US jurisdiction means different regulatory context than the EU.

Neither tool claims to make you untraceable or completely anonymous — and any tool that makes that claim should be treated with suspicion. What both do is reduce the surface area of your real email address exposure significantly.

**Does Firefox Relay work in Chrome or Safari?**
Firefox Relay has extensions for multiple browsers, but its most polished integration is in Firefox. In Chrome and Safari, the extension still functions but lacks the seamless form-detection features available in Firefox. Surfshark Alt ID works equally across all major browsers.

**Will spam senders know I'm using an alias?**
Generally, no. Aliases from both services look like standard email addresses. However, sophisticated marketing platforms sometimes detect relay domains through pattern matching. If an alias gets flagged, you simply create a new one — neither service charges per alias on their respective unlimited tiers.

**Can I reply to forwarded emails without revealing my real address?**
Surfshark Alt ID supports reply-from-alias at all subscription levels. Firefox Relay requires the paid Premium plan ($1.99/month) to reply from aliases. On the free Relay tier, replying to forwarded messages would expose your real address.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my real inbox if I delete an alias?**
Deleting an alias permanently stops forwarding from that address. Any emails sent to the deleted alias simply do not arrive. Your real inbox is unaffected and completely separate. This is the primary appeal of the alias model: surgical removal of individual spam sources without touching your main email account.
