Here is a realistic deployment for a freelancer or remote worker running Surfshark One as their primary security layer:

**Device coverage:** Install Surfshark One on your primary laptop, your smartphone, and any travel tablet. Unlimited connections means no rationing.

**Network rule:** Enable VPN on connect for all networks you do not own. Most nomads set this as a toggle in Surfshark's Settings so it fires automatically when joining new Wi-Fi.

It is not as polished as Google for complex queries, but for everyday lookups while traveling it removes one tracking vector.

**Will the VPN slow down my internet connection?**

All VPNs add some latency. On nearby servers, the speed reduction is typically under 10–15% on a fast connection — imperceptible for video calls and browsing. On distant servers (e.g., routing through a server on another continent), latency increases more noticeably. Surfshark's WireGuard protocol minimizes this overhead compared to older VPN protocols. For bandwidth-heavy work like large file uploads, connect to the geographically closest server rather than a region-specific one.
