## What Your Phone Number Actually Reveals

Most people think of a phone number as a way to call or text. Data brokers think of it differently. They treat your mobile number as a universal ID — a key that unlocks a profile containing your full name, home address, historical addresses, relatives, employer, and sometimes financial records.

Here is what someone can do with your number in 2026:

- Run a reverse lookup on any of dozens of free and paid sites to get your name and address in seconds
- Cross-reference your number against social media platforms that allow search by phone number (several still do, despite stated policy changes)
- Use data broker aggregators like Spokeo, BeenVerified, or Whitepages to pull a full background dossier
- SIM-swap your number with a carrier by social-engineering a customer service agent — a known tactic used to bypass two-factor authentication and steal accounts

The moment you hand your number to a match on a dating app, all of this becomes possible. The person on the other end may be entirely genuine. Or they may not be. You have no way to know before you share.
