## What Is a VPN, and Why Does It Matter on a Hotspot?

A VPN, or Virtual Private Network, creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. All your internet traffic travels through that tunnel before reaching its destination.

Two things happen as a result:

1. **Your traffic is encrypted.** Anyone monitoring the network — a carrier, a hotspot reseller, or someone on the same shared connection — sees only scrambled data instead of your actual activity.
2. **Your apparent location changes.** Websites and services see the VPN server's IP address and geographic location, not your physical location. This is the mechanic that enables region-switching for streaming subscriptions.

For a digital nomad, both matter. The security function protects your work and financial data from carrier-level surveillance and hotspot snooping. The location function is what lets you access your home streaming library — or deliberately switch to a lower-cost region — regardless of where you are physically sitting.

## Why Hotspot Use Creates Specific Risks

### Your Carrier Can See Your Traffic

When you connect through a mobile hotspot, your ISP or mobile carrier is the network operator. Unlike a home broadband connection where you have more control over the equipment, a mobile carrier has full visibility into the traffic flowing through your data plan — unless that traffic is encrypted.

Carriers in many countries are legally permitted to log and sell anonymized browsing data to advertisers. In some regions, government agencies can request access to user traffic logs without a warrant. A VPN encrypts your traffic before it reaches the carrier, making what you do online opaque to anyone monitoring at the network level.

![How a VPN encrypts your traffic over a mobile hotspot connection](/og/vpn-for-digital-nomads-hotspot-2026-inline-1.png)

### Shared Hotspots Are a Security Risk

Portable hotspot devices, travel SIM routers, and co-working space hotspots are frequently shared among multiple users. On these shared networks, the same risks that apply to coffee shop wifi apply: traffic interception, evil twin networks, and packet sniffing tools are all realistic threats if your connection is unencrypted.

A VPN running on your laptop or phone encrypts everything before it touches the shared network. Other users — and the hotspot operator — see only an encrypted stream pointed at a VPN server.

### Your Streaming Library Disappears at the Border

Streaming platforms license content by territory. The library your Netflix subscription covers at home is different from the library visible from an IP address in Portugal, Thailand, or Mexico. The moment your device shows a foreign IP, the platform switches to local content — often missing series, films, or sports coverage you planned to watch.

Connecting your VPN to a server in your home country before opening a streaming app restores your expected library. You are accessing content your existing subscription already covers, from a location where your account is registered.

## How a VPN Works on a Mobile Hotspot: Step by Step

Running a VPN over a hotspot connection works the same as running it over any other internet connection — but a few nuances are worth understanding.

1. **Your phone creates a hotspot** using your mobile data plan. Your laptop or tablet connects to the hotspot as if it were a regular wifi network.
2. **You open the [VPN app on](https://snipstack.io/vpn-app-on-android-2026/) your laptop** (or phone, if using the phone itself) and connect to a server.
3. **All traffic from that device is encrypted** before passing through the hotspot to the mobile carrier.
4. **The carrier sees only an encrypted stream** going to a VPN server. The content of your activity is invisible.
5. **The VPN server forwards your requests** to the internet, and responses come back through the same encrypted tunnel.

One important configuration note: install the VPN on the device you want protected, not necessarily on the hotspot source. If your laptop is the primary work device, run Surfshark on the laptop.
