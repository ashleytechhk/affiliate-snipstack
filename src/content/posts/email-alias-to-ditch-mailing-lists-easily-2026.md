## Why Mailing Lists Keep Coming Back

### How One Address Ends Up on Hundreds of Lists

Every account you've ever created, every checkout you've completed as a "guest," every contest entry and app sign-up — they all captured your email address. Some of those companies sell or share that address with marketing partners. Others get acquired, and the new owner monetizes the old list. A few just get breached, and your address ends up on a dump that spammers buy in bulk.

By the time you notice the flood, you have no idea which of the dozens of services you've used over the years is the source. Your single address has become public property, shared and resold in ways you can't see or control.

### Why "Unsubscribe" Doesn't Actually Fix It

Unsubscribing treats the symptom, not the cause. Even when a link works as advertised, it only removes you from that one list — while your address stays on file, ready to be added to the next campaign, sold to the next partner, or scraped by the next scanner. Some "unsubscribe" links exist purely to confirm your address is active, which makes the spam worse. There's no way to verify compliance, and no way to undo the exposure that already happened.

## FAQ

**Is an email alias the same thing as unsubscribing?**

No. Unsubscribing asks a sender to stop — and depends on them honoring it. An alias removes the address the sender has entirely, so the request isn't optional. If the mailing list keeps sending, deleting the alias ends it regardless of what the sender does.

**Will using an alias break accounts I actually want to keep?**

No, as long as the alias stays active. Alt ID aliases forward to your real inbox indefinitely — they don't expire on a timer the way disposable addresses do. You only lose access if you choose to delete that specific alias.

**Does the Gmail "+" trick work just as well?**

It's a reasonable free starting point, but many marketing platforms automatically strip anything after the "+" symbol, meaning the mail still arrives at your base address. It also doesn't stop your real inbox from being visible in the header of every message you send.

**How many aliases do I actually need to ditch mailing lists?**

Most people start with one alias per new sign-up going forward, plus a handful for recurring categories like shopping or newsletters. [Alt ID](https://snipstack.io/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-to-ditch-mailing-lists-easily-2026__faq) supports unlimited aliases, so there's no need to ration them.

**What happens to mail already sent to a deleted alias?**

Mail already in transit when you delete an alias may still arrive briefly, but new messages sent to that address afterward go nowhere. Within a day, the mailing list effectively stops reaching you.

**Can I still reply to something sent to an alias?**

Yes — replies from your real inbox go out normally. Some setups also support sending *from* the alias itself, which keeps your real address hidden in both directions if that matters for a particular sign-up.
