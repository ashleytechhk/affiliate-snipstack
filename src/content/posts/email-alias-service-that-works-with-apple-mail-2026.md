## [Apple Hide My](https://snipstack.io/apple-hide-my-email-vs-email-alias-2026/) Email: What It Does Well

Hide My Email is Apple's native alias system, available to iCloud+ subscribers on iPhone, iPad, and Mac. It generates unique, random addresses that forward to your real iCloud inbox — or to any email address you configure as your forward destination in iCloud settings.

### Deep Apple Integration

The standout advantage is how seamlessly Hide My Email sits inside the Apple ecosystem. When you fill out a web form in Safari, Mail, or supported apps, iOS and macOS offer to auto-suggest a Hide My [Email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-crypto-exchange-registration-2026/) directly in the keyboard. There is no separate app to open, no dashboard to navigate. The alias appears, you tap to use it, and the forward is configured automatically.

For someone who signs up to services exclusively on their iPhone or Mac and reads everything in Apple Mail, this level of integration is genuinely useful. It removes every point of friction from the alias workflow.

### It Is Already Paid For

If you are paying for iCloud+ — which most iPhone users with more than 5GB of storage are — Hide My Email costs you nothing extra. The entry-level iCloud+ tier that includes Hide My Email runs around $1 per month. For casual use, that is hard to argue with on price.

### Where Hide My Email Falls Short

The integration that makes Hide My Email convenient also limits it. It is built around iCloud and Apple devices. If you read email on an Android phone, a Windows PC, or a non-Apple client, the experience degrades. Forwarding works, but alias management — creating, viewing, and deleting addresses — requires an Apple device or iCloud.com.

The bigger practical limitation is the delete-only model. When an alias starts attracting spam, your only option is to delete it permanently. There is no toggle to temporarily disable forwarding while keeping the address in reserve. Once it is gone, you cannot recover it — meaning any service that was using that alias can no longer reach you until you update your account details with a new address.

For heavy alias users cycling through dozens of addresses across different categories of service, this becomes a real operational burden.

## Alt-ID: What It Does Well

Surfshark's Alt-ID is the [email alias](https://snipstack.io/how-email-alias-2026/) component of the Surfshark [privacy suite](https://snipstack.io/privacy-suite-for-couples-shared-household-2026/). It creates forwarding aliases that route email to any inbox you choose — including your iCloud address, which means everything lands in Apple Mail just as it would with Hide My Email.

### Forward to Apple Mail, Read Anywhere

Alt-ID is client-agnostic. You configure it to forward to your real email address — your iCloud, Gmail, or any other address — and from that point on, all alias mail arrives in whichever app you use to read that inbox. If you read your iCloud mail in Apple Mail on Mac, that is where Alt-ID email appears. If you occasionally check mail on a Windows laptop, that works too.

This flexibility matters for people who use multiple devices across different platforms, or anyone who might switch ecosystems in the future. Your aliases are not tied to Apple.

### Toggle, Don't Delete

The most practical advantage Alt-ID has over Hide My Email is the disable toggle. When an alias starts receiving spam, you can turn it off with one click from the Alt-ID dashboard. Forwarding stops immediately — the spam ends — but the alias still exists in your account. When you need to receive a legitimate email from that same service (a receipt, a password reset), you can re-enable it for a few minutes, then turn it off again.

Hide My Email forces you to delete and recreate. Alt-ID lets you manage aliases like circuit breakers rather than one-use fuses.

### Fake Name and Profile Generation

Beyond just an alias address, Alt-ID can generate a fake name and basic profile to go alongside the alias. When a sign-up form asks for your name and email together, you can provide fictional details that do not link back to your real identity. Hide My Email has no equivalent feature.

### Broader Privacy Context

A spam problem rarely exists in isolation. Your email address got exposed because your data is circulating — on breach databases, marketing lists, broker compilations. Alt-ID addresses the email angle; the broader Surfshark subscription includes a data removal tool that submits opt-out requests to data brokers on your behalf, and a VPN that stops your IP address from being associated with your browsing behaviour.

If you are receiving 50+ spam emails daily, there is a reasonable chance your data is spread more widely than just email. Alt-ID inside Surfshark addresses more of that surface area than a standalone alias tool can.

## FAQ

**Does Alt-ID actually work with Apple Mail?**

Yes. Alt-ID forwards email to whatever real address you configure — including your iCloud address. All forwarded mail arrives in Apple Mail on iPhone, iPad, and Mac exactly as normal email would. The only difference from Hide My Email is that alias management happens in the Surfshark app rather than in iCloud settings. [Set up Alt-ID here](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-service-that-works-with-apple-mail-2026__faq) and configure forwarding to your iCloud address during setup.

**Can I reply from an alias in Apple Mail?**

With Hide My Email, replying from an alias address works natively on Apple devices — Mail.app handles the from-address automatically for iCloud aliases. With Alt-ID, two-way alias reply is supported: when you reply to a forwarded message, the reply routes through Alt-ID's servers so the recipient sees your alias address, not your real one. In both cases, your actual email address stays hidden from the original sender.

**Will using an alias stop spam that is already in my inbox?**

Neither tool removes existing spam from a compromised address. Both tools prevent future exposure by substituting a real address with an alias on every new sign-up going forward. For existing spam hitting your real address, inbox filtering is the short-term fix — aliases are the long-term preventive layer for any new accounts you create.

**Is Hide My Email free?**

Hide My Email is included with any iCloud+ plan, which starts at around $1 per month. It is not available on a completely free Apple ID — you need at least the entry-level iCloud+ tier. If you already pay for extra iCloud storage, you almost certainly have access to Hide My Email already.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) to my Alt-ID aliases if I cancel my Surfshark subscription?**

Alt-ID aliases are tied to an active Surfshark subscription. If you cancel, forwarding on your aliases stops. Before cancelling, update any accounts using Alt-ID aliases to a different address, or note which services need updating. This is a meaningful lock-in consideration to weigh before [switching from](https://snipstack.io/switching-from-mcafee-to-all-in-one-privacy-suite-2026/) Hide My Email.

**Can I use both Hide My Email and Alt-ID at the same time?**

There is no technical barrier. Some users use Hide My Email for Apple service sign-ups (where the integration is seamless) and Alt-ID for everything else. The main overhead is managing aliases across two dashboards. If you prefer a single system, pick the one that fits your primary device and spam volume, and use it consistently.
