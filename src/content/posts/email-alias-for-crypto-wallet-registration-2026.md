## Why Your Crypto Registration Email Is a Target

Crypto platforms are unusually high-value targets for data brokers and spam operators. The reasoning is simple: anyone signing up for a wallet or exchange is, by definition, financially active and likely to respond to financial offers. When an exchange suffers a data breach — and they do, regularly — your email address ends up in dumps sold across underground markets.

The problem compounds because most people use one or two email addresses for everything. The moment one account is compromised, attackers have a direct line to all your other accounts, including your bank, your recovery addresses, and anything else linked to that inbox.

An [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-investment-account-notifications-2026/) breaks this chain. The alias is what gets exposed. Your real address stays hidden. If the alias gets abused, you delete it. Zero impact on your actual inbox or identity.

## SimpleLogin: Best for Power Users

SimpleLogin is an open-source alias service acquired by Proton in 2022. It offers more granular controls than Alt-ID: you can set per-alias display names, block specific senders, and route different aliases to different real inboxes. Paid plans unlock custom domains, PGP encryption for forwarded mail, and team sharing.

For a crypto-savvy user who wants surgical control over every alias, SimpleLogin is compelling. The trade-off is price and complexity — you pay for it separately (or subscribe to Proton Unlimited), and the dashboard has a steeper learning curve than Alt-ID's one-click extension.

**Best for:** developers, privacy enthusiasts, people already in the Proton ecosystem.

## AnonAddy: Best Free Option

AnonAddy (now "addy.io") offers a generous free tier with unlimited aliases, though bandwidth and the number of domains available are capped on the free plan. It is fully open-source and self-hostable, which appeals to technically confident users who want zero third-party trust.

The downside for casual crypto users: the free tier caps forwarded bandwidth, which can be surprising if a crypto newsletter you forgot about suddenly sends a high-volume campaign. Paid plans are cheap ($1/month), but at that price point, Alt-ID's bundled value wins on total cost per feature.

**Best for:** technically minded users who want free or self-hosted alias infrastructure.

## Apple Hide My Email: Only If You're All-In on Apple

Apple's built-in alias feature (part of iCloud+) is deeply integrated into Safari and iOS. For crypto registrations done on an iPhone or Mac, it works seamlessly — a "Use a unique email" button appears inside registration forms. The catch: you cannot reply from these aliases (they're receive-only), and the feature is effectively unusable outside the Apple ecosystem. If you use Chrome, Firefox, or Android for any crypto activity, Hide My Email won't help you there.

**Best for:** iPhone/Mac-only users who rarely interact with crypto on non-Apple devices.

## FAQ

### [Does using](https://snipstack.io/does-using-email-alias-protect-from-spam-2026/) an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-product-warranty-registration-2026/) make my crypto account more secure?

It reduces your attack surface. Phishing campaigns that target crypto users often start with email. If your alias address is not connected to your real identity and is unique per platform, a breach at one exchange cannot be used to cross-reference your accounts elsewhere. That said, an alias does not replace strong passwords or two-factor authentication — it layers on top of them.

### Can crypto exchanges detect that I'm using an alias?

Most cannot, and even those that can typically do not care. An alias is a valid, functional email address that sends and receives mail normally. It behaves identically to a real address from the exchange's perspective.

### Will I still receive important alerts (security notifications, withdrawal confirmations)?

Yes. Alias services forward all incoming mail to your real inbox. Security alerts, 2FA codes, withdrawal confirmations — everything arrives as normal. You reply from the alias if needed.

### [What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if an alias service shuts down?

This is a real risk with any third-party service. For Surfshark Alt-ID, the risk is mitigated by Surfshark being a well-funded, established company. For open-source alternatives like AnonAddy, self-hosting eliminates this risk entirely. Best practice: if you use aliases for high-value accounts, update the email on those accounts periodically to a fresh alias (or your real address) so you are not locked out if a service goes dark.
