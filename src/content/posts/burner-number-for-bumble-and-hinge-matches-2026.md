## Why Sharing Your Real Number on Dating Apps Is Riskier Than You Think

The moment a Bumble or Hinge conversation moves to SMS, the dynamic changes. Inside the app, your personal details stay protected behind a username. The second you hand over your real number, though, you have given a near-stranger a persistent link to your identity.

Here is what that link can open up:

**Reverse phone lookups.** Free tools like TrueCaller, Spokeo, and even basic Google searches can tie a phone number to a full name, home address, employer, and social media accounts in seconds. You may know someone for 48 hours and already have your full profile in their hands.

**Unwanted contact after a bad date.** If things do not go well, blocking on the app is straightforward. But once your number is out, calls, texts, and even WhatsApp messages keep coming — from an account you may not even recognize.

**Data harvested by the match's apps.** When someone saves your number in their contacts, every app on their phone that syncs contacts — Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, LinkedIn — can now cross-reference your profile and suggest you as a connection. You did not consent to any of that.

**SIM-swap vulnerability.** This sounds technical but it is real: fraudsters sometimes use personal details scraped from social media to impersonate you with your carrier and take over your number. The more freely your real number circulates, the more surface area exists for this attack.

None of this requires your match to have bad intentions. Data leaks happen. Phones get stolen. Apps get breached. Your real number is a permanent liability that outlasts any Bumble conversation.
