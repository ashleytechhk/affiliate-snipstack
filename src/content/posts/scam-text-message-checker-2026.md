## What Is Smishing? (And Why Texts Are Riskier Than Emails)

**Smishing** is the SMS equivalent of phishing. The word is a portmanteau of "SMS" and "phishing." A scammer sends you a text that impersonates a trusted sender — a bank, a government agency, a delivery company, or a retailer — and includes a link designed to steal your credentials, install malware, or trick you into handing over personal information.

Smishing has grown sharply in recent years for a straightforward reason: people are more guarded with email now. Spam filters catch the obvious stuff. But texts feel different. Your [phone number](https://snipstack.io/phone-number-privacy-for-social-media-2026/) is more personal than your email address, so a message to your number feels more credible. Texts also arrive in a space alongside messages from family and friends, which lowers your guard.

According to the US Federal Trade Commission, mobile phone scams — including smishing — cost consumers billions of dollars each year, and the numbers have risen every year since 2020.

### Common Types of Scam Texts in 2026

- **Delivery scams:** "Your package could not be delivered. Reschedule here." (Often impersonating FedEx, UPS, or USPS)
- **Bank fraud alerts:** "Unusual activity detected. Verify your account immediately."
- **Prize and lottery texts:** "You have been selected. Claim your reward."
- **Government impersonation:** Fake tax notices, benefit payment alerts, or fine notices
- **Two-factor authentication (2FA) harvesting:** Texts asking you to confirm a code you did not request

## What Does a Scam Text Message Checker Actually Do?

The term "scam text message checker" covers several different tools and approaches, not one single product. Here is what each category actually does.

### 1. Carrier-Level Spam Filters

Mobile carriers — including AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile — use network-level spam detection to block or flag known scam numbers before the text reaches your phone. This works automatically in the background and requires no setup. It catches high-volume blasts from known scam numbers but misses targeted or newly registered scam numbers.

### 2. Built-In Phone Settings

Both iPhone and Android include built-in spam text filtering:

- **iPhone (iOS 17 and later):** Go to Settings > Messages > Filter Unknown Senders. Texts from numbers not in your contacts are sorted into a separate list, with any links disabled until you decide to view them.
- **Android (Google Messages):** Spam protection is enabled by default. Go to Settings > Spam protection to confirm it is turned on. Google Messages uses on-device machine learning to flag suspicious texts before they land in your main inbox.

These tools are free and already on your phone. Turning them on takes about 30 seconds and provides immediate filtering benefit.

### 3. Link-Checking Tools

This is where most of the real work happens. When a scam text includes a link, the most dangerous moment is deciding whether to tap it. Link checkers evaluate the URL before you visit the page:

- They cross-reference the domain against security databases and blacklists
- They assess how recently the domain was registered (newly created domains are a common scam signal)
- They check whether the URL has been reported as a phishing or malware site

Surfshark's Scamcheck works at this layer — it evaluates links in real time as you browse, flagging suspicious destinations before you land on them. This is particularly useful when a scam text sends you to a lookalike site designed to harvest your login credentials.

### 4. Number Lookup Tools

These are useful for verifying whether a number that texted you is known to be fraudulent, though they rely on community reporting and may not catch newer scam numbers.

## Do You Need a Dedicated Scam Text Message Checker?

The honest answer is: it depends on how much scam traffic you already receive and how confident you are in recognising it.

**You are likely fine with free tools if:**
- You receive occasional scam texts and already feel confident spotting them
- You have already enabled iOS Filter Unknown Senders or Android spam protection
- You know to never tap a link in an unexpected text without verifying it another way

**A link checker adds real value if:**
- You receive frequent suspicious texts with links and want instant verification before deciding to tap
- Someone in your household — an elderly parent, for example — is more likely to tap a link before thinking it through
- You have been a victim of a scam before and want stronger protection going forward

**Breach monitoring is worth considering if:**
- You have received scam texts that seem oddly personalised (they know your name, the last four digits of your account number, or a service you actually use)
- You have been notified of data breaches involving services you use
- You want to understand which of your personal details are already in circulation

## [How to Check](https://snipstack.io/how-to-check-if-a-website-is-safe-2026/) a Suspicious Text Message Right Now

You just received a text you are not sure about. Here is [what to do](https://snipstack.io/what-to-do-after-malware-infection-2026/) before you tap anything.

### Step 1 — Do Not Tap the Link

This sounds obvious, but the urgency of scam texts is deliberate. Scammers write "your account will be frozen in 24 hours" specifically to override your caution. Give yourself 60 seconds before touching anything.

### Step 2 — Look at the Sender Number

Legitimate organisations — banks, government agencies, retailers — almost always send texts from a consistent short code or registered number. If you have received texts from your bank before, compare the number. Scammers sometimes spoof the display name but cannot spoof a short code.

### Step 3 — Search the Phone Number

Copy the number and paste it into Google, including any country or area code. Add the word "scam" or "fraud" to the search. If others have received the same scam text, you will find reports within seconds.

### Step 4 — Check the Link Before Tapping It

If you want to verify where the link actually leads without visiting it, copy the URL (on iPhone, press and hold the link — do not tap — then choose Copy) and paste it into a link checker. Surfshark's Scamcheck evaluates URLs against real-time threat databases and flags suspicious domains before you interact with them.

### Step 5 — Go Directly to the Source

If the text claims to be from your bank or a delivery company, do not use the link in the text at all. Open your bank's official app or type the company's real website address directly into your browser. If there is a real issue with your account, you will see it there.

![Checking a suspicious text link safely — step-by-step illustration](/og/scam-text-message-checker-2026-inline-2.png)

### Step 6 — Report the Scam Text

In the US, forward scam texts to **7726** (spells SPAM on your keypad). This free carrier reporting service helps networks flag the sending number and block similar texts for other users. In the UK, forward to **7726** as well — the same system operates across major carriers. On iPhone, you can also tap "Report Junk" beneath a message from an unknown sender.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a scam text message checker?

A scam text message checker is a tool, setting, or process that helps you identify whether a text message — or a link inside one — is fraudulent. This includes built-in phone filters (iPhone's Filter Unknown Senders, Google Messages spam protection), carrier-level blocking, manual number lookup, and real-time link checkers like Surfshark Scamcheck that evaluate URLs before you visit them.

### Can I check a phone number to see if it is a scam?

Yes. Paste the number into Google with the word "scam" added to your search, or use a community reporting site like 800notes.com. These aggregate user-submitted reports of scam numbers and often have results for active scam campaigns within hours of them starting.

### Is there a free scam text checker?

Several free layers of protection are already available to you: carrier spam filtering (automatic), iOS or Android built-in spam protection (free, one-time setup), and forwarding suspicious texts to 7726 (free, manual). For real-time link verification, [Surfshark Scamcheck](/blog/scamcheck?sub=scam-text-message-checker-2026__faq) is included with a Surfshark subscription, which also covers VPN and breach monitoring.

### What should I do if I already tapped a scam link?

Do not panic. Close the browser tab immediately. Do not enter any information on any page that loaded. Change the password of any account the text claimed to be from. If the link loaded a page that asked for payment details, contact your bank. Run a malware scan using your device's built-in security tools. Then report the text to your carrier by forwarding it to 7726.

### How do scammers get my phone number?

Most commonly through data breaches. When a company you signed up with is hacked, the stolen data — including phone numbers — is sold to scammers. Your number can also be harvested from social media profiles, public websites, or purchased from data brokers. Surfshark Alert monitors breach databases and notifies you when your phone number or email appears in a newly discovered breach, so you know when your data has been exposed.

### Will enabling spam protection on my phone stop all scam texts?

No. Built-in spam protection and carrier filtering catch a large proportion of mass scam texts, but targeted smishing campaigns — especially those using newly registered numbers — can still reach your inbox. Manual verification habits (checking the sender number, not tapping links, going directly to official sites) remain important even with filters enabled.
