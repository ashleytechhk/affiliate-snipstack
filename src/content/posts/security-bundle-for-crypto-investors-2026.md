## Why Legacy Suites Fall Short for Crypto Investors

![Crypto threat attack vectors explained — info-stealers, clipboard hijackers, phishing DNS](/og/security-bundle-for-crypto-investors-2026-inline-1.png)

### Clipboard Hijackers Don't Set Off Norton's Alarm

Clipboard hijacking is a category of malware that sits in memory and monitors your clipboard. When you copy a wallet address to paste into a transaction field, the hijacker silently replaces it with an attacker-controlled address. By the time you notice the funds went somewhere wrong, they're gone.

Norton and McAfee both rely heavily on signature-based detection — they catch malware they've seen before and catalogued. Clipboard hijackers are often custom, delivered via malicious browser extensions or bundled software, and they frequently evade signature databases long enough to do their damage. Surfshark's antivirus engine uses a mix of signature and heuristic detection with cloud-based analysis, giving it a better real-time read on novel process behavior.

### Browser Extension Info-Stealers Target MetaMask Directly

MetaMask, Phantom, and other wallet browser extensions store vault data locally. A class of malware called info-stealers — RedLine, Vidar, Raccoon Stealer — specifically targets browser extension storage directories and can extract encrypted MetaMask vaults, which attackers then attempt to crack offline.

Norton and McAfee will catch the known versions. The problem is these stealers ship in modified form constantly. Independent AV test results in 2025 showed that for zero-day and near-zero-day infostealer variants, cloud-connected detection engines consistently outperformed legacy definition-update models. Surfshark's AV uses cloud-backed analysis, meaning new threat signatures propagate faster than a weekly definition update.

You can read more about what successful device compromise looks like — and [how to check](https://snipstack.io/how-to-check-if-a-website-is-safe-2026/) for early signs — in our [guide to detecting if your PC has been hacked](https://snipstack.io/how-to-check-if-your-pc-is-hacked-2026/).

### DNS Phishing Blockers Catch What Browser Toolbars Miss

Norton Safe Web and McAfee WebAdvisor are browser extensions. They check URLs against a threat database when you visit a page. The limitation: by the time you've loaded the page, the domain has already resolved, and injected JavaScript has already run.

CleanWeb operates at the DNS layer. When your device tries to resolve a domain, CleanWeb checks it against a continuously updated blocklist before the connection is established. The fake exchange domain — the one registered two hours ago to mimic a legitimate DEX — never resolves. You never see the page. The phishing kit never runs.

For crypto investors, where a convincing-looking fake exchange can cost thousands in seconds, this distinction matters. We covered [how phishing attacks work and how to spot them](https://snipstack.io/how-to-avoid-phishing-attacks-2026/) in detail if you want to understand the mechanics.
