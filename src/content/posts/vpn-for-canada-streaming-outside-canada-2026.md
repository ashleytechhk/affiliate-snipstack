## What Is a VPN and Why Does It Matter for Canadian Streaming?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. It creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server you select. When your traffic exits through that server, every website and streaming service you visit reads the server's IP address — not yours.

For Canadian streaming content, that IP address swap has immediate, practical effects:

- **Geo-locked Canadian services** like Crave, CBC Gem, CTV, TSN Direct, and Sportsnet Now require a Canadian IP address. Outside Canada, they block access entirely — even for paying subscribers.

- **Expat access** — Canadians living or working abroad for extended periods often want their home content library and home-market subscriptions. A VPN with Canadian servers solves this without requiring a physical presence.

Think of it as a digital address change. The streaming platform asks "where are you?" — your VPN answers "Canada" — and access follows.

## How Canadian Streaming Geo-Blocking Actually Works

Streaming services identify your location through your device's IP address. IP addresses are registered to geographic regions by the same standards bodies that manage internet infrastructure. When your IP shows a Canadian address, platforms grant access to Canadian-licensed content. When it shows anything else, they check whether their licensing agreements cover your territory — and for Canadian-specific services, the answer is no.

The mechanism is not complicated, but the ongoing cat-and-mouse game is. Streaming services maintain blocklists of known VPN IP addresses.
