## 2. iOS Silence Unknown Callers

**Best for: iPhone users who want zero setup.**

Apple baked a surprisingly effective spam filter directly into the Phone app. Head to **Settings > Phone > Silence Unknown Callers** and toggle it on. Any number not in your contacts, recent calls, or Siri suggestions goes straight to voicemail without ringing your phone.

The limitation is obvious: it silences everyone you have not saved, including legitimate calls from new contacts. It also does nothing for SMS spam, and it does not prevent your number from being shared or sold. Think of it as noise reduction, not [privacy protection](https://snipstack.io/privacy-protection-software-review-2026/).

## 3. Android Call Screening (Google Pixel)

**Best for: Pixel owners who want AI-powered screening.**

Google's Call Screen feature, built into Pixel phones, uses on-device AI to intercept calls from unknown numbers and ask callers to identify themselves before the call connects to you. You see a live transcript in real time and decide whether to answer, mark as spam, or hang up.

It handles robocalls exceptionally well. On non-Pixel Android devices, the Google Phone app offers a reduced version of this feature — worth checking your app settings if you have not already. Again, this is reactive: your number is still out there; the phone is just intercepting the noise before it reaches your ears.

## 5. Nomorobo — Robocall Blocking via VoIP

**Best for: People on VoIP home lines or those whose carrier supports simultaneous ring.**

Nomorobo is free for VoIP landlines. On mobile, it becomes a paid product after the trial. The underlying mechanism — intercepting the first ring of a suspected robocall before it completes — is clever and works well when your carrier supports it.

For dating-app scenarios specifically, Nomorobo is less relevant because it targets mass-dial spam operations, not individual numbers. It pairs well with other tools on this list but probably should not be your only layer.
