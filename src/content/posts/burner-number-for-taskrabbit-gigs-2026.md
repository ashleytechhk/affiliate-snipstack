## Why This Is Different From Other Privacy Risks

Most people think about phone privacy in terms of spam calls or telemarketers. The TaskRabbit problem is different in three specific ways.

**Your profile already does half the stalker's work.** A bad actor doesn't need to do any detective work. Your face, approximate neighborhood, service area, and availability are laid out on your public profile. Add a phone number and they have a complete dossier built for them.

**The handoff is built into the platform.** You can't opt out of sharing a number with clients and still take gigs. The number exchange is baked into how TaskRabbit coordinates jobs. This isn't a case of oversharing — it's a structural feature of the service.

**The dating-to-gig bridge is one search away.** If you've given the same number to a dating match, that match can text it, check who it belongs to via reverse lookup, find your gig profile, see your photo, see your reviews, and know roughly where you work. The bridge in the other direction is equally short: a client with your number can run the same lookup and find your dating profile if your number is associated with it.

Most gig workers on TaskRabbit have never considered this because they think of work and dating as separate categories of life. Digitally, they're not. A phone number is a single thread that connects them.
