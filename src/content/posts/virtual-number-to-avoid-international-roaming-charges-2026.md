## The Bill Nobody Warned You About

International roaming fees have dropped over the past decade, but they have not disappeared. Carriers in North America, parts of Asia, and most of Latin America still charge per-SMS or per-minute rates the moment your phone connects to a foreign network — even if you pay for an "international plan." A burst of verification texts from a dating app during a weekend in Tokyo or Lisbon can quietly add $15 to $40 to your bill without a single voice call.

That is the roaming problem. But there is a second, more personal cost that rarely makes it onto the phone bill.

When you give your real number to someone on a dating app, you are handing over a piece of identifying information that is extremely hard to take back. Your mobile number is often tied to your real name through carrier records, social media accounts, and reverse-lookup services. A match who turns sour can use that number to find your address, flood your inbox, or simply refuse to stop calling. According to a 2023 survey by the Consumer Federation of America, roughly one in four dating app users reported receiving unwanted contact from someone they had met online — and most of those interactions started with a phone number exchange.

Combine the financial risk of roaming with the personal safety risk of handing out your real number, and you have a problem that needs a structural fix, not just "be more careful."

## Why Your Real SIM Is the Wrong Tool for Dating Abroad

Your SIM card was designed for one thing: connecting you to your carrier's network. Everything else — your name, your billing address, your payment history — is attached to that same card. When you travel, your carrier either passes those charges along through a roaming agreement or forces you to buy a local SIM (which means a new number, which defeats the point of using dating apps that are tied to your home number for verification).

Neither option is clean. Roaming is expensive. Local SIMs mean juggling two phones or two numbers. And in both cases, anyone you meet gets your actual contact details.
