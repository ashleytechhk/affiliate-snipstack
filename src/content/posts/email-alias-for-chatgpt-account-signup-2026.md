Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Midjourney, Notion AI — most users have signed up for four to ten AI tools in the last two years. Each registration adds another sender to your inbox. Your real email address is now held by all of them, and if any one service gets breached or sold, the address circulates.

**Startup email behavior is aggressive.** Early-stage AI companies depend on email engagement metrics to demonstrate retention to investors. That pressure translates directly into dense onboarding sequences, re-engagement campaigns, "we added a feature" announcements, and pricing nudges. A single trial sign-up can generate 20–40 emails in the first 90 days.

**Data partnerships move your address sideways.** Shared growth infrastructure — CRMs, analytics platforms, enrichment tools — means your email address may pass through companies you never directly interacted with. OpenAI itself operates under a comprehensive privacy policy, but every downstream integration has its own data handling.

A dedicated [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-example-2026/) for your ChatGPT account means none of this connects back to your real address.

### 2. Apple Hide My Email

Apple's iCloud+ feature generates random forwarding addresses in the format `randomstring@privaterelay.appleid.com`. If you already pay for iCloud storage at the 50 GB tier or above, this is available at no extra cost.

**Strengths for ChatGPT sign-ups:** Works natively across Apple devices and Safari. On iPhone or Mac, when you tap an email field on any sign-up page, Safari may suggest "Use Hide My Email" as an autofill option. For quick mobile sign-ups — creating a ChatGPT account from your iPhone — this is the lowest-friction option available.

**Limitations:** The `@privaterelay.appleid.com` domain is well-known, and some platforms apply extra scrutiny to it. More practically, it is not available outside the Apple ecosystem: no Chrome extension, no Android support, no path to use it on a Windows machine when signing up for a web tool. Managing aliases requires navigating iCloud settings rather than a dedicated dashboard.

**Verdict:** Strong if AI sign-ups mostly happen on your iPhone or Mac in Safari. Falls short for cross-platform users.

### 3. SimpleLogin

An open-source alias service with 15 free persistent aliases and an unlimited paid plan at around $4 per month. Acquired by Proton in 2022 and popular in privacy-focused communities.

**Strengths:** Works with any email provider. Browser extensions for Chrome and Firefox. Two-way aliasing — you can reply to emails *from* your alias address, keeping your real email hidden in outbound correspondence. Useful if you ever need to contact ChatGPT support or an AI tool's help desk without exposing your real address.

**Limitations:** The 15-alias free tier is generous but has a ceiling. If you actively test AI tools, that cap fills up. No fake-identity generator. The interface is more technical than Alt-ID.

**Verdict:** Solid choice for privacy-first users who want open-source infrastructure and reply-from capability. The free tier handles a limited stack; heavy AI tool testers need the paid plan or a different option.

### 4. Firefox Relay

Mozilla's alias service offers five permanent forwarding aliases free, with unlimited aliases on the Premium plan at $1.99 per month. A Firefox browser extension auto-suggests an alias when it detects an email field.

**Strengths:** The free tier is workable for minimal use — five aliases covers ChatGPT plus a few other tools. Firefox Relay strips tracking pixels from forwarded emails before delivery, which is meaningful given how heavily tracked AI tool emails tend to be.

**Limitations:** Five aliases is a tight cap for anyone who actively tests new tools. The browser extension is Firefox-only. Deleted aliases cannot be reactivated — only replaced with new ones, which requires updating your account email on the affected services.

**Verdict:** A reasonable free option for Firefox users with a small, stable set of AI tools. Heavy testers hit the cap quickly.

### 5. DuckDuckGo Email Protection

DuckDuckGo's free forwarding service provides a `@duck.com` address and lets you generate unlimited sub-aliases for individual sign-ups. Forwarded emails have tracking pixels stripped before delivery.

**Strengths:** Completely free. No complex account setup. The tracker-stripping is the most aggressive on this list. Unlimited sub-aliases mean you can create one per service without worrying about caps.

**Limitations:** You cannot permanently delete an alias — you can only pause it, which stops forwarding but leaves the address intact. There is no hard close equivalent to Alt-ID's deletion. The `@duck.com` domain is recognized as a privacy relay and may fail on stricter sign-up validators.

**Verdict:** The strongest free option for tracker blocking. Weaker than Alt-ID when you need to permanently cut off a service's email access.

## FAQ

**Can I use an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-to-avoid-spam-2026/) to sign up for a new ChatGPT account?**

Yes. Alt-ID aliases are full forwarding addresses that pass OpenAI's registration flow — including the verification email step. The alias behaves identically to a regular email address from OpenAI's perspective. You receive confirmation, receipts, and account notifications through the alias, forwarded to your real inbox. [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-chatgpt-account-signup-2026__faq) supports this out of the box.

**Will ChatGPT block alias or masked email addresses during sign-up?**

OpenAI does not currently block major alias providers like Alt-ID or SimpleLogin. Services that block well-known disposable-mail domains — Guerrilla Mail, Temp Mail, and similar — do so because those domains appear on public blocklists. Established alias providers use realistic domains that are not on those lists, so registration succeeds normally. Alt-ID's domain is the least likely to trigger validators of any option on this list.

**Can I change my existing ChatGPT account email to an alias?**

Yes. Log into your ChatGPT account, go to **Settings**, and update the email address to your new alias. OpenAI will send a verification email to the alias; it forwards to your real inbox for confirmation. Once verified, the alias becomes your active account email. This is the recommended approach if you already have an account and want to retroactively protect it.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if I delete the alias attached to my ChatGPT account?**

You lose access to account recovery emails and transactional messages from OpenAI until you update your account email. Before deleting any alias you still actively use, update your ChatGPT account email to a new alias or your real address first. [Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-for-chatgpt-account-signup-2026__faq) lets you manage active aliases from one dashboard — you can see exactly which services rely on which address before making changes.

**Does an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-ai-tool-subscriptions-2026/) affect ChatGPT Plus billing or subscription management?**

No. ChatGPT Plus billing is tied to your payment method, not your email address. You can sign up for a paid subscription, manage it, and receive receipts through an alias address without any billing complications. The alias is just the email on file — it has no effect on payment processing.

**If I'm already getting 50+ spam emails per day, will switching to aliases actually fix it?**

An alias fixes the leak point, not the existing flood. Spam arriving on your current real address will continue because that address is already circulating. The immediate benefit is stopping new exposure: every service you sign up for going forward gets an alias, not your real address. For existing spam, the most effective approach is filtering by sender domain and letting it exhaust itself. For ChatGPT and any future sign-ups, the alias prevents the same problem from compounding further.

**Is one alias enough, or should I use different aliases for ChatGPT plugins and integrations?**

One alias covers the ChatGPT account itself. For each plugin, connected third-party service, or platform that OAuth-links to your ChatGPT account, a separate alias gives you more precise tracking. If plugin-related spam starts on a specific alias, you immediately know which integration leaked. Alt-ID's unlimited model makes per-integration aliasing practical — you are not managing a cap.
