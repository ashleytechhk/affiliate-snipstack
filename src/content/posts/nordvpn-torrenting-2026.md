## What to Look for in a Torrenting VPN

Before the list: three technical criteria that actually matter for torrenting, beyond the standard VPN checkbox marketing.

**P2P server coverage.** Some VPNs only allow torrenting on designated servers — often fewer than 10% of their network. That limits your server options and often means worse performance. Full P2P support across all servers is better.

**Kill switch reliability.** If the VPN connection drops mid-torrent, your real IP can briefly leak to peers in the swarm. A kill switch that immediately halts network traffic when the tunnel drops is essential. Look for one that works at the OS level, not just the app level.

**No-logs policy (audited).** A VPN claiming "no logs" means nothing without third-party verification. Audited no-logs policies — confirmed by Cure53, PricewaterhouseCoopers, Deloitte, or equivalent — are the standard to hold providers to.

## FAQ

### Is torrenting legal?

The BitTorrent protocol is legal. What you torrent determines legality — Linux distributions, public domain content, open-source software, and files you own the rights to are all lawful to transfer via torrent. Users are responsible for understanding the copyright status of content they download or share. A VPN does not change the legal status of any content.

### Does NordVPN allow torrenting on all servers?

No. NordVPN designates specific P2P servers for torrenting, which are a subset of its overall network. The app makes these easy to find, and performance on NordVPN's P2P servers is strong. But you're working from a smaller server pool compared to providers that allow P2P across all servers.

### [Does Surfshark](https://snipstack.io/does-surfshark-one-include-email-protection-2026/) work for torrenting in 2026?

Yes. P2P is supported across Surfshark's entire server network with no dedicated-server restrictions. WireGuard protocol delivers fast speeds, the kill switch works at the OS level, and the no-logs policy has been independently audited by Cure53. **[Get Surfshark here](/blog/vpn?sub=nordvpn-torrenting-2026__faq)** if you want to test it within the 30-day refund window.

### What VPN protocol is best for torrenting?

WireGuard is the current recommendation for most users — it offers the best speed-to-overhead ratio and is the default on both Surfshark and NordVPN. OpenVPN is slower but more mature and works through more network configurations. For pure throughput on P2P, WireGuard is the clear choice.

### Can I use a VPN for both torrenting and streaming arbitrage?

Yes — this is exactly the setup where a VPN delivers the best cost-per-use ratio. The same subscription protects your torrent traffic and enables regional pricing on streaming services. Surfshark's unlimited device policy makes this particularly practical: you can run a torrent client on one device while streaming regionally-priced content on another, all under one subscription.

### [How does a](https://snipstack.io/how-does-a-vpn-protect-you-2026/) [VPN kill switch](https://snipstack.io/vpn-kill-switch-how-it-works-2026/) work for torrenting?

A kill switch detects when the VPN tunnel drops and immediately blocks all network traffic until the tunnel reconnects. For torrenting, this prevents your real IP address from briefly appearing to BitTorrent peers during a reconnection window. An OS-level kill switch (which blocks at the network driver level) is more reliable than an app-level kill switch (which only kills traffic from the VPN app's process). Surfshark, NordVPN, ProtonVPN, PIA, and Mullvad all offer OS-level kill switches.

### Is a free VPN good enough for torrenting?

No. [Free VPNs](https://snipstack.io/vs-free-vpns-2026/) consistently fail the three criteria that matter for torrenting: they typically log user data (that's the business model), don't maintain reliable kill switches, and throttle P2P traffic or block it entirely. The partial exception is ProtonVPN's free tier, which is legitimate but does not include P2P support. For torrenting specifically, a paid VPN is the only viable option.
