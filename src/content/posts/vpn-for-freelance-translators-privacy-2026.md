## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

A VPN is software that creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. All your internet traffic travels through that tunnel before reaching the open internet.

Think of it like sending a sealed, opaque envelope instead of a postcard. Anyone who intercepts the postcard can read every word. The sealed envelope arrives intact — the contents are only visible to the intended recipient.

For a freelance translator, this matters in three specific ways:

1. **Data in transit stays private.** When you upload a document to a client portal or download source files from a shared drive, the transfer is encrypted.
2. **Your IP address is masked.** Websites and platforms see the VPN server's IP, not yours. This limits tracking and profiling.
3. **Network eavesdropping is blocked.** On public Wi-Fi, anyone on the same network can potentially intercept unencrypted traffic. A VPN makes that interception useless because the data is encrypted.

The simple rule: any time you're not on your own secured home network, a VPN should be on.

## Common Questions From Freelance Translators About VPNs

### Will a VPN slow down my file transfers?

It adds a small amount of overhead — typically 5-15% on a good connection. With a quality VPN using WireGuard protocol, you are unlikely to notice a difference in day-to-day work. Large file uploads may take a few seconds longer.

### Is it legal to use a VPN?

In most countries, yes. VPN usage is legal in the vast majority of jurisdictions where freelance translators work. Check the laws in your country and any country you work with if you have specific concerns.

### Can my clients see that I'm using a VPN?

They can see a connection coming from the VPN server's IP address rather than your personal IP. This is not inherently suspicious — many businesses route all traffic through VPNs. If a client platform blocks VPN connections, you may need to disconnect briefly to access it.

### Does a VPN protect me from phishing emails?

No. A VPN encrypts your connection but does not filter malicious links in emails or block phishing sites by default. However, some VPNs including Surfshark include a CleanWeb feature that blocks known malicious URLs.
