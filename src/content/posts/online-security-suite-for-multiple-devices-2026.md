## Step 2 — Get Your Current [Security Suite](https://snipstack.io/security-suite-vs-paying-for-each-tool-separately-2026/) Off Cleanly

This step matters more than most guides admit. Running two antivirus products simultaneously causes driver conflicts, degraded performance, and gaps in protection where the two products misread each other as threats.

**For Norton:**

1. Open your Norton account at norton.com (do not cancel yet — just note your renewal date)
2. Turn off auto-renewal now if it's coming up in the next 30 days: Account Settings → Subscription → Turn Off Auto-Renewal
3. If you use Norton Password Manager, export your passwords first: in the Password Manager app, go to Settings → Export Passwords and save the file somewhere secure
4. Download Norton's official removal tool (Norton Remove and Reinstall) from their support site — a standard Windows uninstall leaves leftover drivers that interfere with new security software
5. Run the removal tool and restart your machine

**For McAfee:**

1. Log in to your McAfee account and turn off auto-renewal before proceeding
2. Use McAfee's dedicated removal tool (MCPR) rather than the standard Programs & Features uninstall — same reason as Norton: leftover components
3. Restart your machine after removal

**For other suites:** Check the vendor's support page for their official removal tool. Most major antivirus vendors provide one. A clean removal takes 5–10 minutes and prevents problems down the line.

## Troubleshooting

**"[Surfshark Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-antivirus-vs-kaspersky-comparison-2026/) is flagging a file I know is safe."**
This is called a false positive. Open the Antivirus section, find the flagged file in your scan results, and select **Mark as trusted** or **Exclude from scans.** If you're unsure whether a file is genuinely safe, search the filename alongside "safe or malware" before excluding it.

**"The VPN is slowing my internet connection."**
Switch to the **WireGuard** protocol in VPN settings — it's significantly faster than OpenVPN on most connections. Also try connecting to a server geographically closer to your location. Speed loss over a VPN should be minimal on WireGuard with a nearby server.

**"I still have Norton running alongside Surfshark."**
Stop both immediately and complete the Norton removal process from Step 2. Two active antivirus programs will conflict. The symptoms are sluggish performance and erratic threat alerts from both products simultaneously.

**"Surfshark won't install on my older Windows machine."**
Check that Windows is updated to at least version 10 (1903 or later). [Surfshark One](https://snipstack.io/surfshark-one-plan-comparison-2026/) supports [Windows 10](https://snipstack.io/windows-10-antivirus-free-download-2026/) and 11. If you're running Windows 7 or 8, an OS update is the prerequisite — running unsupported operating systems is itself a security risk regardless of [antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-for-iphone-2026/).

**"I forgot to cancel Norton before it renewed."**
Contact Norton's support team directly and ask about their refund policy — many users report successful refunds within 60 days. For McAfee, check your account's subscription terms for the cancellation window. Act immediately after noticing the charge rather than waiting.

**"Alert isn't finding any breach data for my email."**
No news here is genuinely good news — it means your email addresses have not appeared in known breach databases. Keep them monitored going forward in case future breaches surface older credentials.
