## The Risk Explained: When a Date Gets the Same Number as Your Child's School

Most dating safety advice covers physical precautions — meet in public, tell a friend where you are going. Far fewer people talk about what happens after the date when someone you barely know has the same number saved in their phone as your child's school has on file.

Consider how easily these scenarios unfold:

**The persistent match who won't stop.** You go on two dates and decide it is not working. They disagree. They text repeatedly, call at odd hours, and eventually show up somewhere they should not have known about — except your caller ID revealed your home neighborhood when you called from your landline.

**The data breach exposure.** Dating apps have a significant history of security incidents. When an app is compromised, linked phone numbers are among the first data points extracted and sold. If your dating number is the same as your school emergency contact number, that breach just exposed your entire family contact profile.

**The reverse lookup chain.** Someone with your number runs a free reverse phone lookup. Returns your name, general location, and linked social profiles. Your Instagram bio mentions your child's school by name for a sports team post. Now they know where your children are every weekday afternoon.

None of these scenarios require sophisticated planning. They require only carelessness from one app and one motivated bad actor.
