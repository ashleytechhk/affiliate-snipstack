## Why Your Phone Number Is a Privacy Risk on Dating Apps

Most people think sharing a phone number is safer than sharing a social media profile. It feels more controlled. The reality is the opposite.

Your phone number is tied to your identity in ways most people never think about. These lookups are not restricted to law enforcement. Anyone can pay for them.

Dating apps have improved their in-app messaging, but many daters move off-platform quickly. The moment you hand over your real number, you lose control over what that person can find about you.

## What Is TextNow?

TextNow is a free app-based VoIP service that gives you a US or Canada phone number. It has been around since 2009 and is widely used for budget-conscious texting and calling.

It works by routing all calls and messages through its app over Wi-Fi or data. The other person can text or call your TextNow number from any phone — they do not need the app themselves.

Key points:
- Free tier supported by ads shown inside the app
- Paid tiers [remove ads](https://snipstack.io/remove-ads-from-google-shopping-results-2026/) and add calling minutes
- Numbers are in US/Canada area codes only
- Numbers can be recycled if you do not use the app regularly (the free tier requires activity to keep your number)
- Many carrier-ID services flag TextNow numbers as "VoIP" or "VOIP TextNow" — visible to anyone who runs a quick search on your number

## When TextNow Makes Sense (And When It Does Not)

TextNow is not a bad product. For low-stakes situations — verifying an account, signing up for a service, receiving a one-time code — it does the job at zero cost.

For dating, the gaps matter more:

**VoIP detection**: As mentioned above, caller-ID services routinely flag TextNow numbers. First impressions matter in dating. Starting a conversation where your date has already flagged your number as suspicious is not ideal.
