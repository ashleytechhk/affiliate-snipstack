## Step 1 — Open [Windows Security](https://snipstack.io/windows-security-2026/)

Windows 11 has a built-in app called **Windows Security**. This is where you manage your free [antivirus](https://snipstack.io/vs-antivirus-2026/). Here is how to open it:

1. Look at the bottom of your screen for the **Start button** — it looks like four small squares (the Windows logo).
2. Click the Start button.
3. Type the words **Windows Security** using your keyboard.
4. Press **Enter** on your keyboard, or click the app that appears at the top of the list.

The Windows Security window will open. It has a simple dashboard with colored icons.

![Windows Security dashboard with green protection icons](/og/free-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026-inline-1.png)

**What you want to see:** Green checkmarks next to each item. Green means everything is working. If you see yellow or red icons, do not panic — keep reading. Steps 4 and 5 explain how to fix the most common problems.

## Step 2 — Check That Virus Protection Is Turned On

1. Inside the Windows Security app, click **Virus & threat protection**. It is usually the first item on the left side.
2. Look for the words **"No current threats"** or a green checkmark near the top of the page.
3. Scroll down slightly and look for **"Virus & threat protection settings."** Click **Manage settings** underneath it.
4. Make sure **Real-time protection** shows as **On** — the slider should be blue or green, pointing to the right.

Real-time protection is the part that watches your computer constantly and stops viruses the moment they try to enter. It should always be switched on.

## Step 3 — Update Your Virus Definitions (Very Important)

[Antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-for-iphone-2026/) uses a list of known threats called **virus definitions**. Think of it as a "most wanted" list. The older that list gets, the more new threats can slip past. Windows usually updates this list automatically, but it is worth checking.

1. Still inside **Virus & threat protection**, scroll down until you see **Virus & threat protection updates**.
2. Click **Check for updates**.
3. Windows will connect to the internet and download the latest list. This usually takes less than a minute.
4. When it finishes, you will see a message like "Definitions are up to date."

**Why does this matter?** Hackers release new viruses every single day. An up-to-date definition list makes sure Defender can recognize and stop the newest threats.

## Step 4 — Run a Quick Scan Right Now

A Quick Scan checks the places on your computer where viruses most often hide. It takes about 5 to 10 minutes and is safe to run while you are using your computer.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click the **Quick scan** button. It is a large button near the top of the page.
2. A progress bar will appear showing the scan is running.
3. Wait for it to finish. You can still browse the internet or check your email while the scan runs.
4. When it is done, you will see either **"No current threats"** or a list of items that were found.

**If threats are found:** Click **Start actions**. Windows will remove or safely contain the threat for you. You do not need to do anything technical yourself.

## Step 5 — Fix It If Defender Is Turned Off

Sometimes Defender turns itself off — usually because another antivirus program has taken over, or a setting changed after a Windows update. Here is how to check and fix it:

1. Open **Windows Security** (follow Step 1 above).
2. Click **Virus & threat protection**.
3. Click **Manage settings** under "Virus & threat protection settings."
4. If **Real-time protection** is off (slider is grey and pointing left), click the slider to turn it back on.
5. Windows may ask for your password. Type it in and click **Yes**.

**If you cannot turn it on:** Another antivirus program may be running instead. That is fine — as long as that program is active and up to date, you are still protected. To check, look for an antivirus icon in the bottom-right corner of your screen (near the clock).

## Step 6 — Turn On the Windows Firewall

Defender Antivirus stops viruses inside your computer. The **Windows Firewall** is a separate tool that blocks dangerous connections from coming in over the internet. Both need to be on.

1. In **Windows Security**, click **Firewall & network protection** in the left menu.
2. You will see three sections: **Domain network**, **Private network**, and **Public network**.
3. Each one should say **"Firewall is on."**
4. If any says "off," click that section, then click the toggle to turn the firewall back on.

Most seniors will only use "Private network" (your home Wi-Fi) and "Public network" (at a cafe or library). Make sure both show as on.

## Step 7 — Schedule a Full Scan Once a Month

A Quick Scan is fast but only checks common spots. A **Full Scan** checks every single file on your computer. It takes longer — anywhere from 30 minutes to a few hours — but you should do one at least once a month.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click **Scan options** (you will find this link below the Quick scan button).
2. Select **Full scan** by clicking the circle next to it.
3. Click **Scan now**.
4. Leave your computer on and let it finish. You can walk away — it will keep running on its own.

**Tip:** Run your Full Scan on a weekend morning when you are not planning to use the computer. Leave it plugged in so the battery does not run out.

## Troubleshooting Common Problems

### I get a pop-up saying "Your PC is infected — call this number"

**Do not call that number.** This is a scam. Real Windows security warnings never ask you to call a phone number. Close your browser by pressing **Alt + F4** on your keyboard, or simply restart your computer. Then open Windows Security and run a Quick Scan to check that everything is fine.

### Windows Security will not open

Restart your computer first. If it still will not open, go to **Start > Settings > Windows Update** and install any updates that are waiting. Then try again. Updates often fix problems like this.

### Defender keeps turning itself off

This usually means another security program is installed and conflicting with Defender. Go to **Start > Settings > Apps**, scroll through the list, and look for any antivirus program you did not intentionally install. If you find one, you can uninstall it — Defender will turn itself back on within a few minutes.

### Scans are taking more than four hours

A Full Scan on a computer with a lot of files can take a long time. If it has been running for more than four hours with no sign of finishing, restart your computer and run a Quick Scan instead. Then try the Full Scan again when you have more time.

### My computer feels very slow during a scan

This is normal. A Full Scan uses a lot of your computer's resources. Try to schedule scans for times when you are not actively using the computer — overnight or during a meal break works well.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there really a free antivirus already on Windows 11?

Yes. [Microsoft Defender Antivirus](https://snipstack.io/windows-defender-vs-paid-antivirus-seniors-2026/) is built into every copy of Windows 11 at no charge. You do not need to download, buy, or subscribe to anything to use it. As long as Windows is up to date, Defender is active and working in the background.

### How do I know if my Windows 11 antivirus is working?

Open Windows Security (click Start, type "Windows Security," press Enter). If you see green checkmarks next to "Virus & threat protection" and "Firewall & network protection," your computer is protected. A yellow or red icon means something needs attention — follow Steps 4 and 5 in this guide to fix it.

### Can I add another free antivirus on top of Defender?

Generally, no. Running two full antivirus programs at the same time causes conflicts and can actually make your protection worse — they interfere with each other. Windows will usually disable Defender automatically when it detects a second antivirus. Pick one and use it properly instead of running two at once.

### Does Defender protect me from email scams?

Partially. Defender can catch malicious file attachments in emails, but it does not evaluate the content of the emails themselves. It will not warn you that a message claiming to be from your bank is actually a scam. Learning to spot phishing emails — unexpected urgent requests, links that look slightly wrong, pressure to "act now" — is the best additional protection here.

### What is the difference between a virus and a scam?

A virus is a software program that hides on your computer and causes damage — slowing it down, stealing files, or locking you out. A scam is when a real person (or an automated system) tricks you into giving away your money, passwords, or personal information. [Antivirus software](https://snipstack.io/antivirus-software-2026/) stops viruses. Stopping scams also requires awareness, caution, and sometimes a web protection tool that flags dangerous websites. **[Surfshark Antivirus](/blog/antivirus?sub=free-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026__faq)** covers both device-level threats and common online scam tactics.

### How often should I scan my computer?

Defender scans files automatically in the background every day, so you do not need to do anything manually for routine protection. Running a manual Quick Scan once a week and a Full Scan once a month gives you extra assurance. Run an extra scan any time you notice your computer acting strangely — running slowly for no reason, showing unexpected pop-ups, or opening programs you did not ask for.

### Is it safe to download free antivirus from other companies?

Be careful. There are many websites that offer "free antivirus" downloads but are actually scams themselves — they install unwanted software or steal your information. If you want a third-party option, only download from the official company website, and only after researching the company. For most Windows 11 users, the built-in Defender is a safer and simpler choice than hunting for a trustworthy third-party free tool.
