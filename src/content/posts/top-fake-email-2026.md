### 2. Guerrilla Mail — Best [Free Burner](https://snipstack.io/free-burner-number-app-2026/) Address

Guerrilla Mail has been around since 2006 and remains one of the most reliable free options for quick throwaway email. You land on the site and you're instantly assigned a random address — no signup, no password, nothing. Incoming mail appears within seconds in an in-browser inbox.

You can also compose and send from your Guerrilla Mail address, which is useful if a service needs email verification and a reply. The inbox persists for about an hour by default, though messages can be scrambled after that. Guerrilla Mail also lets you choose your own username on several domains it controls, so if you want something slightly more memorable for a specific signup, you have that option.

The limitation is obvious: once you leave the page or enough time passes, the address is effectively gone. For one-shot signups where you just need to click a confirmation link, this is perfectly fine. For anything requiring ongoing communication or account recovery, you'll need something more durable.

### 3. 10 Minute Mail — Fastest No-Frills Disposable

The name explains everything. 10 Minute Mail generates a random address the moment you visit, gives you a live countdown, and lets you read incoming mail directly on the page. If you need more time, you can extend by another 10 minutes with one click.

It's stripped-down by design — there's no username customization, no send capability, and the inbox disappears when the timer hits zero. For pure speed (arrive, grab address, confirm signup, leave), it's as frictionless as it gets. The site is also clean and ad-light compared to some alternatives, which matters when you're trying to act quickly.

Where 10 Minute Mail falls short is anything beyond instant use. No persistence, no forwarding, no way to retrieve messages later. Think of it as a one-use pass, not a privacy system.

### 4. SimpleLogin — Best Open-Source Alias Manager

SimpleLogin takes a different approach: rather than generating burner addresses that expire, it creates permanent [email alias](https://snipstack.io/how-email-alias-2026/)es that forward to your real inbox. You can create as many aliases as you want (unlimited on paid plans), route them to different real addresses, and delete any alias if it starts attracting spam.

The open-source nature of SimpleLogin is a genuine differentiator. The code is publicly audited, and you can even self-host if you're technically inclined. The free tier allows up to 15 aliases, which is enough for light users. Paid plans unlock unlimited aliases, custom domains, and priority support.

SimpleLogin was acquired by Proton in 2022, which has reassured privacy-focused users about the long-term trajectory. If you're already using ProtonMail, the integration is tight — your aliases and real inbox live in the same ecosystem.

### 5. Apple Hide My Email — Best for iPhone / Mac Users

If you're in the Apple ecosystem, Hide My Email is already built into your device. It's part of iCloud+ (included with any paid iCloud storage plan from 50GB upward). When you sign up for an app or service, Safari can suggest a unique, randomly generated email that forwards to your Apple ID inbox.

Each alias is permanent by default and can be deactivated from the iCloud settings on any Apple device. Apple doesn't read the forwarded email content, and the alias system is end-to-end opaque to senders — they only ever see the icloud.com alias, not your real address.

The catch is that it's Apple-only. If you use an Android phone or a Windows machine as your primary device, this isn't a realistic option. And if you ever leave the Apple ecosystem, managing or migrating your aliases becomes complicated. Within its intended context though, it's seamless — and there's no extra app to install or service to sign up for.

### 6. AnonAddy — Best for Power Users Who Want Full Control

AnonAddy (now rebranded as addy.io) is a privacy-first alias service with feature depth that appeals to people who want granular control. You can create unlimited standard aliases on free shared domains, use your own custom domain, and even set per-alias reply-from addresses so recipients see only the alias, never your real email.

The free tier is functional but capped — you get bandwidth limits on forwarded mail. Paid plans remove those limits and unlock features like catch-all addresses (any email sent to @yourdomain.com forwards to your inbox) and GPG encryption of forwarded mail.

Like SimpleLogin, AnonAddy is open-source and can be self-hosted. The UI is more technical than some alternatives, but that's intentional — this is built for users who want maximum configurability, not a one-click solution.

### 7. Temp Mail — Best for Fast Mobile Access

Temp Mail offers both a website and a mobile app (iOS and Android), making it one of the more accessible options for generating disposable addresses on the go. Open the app, and you have a working inbox immediately. Incoming mail shows up with push notifications if you leave the app open.

The mobile app focus is what distinguishes Temp Mail from similar services. If you frequently sign up for things on your phone — app store downloads, loyalty programs, in-store Wi-Fi portals — having a dedicated app is more convenient than navigating to a browser-based tool every time.

Addresses persist for a few hours, after which they're cleared. There's no account required for the free version, though a premium option extends inbox life and offers more domain choices.
