## Why Your Phone Number Is the Biggest Risk on Dating Apps

Most people focus on avoiding dangerous in-person meetups. That caution is right, but the threat often starts much earlier — the moment you hand over your digits.

Here is what happens when a bad actor has your real number:

- **Reverse phone lookups** can surface your full name, address, and employer within seconds using free tools.
- **Social media cross-referencing** — your number is likely attached to your Facebook, Instagram, or WhatsApp account. One search and your entire profile is visible.
- **Spam and harassment campaigns** — unwanted texts and calls that do not stop, even after you block one number.
- **SIM-swapping attacks** — a sophisticated but increasingly common scam where a criminal convinces your carrier to transfer your number to their device, bypassing two-factor authentication on your bank and email accounts.

The dating app itself may protect your in-app messages, but the second you move to text, you have handed a stranger the key to your entire digital life.
