## Why "Just Don't Click Ads" Is Not a Strategy

The attack vector has changed. In 2025, you do not have to click anything to get hit.

**Drive-by malvertising** loads a malicious payload the moment an ad renders in your browser or app WebView. No interaction required. The ad network serves what it thinks is a legitimate display ad; a compromised creative or a redirect chain buried three hops deep delivers something else. By the time your phone renders the pixel, the connection is already made.

**Tracking SDKs** compound the problem. AdMob — Google's ad network — is present in more than 40% of all apps on the Google Play Store, according to AppBrain's SDK penetration data. Meta Audience Network, AppLovin, and Unity Ads add further coverage, meaning a typical smartphone user passes through four or five ad network SDKs for every hour of app use. Each SDK is a persistent background process logging your behavior and phoning home — often to servers you cannot inspect and in jurisdictions with no meaningful data protection.

**Public Wi-Fi amplifies both threats.** On an unencrypted coffee shop or airport network, ad requests travel in plaintext. A network-level attacker can inject their own content into legitimate ad slots — a technique documented in academic research and used in real-world hotel and airport network attacks.

None of this is solved by being careful. It is solved by blocking at the network level before the request reaches your device.

## The Privacy Side: What Stops When Tracking Stops

Ad blocking is the visible benefit. Privacy protection is the larger one.

When CleanWeb blocks a tracker call from an app, that app can no longer report your behavior to a third-party data broker. Over time, this means:

Mobile trackers are persistent. An app you downloaded three years ago may still be making daily requests to an analytics server you cannot see. Blocking at the DNS level silences those requests without requiring you to audit your app library.

The phishing and malware domain blocking adds a different kind of protection: it is a real-time safety net.
