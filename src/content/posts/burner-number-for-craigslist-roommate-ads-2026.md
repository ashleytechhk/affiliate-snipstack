## Why Craigslist Roommate Ads Are a Privacy Trap in 2026

Craigslist does not verify identities. It never has. The roommate-search category is especially risky because the intent is genuinely to meet a stranger who will live in your home. That is a perfectly normal goal. But it means you are advertising your contact details to an unfiltered audience that includes not just prospective housemates but also:

## What Actually Happens When Your Number Leaks

Let's be specific about the chain of events.

**Step 1 — Your number hits a data broker.** Automated scrapers scan Craigslist continuously. Within hours of your post going live, your number has likely been added to a marketing database. You will start receiving robocalls and SMS spam.

**Step 2 — A reverse lookup reveals your name.** Free services like Spokeo, BeenVerified, and White Pages let anyone search a phone number and see the subscriber's name, approximate address, and sometimes social profiles. All it takes is two minutes and zero technical skill.

**Step 3 — A bad actor has your home neighborhood and your number.** This is the step where a harassment situation or a stalking situation becomes possible. You are no longer anonymous. You are findable.

**Step 4 — You try to delete the Craigslist post.** Too late. The number has already been scraped. Deleting the listing does not remove your number from the databases that already collected it.
