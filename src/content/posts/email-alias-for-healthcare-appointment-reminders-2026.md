### Gmail's `+tag` Trick

Gmail lets you append anything to your address: `yourname+clinic@gmail.com`. Mail arrives in the same inbox, and in theory you could filter and bulk-delete later.

In practice, healthcare platforms and patient engagement tools frequently strip the `+tag` suffix before storing your email. The tag disappears, your base address lands in the CRM, and every partner mailer that follows reaches your permanent inbox — the one you can never revoke. You're left with no kill switch and no way to identify which registration caused the flood.

There's also no [protection against](https://snipstack.io/protection-against-fileless-malware-attacks-2026/) secondary data use. Even if the tag survives, your real Gmail address is now inside the provider's marketing stack. Deleting the tag filter later removes inbox organization, not the underlying exposure.

### Apple Hide My Email

Apple's service (available with iCloud+ from $0.99/month) creates aliases that forward to your iCloud address and can be deleted directly from iOS Settings or iCloud.com. It integrates into Safari autofill, which makes it convenient for in-browser sign-ups.

The limitation is platform lock-in. If your primary inbox is Gmail, Outlook, or anything outside iCloud, you either need to add iCloud as a forwarding address or check a second inbox — neither of which is seamless. It also doesn't generate alternative personal data. Your real name still enters the clinic's intake form.

For people already in the Apple ecosystem who pay for iCloud+, Hide My Email is a workable option. For anyone else, the friction is real enough to make a purpose-built alias tool more practical.

### Free Throwaway Services (Guerrilla Mail, 10 Minute Mail)

Throwaway services give you a temporary address you check in a separate browser tab. No sign-up required, nothing to install, zero cost.

For healthcare appointment reminders, they fail on the two requirements that matter most:

1. **Blocked at registration.** Healthcare platforms and patient portals check submitted addresses against known throwaway domains. Guerrilla Mail and 10 Minute Mail domains appear on most of these lists. Your registration attempt either gets rejected or silently degraded — you may not receive confirmation emails at all.

2. **They don't forward and they don't last.** Your appointment reminder arrives to an inbox that expired hours or days before the appointment. You won't see it. Throwaway services require you to keep a tab open and refresh manually — completely impractical for a care relationship that spans weeks or months.

Throwaway services are the right tool for clicking a single confirmation link and never returning. They are the wrong tool for any ongoing communication.

Free throwaways satisfy none of these beyond the first point. Gmail's trick satisfies delivery but not revocation. A persistent forwarding alias with on-demand deletion — Alt-ID being the most practical option in 2026 — satisfies all four.
