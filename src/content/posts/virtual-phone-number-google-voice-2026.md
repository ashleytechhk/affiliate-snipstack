## Step-by-Step: Setting Up Google Voice for Dating

### Step 1 — Check Eligibility

Google Voice personal accounts are available in the United States only. You need an existing US phone number to verify and a Google account. If you are outside the US, this path is blocked entirely.

### Step 2 — Sign Up at voice.google.com

Go to voice.google.com, sign in with your Google account, and click "Get a voice number." You will be asked to choose an area code.

### Step 3 — Verify With Your Real Number

Google requires you to link a real forwarding number. This is a critical privacy note: Google knows your actual number. The [virtual number](https://snipstack.io/virtual-number-for-google-account-verification-2026/) is a mask to outside callers, not to Google.

### Step 4 — Choose Your Number

Search by area code or city. Pick a number and confirm. You now have a second number that forwards calls and texts to your real device.

### Step 5 — Install the Google Voice App

Download the Google Voice app (iOS or Android). Set notifications on. Texts from dating matches will arrive here rather than your main Messages app.

![Google Voice setup flow and Alt-Number comparison](/og/virtual-phone-number-google-voice-2026-inline-1.png)

### Step 6 — Use the Number on Dating Apps

On apps like Tinder, Bumble, or Hinge, when transitioning to SMS, share your Google Voice number instead of your real one. Incoming messages route to the app.

### Limitations You Will Hit

- **Spam filtering is aggressive.** Google Voice frequently flags messages from unknown numbers, including dating matches, as spam. You may miss texts.
- **Verification codes often fail.** Some services (including certain dating apps themselves) refuse Google Voice numbers for SMS verification because they are flagged as VOIP.
- **Your Google account is the identity.** If your Google account is linked to your real name, your number is traceable.
- **No separation from your Google profile.** Any activity on Google Voice contributes to your Google data profile.
