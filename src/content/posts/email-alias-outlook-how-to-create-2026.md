## How to Create an [Email Alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-healthcare-appointment-reminders-2026/) in Outlook (Microsoft's Built-In Method)

Outlook's native alias system is the fastest no-cost option for personal Microsoft accounts. Here is the shortest path to a working alias.

### Step 1 — Go to Your Microsoft Account

Open [account.microsoft.com](https://account.microsoft.com) in any browser and sign in. In the top navigation, click **Your info**, then select **Manage how you sign in to Microsoft**.

> **Note:** This only works for personal Outlook.com, Hotmail.com, and Live.com accounts. Work or school Microsoft 365 accounts managed by an organisation cannot use this feature — speak to your IT admin instead.

### Step 2 — Add an Alias

On the alias management page, click **Add email**. Select **Create a new email address and add it as an alias**. Choose a username and a domain from the dropdown (outlook.com, hotmail.com, or live.com), then confirm. Microsoft emails a verification code to your primary address — enter it to activate the alias.

### Step 3 — Set Up a Sorting Rule

All alias mail lands in the same inbox as your primary address by default. To separate it, go to **Settings → Mail → Rules** inside Outlook.com and create a rule:

- **Condition:** Recipient address contains [your new alias]
- **Action:** Move to folder → [a new folder, e.g., "Alias Mail"]

### Step 4 — Use the Alias — and Know the Limit

Paste the alias into sign-up forms whenever a site asks for your email. Everything delivered to that alias arrives in your inbox (or sorted folder), and your primary address is never exposed. When a specific alias gets noisy, delete it and create a replacement.

**The ceiling:** Microsoft caps personal accounts at 10 aliases total. Once you hit 10, you cannot add more without deleting an existing one. For most people managing a handful of persistent addresses, this is fine. For anyone who [signs up across dozens of services](/email-alias-for-online-shopping-2026/), 10 fills up fast.

## Where Each Option Falls Short

**Outlook's built-in aliases — the real limitations:**

The 10-alias limit sounds workable until you add it up. Shopping, newsletters, forums, work, banking, travel bookings, streaming trials — you're at 7 before you've covered the basics. And because Outlook aliases are tied to Microsoft accounts only, you cannot use them if your primary inbox is Gmail or another provider. There is also no toggle: once you delete an alias you've used widely, it's gone and you cannot reclaim it if you later want to use it again.

**Alt-ID — what it doesn't offer:**

Alt-ID does not support custom domains. If you want aliases at your own domain (e.g., `you@yourname.com`), you need a different tool — [SimpleLogin](/simplelogin-vs-altid-comparison-2026/) is the main alternative for that use case. Alt-ID also requires a Surfshark subscription, so it is not free. If you genuinely only need two or three permanent aliases and your inbox isn't in crisis, the built-in Outlook route costs nothing.

## FAQ

**Can I create an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-for-investment-account-notifications-2026/) in Outlook without a Microsoft account?**

No. Outlook's native alias feature is only available for personal Microsoft accounts — Outlook.com, Hotmail.com, and Live.com addresses. If you use Outlook as a desktop client but your actual mailbox is hosted elsewhere (such as a corporate Exchange server or Gmail), the Microsoft alias feature does not apply. Alt-ID works regardless of which provider hosts your primary inbox.

**How many aliases can I have in Outlook?**

Personal Microsoft accounts support a maximum of 10 aliases. That includes any combination of outlook.com, hotmail.com, and live.com addresses. Once you reach 10, you must delete one before adding another. Alt-ID has no published hard cap — you can generate as many as your workflow demands. [Get started with Alt-ID](/blog/altid?sub=email-alias-outlook-how-to-create-2026__faq) if the 10-alias limit is already a problem.

**Will a service reject an alias address when I sign up?**

Some services detect and block known alias or forwarding domains. This is more common with dedicated alias services than with Microsoft's native aliases, which look like standard outlook.com or hotmail.com addresses and are rarely flagged. If an Alt-ID alias gets rejected by a specific site, you can try a different alias from a different domain if Alt-ID provides multiple, or fall back to an Outlook built-in alias for that particular account.

**Can I send email from my Outlook alias, not just receive it?**

Yes for Outlook built-in aliases. When composing a new message in Outlook on the web, click the **From** field and select your alias address — the recipient sees the alias, not your primary address. Alt-ID also supports two-way communication: mail you receive at an alias can be replied to via the alias forwarding system, keeping your real address out of the reply header.

**Does deleting an Outlook alias delete my emails?**

No. Deleting an Outlook built-in alias removes the address and stops future delivery, but emails already received remain in your mailbox. The same applies to Alt-ID: disabling or deleting an alias stops new forwarding but does not remove mail already delivered to your inbox.

**Is an [email alias](https://snipstack.io/email-alias-service-that-works-with-apple-mail-2026/) the same as a forwarding address?**

They are related but not identical. A Microsoft Outlook alias is a second identity on the same mailbox — no forwarding involved, mail arrives directly. Alt-ID is a forwarding alias: it is a separate address at Surfshark's domain that routes mail to your real inbox. Both hide your primary address from the outside world; they just use different plumbing.
