## What Is a Home Media Server — and Do You Have One?

A home media server is any device in your home that stores and shares files — photos, videos, music, or recorded TV — across your home network. You might have one and not even realize it.

Common examples include:

- A **Plex or Jellyfin server** running on an old computer or laptop
- A **NAS drive** (Network Attached Storage) — a small box that stores files you access from your TV or phone
- A **desktop PC** you leave on all the time so your family can stream movies from it
- A **Raspberry Pi** set up to store and share home videos

If any of these sound familiar, you have a home media server. And if it is connected to your home Wi-Fi — which it almost certainly is — it needs protection.

## Why Hackers Target Home Media Servers

You might be wondering: why would anyone bother hacking my home media server? I am not famous. I do not have millions in the bank.

Here is how hackers actually think. They do not target individuals — they scan millions of internet addresses automatically, looking for any device that responds. Your media server responds. The moment it does, automated tools probe it for weaknesses.

Once inside, a hacker can:

- **Lock your files with ransomware** — demanding payment before you can access your own photos and videos again
- **Use your device as a relay** — running illegal activity through your home internet connection, which could get you in serious trouble
- **Steal personal information** — names, addresses, and any documents stored on the server
- **Watch your home network** — using your media server as a foothold to reach your computer, phone, and tablet

The worst part? Most media servers run silently 24 hours a day. You never look at them. A hacker could be inside your device for weeks before you notice anything wrong — if you notice at all.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I really need antivirus just for a media server?

Yes. Any device connected to your home network can be targeted, and media servers are particularly attractive to hackers because they run constantly and are rarely monitored. Antivirus adds a layer of protection that prevents your stored files — including irreplaceable family photos and videos — from being stolen or locked by ransomware.

### My media server is just an old computer — is it worth protecting?

Absolutely. Older computers running older software are actually more vulnerable, not less, because they may have security weaknesses that have never been patched. Protecting an older device is just as important as protecting a new one — sometimes more so. **[Surfshark Antivirus](/blog/antivirus?sub=antivirus-for-home-media-server-2026__faq)** works on older Windows and Mac systems and can help catch threats that have been sitting undetected for a long time.

### Can hackers really find my home device without knowing my address?

Yes. Automated scanning tools probe millions of internet addresses every minute looking for any device that responds. Your media server, if it is accessible through your router, can be discovered without anyone specifically targeting you. This is why protection matters even if you feel like an unlikely target.

### Will antivirus slow down my media server?

Modern antivirus is designed to run quietly without disrupting normal use. Surfshark Antivirus in particular is lightweight — it monitors in the background without noticeably affecting streaming or storage performance. You may notice a slight slowdown during a full scan, which is normal. Schedule those for overnight or times when you are not using the device.

### What if I already have antivirus on my main computer?

That only protects your main computer — not the media server, unless the media server software is running on that same computer. If your media server is a separate device (like a NAS drive or a second computer), it needs its own protection. If it runs on the same Windows or Mac computer you use daily, and that computer has antivirus installed, check that the antivirus covers all files on that machine, not just the ones you personally use.

### Is it too late if I have had a media server for years with no protection?

It is never too late to start. Installing antivirus and running a full scan can find and remove threats that have already made it onto your device. It cannot undo damage that has already been done — for example, files that were already stolen — but it will stop any ongoing threats and prevent new ones going forward.
