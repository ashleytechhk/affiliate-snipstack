## What You Need Before You Start (Prerequisites)

Before following the steps below, make sure you have:

- A computer running **Windows 11** (any version — Home, Pro, or S Mode)
- An active internet connection so Defender can download the latest threat updates
- Your Windows account password handy (you may be asked for it)

That is all. You do not need to buy anything or install any extra software to use Microsoft Defender.

## Step 1 — Check That Microsoft Defender Is Turned On

First, let us make sure Defender is active on your computer.

1. Click the **Start** button (the Windows logo at the bottom-left of your screen).
2. Type **"[Windows Security](https://snipstack.io/windows-security-2026/)"** and press **Enter**.
3. The Windows [Security app](https://snipstack.io/security-app-bundle-with-webcam-protection-feature-2026/) opens. Look for **Virus & threat protection** — it should show a green checkmark.

![Windows Security dashboard showing green checkmarks](/og/microsoft-defender-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026-inline-1.png)

If you see a green checkmark next to "Virus & threat protection," Defender is on and working. If you see a red or yellow warning, keep reading — Step 5 covers how to fix it.

---

## Step 2 — Make Sure Virus Definitions Are Up to Date

Defender needs fresh "definitions" — a list of known threats — to catch the newest viruses. Windows usually updates these automatically, but it is worth checking.

1. In the **Windows Security** app, click **Virus & threat protection**.
2. Scroll down to the section called **Virus & threat protection updates**.
3. Click **Check for updates**.
4. Windows will download any new definitions. This usually takes less than a minute.

**Why does this matter?** Hackers create new viruses every day. An out-of-date definition list is like a security guard who has not seen the latest list of known troublemakers.

---

## Step 3 — Run a Quick Scan

A Quick Scan checks the most common places where viruses hide. It takes about 5–10 minutes.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click the **Quick scan** button.
2. A progress bar appears. Let it run — you can still use your computer while it scans.
3. When it finishes, you will see either **"No current threats"** or a list of items found.

**What to do if threats are found:** Do not panic. Click **Start actions** and Windows will remove or quarantine the threat for you automatically.

---

## Step 4 — Run a Full Scan (Recommended Once a Month)

A Full Scan checks every file on your computer. It takes longer (30 minutes to a few hours) but is more thorough.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click **Scan options**.
2. Select **Full scan**.
3. Click **Scan now**.
4. Let the scan run. Your computer may feel a little slower during this time — that is normal.

**Tip:** Schedule your full scan for a time when you are not using the computer, such as overnight or during lunch.

---

## Step 5 — Turn On Real-Time Protection (If It Is Off)

Real-time protection watches your computer constantly, stopping threats before they can cause harm. It should always be on.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click **Manage settings** under "Virus & threat protection settings."
2. Make sure **Real-time protection** is toggled **On** (the toggle should be blue).
3. If it is off, click the toggle to turn it on. Windows may ask for your password.

If you cannot turn it on, another antivirus program may have taken over. That is usually fine — just make sure that other program is also up to date.

---

## Step 6 — Check Windows Firewall Is Active

Defender Antivirus and the Windows Firewall work as a team. The Firewall blocks unwanted connections from outside your computer.

1. In the **Windows Security** app, click **Firewall & network protection**.
2. You will see three network types: Domain, Private, and Public.
3. All three should say **"Firewall is on."** If any says "off," click it and toggle the Firewall back on.

![Windows Firewall settings panel with all three networks active](/og/microsoft-defender-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026-inline-2.png)

## Step 7 — Review Your Protection History

Want to see what Defender has caught recently? You can view a full log.

1. In **Virus & threat protection**, click **Protection history**.
2. A list appears showing any threats detected, quarantined, or removed.
3. If a file was quarantined that you believe is safe, you can click it and choose **Restore** — but only do this if you are certain the file is not harmful.

## Troubleshooting Common Problems

### Defender says it is turned off but I cannot turn it back on

This usually means another antivirus program is installed and has taken control. Open your list of installed programs (Start > Settings > Apps) and check whether a third-party antivirus is listed. If you no longer want that program, uninstall it and Defender will reactivate automatically.

### Scans are taking forever

A Full Scan on a large hard drive can take several hours. Leave the computer plugged in and let it finish. If it seems truly stuck for more than 4 hours, restart your computer and try again.

### I keep getting pop-up warnings that say "Your PC is infected — call this number"

**Do not call that number.** These are scam pop-ups designed to trick you. Close your browser by pressing **Alt + F4**, or restart your computer. Then run a Quick Scan in Windows Security to confirm your PC is clean.

### Windows Security itself will not open

Restart your computer. If the app still will not open, go to Start > Settings > Windows Update and install any pending updates, then try again.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Microsoft Defender Antivirus free on Windows 11?

Yes, it is completely free. Microsoft includes it with every copy of Windows 11 at no extra charge. You do not need to buy a subscription or enter a credit card.

### Does Defender update automatically?

Yes. As long as your computer is connected to the internet and Windows Update is enabled, Defender downloads new virus definitions automatically — usually several times a day. You do not need to do anything.

### Can I use Microsoft Defender alongside another antivirus?

In most cases, no. Running two full antivirus programs at the same time can cause conflicts and slow your computer down. Windows will usually disable Defender automatically when it detects another antivirus. Choose one and stick with it.

### Does Defender protect me on public Wi-Fi?

No. Defender watches your files and programs, but it does not secure your internet connection. On public Wi-Fi — in a coffee shop, library, or airport — your browsing can potentially be seen by others on the same network. For that kind of protection you need a VPN or a full [security suite](https://snipstack.io/security-suite-for-android-and-windows-together-2026/). **[Surfshark's security tools](/blog/surfshark?sub=microsoft-defender-antivirus-for-windows-11-2026__faq)** cover both device threats and connection privacy.

### How often should I run a manual scan?

Defender scans automatically in the background, so you do not need to run one every day. A manual Quick Scan once a week and a Full Scan once a month is a good routine. Run an extra scan any time you download a file from an unfamiliar source or notice your computer behaving strangely.

### What is the difference between a quarantined file and a deleted file?

When Defender quarantines a file, it moves the file to a safe, locked folder so it cannot cause harm — but it keeps a copy in case the detection was a mistake. A deleted file is gone permanently. Defender usually quarantines first, giving you a chance to review the decision.
