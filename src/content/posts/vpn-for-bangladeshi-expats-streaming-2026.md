## What Exactly Is a VPN?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. When you connect to the internet normally, your device talks directly to websites and apps. Those services see your real location — your IP address reveals the country you are in.

A VPN puts a secure tunnel between your device and the internet. Instead of your real IP address, websites see the IP address of the VPN server you connected to. If that server is in Bangladesh, websites think you are in Bangladesh. If the server is in the UK, they think you are in the UK.

Two things happen as a result:

1. **Geo-blocks disappear.** Content libraries that are restricted to certain countries become accessible.
2. **Region pricing becomes available.** Subscription services — Netflix, Spotify, YouTube Premium, and others — charge different prices depending on the country. A server in a lower-cost country gives you access to that country's pricing.

This is not a loophole that requires technical expertise. You install an app, pick a server location, and turn it on. That is the entire workflow.
