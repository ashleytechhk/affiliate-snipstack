## What Is a VPN, Exactly?

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. The name sounds technical, but the concept is straightforward: a VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a server operated by the VPN provider. All your internet traffic travels through that tunnel before reaching the open web.

Here's what that means in practice:

- **Your real IP address is hidden.** Websites and services see the VPN server's IP address, not yours.
- **Your traffic is encrypted.** Anyone monitoring your connection — your ISP, a coffee shop router, a public hotspot — sees scrambled data instead of readable content.
- **Your apparent location changes.** If you connect to a Netherlands server, you appear to be browsing from the Netherlands, regardless of where you physically are.

These three effects — IP masking, encryption, and location shifting — are the foundation of everything a VPN does for privacy and for subscription savings.

## How Netherlands Server Privacy Actually Works

When you connect to a VPN server located in the Netherlands, the process looks like this:

1. Your device encrypts all outgoing data before it leaves your network.
2. The encrypted data travels to the Netherlands VPN server.
3. The server decrypts the data and forwards your request to the destination website or service.
4. The response comes back to the Netherlands server, gets encrypted again, and is sent back to you.

From the perspective of any website you visit, the request originated in the Netherlands. Your actual location, your real IP address, and the contents of your request are invisible to them.

From your ISP's perspective, they can see that you're connected to a VPN server — but they cannot see what you're doing. The encrypted tunnel blocks that view entirely.

This is what "Netherlands server privacy" actually means: your traffic appears to originate in the Netherlands, and the journey there is shielded from outside observers.

## What to Look for in a Netherlands VPN

Not every VPN is equally useful for Netherlands server privacy. Here's what matters:

### Server Reliability

A Netherlands server that's congested or frequently offline defeats the purpose. Look for providers with multiple server locations within the Netherlands — Amsterdam in particular is one of the world's most significant internet exchange points, which means lower latency and more reliable connections.

### No-Logs Policy

For genuine privacy, the VPN provider shouldn't keep records of your activity. A no-logs policy that has been independently audited is the gold standard. This means even if someone compelled the VPN provider to hand over data, there'd be nothing meaningful to share.

### Encryption Standards

AES-256 encryption is the current benchmark. Paired with modern protocols like WireGuard or IKEv2, it provides fast connections without sacrificing security.

### Kill Switch

A kill switch cuts your internet connection if the VPN drops unexpectedly. Without it, your real IP could be exposed for a few seconds during a reconnect — enough to leak your location to a service you're trying to access privately.

### Unlimited Devices

If you're managing multiple streaming accounts or devices (TV, phone, laptop, tablet), an unlimited simultaneous connections policy means one subscription covers everything.
