## The Risk You Are Taking With an Outdated Security Stack

The threat landscape in 2026 is not the same one your legacy suite was designed to handle.

Modern malware is increasingly credential-focused rather than device-focused. The goal is not to brick your laptop — it is to harvest your passwords, financial details, and identity data silently over weeks or months. That shift has made VPN protection and breach monitoring as important as antivirus scanning, which is exactly why selling them as separate paid add-ons is a problem.

Consider the exposure chain: your work email is breached in a corporate data leak. You use the same password on a shopping account. That shopping account has your home address and a saved card. A credential-stuffing attack hits the shopping site within days of the breach becoming available on dark web forums. Without a breach alert service watching your email addresses, you have no way of knowing this chain started until the fraudulent charge appears.

Legacy antivirus catches the virus. It does not break the chain above, because that chain never involves a virus — it involves credentials and identity data, not executable malware. A product that only scans for malware is not a complete security solution in 2026.
