## Why Healthcare Professionals Have Different Privacy Needs

Clinicians, nurses, allied health workers, and administrative healthcare staff browse a pattern that most consumer security products aren't designed around:

- **Public and semi-public Wi-Fi** — hospital break rooms, medical conference hotel networks, clinic waiting areas
- **Multiple devices** — personal phone, home laptop, travel tablet, potentially a shared workstation at a satellite location
- **High-value account targets** — clinical email addresses and health system accounts are attractive for credential stuffing attacks
- **Sensitive personal research** — clinical literature access, medication lookups, patient condition research done on personal devices outside the EHR

None of this requires a product marketed specifically to healthcare. It requires a product that handles your *personal* devices and *personal* accounts effectively — the layer outside what your employer's IT department manages.

That's where Norton 360 and McAfee have historically underdelivered, not because their antivirus is bad, but because the suite model forces you to buy five features to get the two you want, then nickel-and-dimes you on device limits and VPN data caps.
