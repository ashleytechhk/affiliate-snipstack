## What Is a VPN? (A Plain-English Definition)

VPN stands for Virtual Private Network. The name sounds technical but the concept is simple: a VPN is software that connects your device to the internet through a server in a location you choose, rather than sending your traffic directly from wherever you are physically sitting.

When you connect to a VPN server in Prague, your internet traffic exits through that Prague server. Every website and streaming platform you visit sees a Czech IP address — the digital equivalent of a local postal address. They have no visibility into the fact that you're actually in Vienna, Dublin, or Melbourne.

This location-masking capability is what makes a VPN useful for accessing geo-restricted streaming content. Alongside that, a VPN encrypts your internet traffic, which adds a layer of privacy — but for Czech and Slovak TV access specifically, the server location feature is the one that matters.

## Why Czech and Slovak Streaming Platforms Block Viewers Abroad

Broadcasting rights are licensed on a territory-by-territory basis. When CT (Czech Television) acquires rights to a documentary series or sports fixture, the license covers only the Czech Republic. When Markíza broadcasts a Slovak drama or live football, the rights extend to Slovak territory.

Streaming these programs to viewers outside those licensed territories would put the broadcaster in breach of its licensing agreements. To stay compliant, platforms check the IP address of each incoming viewer. If your IP says you're in the Netherlands or the United States, you get the error message. The content itself is right there — you're simply blocked by your geographic location.

The main Czech and Slovak streaming platforms that apply these geo-restrictions include:
