The fix is clean separation: one number for listings, a different number for your personal and dating life. Neither connects the two contexts.

## Google Voice — Best Free Option With Real Limits

Google Voice is free, available without a SIM swap, and gives you a US number you can use for Craigslist listings immediately. For light listing use — selling something once every few months, no concern about the number being flagged — it covers the basics.

The limitations are real and increasingly relevant in 2026:

**Craigslist itself recognizes many Google Voice number ranges.** Listings posted with known VoIP numbers sometimes trigger Craigslist's flagging system, particularly in high-volume categories.

**Dating apps increasingly block Google Voice numbers at verification.** If you're trying to verify a new Hinge account or create a Bumble profile with a Google Voice number as your [second number](https://snipstack.io/get-a-second-number-for-texting-2026/), rejection rates have climbed as platforms tighten their VoIP detection.

**It's US only.** If you're outside the US, Google Voice is not an option.

**The number is tied to your Google account.** That's a meaningful privacy consideration if your goal is keeping your contact information siloed from your broader online identity.

Google Voice remains the go-to free choice for US users who aren't concerned about VoIP flagging and don't use the number for dating app verification. For anyone who does both — list on Craigslist and date actively — its limitations show up quickly.

## TextNow — Free but Most Easily Flagged

TextNow gives you a free US number with texting and calls over WiFi. The free tier is ad-supported. It works for Craigslist listings in a technical sense — you get a number, you put it in the listing, people can text it.

The problem is that TextNow numbers are among the most widely recognized VoIP ranges in use. Craigslist users who run reverse lookups on numbers before calling them (more common than you'd think, particularly in categories like housing) will see the number flagged as VoIP. Some listing categories on Craigslist have informal community norms around not texting VoIP numbers.

On dating apps, TextNow numbers fail SMS verification at a high rate. If you're using this number for both Craigslist listings and dating life, expect repeated friction at app signup screens.

TextNow is worth trying if you want to test the concept of a private listing number before spending money. But for anyone dating actively alongside listing on Craigslist, it's the weakest option on this list.
