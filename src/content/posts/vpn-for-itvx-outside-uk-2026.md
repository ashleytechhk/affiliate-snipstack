## Why ITVX Is Geo-Locked to the UK

Geo-blocking is driven by rights licensing, not arbitrary restriction. When ITV acquires broadcast rights for a show, those rights are typically sold territory by territory. A programme that ITV commissioned may have its international rights sold to a separate distributor in Australia, North America, or continental Europe. If ITVX allowed global access, it would undercut those distribution deals and, eventually, the commercial relationships that fund new commissions.

The mechanism is simple: every time you load ITVX, its servers check your IP address against a database of known geographic locations. A UK IP passes. Everything else gets the wall.

This means the fix is equally simple in principle — you need a UK IP address. In practice, the wrinkle is that ITVX (like most major streaming platforms) also maintains a blocklist of known VPN server IP ranges. Get a VPN that isn't actively blocked, and you're in.

## How a VPN Works for ITVX

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — creates an encrypted connection between your device and a server run by the VPN provider. When you connect to a UK server:

1. Your real IP address is hidden from sites you visit.
2. Your traffic appears to originate from the VPN server's UK IP.
3. ITVX reads a UK IP and grants access.

The VPN isn't exploiting a loophole in ITVX's code. It's presenting a valid UK address, which is all the geo-check requires. The rest of your traffic — browsing, other apps — is also encrypted and routed privately while the VPN is active, which is a useful side benefit when you're on hotel Wi-Fi or public networks.
