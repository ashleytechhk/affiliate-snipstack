## What Is the Dark Web, and Why Should a Retired Couple Care?

The internet you use every day — your bank's website, email, online shopping — is only a small part of what's out there. The **dark web** is a hidden section that requires special software to access. It is where stolen data gets bought, sold, and traded: leaked passwords, credit card numbers, medical records, and Social Security numbers, often stolen years ago from companies you never even knew got hacked.

You do not need to visit the dark web to be affected by it. If your email and an old password from some retailer's 2019 data breach show up for sale, a criminal can use that information to guess your way into your bank login, your Medicare portal, or your email account — the one that controls password resets for everything else.

**Dark web monitoring** is a service that quietly checks these leaked databases for your information and tells you the moment something with your name or email appears.

## Why Scammers Target Retired Couples Specifically

![Illustrated flowchart showing how scammers find and target retired couples using leaked dark web data](/og/antivirus-with-dark-web-monitoring-for-retired-couple-2026-inline-1.png)

Criminals are strategic, not random. Retired couples check a lot of boxes that make them attractive:

- **Stable, sizable savings.** Decades of contributions to retirement accounts mean there is real money to chase.
- **Two identities, one household.** A shared address, joint accounts, and overlapping information give scammers twice the opportunity to find a way in.
- **Trust in official-looking communication.** Emails that look like they're from a bank, Medicare, or the Social Security Administration are taken at face value more often.
- **Older accounts, older passwords.** Many retirees have used the same email address and a handful of passwords for fifteen or twenty years — plenty of time to appear in multiple breaches.
- **Less day-to-day monitoring.** Adult children may check on parents' safety, but rarely check their parents' digital footprint.

Scammers do not need to hack you directly. They only need one leaked password from an old account to start building a convincing story.
