## What Is a [Temporary Email](https://snipstack.io/temporary-email-stop-spam-2026/) and Password — and Why You Need One

A temporary email address is a disposable alias that forwards mail to your real account. When a site asks for your email during signup, you hand over the alias instead of your real address. If that site leaks your data or sells it to spammers, the alias takes the hit — not your actual inbox. You delete the alias, the spam stops.

A temporary password works the same way. Instead of reusing your personal password (which then appears in breach databases), you generate a strong, random password tied only to that throwaway account.

Together, a temp email and password form a firewall between your digital identity and the internet's endless appetite for personal data.

## Troubleshooting

**I am not receiving forwarded emails.**
Check that your forwarding address is correct in the Alt-ID settings. Also check your real inbox's spam folder — forwarded mail occasionally triggers spam filters on first delivery. Mark one as "not spam" and subsequent messages will come through cleanly.

**The website rejected my alias.**
Some aggressive spam filters block email domains they do not recognize. In this case, try generating a different alias — Alt-ID offers multiple alias domains. Alternatively, use a service-specific alias with a more familiar-looking domain if Alt-ID provides that option in your plan.

**I forgot which alias I used for a site.**
Open the Alt-ID panel and check the alias list. If you named or labeled the alias when you created it, it will be easy to find. Going forward, use the label field during creation ("Netflix trial," "that shoe site," etc.).

**My generated password was not accepted.**
Some older sites cap password length at 12 or 16 characters or disallow certain symbols. Use the password generator's length and character controls to produce a compliant password without weakening it significantly.

## FAQ

**Is a temporary email address actually private?**
With Alt-ID, yes. Your real address is stored in your Surfshark account only, encrypted. The websites you sign up for see only the alias. Surfshark does not sell your data to third parties.

**Can I use the same alias on multiple sites?**
You can, but it defeats the purpose. Using a unique alias per site means you can trace exactly which service caused a spam leak. Create one alias per signup for maximum signal.

**Will this work for two-factor authentication emails?**
Yes. 2FA emails sent to your alias forward to your real inbox just like any other email. There is a slight delay inherent in forwarding, but it is typically under 30 seconds — well within the validity window of most 2FA codes.

**[What happens](https://snipstack.io/what-happens-when-email-is-in-data-breach-2026/) if Surfshark goes down?**
During any service interruption, emails to your alias may queue or bounce temporarily. For critical accounts, keep your real address as a backup recovery option in the account settings of that service.

**How many aliases can I create?**
Alt-ID allows multiple aliases per account. The exact limit depends on your plan tier. For most users, even a modest limit is more than enough — the goal is one alias per service, and most people interact with far fewer services than they think.
